## Combat and Hacking Mechanics

The combat in Pragmata is a blend of precision shooting and resource management. Hugh utilizes a variety of firearms and melee tools, but his most potent asset is the "Overdrive Protocol." This mode significantly increases damage output and speed, allowing you to tear through tougher robotic guards.

### Working with Diana
Diana is not just a passive observer. Throughout the **pragmata demo steam** experience, you will need to protect her while she interfaces with the station's mainframe. 
*   **Scanning:** Use the 'Q' key (on PC) to scan the environment for terminals.
*   **Hacking:** Diana can disable emergency lockdowns, allowing Hugh to progress into new sectors.
*   **Combat Support:** During the final boss fight in the demo against Edith, Diana can initiate hacks that temporarily stun the sector guard, opening a window for Hugh to strike.

### Enemy Types in the Demo
1.  **Walkers:** Basic robotic units. They are dangerous in groups but can be dispatched easily with well-placed headshots.
2.  **Long-Range Drones:** These enemies hover out of reach and require precise aiming to take down.
3.  **Sector Guard (Edith):** The final encounter of the demo. Edith uses "Overdrive" protocols and requires the player to balance aggressive attacks with defensive dodging.

| Action | Control (PC Default) | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scan Environment** | Q | Highlights objectives and terminals |
| **Interact/Hack** | E | Commands Diana to interface with objects |
| **Overdrive Mode** | Shift + R | Temporary boost to all combat stats |
| **Dodge Roll** | Space | Avoid incoming projectiles and melee hits |

## Exploring the Lunar Research Station

The atmosphere of Pragmata is one of its strongest selling points. As you move through the station, you will find lore fragments detailing the sudden malfunction of the "bots" and the frantic attempts by the staff to contact Earth. The level design is vertical, often requiring Hugh to drop down into maintenance shafts or use elevators to reach the top floors.

Capcom has hidden several "secret" data logs within the demo. Finding these provides more context on Hugh's background and the nature of Diana’s existence. Replaying the demo is encouraged, as certain environmental interactions change upon subsequent playthroughs, revealing new dialogue between the two protagonists.
