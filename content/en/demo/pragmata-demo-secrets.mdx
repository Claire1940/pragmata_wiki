## Unlocking Pragmata Demo Secrets: The Hidden Elevator Enemy

The most significant secret currently found in the Pragmata demo is the hidden elevator enemy. This encounter is not just a bonus challenge; it is a mandatory requirement for players who want to unlock Diana’s second drawing in the sketchbook. To trigger this encounter, you must follow a very specific set of rules during the elevator sequence where you are attacked by three standard security bots.

The core requirement is weapon variety. You cannot simply blast through the bots with your primary weapon if you want the secret boss to appear. Instead, you must ensure that each of the three bots is "finished off" using a different damage source.

### The Different Weapon Rule

To successfully trigger this secret, you must manage the health of the three initial bots carefully. While you can weaken them with any weapon, the final blow—the shot that reduces their health to zero—must be unique for every bot.

| Bot Number | Recommended Finishing Weapon | Alternative Option |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bot 1** | Standard Pistol | Melee Strike |
| **Bot 2** | Shockwave Gun | Grenade/Explosive |
| **Bot 3** | Hacking/Terminal Override | Heavy Weaponry |

> **💡 Tip:** It is often safer to use only one weapon per bot from start to finish. This prevents accidental "over-damage" where a stray bullet from the wrong gun might accidentally land the killing blow.

### Managing Damage Sources

One common point of confusion among players is how "multi-hack" damage interacts with this secret. Based on community testing, damage dealt via the multi-hack ability does not disqualify you from the secret, provided the final killing blow on each bot remains distinct. As long as you do not finish off more than one enemy with the same single damage source, the hidden enemy will spawn once the third bot falls.

## Diana’s Sketchbook and Hidden Drawings

The Sketchbook serves as the demo’s primary progression tracker. While most players will naturally unlock the first few entries by playing through the main path, the second drawing is notoriously difficult to find without knowing the **pragmata demo secrets** associated with the elevator fight.

### How to Get the Second Drawing
The second drawing is directly linked to the hidden boss mentioned above. Once you have successfully defeated the three bots with different weapons, a unique, high-tier enemy will enter the arena. Defeating this hidden bot completes the requirement. After the combat encounter ends, check the Sketchbook menu; Diana will have added a new illustration depicting the encounter.

| Drawing Slot | Requirement | Reward/Lore Hint |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Drawing 1** | Complete the Tutorial | Introduction to the Moon Base |
| **Drawing 2** | Defeat the Secret Elevator Boss | Insight into "Intruder" detection |
| **Drawing 3** | Find the Hidden Cat Hologram | Information on Earth's "Ghost" data |

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you accidentally kill two bots with the same weapon, you must restart the demo or reload the checkpoint before the elevator sequence. There is currently no way to "reset" the fight once the elevator reaches its destination.

## Combat Mechanics and Weapon Variety

To master the **pragmata demo secrets**, you need a firm grasp of the protagonist's arsenal. The demo provides a limited but versatile set of tools that interact with the environment and enemies in unique ways.

### 1. The Standard Pistol
This is your most reliable tool for precision. When trying to unlock the secret boss, use the pistol for the first bot. Its low damage per shot makes it the easiest weapon to use when you need to ensure you don't accidentally kill the enemy too early with a different source.

### 2. The Shockwave Gun
The Shockwave Gun is excellent for crowd control, but it has a wide spread. When using this for the second bot, try to lure the bot away from its companions. If the shockwave hits and kills two bots simultaneously, it counts as using the same weapon for both, which will void the secret unlock.

### 3. Hacking and Environment Interaction
The hacking mechanic is perhaps the most "Pragmata" element of the combat. By hacking terminals or the bots themselves, you can turn the environment against your foes. For the third bot in the secret sequence, using a terminal hack to deliver the final blow is a highly effective and stylish way to satisfy the "different weapon" requirement.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I unlock the secret enemy if I use the same weapon but different ammo types?**

A: No. The game tracks the weapon ID for the killing blow. To satisfy the requirements for the **pragmata demo secrets**, you must switch to a completely different tool in your inventory (e.g., switching from the Pistol to the Shockwave Gun).

**Q: Does the secret boss drop any special loot?**

A: In the demo, the secret boss does not drop physical loot that carries over, but it is the only way to unlock the second drawing in Diana's Sketchbook, which is considered the "ultimate" collectible for this version.

**Q: Is there a time limit to complete the elevator challenge?**

A: There is no strict timer, but if the elevator reaches its destination floor before you have defeated the bots, the encounter ends automatically. It is best to work quickly but carefully to ensure the killing blows are correct.

**Q: Will progress from the demo secrets carry over to the full game?**

A: Capcom has not confirmed direct save transfers, but traditionally, completing "100%" in their demos (like Resident Evil or Monster Hunter) often grants a small cosmetic or starter pack reward in the final release. Filling the Sketchbook is the most likely trigger for such a reward.
