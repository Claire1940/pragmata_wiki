## Pragmata Demo Game Length Breakdown

The *Pragmata Sketchbook* demo is relatively short but highly replayable. Most players will find that a standard first playthrough takes less than half an hour. However, the game encourages multiple runs to experiment with different weapons and hacking modifiers.

| Playstyle | Estimated Time | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **First Playthrough** | 15–20 Minutes | Learning controls, reading lore bits, and fighting the boss. |
| **Completionist Run** | 25–30 Minutes | Finding all hidden containers and vanity cosmetics. |
| **Speedrun** | 8–10 Minutes | Skipping non-essential fights to reach the final encounter. |
| **Total Content** | **~45 Minutes** | Time spent if you explore all weapon combinations. |

> **Warning:** The Sketchbook demo does not feature a save system. If you exit the application before finishing the final boss encounter, you will need to start from the beginning of the lunar station.

## What is Included in the Sketchbook Demo?

Despite its brevity, the demo is packed with features that showcase Capcom’s vision for the 2026 release. You play as Hugh Williams, an astronaut with a droll sense of humor, accompanied by Diana, a mysterious and highly capable droid. The demo takes place within an abandoned research station on the moon, leading up to a climactic encounter against a massive, "Metal Gear-esque" robotic threat.

### Core Gameplay Features
*   **Dual-Character Combat:** Hugh handles the heavy firepower while Diana manages the "hacking grid."
*   **Hacking Minigame:** A grid-based puzzle that pops up during combat. Solving it increases your damage multipliers.
*   **Traversal Tools:** Hugh’s suit features thrusters for hovering and quick dashes to evade enemy fire.
*   **Finisher Moves:** Satisfying, cinematic strikes triggered after successfully weakening an enemy's armor through hacks.

## How to Maximize Your Playtime

If you feel the **pragmata demo game length** is too short, there are several ways to extend your experience and see everything the "Sketchbook" has to offer:

1.  **Explore Every Nook:** There are hidden containers scattered throughout the station. These contain currency and lore entries that provide context for the "Lunum" ore and the "Lunafilament" technology.
2.  **Master the Hacking Multipliers:** Instead of just solving the grid quickly, try to stack as many damage modifiers as possible before "solving" the puzzle. This allows you to witness Hugh's most powerful finisher animations.
3.  **Experiment with Hovering:** The demo level has several large gaps that can be crossed using the thruster's hover ability. Some of these lead to vanity cosmetics that can be equipped on Hugh's suit.
4.  **Practice the Boss Fight:** The final boss, a bull-like robotic creature, has multiple phases. Try defeating it using only specific weapon types to challenge yourself.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Tram" system icons found in the UI to identify fast-travel points. While limited in the demo, these give you a preview of how the full game's world will be interconnected.

## Full Game Release vs. Demo Scope

It is important to remember that the *Pragmata Sketchbook* is just a "proof of concept." Many features mentioned in recent previews—such as the Shelter home base, 3D printing upgrades, and the affable robot "Cabin"—are entirely absent from this build. 

Capcom has confirmed that the full game, launching on **April 17, 2026**, will feature a much more expansive 12–15 hour campaign. The full version will include a relationship system with Diana, where you can provide her with gifts to unlock new hacking abilities and dialogue options.

## FAQ

**Q: How long is the Pragmata demo game length on a first run?**

A: For most players, the first playthrough will take approximately 15 to 20 minutes. This includes time spent learning the hacking mechanics and the final boss fight.

**Q: Is the Pragmata Sketchbook demo free?**

A: Yes, the demo is free to download on Steam, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and Nintendo Switch 2 as of early 2026.

**Q: Does progress from the demo carry over to the full game?**

A: No, Capcom has stated that the Sketchbook is a standalone preview. However, players who complete the demo may receive a small cosmetic reward in the full game upon release.

**Q: Why is the demo called "Sketchbook"?**

A: The name reflects the "draft" nature of the demo, intended to show off the core mechanics and art style without revealing the full narrative or the deep upgrade systems found in the final version.
