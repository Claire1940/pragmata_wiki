## Core Gameplay Mechanics and Controls

Pragmata blends traditional third-person shooter mechanics with high-mobility platforming. Hugh’s suit allows for "Space Boosts" and air dashes, though these actions are limited by an internal energy meter that refills upon landing. Understanding your movement options is the first step toward mastering the demo.

| Action | Control (PS5/PC) | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Scan Environment** | Q / L1 | Highlights interactive terminals, items, and enemy weak points. |
| **Dodge / Shift** | R1 / RB | A quick dash. Timing this perfectly triggers a slow-motion "Witch Time" effect. |
| **Air Dash** | X + X / A + A | Extends jump distance and allows for vertical repositioning. |
| **Hack Command** | Square / X | Signals Diana to interface with nodes or terminals. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Scan function (Q/L1) constantly. Not only does it highlight hidden refined minerals, but it also reveals the "EXE nodes" required to progress through locked sectors.

## Pragmata Demo Guide: Hacking and Diana’s Abilities

Hacking is not just a narrative tool; it is a core combat mechanic. Diana can interface with "Hacking Nodes" scattered throughout the environment. These nodes often have special effects that trigger when you pass through them during a sequence.

### Hacking Node Types
*   **Intel Disruption:** Temporarily increases the damage enemies take. Repeated use on the same enemy reduces effectiveness unless you chain multiple nodes.
*   **Multi-Target Link:** Links nearby enemies together, allowing damage dealt to one to resonate through the entire group.
*   **Overdrive Protocol:** Diana’s ultimate ability. Once the gauge is filled via successful hacks, it immobilizes all nearby enemies and stops incoming projectiles (like missiles) in mid-air.

To complete a hack, you must often press prompted buttons in order from the outer ring inward. Speed is essential here, as Hugh is vulnerable to physical attacks while Diana is busy with the terminal.

## Weaponry and Combat Strategy

The demo provides a variety of tools to deal with the "Walkers" and other haywire bots. Managing your arsenal is key to maintaining momentum.

| Weapon | Best Use Case | Reload Type |
|---|---|---|
| **Grip Gun** | Standard encounters; consistent DPS at mid-range. | **Automatic** |
| **Shotgun** | High burst damage for close-quarters "Rat in the Maze" bots. | **Manual** |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Crowd control; knocks back multiple smaller units. | **Manual** |
| **Charge Piercer** | Long-range rail cannon; pierces through armored plating. | **Charge-based** |

The **Charge Piercer** is a hidden gem in the demo. To find it, look for a "Part Cartridge Holder" located in a side maintenance tunnel before reaching the main lift to the top floor. This weapon is color-coded green and significantly simplifies the final boss encounter by allowing you to snipe armor pieces from a safe distance.

## Navigating the Solar Panel Plant

Your primary objective in the demo is to reach the top floor of the facility to reboot the main power terminal. The path is divided into several combat arenas and "Escape Hatches," which serve as checkpoints.

1.  **The Entry Hall:** Use your Scan to find the first three EXE nodes. This will disable the initial lockdown.
2.  **The Maintenance Tunnels:** Watch out for "Stasis Nets." These floor traps will hold Hugh in place, making him an easy target for Walkers. Use a jump-dash to clear these gaps.
3.  **The Lift Ascent:** While Diana hacks the lift, you must defend her from waves of airborne drones. This is the best time to use the Shockwave Gun to keep the platform clear.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Falling off the map during the lift sequence results in an immediate reset to the last Escape Hatch. Keep your feet on the moving platform!

## Boss Guide: EDIS (Sector Guard Protocol)

The demo concludes with a massive battle against **EDIS**, a high-mobility guard bot. EDIS utilizes a mix of heavy machine gun fire and "Luna-made" missiles.

*   **Phase 1:** Focus on the missiles. When EDIS launches a volley, wait for Diana to call out the "Overdrive Protocol." Activating this will freeze the missiles in the air, allowing you to shoot them back at the boss for massive damage.
*   **Phase 2:** EDIS will begin using a charging physical attack. Use your R1/RB dodge just before impact to trigger the slow-motion window. During this time, switch to the Shotgun or Charge Piercer to hit the glowing core on its chassis.
*   **Final Stand:** Once the power levels reach 90%, EDIS will enter a desperate "Neutralization Mode." Unleash all your remaining heavy ammo. If you have the Charge Piercer, a fully charged shot to the head will end the fight instantly.

## Speedrunning and Secret Unlocks

Capcom has encouraged replayability in the Pragmata demo by including a clear-time ranking system. Your performance is rewarded with different "drawings" by Diana at the end of the game, numbered 1 through 8.

*   **Rank 7/8:** Standard completion.
*   **Rank 6:** Requires a clear time of under 10 minutes.
*   **Secret Rewards:** Achieving a high rank unlocks the **Lunar Suit (Scribble Version)**, which features Diana’s drawings all over Hugh’s armor. Additionally, you may unlock the **Charge Pinser** ability for use in subsequent demo runs.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the different endings/drawings in the Pragmata demo?**

A: The drawings shown at the end of the demo are based on your "Clear Time" and overall combat efficiency. To see all eight variations, you will need to complete the demo multiple times, aiming for both slow, exploratory runs and sub-10-minute speedruns.

**Q: Can I heal during the EDIS boss fight?**

A: Yes, refined minerals found in crates throughout the arena can be used to restore health. However, using a healing item takes a few seconds, so it is best to wait for a "Witch Time" window or when EDIS is stunned after a missile reflection.

**Q: What does the "Multihack" ability do?**

A: Multihack is an unlockable skill in the **pragmata demo guide** that allows Diana to link multiple terminals or enemies simultaneously. This reduces the time spent in hacking mini-games and increases Hugh’s damage output against groups of enemies.

**Q: Is the Charge Piercer weapon permanent?**

A: In the demo, the Charge Piercer replaces your Shockwave Gun slot once picked up. It does not carry over between deaths unless you have reached a checkpoint after acquiring it. In the full game, weapon slots are expected to be more customizable.
