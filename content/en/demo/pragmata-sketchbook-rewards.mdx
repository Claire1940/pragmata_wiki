## Unlocking the Hidden Elevator Boss

The most sought-after encounter in the Sketchbook demo occurs during the elevator ascent. Most players will breeze through this section by destroying the security bots with their standard-issue pistol, but doing so locks you out of the secret boss and the subsequent rewards. To trigger the hidden enemy, you must demonstrate mastery over the protagonist's diverse arsenal.

The key requirement involves the three bots that ambush you on the elevator. To unlock the hidden boss, you must finish off each of the three bots with a different weapon or ability. While you can weaken them with any tool, the "killing blow" is the only metric the game tracks for this specific challenge.

### Weapon Execution Requirements

| Bot Order | Recommended Softening Tool | Required Finishing Move | Reward Triggered |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First Bot** | Pistol (Standard Rounds) | **Pistol** | Progression Check 1 |
| **Second Bot** | Pistol (Standard Rounds) | **Shockwave Gun** | Progression Check 2 |
| **Third Bot** | Pistol / Shockwave Gun | **Hack / Takedown** | Hidden Boss Spawn |

> **Warning:** If you accidentally destroy two bots with the same weapon, the hidden boss will not spawn, and you will need to restart the checkpoint to claim the associated **pragmata sketchbook rewards**.

By successfully varying your finishers, the elevator will malfunction, leading to an encounter with a high-mobility hidden bot. Defeating this boss is the primary method for obtaining Diana’s second drawing, a crucial piece of the sketchbook collection.

## Combat Mastery and Weaponry Tips

To efficiently farm **pragmata sketchbook rewards**, you must become proficient with the three main interaction types available in the demo: the Pistol, the Shockwave Gun, and the Hacking tool. Each serves a distinct purpose in both crowd control and triggering secret events.

1.  **The Pistol**: Your primary source of consistent damage. It is best used for "softening" enemies. In the elevator challenge, use the pistol to bring all three bots down to roughly 10% health before switching to your finishers.
2.  **The Shockwave Gun**: This weapon excels at environmental interaction and staggering enemies. It has a slower fire rate, making it ideal for the second kill in the elevator sequence where precision timing is required.
3.  **The Hacking Ability**: This is often overlooked by players focusing on raw firepower. To use the Hack as a finisher, you must be within close proximity to a weakened enemy. It provides a unique animation and is the final requirement for the hidden boss trigger.

> **💡 Tip:** You can track your "Intruder Detected" status in the UI. If the alert stays red after the third bot, you have successfully triggered the hidden encounter.

## FAQ

**Q: How many drawings are there in the Pragmata Sketchbook rewards?**

A: There are currently four main drawings to unlock in the demo. The first is automatic, the second requires defeating the hidden elevator boss, the third is found via exploration, and the fourth is rewarded for a "Perfect" (no damage) run.

**Q: Can I unlock the hidden boss if I use the Shockwave Gun for all three kills?**

A: No. The trigger for the **pragmata sketchbook rewards** specifically requires that each of the three elevator bots be finished off with a *different* weapon. Using the same weapon for more than one kill will result in the standard mission ending.

**Q: Is there a reward for collecting all the sketches?**

A: While the demo doesn't provide a physical item, collecting all sketches triggers a special "Thank You" screen from the development team and saves a "Completion" flag to your system data, which may be used for rewards in the full game.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get the "The Void" drawing?**

A: To get this reward, focus on using the Shockwave Gun to keep enemies at a distance. Since the requirement is to take zero damage, playing defensively and utilizing the Hacking ability to disable bots from afar is the most reliable strategy.
