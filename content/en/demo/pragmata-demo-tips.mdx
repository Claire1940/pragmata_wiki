## Core Combat: Synergizing Hugh and Diana

Combat in Pragmata is not a traditional solo shooter experience. Hugh’s weapons, while visually impressive, often deal negligible damage to armored enemies unless Diana intervenes. The gameplay loop revolves around Hugh creating openings for Diana to bypass enemy defenses.

One of the most effective ways to create these openings is by utilizing the perfect dodge. By pressing **R1** (on PlayStation) or the equivalent dodge button just before an attack lands, you trigger a "Witch Time" style slowdown effect. This slowdown isn’t just for Hugh’s safety; it provides Diana with the critical window needed to initiate hacks on hostile units.

| Mechanic | Input | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perfect Dodge** | R1 (Timed) | Slows time and creates a hacking window. |
| **Diana’s Hack** | Prompted | Disables armor systems on targeted robots. |
| **Overdrive Protocol** | Fill Gauge | Immobilizes nearby enemies and deals massive AOE damage. |
| **Air Dash** | X / A (In Air) | Repositions Hugh; limited by thruster charges. |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not waste your heavy ammunition on enemies that haven't been hacked. Wait for Diana to disable their "Luna Filament" armor before unleashing your high-damage rounds.

## Mastering the Hacking Mini-Game

Hacking in the Pragmata demo is a grid-based loop that requires speed and precision. Unlike many games where hacking is a passive wait-time, here it is an active part of the combat flow. Diana’s hacking nodes often have special effects that trigger when you pass through them while completing the sequence.

### Hacking Node Variations
Passing through multiple nodes of the same type boosts the specific effect. For example, if you pass through multiple damage-boosting nodes, Hugh’s next shot will hit significantly harder.

*   **EXE Nodes:** Standard nodes required to complete the hack.
*   **Damage Boosters:** Temporarily increases the damage Hugh deals to the hacked target.
*   **Multi-Target Links:** Links multiple enemies together, allowing damage dealt to one to affect the entire group.
*   **Stasis Nodes:** Briefly freezes the enemy's internal systems, preventing them from counter-attacking.

> **Warning:** Repeatedly using the same hacking node on the same enemy type reduces its effectiveness over time. Vary your approach to keep the AI off-balance.

## Weapon Loadouts and Resource Management

Hugh’s space suit features four modular units where weapons can be slotted. In the demo, you will encounter different weapon types that serve specific roles. It is important to note that while the basic **Grip Gun** reloads automatically, specialized weapons like the **Shockwave Gun** or **Charge Piercer** require manual intervention or specific cooldowns.

### Demo Weapon Comparison

| Weapon Name | Range | Best Use Case | Reload Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grip Gun** | Mid | Standard encounters; consistent fire. | **Automatic** |
| **Shotgun** | Close | Clearing groups of small "Walkers." | Manual |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Close | Staggering heavy units and clearing "Goop." | Cooldown |
| **Charge Piercer** | Long | Boss fights; high armor penetration. | Manual/Charge |

The **Charge Piercer** acts as a rail cannon. Its power increases the longer you hold the charge, and it is largely unaffected by range, making it the premier choice for the demo's final boss encounter against the rogue AI, Idas.

## Exploration and The Shelter

While the demo focuses heavily on action, exploring the "Cradle" facility is vital for survival. You will find **Refined Minerals** and **Luna Filament** materials scattered in lockers and hidden corners. These materials are used for 3D printing upgrades at **The Shelter**.

Although the full version of The Shelter is restricted in "The Sketchbook" demo, you can still interact with **Escape Hatches**. These hatches serve as checkpoints. If you are incapacitated, you will return to the last activated hatch. 

1.  **Scan Frequently:** Use the scan function (Q on PC) to highlight terminals, hidden items, and enemy weaknesses.
2.  **Collect Cartridge Holders:** These items increase your maximum ammunition capacity for specialized weapons.
3.  **Check Surveillance Logs:** Interacting with terminals provides lore about the "Delphy" corporation and the 24-hour surveillance policies of the moon base.

## Speedrunning and Ranking Rewards

Capcom has encouraged players to replay the demo by including a clear-time ranking system. Upon completing the demo, Diana will show Hugh a drawing she was working on. The content of this drawing changes based on your performance and clear time.

| Clear Time | Drawing Number | Reward/Unlock |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Over 15 Minutes** | #8 | Standard Ending |
| **10 - 15 Minutes** | #7 | Advanced Combat Tips |
| **Under 10 Minutes** | #6 | New Suit Scribbles / Special Ability |

To achieve a sub-10-minute run, you must minimize exploration and maximize Diana’s **Overdrive Protocol**. This ability fills up as you successfully complete hacks. When activated, it clears the screen of projectiles and freezes all enemies, allowing you to bypass entire combat encounters or melt the boss's health bar.

## PC Requirements and Technical Optimization

To get the most out of these **pragmata demo tips**, ensure your hardware is configured correctly. The game utilizes heavy Ray Tracing for its shiny, metallic lunar environments. If you experience frame drops, consider disabling Ray Tracing, as the "strand tech" used for characters like Diana is very resource-intensive.

*   **Minimum Specs:** Windows 11, Intel Core i5-8500 / Ryzen 5 3500, 16GB RAM, GTX 1660 / RX 5500 XT.
*   **Recommended Specs:** Intel Core i7-8700 / Ryzen 5 5500, RTX 2060 Super / Radeon RX 6600.
*   **Storage:** 40 GB (SSD Recommended for fast asset streaming).

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the different drawings at the end of the Pragmata demo?**

A: The drawings are awarded based on your clear time. To see different images (like Number 6 or 7), you need to finish the demo faster. Replaying the demo and focusing on speedrun tactics is the most reliable way to unlock them.

**Q: Can I use Diana's Overdrive Protocol at any time?**

A: No, the Overdrive Protocol must be charged by successfully hacking enemies. Once the gauge is full, you can activate it to immobilize enemies and deal significant damage.

**Q: Does the Pragmata demo support Ray Tracing on all platforms?**

A: Ray Tracing is supported on PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X. However, the developers have explicitly stated that Ray Tracing is not supported on the Xbox Series S version of the demo.

**Q: What is the benefit of passing through multiple hacking nodes?**

A: Passing through multiple nodes of the same type (like damage boosters) stacks the effect. This makes Hugh's subsequent attacks much more powerful, which is essential for taking down high-health bosses quickly.
