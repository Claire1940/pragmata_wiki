## Combat Mechanics: Hugh and Diana’s Synergy

The gameplay in Pragmata is a evolution of the third-person shooter formula Capcom perfected in recent *Resident Evil* titles, but with a significant focus on verticality and partner synergy. You play as Hugh, a "spaceman" equipped with a high-tech suit, and are accompanied by Diana, an android with specialized hacking capabilities.

### Hugh’s Arsenal and Movement
Hugh’s movement is defined by his thruster-assisted suit. Unlike traditional shooters, you aren't glued to the ground. You have a stamina gauge that governs jumping, hovering, and dashing. Mastering the "Perfect Dodge" is critical; dodging at the exact moment of an enemy strike triggers a brief time-slow effect, allowing for massive counter-damage.

| Weapon Category | Weapon Type | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | **Infinite Pistol** | Consistent chip damage and hitting weak points. |
| **Green Category** | **Energy Net** | Trapping fast-moving robots and dealing DOT. |
| **Red Category** | **Shockwave Shotgun** | High stagger and close-range crowd control. |
| **Red Category** | **Railgun (Secret)** | Massive burst damage against armored bosses. |

### Diana’s Hacking System
Diana isn't just an escort character; she is a tactical asset. During combat, you can trigger a hacking grid on enemies. This is a real-time puzzle where you must connect blue nodes to a green execution node while dodging incoming fire. 

1. **Connect Nodes:** The more blue nodes you chain, the higher the damage multiplier.
2. **Yellow Nodes:** These provide temporary buffs, such as "Defense Down" or "Multi-Hack" for groups.
3. **Overdrive Protocol:** Filling Diana's gauge allows for a screen-clearing blast attack.

## PC Performance and System Requirements

Built on the RE Engine, Pragmata is a technical marvel that utilizes ray-traced reflections and indirect lighting to create its "ultra-clean" sci-fi aesthetic. Based on 2026 hardware benchmarks, the game is exceptionally well-optimized for mid-range systems, though ray tracing significantly increases VRAM demands.

| Requirement Level | GPU Recommendation | Expected Performance |
|---|---|---|
| **Minimum (1080p)** | GTX 1660 / RX 5500 XT | 45 FPS (Performance Mode) |
| **Recommended (1080p)** | RTX 2060 Super / RX 6600 | 60 FPS (High Settings) |
| **Ray Tracing (1440p)** | RTX 3060 12GB / RX 6700 XT | 60 FPS (RT On) |
| **Ultra (4K)** | RTX 4080 / RX 7900 XTX | 90+ FPS (Max Settings) |

The demo includes support for the latest upscaling technologies, including DLSS and the machine-learning-based FSR 4. Testing shows that turning off ray tracing can provide a 15-20% performance boost on older cards, but given the game's heavy use of reflective surfaces (glass, polished metal, lunar tiles), the visual trade-off is noticeable.

> **Warning:** Players with 8GB VRAM cards should be cautious when enabling Ray Tracing at 1440p or higher. You may experience texture flickering or frame time spikes as the VRAM buffer overflows.

## FAQ

**Q: Pragmata demo how long is the total playtime for a single run?**

**Q: Is the Pragmata demo available on PS5 or Xbox Series X?**

A: You must complete the demo at least once. On your second playthrough, a previously locked door in the research facility will be accessible, containing the Red Category Railgun weapon.
