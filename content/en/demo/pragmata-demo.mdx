## Story Premise: Navigating the Cradle

In the demo, players step into the heavy boots of Hugh Williams during an investigation of "abnormalities" within the lunar colony. The primary objective involves escorting Diana to the top floor of a massive solar power plant. The goal is simple yet daunting: restore main power to the facility so the duo can establish contact with Earth.

The narrative tension is built through the interaction between Hugh and Diana. While Hugh handles the heavy lifting and direct combat, Diana provides technical support, acting as the key to bypassing the colony's sophisticated security systems. This partnership is the heartbeat of the gameplay, requiring players to protect Diana while she performs vital tasks.

## Combat Mechanics and Weaponry

Combat in the **pragmata demo** is a departure from traditional "run-and-gun" shooters. It emphasizes positioning, resource management, and the tactical use of specialized gadgets. Hugh utilizes a "Sketchbook" system to manage his loadouts, allowing players to swap between different tools depending on the threat level.

### Weapon Types and Usage
Players have access to a variety of tools, ranging from long-range rifles to high-impact close-quarters weapons. The demo introduces a "Standard" rifle for general engagements and a specialized close-range weapon that deals massive damage to "tanky" robotic enemies. 

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on your yellow "Stasis" meter. Using stasis can freeze enemies in place, allowing you to focus fire on weak points or buy time for Diana to complete a hack.

## The Hacking Minigame

One of the standout features of the **pragmata demo** gameplay is the hacking system. Unlike many games that use simple button prompts, Pragmata requires active participation in a node-based minigame. When Hugh encounters a locked terminal or a security door, he must protect Diana while she interfaces with the system.

### How to Hack Effectively
The hacking interface involves navigating a cursor through a series of "nodes." While you don't necessarily have to hit every single blue node to succeed, clearing more of them often yields better results or faster bypasses. 

1. **Initiate the Hack:** Approach the terminal and prompt Diana to begin.
2. **Navigate the Maze:** Use the directional controls to move through the node path.
3. **Avoid the Lockout:** Moving too slowly or hitting red interference nodes can cause a system lockout, forcing a manual reset.
4. **Repair if Needed:** If the hack fails, you may need to use repair tools (often mapped to 'Q' on PC) to restore the terminal's integrity.

## Boss Fight: The Sector Guard

The climax of the **pragmata demo** is a confrontation with a massive robotic guardian. This boss, part of the "Eaters" protocol, serves as a skill check for everything learned in the demo. It features multiple phases, including long-range missile barrages and high-speed charges.

During this fight, players must manage their ammunition carefully. The boss has a "core" or a pilot unit located on top, which is its primary weak point. While you can deal damage to the main body, focusing fire on the upper chassis is the fastest way to deplete its 4,500+ HP pool.

> **Warning:** The Sector Guard is significantly stronger than the standard "Walker" units. If you find yourself pinned down, use the environment to break line-of-sight and wait for Diana's "Overdrive Protocol" to charge.

## Technical Impressions and Controls

Capcom has designed Pragmata to feel weighty and deliberate. The **pragmata demo** suggests that while the game is playable on Mouse and Keyboard, the movement and hacking mechanics feel highly optimized for a controller. 

*   **Sensitivity:** Some players may find the default sensitivity low; it is recommended to adjust this in the settings menu immediately to handle the fast-pacing of the later boss encounters.
*   **Perfect Dodge:** Timing your movement just as an attack lands triggers a slow-motion window, allowing for a devastating counter-attack.
*   **UI Feedback:** The game uses minimal HUD elements to maintain immersion, but pay close attention to the visual cues on Hugh’s suit for health and ammo status.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the age you enter at the start of the pragmata demo affect the gameplay?**

A: No, the age entry is primarily for data collection and age-rating compliance. It does not change the difficulty, story, or mechanics of the demo, though once entered, it cannot be changed for that save file.

**Q: What are the "Sugar Cubes" used for in the demo?**

A: In the current demo, Sugar Cubes appear to be a collectible or a minor resource found in crates. While their full utility isn't explained in the preview, they are often rewarded after clearing combat rooms or finding "Special Christmas Gift" loot boxes.

**Q: Can I play the pragmata demo on PC with a controller?**

A: Yes, the demo fully supports controllers. Many players find the hacking minigame and the third-person movement more intuitive on a controller compared to a mouse and keyboard setup.

**Q: Is the demo representative of the final game's length?**

A: No, the demo is a very short vertical slice intended to showcase the combat and hacking mechanics. The final game is expected to be a full-length cinematic adventure with a much larger map and deeper character progression.
