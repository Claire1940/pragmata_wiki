## Factors Influencing Playtime: Exploration and Upgrades

While the game follows a linear structure, it is far from a "simple corridor." Capcom has integrated several layers of depth that can significantly extend your time on the lunar base. The environment is riddled with secrets that are not immediately accessible, often requiring specific abilities or "nodes" that you unlock as you progress through the story.

### The Role of the Refuge (Hub World)
The Refuge serves as your central hub where you can manage your resources and plan your next move. Much of the additional **pragmata duracion** comes from the time spent here optimizing your build. You can interact with various terminals to:
*   **Enhance Hugh’s Combat Gear:** Improve health, resistance, and weapon damage.
*   **Upgrade Diana’s Hacking Capabilities:** Increase the time window for minigames and unlock the ability to hack higher-tier robotic enemies.
*   **Equip Modules:** Specialized nodes that alter gameplay mechanics, such as faster movement or improved resource gathering.

### Backtracking and Hidden Areas
The game encourages a "Metroidvania-lite" approach to its level design. As you gain new hacking modules for Diana or traversal tools for Hugh, previously locked doors in earlier sectors of the moon base become accessible. The map explicitly tracks which items you have missed, serving as a constant nudge for players who enjoy 100% completion. These optional areas often contain "Cabin's Stamp Club" items and unique outfits that add flavor to the experience.

## Combat and Puzzles: A Dual-Protagonist Dynamic

The gameplay loop is divided between high-octane third-person shooting and tactical hacking. This duality is the core of the game's identity and directly affects the pacing. Hugh is the muscle, handling the robotic threats with heavy weaponry and defensive maneuvers. Meanwhile, Diana provides support through a real-time hacking minigame.

If you find the combat challenging, your **pragmata duracion** might increase as you engage in "resource runs." These are short bursts of exploration meant to gather the materials needed for the next big upgrade.

## Progression Systems and Replayability

Capcom has ensured that the game feels rewarding through a robust progression system. The "Refuge" acts as the heart of your journey, where every resource collected on the moon’s surface is put to use. Players who enjoy tinkering with stats and builds will find themselves spending several extra hours here.

1.  **Weapon Refinement:** Beyond simple damage buffs, you can modify how Hugh's weapons behave, such as adding elemental effects or changing fire modes.
2.  **Diana's Evolution:** As Diana grows more powerful, her hacking becomes more than just a way to open doors; she can eventually turn enemies against one another, changing the flow of combat entirely.
3.  **Optional Challenges:** Scattered throughout the lunar base are "Combat Trials" that test your efficiency. Completing these is essential for those aiming for the 100% mark.

The inclusion of multiple difficulty settings also impacts the total **pragmata duracion**. A first run on "Normal" provides a balanced experience, but "Hard" or "Lunar Survival" modes demand a much more methodical approach, significantly slowing down your progress as you carefully manage health and ammunition.

> **Warning:** Resources are finite in the higher difficulty modes. Wasting materials on low-tier upgrades early on can make the late-game bosses significantly harder to overcome.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a New Game Plus mode that extends the pragmata duracion?**

A: Yes, completing the game once unlocks a New Game Plus mode where you retain your upgrades and equipment. This mode often features tougher enemy placements and exclusive rewards, encouraging a second playthrough.

**Q: Can I complete the game faster if I skip the hacking minigames?**

A: Most hacking sequences involving Diana are mandatory for story progression. While you can become faster at them with practice and upgrades, they cannot be skipped entirely as they are integrated into the combat and puzzle mechanics.

**Q: How many collectibles are there for 100% completion?**

A: There are over 100 individual collectibles, including audio logs, "Stamp Club" stamps, and hidden weapon modules. Finding all of them will roughly double your total playtime compared to a story-only run.

**Q: Does the game have multiple endings?**

A: While the main story path is linear, there are minor variations in the epilogue based on how many side objectives and character interactions you completed throughout the journey. To see the "True Ending," you will likely need to engage with a significant portion of the side content.
