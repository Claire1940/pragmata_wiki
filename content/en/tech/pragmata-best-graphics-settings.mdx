### Understanding Ray Tracing in Pragmata
Pragmata utilizes Ray Tracing (RT) for both indirect lighting and reflections. Unlike some earlier RE Engine titles where RT reflections appeared grainy, the implementation here is much cleaner. However, it is a significant drain on resources. If you are struggling to maintain 60 FPS on a card like the RTX 3060, turning RT off can provide a 15-20% performance boost. While you will lose the detailed reflections of the player character in glass surfaces, the baked lighting remains high-quality enough for a great experience.

## Advanced Upscaling: DLSS and FSR

In 2026, upscaling technology has become a mandatory tool for high-resolution gaming. Pragmata supports the latest versions of DLSS and FSR, including machine-learning-based frame generation.

1. **DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling):** For Nvidia users, DLSS Balanced at 1440p provides a near-native look with a massive frame rate jump.
2. **FSR (FidelityFX Super Resolution):** Pragmata includes FSR 3.1.4 and the newer ML-based upscaling (formerly FSR 4). This is the go-to for AMD and Intel users.
3. **Frame Generation:** If your GPU supports it, Frame Gen can effectively double your perceived frame rate. However, ensure your base frame rate is at least 40-50 FPS before enabling it to avoid noticeable input lag.

| Upscaling Mode | Recommended Resolution | Performance Gain |
|---|---|---|
| **DLSS Quality** | 1080p / 1440p | ~30% |
| **DLSS Balanced** | 4K | ~50% |
| **FSR 4 ML Balanced** | 1440p / 4K | ~45% |
| **Frame Generation** | All | ~80-100% |

> **💡 Tip:** If you notice "ghosting" behind the player character while moving, try switching from "Balanced" to "Quality" mode or updating your GPU drivers to the latest April 2026 release.

## Technical Troubleshooting for 2026
Even with the **pragmata best graphics settings**, some users may encounter technical hurdles. The RE Engine performs a shader compilation step upon the first launch. It is vital to let this process finish completely to avoid "compilation stutter" during gameplay.

*   **Vsync:** If you experience screen tearing, enable Vsync. If you prefer the lowest possible latency, disable it and use a frame rate cap via your GPU control panel.
*   **VRAM Overages:** If your VRAM usage exceeds your card's capacity (e.g., using 10GB on an 8GB card), the game will likely stutter or crash. Lower the **Texture Quality** or **Shadow Cache** to stay within limits.
*   **Fullscreen vs. Borderless:** Most modern systems perform best in "Borderless Window" mode, allowing for faster alt-tabbing and better integration with Windows 11/12 optimization features.

For more information on official patches and driver support, visit the [official Pragmata Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1368060/PRAGMATA/).

## FAQ

**Q: What are the pragmata best graphics settings for a mid-range PC?**

A: For a mid-range PC (like an RTX 3060 or RX 6700), use a mix of High settings with Ray Tracing enabled, provided you have at least 12GB of VRAM. Set DLSS or FSR to "Quality" to maintain a stable 60 FPS at 1080p or 1440p.

**Q: Does Pragmata support Ray Tracing on 8GB GPUs?**

A: Yes, but it is not recommended for higher resolutions. At 1080p, it may work, but at 1440p or 4K, 8GB of VRAM is often insufficient for the combined demands of high-resolution textures and ray-traced reflections, leading to potential stuttering.

**Q: Why is my frame rate capped at 60 FPS?**

A: Check if Vsync is enabled in the graphics menu. Additionally, ensure that your monitor's refresh rate is set correctly in Windows settings and that no external frame rate limiters (like Rivatuner) are active.

**Q: Is there a significant difference between FSR and DLSS in Pragmata?**

A: In the 2026 version of the game, both DLSS 4.5 and FSR 4 (ML-based) offer excellent results. DLSS typically has a slight edge in temporal stability (less shimmering), while FSR 4 is highly competitive and available for all GPU manufacturers.
