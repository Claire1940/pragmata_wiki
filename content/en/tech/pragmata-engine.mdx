## The Power of RE Engine: Path Tracing in Pragmata

At the heart of *Pragmata*'s visual spectacle is Capcom's advanced implementation of path tracing within the RE Engine. Unlike traditional rasterization or even hybrid ray tracing approaches, path tracing simulates light transport more accurately, leading to incredibly realistic lighting, reflections, and shadows. This method traces millions of light paths, replicating how light behaves in the real world to create a "lively scene" with "high quality images."

Capcom's Hitoshi Mishima, a key figure in the RE Engine's development, highlighted how path tracing in *Pragmata* (and *Resident Evil Requiem*) offers distinct advantages over standard ray tracing. While ray tracing typically focuses on specific light interactions like reflections or shadows, path tracing provides a more holistic and physically accurate representation of light. This results in a superior visual experience with greater depth and realism.

Here’s a comparison of how path tracing elevates *Pragmata*'s visuals:

| Feature | Path Tracing (Pragmata) | Ray Tracing (General) |
|---|---|---|
| **Lighting Accuracy** | Real-time, physically accurate global illumination | Real-time, often hybrid with rasterization for indirect lighting |
| **Shadows** | More detailed, softer, and realistically diffused | Good, but can sometimes appear less natural or precise |
| **Reflections** | Sharper, more stable, and highly accurate | Can be prone to graininess or less detail in certain RE Engine titles |
| **Indirect Lighting** | Fully simulated, resulting in superior quality and bounce light | Often approximated or less dynamic, leading to less realistic global illumination |
| **Occlusion** | Stricter and more realistic ambient occlusion | Good, but may lack the subtle depth of full path tracing |
| **Denoising** | More stable and effective, especially with DLSS RR | Can sometimes exhibit more artifacts, depending on implementation |

## Behind the Scenes: RE Engine's Path Tracing Innovations

The impressive visuals and performance of the Pragmata engine are the result of extensive technical innovations and optimizations within Capcom's RE Engine. NVIDIA's Calvin Shu, who collaborated with Capcom, detailed many of these advancements at GDC, focusing on how they achieved real-time path tracing with efficiency.

A crucial element is the integration with NVIDIA DLSS Ray Reconstruction (DLSS RR). This AI-powered technology is designed to enhance ray-traced images, providing superior denoising and reconstruction, which is vital for maintaining image quality in path-traced scenes.

Key optimization techniques developed for the RE Engine include:

| Optimization | Purpose | Impact on Visuals | Impact on Performance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Simplified BSDF (RIS)** | Efficiently evaluate light samples during rendering | Maintains visual quality while reducing computational load | Contributed to a ~15% performance gain in stress tests |
| **Light Grid Culling** | Accelerate calculation for punctual lights by culling irrelevant sources | Ensures accurate shadows and efficient lighting calculation | Identified as the most significant factor for speedup |
| **Brightness Compensation** | Addresses variance issues with high-intensity spotlights or IBLs | Achieves consistent lighting, improving DLSS RR quality | Essential for stable path tracing results in diverse scenes |
| **Emissive Polygon Sampling** | Reduces noise generated by emissive lighting sources | Clearer and more stable emissive effects | Efficient sampling using Walker's alias method |
| **ReSTIR GI (Resampled Real-time Global Illumination)** | Stabilizes DLSS RR, reduces noise from indirect lighting | Minimizes ghosting and blurry artifacts in indirect light | Improves overall image quality and temporal stability |
| **Guide Buffers & Disocclusion Masks** | Resolves specific DLSS RR artifacts in complex scenarios | Clearer hair, precise reflections in glass, accurate rain/puddles, animated light | Crucial for maintaining visual fidelity and preventing blurriness |
| **Strand BVH for Hair** | Manages hair strands in low-gravity environments | Enables realistic hair movement and accurate self-shadowing | Addresses visual inconsistencies found in previous RE Engine titles |
| **DXR 1.2 with SER (Shader Execution Reordering)** | Modern ray tracing API for improved GPU throughput | Better utilization of hardware for complex ray workloads | Offers "speed of light throughput" with less overhead |

One notable challenge was meeting artists' expectations, particularly concerning environmental indirect lighting. The engine team worked closely with technical artists to resolve issues like subtle changes in occlusion when ReSTIR GI was enabled. The unique low-gravity environment of *Pragmata* also necessitated specialized solutions for character hair, requiring a "strand BVH" to ensure realistic movement and shadows that were consistent with the path-traced lighting.

Performance improvements were continuous, with initial stress tests showing a reduction from 21 milliseconds to 13.3 milliseconds for a 4K DLAA scene, thanks to optimizations like consolidating light loops and leveraging DXR 1.2 with SER. These efforts highlight Capcom's commitment to delivering a polished and visually stunning experience with the Pragmata engine.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the Pragmata engine?**

A: The Pragmata engine refers to Capcom's proprietary RE Engine, which has been significantly enhanced for the game *Pragmata*. It features advanced real-time path tracing technology for highly realistic lighting, reflections, and shadows, alongside various performance optimizations.

**Q: Does Pragmata use ray tracing or path tracing?**

A: *Pragmata* utilizes full path tracing, which is an evolution of ray tracing. Path tracing simulates light more comprehensively than traditional ray tracing, leading to more detailed shadows, sharper reflections, and superior indirect lighting.

**Q: What are the general system requirements for Pragmata?**

A: For a recommended 1080p 60 FPS experience, you'll need an RTX 2060 Super 8GB or RX 6600 8GB GPU, an Intel Core i7-10700 or Ryzen 5 5500 CPU, and 16GB of RAM. For ray tracing, an RTX 3060 12GB or RX 6700 XT 12GB is recommended.

**Q: How does RE Engine ensure good performance with advanced graphics in Pragmata?**

A: The RE Engine employs numerous optimizations, including simplified BSDF for light sampling, light grid culling, brightness compensation, efficient emissive polygon sampling, and technologies like ReSTIR GI. It also heavily leverages upscaling solutions like NVIDIA DLSS and AMD FSR, and modern APIs such as DXR 1.2 with Shader Execution Reordering (SER) to ensure smooth performance.
