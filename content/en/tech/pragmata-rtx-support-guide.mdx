## Understanding Ray Tracing Features in Pragmata

Capcom has implemented two primary ray-tracing pillars in Pragmata: **Ray-Traced Reflections** and **Ray-Traced Indirect Lighting**. In the RE Engine, these features work in tandem to eliminate the "glow" often seen around objects in traditional rasterized lighting.

### Ray-Traced Reflections
The game features numerous glass partitions, metallic surfaces, and lunar modules that benefit immensely from real-time reflections. While standard screen-space reflections (SSR) are available, they often fail to reflect objects off-screen. Ray tracing ensures that the player character, Diana, and the surrounding environment are accurately mirrored in every shiny surface.

### Ray-Traced Indirect Lighting
Indirect lighting (often referred to as Global Illumination) calculates how light bounces off surfaces to illuminate shaded areas. In the dark corridors of the solar power plant, this prevents the "pitch black" look of older games, instead providing soft, realistic color bleeding from light sources onto the lunar floor.

## Performance Optimization Steps

To achieve the best balance in this **pragmata rtx support guide**, follow these optimization steps to ensure your frame times remain consistent during heavy combat sequences.

1.  **Shader Compilation:** Upon the first launch of Pragmata in 2026, allow the game to finish its shader compilation step. Skipping this or interrupting it will lead to significant stuttering during gameplay.
2.  **Toggle V-Sync:** For benchmarking, turn V-Sync off to see your true ceiling. However, for general play, keeping V-Sync on is recommended to prevent screen tearing, as the RE Engine can be sensitive to frame pacing.
3.  **Mesh Quality:** Set Mesh Quality to **Medium** if you are on mid-range hardware. The visual difference between High and Medium is negligible during fast-paced combat, but it saves valuable VRAM for ray-tracing calculations.
4.  **Ray Tracing Toggle:** Note that Ray Tracing can only be toggled from the Main Menu. You cannot change this setting mid-game, as it requires a re-initialization of the lighting engine.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Image Quality** | 100% (Native) | High |
| **Ray Tracing** | **On** (if 12GB+ VRAM) | Very High |
| **Texture Quality** | High (4GB - 8GB) | Medium |
| **Shadow Quality** | Medium | High |
| **Mesh Quality** | Medium | Low |

## Upscaling: DLSS and FSR 4.0

Pragmata is at the forefront of upscaling technology in 2026, supporting NVIDIA DLSS and the latest machine-learning-based FSR (formerly known as FSR 4). Using these tools is the most effective way to maintain high framerates while keeping ray tracing active.

*   **DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling):** Available for RTX users. The "Balanced" preset at 1440p provides a significant boost, often pushing mid-range cards like the 5060 Ti over the 100 FPS mark.
*   **FSR (FidelityFX Super Resolution):** Version 3.1.4 and above includes ML-based upscaling and frame generation. This is the go-to for AMD Radeon users, providing comparable image quality to native resolution.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid using "Ultra Performance" modes unless you are playing at 4K. At 1080p, these modes can make the ray-traced reflections appear grainy or "ghosty" behind moving objects.

## Combat and Hacking Performance

The unique gameplay loop of Pragmata involves a hacking matrix that overlays the screen. Fortunately, our testing shows that the hacking UI has almost zero impact on performance. Even when the screen is filled with lunar filaments and explosive effects from the shockwave gun, the frame rate remains remarkably stable. 

If you notice drops during combat, it is likely due to **Dynamic Resolution** being turned off. Enabling Dynamic Resolution allows the game to subtly drop the internal rendering resolution during intense scenes to maintain your target framerate (e.g., 60 FPS).

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pragmata support Ray Tracing on AMD cards?**

A: Yes, Pragmata supports Ray Tracing on AMD hardware through DirectX Raytracing (DXR). Cards like the RX 6700 XT and newer are fully compatible, though NVIDIA's dedicated RT cores may offer slightly higher efficiency in reflections.

**Q: Why do my reflections look grainy even with RTX on?**

A: This is often a result of using a low-quality upscaling preset or having "Image Quality" set below 100%. To fix this, use DLSS or FSR at "Quality" or "Balanced" settings, and ensure your Mesh Quality is at least set to Medium.

**Q: Can I run the pragmata rtx support guide settings on a Steam Deck?**

A: While Pragmata runs on the Steam Deck, enabling Ray Tracing is not recommended for the handheld's APU. For the best Steam Deck experience, turn RT off and use FSR 3.1 in Performance mode to maintain a steady 30-40 FPS.

**Q: Does the game support Frame Generation?**

A: Yes, Pragmata supports both DLSS Frame Generation and FSR ML-based Frame Generation. These features are highly effective for reaching high-refresh-rate targets (120Hz+) on compatible monitors, provided your base framerate is already above 60 FPS.
