## Best Graphics Settings for Optimization

To achieve the best balance of visuals and fluidity, you should adjust your settings based on your hardware's VRAM and raw compute power. Pragmata includes a "Shader Compilation" step at the initial launch, which drastically reduces in-game stuttering. If you experience "hitch points" during fast movement or combat, ensure your GPU drivers are updated to the latest 2026 versions.

### Recommended Settings for Mid-Range PCs (RTX 3060 / RX 6700 Class)
*   **Resolution Scaling:** Native (100%) or DLSS/FSR Quality
*   **Ray Tracing:** On (If 12GB VRAM is available)
*   **Mesh Quality:** Medium
*   **Texture Quality:** High (4GB-6GB allocation)
*   **Indirect Lighting:** Ray Traced
*   **Reflections:** Ray Traced
*   **Dynamic Resolution:** Off
*   **Frame Generation:** Off (unless targeting 120Hz+)

> **💡 Tip:** Turning off Ray Tracing can provide a 15-20% performance boost. If you are struggling to maintain a locked 60 FPS, this should be the first setting you disable. While the game still looks excellent with standard SSR (Screen Space Reflections), the ray-traced reflections on glass and metallic surfaces in the Cradle are a significant visual upgrade.

## Advanced Features: Ray Tracing and Upscaling

Pragmata is one of the first major titles to support the latest iterations of machine-learning-based upscaling. For AMD users, the game supports FSR 4 (ML-based upscaling), while Nvidia users can utilize DLSS 3.5+. These technologies are essential for playing at 4K or for maintaining high frame rates on older hardware.

### Hacking and Combat Effects
During combat, effects like the "Shockwave Gun" and the "Hacking Matrix" UI overlays can cause minor frame time spikes. However, the RE Engine handles these alpha effects well. If you notice drops during hacking sequences, consider lowering **Effect Quality** to Medium. This reduces the particle count of the Lunum mineral filaments without sacrificing the clarity of the hacking puzzle.

### VRAM Management for 8GB Cards
If you are playing on an 8GB card like the RTX 5060 Ti (8GB version) or an older RTX 3070, you must be cautious. High-resolution textures combined with Ray Tracing can lead to a "VRAM swap," where the game attempts to use system RAM, causing massive frame rate drops. 

1.  Keep **Texture Quality** at the "High (3GB)" or "Medium" preset.
2.  Disable **Ray Traced Indirect Lighting** but keep **Ray Traced Reflections** if desired.
3.  Use **DLSS or FSR Balanced** mode to reduce the internal rendering resolution.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pragmata have shader compilation stutter?**

A: No, the game includes a mandatory shader compilation step on the first launch. This process takes a few minutes but ensures that gameplay remains smooth without the "stutter struggle" common in other modern PC releases.

**Q: What is the best upscaling method for this pragmata pc performance guide?**

A: If you have an Nvidia RTX card, DLSS remains the gold standard for image reconstruction. However, for AMD users, the new ML-based FSR 4 (version 3.1.4 and above) provides a massive leap in quality over previous versions, making it a viable alternative even at 1080p.

**Q: Can I run Pragmata on a Steam Deck?**

A: While not officially "Verified" at launch, the game runs on the Steam Deck using the "Performance" preset at 720p with FSR set to Balanced. Expect frame rates in the 30-40 FPS range.

**Q: Why do my reflections look grainy?**

A: This is often a result of low-resolution Ray Tracing or aggressive upscaling. If your reflections appear "noisy," try increasing your **Internal Resolution Scale** or switching your upscaler to "Quality" mode. Ensure your **Mesh Quality** is set to at least Medium to provide enough geometry for the reflections to bounce off accurately.
