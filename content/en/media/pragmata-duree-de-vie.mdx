## Main Story Length: A Focused Lunar Journey

The core narrative of the game follows Hugh Williams, a lunar contractor, and a mysterious girl named Diana as they attempt to escape a malfunctioning moon base. The game is structured into distinct sectors that function similarly to dungeons in classic adventure titles, each culminating in a spectacular boss encounter. 

For the average player focusing primarily on the narrative objectives, the **pragmata duree de vie** for the main story sits at approximately **10 hours**. This "lean" approach ensures that the tension remains high and the environments—ranging from 3D-printed recreations of Time Square to overgrown botanical domes—never lose their visual or mechanical novelty.

| Playstyle | Estimated Time | Key Focus Areas |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main Story Only** | 9 - 11 Hours | Primary objectives, minimal gear upgrades. |
| **Story + Side Missions** | 15 - 18 Hours | Training simulations, Shelter upgrades, exploration. |
| **Completionist (100%)** | 25+ Hours | All collectibles, Omega-rank gear, all difficulty modes. |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't let the 10-hour clock fool you. The game is designed to be highly replayable, with combat mechanics that reward mastery and environmental puzzles that often have multiple solutions.

## Exploration of the Lunar Sectors

The campaign is divided into several major "Sectors," each utilizing a unique resource known as Lunafilament. This substance allows the base's AI, Edis, to manifest physical environments based on human memories. This environmental variety is a major factor in why the **pragmata duree de vie** feels so dense and rewarding, as no two hours of gameplay feel the same.

1.  **The Pit:** The introductory zone where Hugh and Diana first link up. It serves as a tutorial for the "Hacking Matrix" mechanics.
2.  **Time Square (Lunum Recreation):** A distorted, vertical version of New York City on the Moon. This area introduces the "Walkers" and tests your movement abilities.
3.  **The Terror Dome:** A massive lunar forest designed for oxygen research. This sector shifts the gameplay toward stealth and tactical combat against invisible "Scorpion" units.
4.  **The Cradle Core:** The final gauntlet where your combat efficiency and hacking speed are put to the ultimate test.

Each of these sectors contains hidden blueprints and "Read Earth" memories. If you take the time to explore every corner of these 3D-printed worlds, you can easily add several hours to your total **pragmata duree de vie**.

## Side Activities and the Shelter System

A significant portion of your time will be spent in the **Shelter**, the game's central hub. Here, you can customize Hugh’s equipment, interact with Diana to strengthen your bond, and engage in "Simulated Training Missions." These missions are not merely filler; they are the primary way to earn currency for health and damage upgrades.

The training missions often feature unique gameplay twists, such as time-attack platforming or complex hacking puzzles that aren't found in the main campaign. Completing these is essential if you plan to tackle the game on higher difficulties, as the stat boosts they provide are significant.

| Activity Type | Time Impact | Reward/Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simulated Training** | High (5-7 hours) | Health/Damage upgrades and Hacking Nodes. |
| **Read Earth Collecting** | Medium (3-4 hours) | Narrative depth and "Shelter" decorations. |
| **Gear Crafting** | Low (1-2 hours) | Specialized weapon types and cosmetic skins. |

## Combat Mechanics and Speed of Completion

The combat system uses a "Dual-Focus" approach. While Hugh handles the traditional third-person shooting—reminiscent of the *Resident Evil 4* remake—Diana manages the "Hacking Matrix." This is a grid-based mini-game that occurs in real-time during firefights. 

Players who master the Hacking Matrix quickly will find that boss fights conclude much faster, potentially shortening the **pragmata duree de vie**. Conversely, players who struggle with the multitasking aspect of the combat may find themselves spending more time in the training simulations to compensate for their lack of efficiency.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when selling rare Lunafilaments early in the game. You will need these specific resources to craft the "Omega-rank" weapons required for the post-game difficulty spikes.

## Post-Game Content: The Capcom Influence

True to Capcom’s heritage, the end of the story is just the beginning for many players. Once the 10-hour credits roll, several new modes unlock that significantly extend the **pragmata duree de vie**. These include:

*   **New Game Plus (NG+):** Carry over all weapon upgrades, hacking nodes, and cosmetics to a new playthrough.
*   **Lunar Veteran Difficulty:** A brutal mode that changes enemy placements and increases aggression, requiring perfect mastery of Hugh and Diana's synergy.
*   **Score Attack Mode:** Similar to "Mercenaries" from the *Resident Evil* series, this mode challenges you to clear rooms of enemies as quickly as possible for global leaderboard rankings.

For those aiming to master every mechanic and earn the Platinum Trophy or 1000 Gamerscore, the total time spent can easily exceed 30 hours.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the difficulty setting change the pragmata duree de vie?**

A: Yes. Playing on "Hard" or "Lunar Veteran" will likely add 3 to 5 hours to your playtime due to the increased complexity of boss patterns and the need for more careful resource management in the Shelter.

**Q: Are there multiple endings based on my playtime?**

A: The main story follows a single path, but the number of "Read Earth" memories you collect and your level of interaction with Diana will slightly alter the dialogue and scenes during the game's epilogue.

**Q: Can I skip the simulated training missions?**

A: While they are technically optional, it is not recommended to skip them. These missions provide the necessary currency to upgrade Diana's hacking abilities, which become vital for the final two sectors of the game.

**Q: Is there a photo mode to capture the lunar scenery?**

A: Yes, the game includes a robust photo mode. Many players find themselves spending an extra 2-3 hours just capturing the "NASA-punk" aesthetic of the moon base.
