Capcom’s official press kits are the best source for these, as they provide uncompressed stills from the game's engine.

If you are using an Ultrawide monitor, you might find that standard 16:9 images leave black bars on the sides. In these cases, look for "concept art" rather than "in-game screenshots," as concept pieces are often painted in wider aspect ratios that can be cropped without losing the central focus of the image.

## How to Customize Your Desktop Experience

Simply setting a static image as your **pragmata wallpaper** is just the beginning. With modern software, you can create a truly "next-gen" desktop that mirrors the futuristic vibes of the game.

1.  **Wallpaper Engine:** Search the Steam Workshop for animated versions of the lunar surface. These often include moving particles, flickering lights on the space station, and subtle breathing animations for the characters.
2.  **Rainmeter Skins:** You can find skins that mimic the "Cradle" security protocol interface. Pairing these with a dark **pragmata wallpaper** can make your Windows desktop look like a terminal from the game.
3.  **Color Matching:** Use the Windows "Accent Color" feature to pick a color from the wallpaper. For this game, a "Deep Space Blue" or "Lunar Grey" usually works best.

> **Warning:** Be cautious of third-party wallpaper sites that require you to download an "installer" to get the image. Always use "Save Image As" or official [Capcom Press Center](https://www.capcom.com) links to avoid malware.
