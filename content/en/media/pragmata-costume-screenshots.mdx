## The Cabin Stamp System Explained

The primary method for obtaining new gear in Pragmata involves the Cabin Stamp system. Located within your central hub, the Shelter, this system allows you to spend Cabin Coins on various rewards. To maximize your collection and see all the **pragmata costume screenshots** in your own gallery, you must prioritize gathering coins through Training Sim missions.

As you progress through the game’s five sectors, you will level up your Shelter. Each level unlocks a new Stamp Card, for a total of five cards. Some outfits are found on individual slots within the cards, while others are "Blackout" rewards—exclusive items granted only after you have filled every single slot on a specific card.

| Shelter Level | Unlock Requirement | Stamp Card Reward Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Complete Sector 1 | Basic Exolayer gear |
| **Level 2** | Complete Sector 2 | Type B Variants |

| **Level 4** | Complete Sector 4 | Spy-themed White sets |

## Hugh’s Unlockable Outfits

Hugh, the protagonist and protector, features a variety of heavy-duty exolayers and atmospheric suits. His wardrobe ranges from standard-issue military gear to experimental shadow-tech found only in the deepest parts of the moon base.

### Stamp Card Rewards for Hugh

Hugh’s progression is tied heavily to the first three Stamp Cards. His initial **Deli Suit** is functional, but players often look for more specialized gear quickly. 

1.  **Exolayer Outfit:** Found on the first Stamp Card. This is a direct upgrade to the starting suit, offering a more armored aesthetic.
2.  **Exolayer Type B:** Located on the second Stamp Card. It features different color accents and minor structural changes.
3.  **Legion Zero:** This is the Blackout reward for the second Stamp Card. It is a fan-favorite for those seeking a more aggressive, tactical look.
4.  **Astroclassic:** Found on the third Stamp Card. This suit is highly sought after for its "retro-futuristic" aesthetic, reminiscent of mid-century sci-fi concepts.
5.  **Legion Zero Type B:** The Blackout reward for the third Stamp Card, offering a darker, more stealth-oriented palette.

Diana’s progression requires a bit more effort, as many of her best looks are locked behind Blackout rewards rather than simple slot purchases.

*   **Blue Cradle Jacket:** Diana’s default starting outfit.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there any outfits locked behind the Lunatic difficulty?**

A: Currently, most outfits are obtainable through the Stamp Cards and post-game missions on any difficulty. However, some players have reported unique emblems or minor armor decals that only appear after clearing specific missions on Lunatic.

**Q: Can I change Diana's hair independently of her outfit?**

A: No, hairstyles are currently tied to specific outfits. For example, the Star Lily outfit forces a shorter hairstyle, while the Lunar White maintains her standard length. You can see the difference in various **pragmata costume screenshots** shared by the community.

**Q: What is the "Blackout" reward?**

A: A Blackout reward is a special bonus given when you fill every single square on a Cabin Stamp Card. This usually requires 25 stamps per card, meaning you must participate in several side activities to earn enough coins.

**Q: How do I access the "Unknown Signal" missions?**

A: These missions appear in the Shelter's mission terminal only after you have defeated the final boss and watched the credits. They are significantly harder than the main story missions and are intended for players who have fully upgraded their gear.
