## Hugh Williams: Ballistic Weaponry and Combat Basics

Hugh serves as the primary damage dealer in the duo. Unlike traditional shooters, Pragmata utilizes a "fragility" system for its special weaponry, making resource management as important as aiming. Hugh’s primary weapon is a versatile pistol that features infinite ammunition, though it requires a short cooldown period to replenish its energy cells if fired continuously.

Beyond the standard pistol, Hugh can scavenge more powerful tools from the environment. These special weapons offer high utility but come with a significant catch: once their ammo is depleted, the weapon breaks and must be replaced by finding a duplicate on the battlefield.

| Weapon Name | Type | Primary Effect | Ammo Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Pistol** | Ballistic | Consistent mid-range damage. | **Infinite** (Replenishes over time). |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Heavy | High damage; staggers enemies. | **Limited** (Weapon breaks at 0 ammo). |
| **Stasis Net** | Tactical | Stuns and freezes enemies. | **Limited** (Non-lethal utility tool). |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on your special weapon's ammo counter. If a Shockwave Gun is near breaking, look for a "glow" on the floor that indicates a replacement weapon is available nearby.

## Diana’s Hacking Protocols: A Pragmata Ability Guide

While Hugh handles the physical threats, Diana provides the technical support necessary to bypass enemy defenses. Most advanced enemies, such as the "Walker" or the "Sector Guard," possess heavy armor that negates standard ballistic damage. To defeat them, Diana must initiate a hack to "open" the enemy, exposing their core for Hugh to attack.

The hacking process is a mini-game that requires the player to navigate a grid while Diana maintains her aim on the target. If Diana’s line of sight is broken or she is interrupted by an attack, the hacking progress may stall or reset.

### The Hacking Grid Nodes

During a hack, you will see a graph grid with various colored squares. Your goal is to chart a path from your starting position to the green exit node.

| Node Color | Effect on Combat | Tactical Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue Node** | Standard connection point. | Required to build a path to the exit. |
| **Yellow Node** | **Defense Debuff**: Lowers enemy armor. | High; pass through these to increase Hugh's DPS. |
| **Red Node** | **Hazard**: Triggers a system lockout. | Avoid at all costs to prevent hack failure. |
| **Green Node** | **Success**: Finalizes the hack. | The end goal of every hacking sequence. |

## Advanced Synergy: Overdrive Protocol and Weak Points

Once Diana successfully hacks an enemy, their armor plates will shift or retract, revealing a glowing blue core. This is when Hugh should switch to his high-damage weapons, like the Shockwave Gun, to maximize damage. However, the most powerful tool in your **pragmata ability guide** is the **Overdrive Protocol**.

The Overdrive Protocol is an "Ultimate" ability that charges as Diana performs successful hacks and Hugh deals damage. When the gauge is full, players can trigger it by pressing **L3 + R3** (on console).

*   **AOE Exposure:** Overdrive immediately stuns all nearby enemies and exposes their weak points without requiring a manual hacking mini-game.
*   **Time Dilation:** The world slows down slightly, allowing Hugh to pick off multiple targets in rapid succession.
*   **Emergency Recovery:** If you are surrounded by "Eaters," Overdrive acts as a perfect "get out of jail free" card to reset the engagement.

> **Warning:** Overdrive takes a significant amount of time to charge. Generally, it is recommended to save this ability for boss encounters or large waves of "Walker" units rather than using it on basic drones.

## Resource Management and Survival

Survival in Pragmata is tied to your ability to maintain your equipment. Because special weapons are fragile, you must constantly cycle through your inventory. If you find yourself without a Shockwave Gun or Stasis Net, you must rely on Hugh's mobility and the Primary Pistol's recharge rate.

1.  **Scavenge Constantly:** Look for crates and terminal stations. These often contain "Sugar Cubes" (healing items) or ammo refills.
2.  **Perfect Dodge:** Timing your dodge just before an enemy strike triggers a brief slow-motion effect, giving you a window to reposition or start a hack.
3.  **Escape Hatches:** If Hugh’s health drops to zero, Diana can attempt to teleport him to a nearby escape hatch. This acts as a checkpoint system, but it requires Diana to remain stationary for several seconds to "unlock" the hatch via a quick hack.

Check the [official Capcom Pragmata website](https://www.capcom.com/pragmata/) for the latest developer updates and patch notes regarding combat balancing and new weapon additions.

## Tactical Guide to Boss Encounters: The Eaters

Bosses in Pragmata, such as the massive tank-like "Sector Guard," require a multi-stage approach. You cannot simply "blast" your way through these encounters. 

*   **Stage 1: Observation.** Avoid the boss's initial barrages. Look for the blue nodes on their body; these are the points Diana needs to target.
*   **Stage 2: The Hack.** While Hugh draws aggro, Diana must complete a complex grid hack. During this phase, Hugh should use the Stasis Net to keep the boss stationary.
*   **Stage 3: The Core.** Once the hack is complete, the boss will usually collapse or reveal its pilot/core. This is the only time they are vulnerable to massive damage. Use your Shockwave Gun or Overdrive Protocol here to end the fight quickly.

| Rapid Charge Attack | Hugh's Stasis Net | Boss is frozen in place. |
| Area-of-Effect Slam | Perfect Dodge | Triggers slow-motion window. |
| Shield Generation | Yellow Node Hack | Lowers shield integrity. |

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important part of the pragmata ability guide for beginners?**

A: The most important aspect is understanding the synergy between Hugh and Diana. Hugh cannot deal significant damage to armored enemies until Diana has completed a hack. Focus on protecting Diana while she navigates the hacking grid.

**Q: Do weapons like the Shockwave Gun ever get permanent ammo?**

A: No. In Pragmata, special weapons are designed to be temporary power-ups. You must manage their ammo carefully and be prepared to switch back to your Primary Pistol once they break.

**Q: How do I charge the Overdrive Protocol faster?**

A: You can speed up the charge rate by passing through optional "Bonus Nodes" (Yellow Squares) during the hacking mini-game and by performing Perfect Dodges during combat.

**Q: Can Diana be killed during a hack?**

A: Diana can be damaged and interrupted, which will cause the hack to fail. If her health reaches zero, you will lose access to all hacking and Overdrive abilities until you can revive her or reach a checkpoint.
