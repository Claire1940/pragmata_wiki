## The Final Descent: Understanding the Choice System

Unlike traditional linear action games, the final act of the game hinges on the "Synchronization Level" between the Astronaut and the young girl. This mechanic, introduced early in the abandoned city sectors, tracks how often you utilized the girl's "Digital Echo" abilities versus relying on brute force. 

The narrative reaches its climax once the duo successfully bypasses the satellite debris field and reaches the Lunar Core. At this juncture, three primary factors determine which conclusion you will witness:

1.  **The Girl’s Autonomy:** Did you allow her to interface with the central AI, or did you keep her shielded?
2.  **Data Fragments:** The number of "Memory Cubes" collected from the digital echoes of the abandoned city.
3.  **The Final Flare:** The specific type of flare round used during the final vacuum sequence.

| Ending Name | Requirement | Primary Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Lunar Paradox** | Low Sync Level (&lt;40%) | The Astronaut remains on the Moon to stabilize the AI; the girl is sent to Earth alone. |
| **Earthbound Sacrifice** | High Sync (>70%), but missing Data Fragments | The girl restores Earth's atmosphere at the cost of the Astronaut's digital consciousness. |
| **The New Dawn (True Ending)** | 100% Sync + All 12 Data Fragments | Both characters survive and unlock the secret post-credits scene detailing the colony's rebirth. |

> **💡 Tip:** To maximize your Synchronization Level, ensure you interact with the holographic cat at every rest point. This hidden mechanic significantly boosts the girl's trust rating.

## Detailed Breakdown of the "Lunar Paradox" Ending

This is often considered the "Standard" ending for players who focus primarily on the combat and exploration without hunting for every collectible. In this scenario, the Astronaut realizes that the "sky screen" protecting the lunar colony is failing due to a logic loop in the central AI.

To save the girl, the Astronaut uses his gear to create a localized warp distortion, similar to the one seen in the game's initial reveal. He stays behind to manually override the system, becoming a permanent "Echo" within the lunar network. The girl is safely launched toward Earth in a protective mesh of springy cables, clutching the holographic cat's data core. 

While bittersweet, this ending sets up a potential sequel where the girl returns to the moon to rescue the Astronaut. If you are following this **pragmata ending guide** to see all content, this is the best ending to achieve on your first "speed-run" through the game.

## Unlocking the "New Dawn" True Ending

Achieving the True Ending is the ultimate goal for completionists. This path requires a meticulous approach to the "Digital Echo" side-quests found in the modern city simulation. These echoes aren't just lore; they are the keys to repairing the girl's fragmented memory.

### Step-by-Step True Ending Requirements:

*   **Collect all 12 Memory Cubes:** These are located in the "Echo Zones" where the sky appears to crack.
*   **The Cat’s Secret:** You must find the "Transparent Guts" upgrade for the holographic cat in Chapter 4. This allows the cat to detect hidden traps in the final vacuum sequence.
*   **The Final Choice:** When prompted to "Sever the Connection" or "Integrate," you must choose **Integrate**. This allows the girl to merge her consciousness with the Astronaut's suit, creating a dual-entity that can survive the AI's purge.

| Collectible Type | Location | Narrative Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Memory Cube #1-4** | Abandoned City Streets | Explains the origin of the "Sky Screen" project. |
| **Memory Cube #5-8** | Satellite Debris Field | Details the Astronaut's previous mission failures. |
| **Memory Cube #9-12** | Lunar Core | Reveals the girl's true identity as a bio-synthetic AI. |

## The Role of the Holographic Cat

One of the most discussed elements of the **pragmata ending guide** is the fate of the cat. While it initially seems like a cosmetic companion, the cat acts as a bridge between the digital and physical worlds. In the True Ending, the cat's transparent skin begins to glow, revealing that it is the "fail-safe" for the entire colony.

If you have upgraded the cat fully, it will sacrifice its physical form during the satellite collision to create a "Springy Cable" shield that protects both the Astronaut and the girl. Without these upgrades, the cat simply vanishes during the final cutscene, leading to the "Earthbound Sacrifice" ending.

## Post-Credits and Secret Scenes

Once the credits roll, do not turn off your console. Depending on your performance, there are two distinct post-credits scenes:

1.  **The Earth Signal:** (Unlocked in all endings) A short clip of a radio tower on Earth receiving a signal from the Moon, suggesting that humanity is still alive on the surface.
2.  **The 2026 Transmission:** (Unlocked in True Ending only) A first-person view from the Astronaut's helmet as he wakes up in a lush, green environment. The girl walks into the frame, no longer wearing her bomber jacket, implying that they have successfully reached a terraformed Earth or a new simulated paradise.

## Final Lore Analysis: What It Means

The ending of the game suggests that the "Earth" we see throughout the story is a simulation designed to keep the inhabitants of the lunar colony sane. The "crashing sky" is the literal breakdown of that simulation. By choosing to "Integrate," the player is essentially choosing to accept a new reality where the digital and physical are one. This profound story and setting, as Capcom describes it, challenges the player's perception of what is "real" in a high-tech future.

> **Warning:** Choosing the "Sever" option in the final dialogue will permanently lock you out of the True Ending for that save file. Always maintain a backup save before entering the Lunar Core.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my ending after reaching the Lunar Core?**

A: Only if you have a save file from before the final boss encounter. The game locks in your ending based on the "Synchronization Level" achieved throughout the entire campaign, though the final choice can sway the result between two of the three endings.

**Q: Is there a "Bad Ending" in Pragmata?**

A: While there is no traditional "Game Over" ending, the "Lunar Paradox" is considered the least favorable outcome as it separates the two main characters indefinitely. However, this **pragmata ending guide** recommends seeing it at least once for the unique lore entries it provides.

**Q: How do the Data Fragments affect the ending?**

A: The Data Fragments (Memory Cubes) provide the necessary context for the "Integrate" choice. Without them, the Astronaut does not have enough information to successfully merge with the AI, leading to a system crash and the "Earthbound Sacrifice" ending.

**Q: Does the difficulty setting affect which ending I get?**

A: No, the difficulty setting only affects combat and resource management. You can achieve the True Ending on any difficulty, provided you meet the Synchronization and collectible requirements.
