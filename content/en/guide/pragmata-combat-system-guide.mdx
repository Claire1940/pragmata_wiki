## Weaponry and Resource Management

Hugh’s arsenal is divided into two distinct categories: permanent primary weapons and temporary special armaments. Understanding the "breakage" mechanic is vital for maintaining offensive pressure during intense encounters.

### Primary vs. Special Weapons

Your primary weapon, typically a versatile pistol, is your most reliable tool. It features a self-replenishing ammo system. While you can deplete the magazine, it will automatically recharge in the background, ensuring you are never truly defenseless.

Special weapons, however, operate on a high-risk, high-reward basis. These tools, such as the Shockwave Gun and the Stasis Net, do not recharge. Once the ammunition is spent, the weapon itself breaks, requiring you to scavenge the battlefield for a replacement.

| Weapon Type | Primary (Pistol) | Shockwave Gun | Stasis Net |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Range** | Medium to Long | Short Range | Medium Range |
| **Main Function** | Consistent DPS | Stagger & High Damage | Crowd Control/Stun |
| **Ammo Source** | Automatic Recharge | Floor Pickups | Floor Pickups |
| **Durability** | Infinite | **Breaks when empty** | **Breaks when empty** |

> **Warning:** Do not hoard your special weapons. Since they break upon depletion and duplicates frequently drop from enemies, it is often better to use them aggressively to clear crowds rather than saving them for a "perfect" moment that may never come.

## The Hacking Mechanic: Diana’s Role

The most defining feature of the **pragmata combat system guide** is the hacking interface. Most enemies encountered in 2026 possess thick exterior plating that renders standard gunfire nearly useless. To deal significant damage, you must initiate a hack through Diana.

### How to Initiate a Hack
To begin the process, Hugh must protect Diana while she aims at a target. Once the connection is established, a grid-based minigame appears on the screen. This is a "live" combat mechanic, meaning enemies will continue to move and attack while you navigate the graph.

1.  **Maintain Line of Sight:** If Diana’s aim drifts off the enemy, the connection is interrupted. While you can resume from where you left off, the delay can be fatal.
2.  **Navigate the Grid:** You must move a cursor through a series of blue nodes, eventually reaching the green exit node.
3.  **Avoid Red Hazards:** Red nodes with caution marks will penalize your progress or cause the hack to fail.

### Hacking Node Types

| Node Color | Effect on Combat | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue Square** | Standard progression node; required to reach the end. | **High** |
| **Green Square** | Finalizes the hack and exposes enemy weak points. | **Essential** |
| **Yellow Square** | Bonus node found on the floor; lowers enemy defense. | **Optional/Strategic** |
| **Red Square** | Warning node; causes failure or resets progress. | **Avoid** |

## Advanced Combat: Overdrive and Synergy

As you successfully complete hacks, Diana’s **Overdrive Protocol** gauge will fill. This is your "ultimate" ability, designed for situations where Hugh is completely surrounded by hostile forces.

### Triggering Overdrive Protocol
By pressing **R3 + L3** simultaneously when the gauge is full, Diana releases a massive energy burst. This attack serves two purposes:
*   It provides an immediate stun to all enemies within a wide radius.
*   It automatically "opens up" every enemy in the area, bypassing the need for individual hacking minigames for a short duration.

> **Tip:** Save your Overdrive Protocol for boss phases or "ambush" rooms where the sheer number of armored enemies makes individual hacking impossible.

## Survival and Checkpoints

Despite your best efforts, the lunar environment is unforgiving. Pragmata utilizes a unique checkpoint system centered around "Escape Hatches."

When Hugh’s health is depleted, you aren't met with a traditional "Game Over" screen. Instead, you are teleported back to the last activated Escape Hatch. However, these hatches are not automatically active. When you find one, you must prompt Diana to hack it by holding **L1**. This "initializes" the checkpoint, ensuring that your progress is saved.

## Section Summary: The Combat Flow

To excel in the **pragmata combat system guide**, players should follow this basic combat loop:
1.  Identify the most dangerous armored threat.
2.  Use the **Stasis Net** to freeze the target in place.
3.  Lock on with **L2** to allow Diana to begin the hacking grid.
4.  Navigate the grid while dodging other enemies, collecting **Yellow Nodes** if they are available on the floor.
5.  Once the hack is complete and the "insides" are exposed, switch to the **Shockwave Gun** or **Pistol** to finish the enemy off.

## FAQ

**Q: Why did my Shockwave Gun disappear from my inventory?**

A: In Pragmata, special weapons like the Shockwave Gun have limited durability based on their ammo count. Once you run out of shots, the weapon breaks. You must find a new one on the battlefield to use that weapon type again.

**Q: Can I complete the game without using the hacking system?**

A: While you can damage some minor enemies with standard gunfire, the **pragmata combat system guide** emphasizes that elite and armored enemies have massive damage reduction. Hacking is the only efficient way to expose their weak points and deal "big damage."

**Q: What happens if Diana’s aim is interrupted during a hack?**

A: The hacking grid will pause or disconnect. You will need to re-establish the lock-on with L2. Fortunately, you can usually pick up the hack from your last progress point rather than starting the entire grid from scratch.

**Q: How do I get more Yellow Nodes to appear in the hacking grid?**

A: Yellow nodes are physical pickups found on the environment floor during combat. If you walk over them before initiating a hack, they will be "slotted" into the next hacking grid, allowing you to pass through them for massive debuffs to enemy defense.
