## Meet the Pragmata Cast: Main Characters and Roles

The story of Pragmata begins when communication between Earth and a vital lunar research station is abruptly severed. A team is dispatched to investigate, but things quickly go south following a massive "lunar quake." ### Hugh Williams: The Reluctant Hero
Hugh Williams serves as the primary playable character. Separated from his original team during the lunar quake, he is a man who relies on his wits and his customized spacesuit to survive. While he possesses a droll sense of humor that often lightens the tension of the desolate station, he is notably lacking in technical expertise. 

Hugh’s gameplay is focused on high-intensity gunplay and mobility. His suit is equipped with thrusters that allow for hovering and rapid dashes, which are vital for avoiding the lethal strikes of rogue androids. However, Hugh’s weapons are often ineffective against the advanced armor of the enemies unless he works in perfect synchronization with his companion.

### Diana: The Heart of the Station
Diana is arguably the most intriguing member of the **pragmata cast**. A friendly droid who rescues Hugh following the lunar quake, her origins are shrouded in mystery. She is a product of the "Lunafilament"—a revolutionary 3D printing technology derived from the lunar ore known as Lunum. 

Unlike typical droids, Diana harbors a deep-seated wish to see life beyond the confines of the research station. In combat, she is indispensable; she can hack into enemy systems to disable their armor, creating windows of opportunity for Hugh to strike. This synergy forms the backbone of the Pragmata experience, making her much more than a simple escort character.

## Supporting Entities and the Antagonist

While Hugh and Diana are the faces of the journey, the broader **pragmata cast** includes AI entities that define the environment and the threats you face.

### Idas: The Rogue AI
Idas is the primary threat within the station. Once a system designed to manage the lunar operations, Idas has turned hostile to all biological life. It controls a vast army of "rank and file" robots that are highly resistant to standard gunfire. Idas’s goal is simple: prevent Hugh and Diana from escaping the station at all costs. The narrative suggests that Idas's turn to violence is linked to the dangers of generative AI gone wrong, a central theme in the game’s 2026 storyline.

### Cabin: The Shelter Guardian
Not all AI on the station is hostile. Cabin is an affable, independent robot who resides in the "Shelter"—a safe zone where players can perform maintenance and upgrades. Cabin is connected to a larger network (also called Cabin) and provides essential support by helping players upgrade their gear using 3D printing technology.

> **Warning:** While the Shelter is a safe zone, reaching it requires finding escape hatches strewn throughout the levels. Always keep an eye on your map, as these hatches also serve as your primary checkpoints.

## Gameplay Synergy: How the Cast Works Together

The relationship between the members of the **pragmata cast** isn't just for show; it is a mechanical necessity. The "Codependent Cooperation" system requires players to manage both Hugh’s physical attacks and Diana’s digital intrusions.

### Combat Mechanics Table
| Feature | Hugh's Role | Diana's Role | Synergy Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Armor Breaking** | Distracts enemies with fire | Performs grid-based hack | Disables enemy defenses |
| **Mobility** | Uses suit thrusters | Rides on Hugh's back | Allows for vertical traversal |
| **Finishers** | Executes deadly strikes | Identifies weak points | Instant-kill on weakened foes |
| **Resource Management** | Collects upgrade materials | Processes tech in Shelter | Unlocks advanced weapon slots |

Hugh’s space suit features four distinct units for weapon slotting. These weapons range from standard tactical rifles to heavy defense units. However, many powerful weapons have limited ammo or can even break under stress. This adds a layer of tactical thinking, as you must decide when to use your best gear and when to rely on Diana's hacking to conserve resources.

## Exploring the Moon: Fast Travel and Exploration

The world of Pragmata is surprisingly large, necessitating a fast travel system known as "The Tram." This system allows the **pragmata cast** to move between unlocked escape hatches and the central Shelter. 

1. **Unlock Escape Hatches**: These serve as checkpoints and fast travel nodes.
2. **Collect Lunum**: Use this ore to fuel the 3D printers in the Shelter.
3. **Synergize**: Use Diana’s hacking to open locked doors and access hidden sectors.
4. **Upgrade**: Visit Cabin to improve weapon durability and hacking speed.

As you progress, you will unlock new abilities that allow you to return to previous areas and access secrets that were once out of reach. This "Metroidvania" style of exploration ensures that players spend plenty of time learning the layout of the station while uncovering the backstory of the **pragmata cast**.
