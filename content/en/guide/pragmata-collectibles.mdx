Navigating the lunar surface in Capcom’s latest sci-fi epic requires more than just combat prowess; it demands a keen eye for **pragmata collectibles**. Finding all **pragmata collectibles** is essential for those looking to fully decorate their shelter and uncover every secret the game has to offer. Unlike standard data logs or equipment upgrades, these figurines do not appear on Diana’s scans, nor do they contribute to the percentage markers on your map. ## The Mystery of Pragmata Collectibles

Before diving into the specific locations, it is important to understand how these figurines function. Unlike other **pragmata collectibles**, Mini Cabin Figurines provide an auditory cue rather than a visual highlight on your HUD. When you are near one, you will hear a distinct singing or mechanical spinning sound. 

To "collect" a figurine, you must locate it in the environment and shoot it. This adds the item to your permanent collection back at the shelter. Because Diana cannot scan for these, you must rely on your ears and our detailed instructions below to find them all.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are struggling to hear the figurines, try turning down the music volume in the settings menu. The mechanical "chirping" sound they make is directional, which helps you pinpoint their height and distance.

## Sector 1: Solar Power Plant

The Solar Power Plant serves as your introduction to many game mechanics, including the hunt for **pragmata collectibles**. There are three figurines located here.

| Figurine # | Location Description | Specific Instructions |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Tunnel near Stasis Net | After getting the stasis net, clear the tunnel enemies. Look right in the corner on top of some objects. |
| **2** | Shelter Bunk Beds | Near the globe in the main control lobby shelter. Check the mesh-covered bunk beds on the left wall. |
| **3** | Vertical Zipline Pipes | Past the main control lobby, look up and behind you when you reach the vertical zipline. |

The second figurine in the shelter is particularly tricky because of the "fussy" angles required to hit it. You may need to move back and forth along the bunk beds until the figurine is clearly visible through the mesh. Once shot, it will immediately register in your collection.

## Sector 2: Mass Production Array

Sector 2, also known as the shopping district and industrial hub, hides its figurines in plain sight but at extreme heights. You will need to make frequent use of the vertical ziplines to spot these **pragmata collectibles**.

### Shopping District Ledge
The first figurine in this sector is located right next to the orbit mannequin display. There is a vertical zipline nearby; ride it to the top. Once you reach the ledge, look up and to your left. The figurine is sitting on the very top of a nearby building ledge, singing its distinct tune.

### Side Alley Truck
After you climb the central tower in the side alley, you will cross over to another zipline. Instead of rushing forward, stop and aim along the building wall. You will see a figurine sitting on the back of a parked truck suspended in a precarious position.

### Recycling Control Storage
The final figurine for Sector 2 is found in the same room as the recycling control station shortcut. Look for a large storage container in the corner of the room. The figurine is tucked inside, singing away.

## Sector 3: The Terror Dome

The Terror Dome is a more organic environment, featuring trees and roots that make spotting small spinning models much more difficult. This sector contains three of the most well-hidden **pragmata collectibles** in the game.

| Figurine # | Area | Navigation Note |
|---|---|---|
| **7** | Eco Modeling Lab | Near the first gen chip socket. Look up at the tree roots near the ceiling. |
| **8** | Soil Research | After the elevator fight with three robots. Look up at the tree over the railing. |
| **9** | Geoscience Lab | Inside a box in the room past the second jamming encounter. |

For the ninth figurine, you have two options. You can either climb the nearby crates to get a narrow angle through the side of the box, or you can use a **Riot Blaster** shot to cause an explosion that triggers the collectible from the inside. This is one of the few figurines that requires a specific weapon type to collect easily.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Soil Research figurine (Figurine 8) is easily missed because it is located behind you after a combat encounter. Always look up before proceeding through the blocked doorway.

## Sector 4: Lunum Mines

The Lunum Mines are characterized by industrial machinery and vast warehouses. The **pragmata collectibles** here are often tucked into the architecture of the mining equipment.

1.  **Crane Operation Yard:** Halfway through the yard, you will hack a panel to drop a container. Before flying toward the container, make a sharp right turn. The figurine is tucked away in the dark corner of the yard.
2.  **Warehouse Ceiling:** After moving blocks in the warehouse, you will enter a square room. Before taking the final zipline out, hop onto the ledge to the right (where you find training data). Turn around and look at the ceiling pipes to find the figurine.
3.  **Nexus Tower:** In the circular room before the drop-off, stand in front of the chest against the far wall. Look up and to the left toward the window to find the buddy sitting on the sill.

## Sector 6: Central Port

Note that Sector 5 contains zero figurines, so you can focus entirely on the story and other **pragmata collectibles** during that portion of the game. Sector 6, the Central Port, contains the final three figurines.

### Cargo Terminal Pit
After fighting through the yellow combat zone, face away from the orange airlock door. Walk toward the railing overlooking the massive pit. If you look slightly to your left across the gap, you can see the figurine spinning on a distant ledge. You will need a scoped weapon or steady aim to hit this one from across the chasm.

### Research Sector Hallway
From the lobby station, take the left fork into a narrow hallway filled with corrupted enemies. Navigate past the dead filament and reach the hacking panel at the end. Once the door is open, enter the next room to find a safe box containing a cabin coin. Turn around and look above the doorway you just entered; the figurine is spinning right at the top of the frame.

### Laboratory Main Entrance
The final figurine is located near the trail of "limb" (the game's mysterious white substance). Follow the trail to a walkway and head downstairs. Near the bottom, where you find an upgrade component, look past the spiky filament toward the open pit. The 15th and final Mini Cabin Figurine is hanging out over the edge.

## Completing Your Collection

Once you have shot all 15 figurines, you can view them all in one place. Return to the **Shelter** and check the benches near the main hub. All your "buddies" will be lined up, spinning and singing in unison. This is a great way to verify which ones you might have missed, as each bench corresponds to a specific sector.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need all Pragmata collectibles to finish the story?**

A: No, the Mini Cabin Figurines are optional collectibles. However, finding them unlocks unique decorations for your shelter and is required for the "Master Collector" achievement/trophy.

**Q: Why can't I see the figurines when I use Diana's scan?**

A: The Mini Cabin Figurines are specifically designed to be "off-grid." They do not show up on scans or the map, forcing players to rely on the audio cues (singing/spinning sounds) to find them.

**Q: Can I go back to previous sectors to find missed figurines?**

A: Yes, once you unlock the Chapter Select or the free-roam capability late in the game, you can revisit any sector to hunt down the remaining **pragmata collectibles**.

**Q: What happens if I shoot a figurine but die before reaching a checkpoint?**

A: Collectibles in Pragmata save instantly upon being shot. Even if you die or restart the checkpoint, the figurine will remain in your collection.
