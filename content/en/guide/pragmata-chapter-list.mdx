## Character Synergy and Combat Mechanics

The heart of the experience lies in how Hugh and Diana interact. While Hugh handles the traditional "firearms and mobility" aspects of a third-person shooter, Diana provides the "tactile strategy" mentioned in the official overview. Progressing through the **pragmata chapter list** requires players to switch mindsets frequently. Hugh is your shield and sword, but Diana is the key that unlocks the world.

### Hugh: The Combat Specialist
Hugh’s gameplay is defined by weight and momentum. His suit allows for high-impact movement, but he is vulnerable without Diana’s support. As you move through the **pragmata chapter list**, you will unlock various firearm modules:

*   **Standard Pulse Rifle:** Effective at mid-range against basic filament enemies.
*   **Heavy Railgun:** Slow fire rate but essential for piercing the armor of Edas’s elite guards.
*   **Mobility Thrusters:** Used for dodging lunar filament traps and clearing large gaps.

### Diana: The Hacking Prodigy
Diana might appear carefree, but her ability to "crack open" robots to reveal their vulnerable inner workings is what makes survival possible. Her hacking system is intuitive yet deep, requiring players to manage the space around them while she interfaces with the station's hostile hardware.

## Detailed Chapter Walkthroughs

### Chapter 1: The Lunar Quake
The story begins with a catastrophic event on Cradle. Players are introduced to the basic movement controls as Hugh. After a near-fatal lunar quake, you encounter Diana. This chapter serves as a tutorial for the "hacking and shooting" system. You must protect Diana while she overrides the door locks to the Habitation Sector.

### Chapter 2: Cradle’s Echo
Once inside the inner station, the threat of Edas becomes more apparent. The rogue AI begins sending twisted lunar filament creations—mechanical horrors that mimic biological life. Within this part of the **pragmata chapter list**, players learn how to use Diana to reveal enemy weak points. 

| Enemy Type | Weakness | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Filament Crawler** | Exposed Core | Use Hugh’s pulse rifle after Diana hacks the shell. |
| **Drone Sentry** | Sensor Array | Diana can override these to fight for you temporarily. |
| **Stalker Mech** | Leg Joints | Focus fire on the joints to slow its movement. |

### Chapter 3: The Filament Wastes
Leaving the safety of the station, Hugh and Diana must traverse the 3D-printed lunar landscape. This area is "crammed with twisted lunar filament creations" and "buried enemies." The verticality of this chapter increases significantly, requiring frequent use of Hugh’s mobility thrusters.

> **Warning:** The lunar dust in the Wastes can interfere with Diana's hacking range. Keep Hugh close to her to maintain a stable connection.

## Boss Encounters: Facing Edas

The primary antagonist, Edas, is not just a voice in the walls; it manifests as massive, complex entities throughout the **pragmata chapter list**. Each boss fight is a puzzle that tests your mastery of the hacking and shooting mechanics.

1.  **The Sentinel (Chapter 2 Boss):** A massive security bot that requires Diana to hack three different terminals while Hugh draws its fire.
2.  **The Weaver (Chapter 4 Boss):** A filament-based entity that creates clones. Players must use Diana’s "Scan" ability to find the real Weaver among the illusions.
3.  **Edas Core (Final Boss):** A multi-stage fight involving platforming, hacking, and precision shooting.

## FAQ

**Q: How many missions are in the pragmata chapter list?**

A: There are 5 main story chapters in the base game. However, each chapter contains multiple sub-objectives and side paths that can significantly extend your playtime.

**Q: Can I replay chapters to find missed secrets?**

A: Yes, once you complete a chapter, it becomes available in the "Mission Select" menu. This is the best way to find all the surprising secrets buried in the lunar landscape.

**Q: Is there a demo available for Pragmata?**

A: Yes, a demo is currently available on PC and consoles. It features a portion of Chapter 1 and Chapter 2, allowing players to get to grips with the hacking and shooting system before the full game's release in 2026.

**Q: Does Diana have her own skill tree?**

A: While not a traditional skill tree, Diana gains new hacking capabilities as you progress through the story. These are often unlocked at pivotal moments in the **pragmata chapter list** and can be further enhanced at upgrade stations.
