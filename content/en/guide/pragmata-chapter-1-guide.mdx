## Chapter 1: Core Mechanics and Navigation

The first mission serves as an extended tutorial that introduces the symbiotic relationship between the protagonist and Diana. Unlike traditional escort missions, Diana provides tactical support, including hacking and scanning capabilities that are required to bypass the facility's emergency lockdown protocols.

### Essential Controls and Scanning
Early in the chapter, you will find yourself in a locked area. The game encourages the use of the **Scanning pulse**. This mechanic highlights interactable objects and current objectives. If you find yourself lost, scanning will usually reveal the path to the next power terminal or a hackable door.

| Action | Function | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scanning** | Highlights objectives | Use when lost or looking for terminals |
| **Diana's Hack** | Disables lockdowns | Mandatory for progressing through sealed doors |
| **Throw/Boost** | Launches Diana upward | Reaches high-access vents or switches |
| **Terminal Interaction** | Enhances damage | Temporary buffs for difficult combat encounters |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep Diana close during combat. While she can defend herself to a degree, her hacking speed is significantly increased when the protagonist is nearby to provide cover fire.

## Navigating the Facility Lockdown

The primary objective of Chapter 1 is to restore power to the facility to contact Earth. This requires navigating through several sub-levels before reaching the top floor. You will encounter your first major obstacle when the "Emergency Lockdown" protocol is initiated by the station's AI.

### Restoring the Main Power Terminal
To progress, you must locate and reboot three sub-terminals. These are spread across the lower levels and are guarded by "Walker" bots. These enemies are not particularly fast, but they can overwhelm you in groups of four or more.

1.  **First Terminal:** Located near the initial drop-off point. Have Diana initiate the hack while you fend off the first wave of bots.
2.  **Second Terminal:** Found on the mezzanine level. Use the scan function to locate the power lines running through the floor.
3.  **Third Terminal:** Situated near the elevator shaft. This terminal requires a manual reboot, which leaves you vulnerable for several seconds.

## Combat Strategies: From Walkers to Sector Guards

Combat in Pragmata is methodical. Your suit's weapons are effective at mid-range, but the "Walker" bots often attempt to close the distance. Chapter 1 introduces the concept of "hitting harder" by using specialized terminals to overclock your weapon systems.

### Dealing with the Sector Guard
The climax of Chapter 1 involves a confrontation with the **Sector Guard**, an EDS (Electronic Defense System) unit that views you as an unregistered guest. This boss is significantly tougher than the standard bots and utilizes long-range missile volleys.

| Phase | Enemy Behavior | Counter-Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1** | Missile Barrage | Strafe laterally and use cover; do not stay stationary |
| **Phase 2** | Shielded Advance | Use Diana's hack to disrupt the guard's energy field |
| **Phase 3** | Overdrive State | Activate your own **Overdrive Protocol** to bypass armor |

> **Warning:** The Sector Guard's missiles have a slight tracking capability. Waiting until the last second to dodge is generally more effective than spamming the dash button.

## The Overdrive Protocol and Power Restoration

As you reach the final terminal on the top floor, the power restoration process will begin. This is a scripted sequence where you must defend the terminal while the energy levels climb from 30% to 100%. At approximately 60%, the EDS will attempt to sever the connection, triggering a final wave of intruders.

This is the ideal time to trigger the **Overdrive Protocol**. This state increases your fire rate and movement speed, allowing you to "kick" through the remaining resistance. Once the energy levels reach 100%, the lockdown will disengage, and the path to the next chapter will open.

### Upgrade Terminal Locations
Throughout Chapter 1, keep an eye out for smaller, orange-glowing terminals. These are not required for the story but provide permanent or semi-permanent buffs to your suit.

*   **Terminal 1:** Behind the crates in the first combat arena (Increases Scan Range).
*   **Terminal 2:** In the maintenance hall before the elevator (Increases Ammo Capacity).
*   **Terminal 3:** To the left of the main power controller (Decreases Overdrive Cooldown).

## Conclusion of Chapter 1

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the main power terminal in Pragmata Chapter 1?**

A: The main power terminal is located on the top floor of the facility. To reach it, you must first disable the emergency lockdown by rebooting three sub-terminals found on the lower levels. Use your scan ability frequently to see the objective markers through walls.

**Q: What is the best way to protect Diana during combat?**

A: Diana is quite resilient, but she cannot hack while being attacked. It is recommended to use your "Step off" melee move to knock back enemies that get too close to her. If she is targeted by missiles, you can pick her up or throw her to a safer location.

**Q: How does the Overdrive Protocol work in this pragmata chapter 1 guide?**

A: The Overdrive Protocol is a temporary power-up that increases your combat effectiveness. It is best saved for the Sector Guard boss fight or the final defense sequence when power levels are between 60% and 80%. It recharges over time as you defeat standard enemies.

**Q: Can I miss any upgrades in Chapter 1?**

A: Yes, several suit upgrade terminals are hidden in side rooms. While they aren't mandatory to finish the chapter, they make future encounters much easier. Always explore the side corridors before interacting with the main elevator or power switches.
