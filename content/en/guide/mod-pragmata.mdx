## Optimizing Graphical Settings for VR

One of the most common issues when using the **mod pragmata** VR setup is a shimmering or "ghosting" effect, particularly around character models and high-contrast edges. This is often caused by the game's advanced lighting features, such as Ray Tracing and Subsurface Scattering, which do not always play nicely with dual-lens VR rendering.

To achieve a "cleaner" image, players should navigate to the options menu immediately upon launching the Sketchbook demo. Turning off Ray Tracing is the single most effective way to stabilize the image and reduce the shimmering effect. Additionally, disabling Subsurface Scattering helps clarify the textures on character faces, which can otherwise appear blurry in the headset.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact on VR Clarity |
|---|---|---|
| **Ray Tracing** | **Off** | High - Eliminates shimmering |
| **Subsurface Scattering** | **Off** | Medium - Cleans up character models |
| **DLSS / FSR** | **Quality** | High - Improves FPS without sacrificing depth |
| **HUD / UI** | **Off (Optional)** | High - Fixes double-image UI issues |

## Navigating the Pragmata Sketchbook Gameplay

The Sketchbook demo serves as a vertical slice of the full game, focusing on the relationship between Hugh and Diana. In VR, the sense of height when jumping or floating is particularly striking. Players must master the "Cradle" environment, which involves a mix of third-person combat and intricate hacking mini-games.

### Combat and Weapons
Combat in Pragmata is highly kinetic. You have access to various weapons suited for different ranges. For example, the close-range scattergun is devastating against smaller "Eater" bots, while the stasis rifle can be used to freeze larger threats in place. When running the **mod pragmata** VR version, aiming can be tricky due to the "double cursor" bug. To mitigate this, many players enable aim assist or rely on the laser sight attachments found on specific weapons.

### The Hacking Mechanic
Throughout the demo, you will encounter locked terminals that require Diana’s assistance. This triggers a mini-game where you must navigate a series of blue nodes.
1. **Initiate the Hack:** Stand near the terminal and prompt Diana.
2. **Follow the Sequence:** Use the directional inputs (Down, Across, Up) as they appear on the screen.
3. **Speed is Key:** There is often a hidden time limit. Failing to input the commands quickly enough will result in a reset, forcing you to start the sequence over.

> **💡 Tip:** If the HUD is causing visual discomfort in VR, you can turn it off in the settings. You will still be able to see Waypoints in the world, which provide enough direction to complete the demo.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the mod pragmata VR version support motion controllers?**

A: Currently, most users recommend playing with a standard gamepad. While the VR mod allows you to see the world in 3D, the interaction and aiming are still tied to traditional controller inputs. Motion controller support is a frequent request and may be added in future community updates.

**Q: How do I fix the "Double Image" HUD in VR?**

A: The current work-in-progress mod often renders the HUD at a different depth than the game world. The best solution is to go into the Game Settings and turn the HUD "Off." This removes the distracting double image and forces you to rely on in-game visual cues and waypoints.

**Q: Is the Pragmata Sketchbook demo free to play?**

A: Yes, the Sketchbook demo was released as a free preview to showcase the game's mechanics and visual fidelity. The **mod pragmata** files are also free to download from community forums, though you must own the demo files on your PC to use them.

**Q: Can I play the VR mod on a PlayStation VR2?**

A: The VR mod is specifically designed for the PC version of the game. To use it, you would need to connect your PSVR2 to a PC using a compatible adapter and run the game through a PCVR environment. There is no native "mod" support for the console version of Pragmata.
