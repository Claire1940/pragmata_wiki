## Understanding the Boss Mechanics: Edith

The primary threat in the power plant sector is Edith, an AI-driven security system that utilizes the Sector Guard Protocol. Unlike standard enemies, Edith controls the environment and deploys specialized hardware designed for "unregistered guest" neutralization. Success depends on maintaining mobility while Diana works to bypass the emergency lockdowns.

| Mechanic | Description | Countermeasure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sector Guard Protocol** | Automated scanning that targets the player with high-precision lasers. | Maintain constant movement; use dash during the final "lock-on" sound. |
| **Missile Barrage** | A volley of tracking projectiles launched from the ceiling or wall mounts. | Use the heavy weapon to intercept or find cover behind structural pillars. |
| **Overdrive Protocol** | A high-damage state triggered by Diana to create an opening. | Focus all firepower on the central core while the blue aura is active. |
| **Emergency Lockdown** | Barriers that segment the arena and restrict movement. | Protect Diana while she performs the manual reboot of the terminal. |

> **Warning:** Do not attempt to stay in one position for more than three seconds. Edith's targeting sensors are designed to punish stationary targets with an immediate orbital-style strike.

## Combat Phases and Strategy

The **pragmata boss fight** against the sector guard is divided into distinct phases based on the power restoration levels. As the energy percentage climbs from 30% to 100%, the aggressiveness of the automated defenses increases.

### Phase 1: The Power Restoration (30% - 60%)
Initially, the fight is about crowd control. You must defend the primary power terminal while "haywire bats" and small drones attempt to interrupt the connection. Use your long-range scanner to identify incoming threats before they reach your perimeter.

### Phase 2: Sector Guard Initiation
Once the power reaches 60%, Edith severs the primary connection. This is where the true boss encounter begins. The environment becomes your enemy as the room itself begins to shift. You will need to utilize the "Luna-made" missiles against the guard's own shielding.

When the boss's health drops below 25%, Diana will initiate the Overdrive Protocol. This is your window to "kick some butt" and end the fight. During this window, your damage output is tripled, but your defensive capabilities are lowered.

## Advanced Combat Tips

To achieve a "No Damage" run or simply to survive on higher difficulties, you must integrate advanced movement techniques into your strategy. The **pragmata boss fight** rewards players who can weave together offensive bursts with environmental awareness.

*   **Environmental Interaction**: Look for the yellow-coded terminals. These can be used to trigger temporary EMP bursts that stun the sector guard.
*   **Diana’s Assistance**: When Diana shouts "I got it," she has successfully opened a flaw in the enemy's logic. Redirect your fire immediately to the highlighted area.
*   **Ammo Management**: Don't waste your heavy missiles on the small drones. Use your basic fire for "haywire bats" and save the heavy ordnance for Edith's main chassis.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I stop the missile barrage during the pragmata boss fight?**

A: The most effective way to handle the missile barrage is to use your heavy weapon to shoot them out of the air before they impact. Alternatively, you can time your dash to move toward the missiles, which often confuses their tracking sensors.

**Q: What should I do when the "Unauthorized Access Detected" warning appears?**

A: This warning signals the transition into the boss phase. Immediately move away from the central power terminal and seek cover, as Edith will perform an area-of-effect blast to clear "unregistered guests" from the immediate vicinity.

**Q: Is Diana able to take damage during the fight?**

A: While Diana is highly resilient, her progress on hacking terminals can be reset if she is hit by major attacks. Your primary goal is to draw aggro away from her so she can complete the Overdrive Protocol.

**Q: How do I restore power to 100%?**

A: Power restoration is tied to the boss's health. Each time you deplete a bar of Edith's health, the system will reconnect, increasing the energy levels. The fight ends when the power reaches 100% and the Sector Guard Protocol is fully disabled.
