## Hugh Williams: The Protagonist of Pragmata

Hugh Williams is not your typical invincible action hero. He is introduced as a spacefarer investigating a lunar research station that has gone dark. Early footage from 2026 reveals that Hugh is vulnerable; an early injury during his investigation sets the stakes high from the beginning. His survival depends entirely on his "Suit," which monitors his vitals and provides the necessary life support to endure the vacuum and the station's harsh interior.

Hugh’s character design emphasizes a "lived-in" sci-fi aesthetic. His gear is bulky and functional, reflecting a reality where space travel is dangerous and industrial. Unlike his companion Diana, who possesses supernatural digital capabilities, Hugh relies on conventional weaponry and physical maneuvers. However, his most important asset is his ability to coordinate with Diana to overcome the station's "near-impenetrable" robotic sentries.

## The Role of Diana and the Hacking System

While you spend much of your time as **hugh pragmata**, the gameplay is fundamentally a cooperative experience. Diana is a "Pragmata," a highly advanced android with the ability to interface directly with the station's AI. Because the enemy Bots encountered on the moon feature armor that standard bullets cannot pierce, Diana’s hacking skills are your primary method of offense.

The hacking mechanic is a real-time mini-game that occurs during combat. When Diana initiates a hack, a digital interface appears over the enemy. Players must move a cursor through various panels toward a green goal. This isn't just a binary "win/loss" mechanic; the path you choose through the nodes determines the outcome of the hack.

| Node Type | Effect on Enemy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Red Node** | Increases raw damage taken after hack |
| **Blue Node** | Disables enemy movement/weapons |
| **Yellow Node** | Drops extra energy or resources |
| **Green Goal** | Completes the hack and exposes the weak point |

By successfully navigating these nodes, you can modify the damage and status effects inflicted on the bots. Once the weak point is exposed, Hugh can use his weaponry to finish the job. This synergy ensures that combat feels like a constant dance between digital manipulation and physical firepower.

## Lunar Research Station: The Setting and Story

The primary setting for Hugh's adventure is a lunar research station controlled by an entity known as the station AI. This AI views Hugh and Diana as "unauthorized intruders" and "unregistered guests," triggering the "Cradle Security Protocol." The narrative suggests that the station was once a sanctuary or a place of great scientific importance, often referred to as the "Cradle."

As Hugh, you will find remnants of Earth's influence—mentions of the sea, houses with views, and the distance between souls. These nostalgic elements contrast sharply with the cold, metallic reality of the moon. The mystery of why Diana, an android, seems to possess such human-like curiosity is a driving force of the plot.

### Key Locations in the Station
1.  **The Awakening Chambers**: Where Hugh’s vitals are stabilized at the start of the journey.
2.  **The Hangar Bay**: A massive area filled with decommissioned bots and heavy machinery.
3.  **The AI Core**: The central hub where the station's security protocols originate.
4.  **The Observation Deck**: A rare spot where Earth is visible, triggering dialogue about "home."

## Combat Mechanics and Overdrive Protocol

Combat in **hugh pragmata** is tactical rather than "run-and-gun." Because the Bots are heavily armored, players must wait for the right moment to strike. This often involves kiting enemies while Diana completes her hacking sequence. However, when things get dire, players can trigger the **Overdrive Protocol**.

The Overdrive Protocol is a high-energy state where Hugh and Diana’s systems synchronize perfectly. During this mode, time slows down, and Hugh’s weapons gain a massive boost in fire rate and penetration. It is the ultimate "get out of jail free" card when surrounded by multiple AI-controlled bots.

| Combat Action | Primary User | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Shot** | Hugh | Minor stagger, no damage to armored parts |
| **Initiate Hack** | Diana | Opens the hacking mini-game interface |
| **Weak Point Strike** | Hugh | Massive damage to exposed internal circuitry |
| **Overdrive Protocol** | Both | Slows time and maximizes damage output |

> **Warning:** Overdrive Protocol consumes a significant amount of energy. If you deplete your energy bar entirely, Diana may become temporarily unresponsive, leaving Hugh without hacking support.
