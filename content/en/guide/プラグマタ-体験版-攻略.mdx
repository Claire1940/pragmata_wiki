## Basic Mechanics and Controls

Pragmata blends traditional third-person shooter (TPS) elements with high-mobility platforming. Hyu, an engineer equipped with a high-tech EVA suit, relies on internal energy to power his "Space Boost" and air dashes. Understanding how to manage this energy while coordinating with Diana is the first step to success.

| Action | Control (PS5/PC) | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Environment Scan** | L1 / Q | Highlights interactive terminals, items, and enemy weak points. |
| **Shift / Evade** | RB / R1 | A quick dash. Perfect timing triggers a slow-motion "Witch Time" effect. |
| **Air Dash** | A + A / X + X | Extends jump distance and allows for vertical repositioning. |
| **Hacking Instruction** | X / Square | Directs Diana to interface with specific nodes or terminals. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep your Scan active. It doesn't just find loot; it reveals the "EXE Nodes" required to lift sector lockdowns, which is a vital part of any **プラグマタ 体験版 攻略** run.

## Combat Systems: Hacking and Gunplay

Combat in Pragmata is a multi-layered process. It is rarely about just pulling the trigger; you must first expose enemy vulnerabilities through Diana’s hacking abilities. The core loop involves using Diana to disable armor, then following up with Hyu's heavy weaponry.

### The Hacking Mini-Game
When Diana interfaces with a terminal or a large enemy, a holographic panel appears. You must guide a pulse from the starting point to the green "End" node.
1.  **Armor Stripping:** Successfully reaching the green node removes enemy shielding.
2.  **Time Extension:** Passing through blue nodes during the hack extends the duration the enemy remains vulnerable.
3.  **Weak Point Highlight:** Once the hack is complete, the enemy's core will glow yellow. Aim specifically for these highlights to deal massive damage.

### Hacking Node Types
During larger skirmishes, Diana can connect to environmental nodes to provide buffs or debuffs.

*   **Intel Disruption:** Temporarily increases the damage enemies take from all sources.
*   **Multi-Target Link:** Chains nearby enemies together, causing damage dealt to one to resonate through the entire group.
*   **Overdrive Protocol:** Diana’s ultimate ability. Once the gauge is full, it neutralizes all nearby threats and freezes incoming projectiles in mid-air.

## Solar Panel Factory Walkthrough

The demo mission tasks you with reaching the facility's summit to reboot the main power terminal. The path is divided into three distinct phases.

### 1. The Entrance Hall
Your first objective is to locate three EXE Nodes. Use your Scan (L1/Q) to find them hidden behind crates and upper walkways. Once all three are activated, the lockdown will lift. Watch out for "Walkers" that drop from the ceiling; use your Grip Gun to keep them at bay while Diana works on the terminal.

### 2. Maintenance Tunnels and Verticality
This section tests your platforming. Use your Air Dash to clear gaps, but be wary of "Stasis Nets" on the floor. These traps will root Hyu in place, leaving him vulnerable to sniper bots.
*   **The Lift Sequence:** While Diana hacks the lift to move you upward, you must defend the platform from waves of drones. The Shockwave Gun is perfect here for knocking drones off the platform without needing precise aim.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Falling off the platform during the lift sequence results in an immediate reset to the last Escape Hatch. Keep your feet planted and focus on the drones.

### 3. Escape Hatches and the Shelter
Throughout the factory, you will find **Escape Hatches**. These act as checkpoints. Opening them allows you to return to the "Shelter," where you can use gathered minerals to upgrade Hyu’s suit or restore health. Never pass a hatch without activating it.

## Boss Guide: EDIS (Sector Guard Protocol)

The demo concludes with a battle against **EDIS**, a high-mobility guard bot. This fight requires you to utilize every mechanic learned so far.

*   **Phase 1: Missile Management.** EDIS will launch volleys of "Lunar" missiles. Wait for Diana to shout about the "Overdrive Protocol." Activating it will freeze the missiles, allowing you to hit them back at EDIS for a massive stagger.
*   **Phase 2: Physical Aggression.** EDIS will begin charging at you. Use your Shift (R1/RB) just before impact to trigger Witch Time. While time is slowed, switch to your Shotgun or Charge Piercer and blast the glowing core on its chest.
*   **Final Stand:** At 10% health, EDIS enters "Neutralization Mode," firing wildly. Use your remaining Attack Unit ammo to finish it quickly. A fully charged shot from the Charge Piercer to the head will end the fight instantly.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the different drawings/endings in the demo?**

A: The drawing Diana creates at the end is tied to your "Clear Time" and combat efficiency. To see all 8 variations, you must complete the demo multiple times, ranging from slow exploration runs to sub-10-minute speedruns.

**Q: Can I heal during the EDIS boss fight?**

A: Yes. There are crates around the arena containing refined minerals. However, using a healing item takes several seconds. It is best to heal during "Witch Time" or while EDIS is staggered from a missile reflection.

**Q: What does the "Multi-Hack" ability do?**

A: Multi-Hack is an unlockable skill that allows Diana to link multiple terminals or enemies simultaneously. In the context of the **プラグマタ 体験版 攻略**, this is used to speed up the lift sequence and clear groups of Walkers faster by sharing damage across the entire linked group.

**Q: Is the Charge Piercer a permanent weapon?**

A: In the demo, the Charge Piercer replaces your Shockwave Gun slot once picked up. It does not carry over if you die unless you have reached a checkpoint (Escape Hatch) after picking it up. In the full version of Pragmata, weapon loadouts are expected to be fully customizable.
