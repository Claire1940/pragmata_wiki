## Core Mechanics of Pragmata Combat

At its heart, the combat system is built around the concept of "Protective Synergy." The protagonist serves as the primary damage dealer and tank, while Diana provides essential utility that can shift the tide of battle. Players must manage their positioning carefully, as many enemies are designed to punish those who lose focus on their surroundings.

### The Explorer’s Arsenal
The protagonist utilizes a variety of high-tech weaponry designed for lunar conditions. These range from long-range precision rifles to heavy-hitting melee tools used to "beat things faster." The versatility of the suit allows for rapid switching between these modes, which is vital when transitioning from open-area skirmishes to tight corridor brawls.

| Weapon Type | Ideal Range | Primary Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Energy Rifle** | Long | Neutralizing targeting sensors and distant drones. |
| **Heavy Impact Maul** | Close | Breaking armor on "tough buggers" and staggering bosses. |
| **Luna Missiles** | Medium/Area | Dealing with clusters of airborne threats like bats. |
| **Shock Grenades** | Short | Disabling electronic shields and stunning Sector Guards. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some enemies are specifically designed to excel at long distances. If you find yourself outgunned, use scanning to find cover or close the gap quickly using suit thrusters.

## Diana’s Support and Hacking Systems

You cannot master **pragmata combat** without effectively utilizing Diana’s unique skill set. She is not merely an NPC to be protected; she is a powerful tactical asset capable of overriding the moon's ancient infrastructure. During intense encounters, Diana can perform "hacks" that disable enemy shields or unlock environmental traps.

### Hacking and Environmental Interaction
When the protagonist is pinned down, calling for a "Diana hack" can provide the breathing room needed to recover. Diana’s ability to interface with the local network allows her to:
*   Disable emergency lockdowns.
*   Open secure blast doors to create escape routes.
*   Reboot power terminals to restore area-of-effect defenses.

### The Overdrive Protocol
One of the most devastating elements in the **pragmata combat** toolkit is the **Overdrive Protocol**. When certain conditions are met—usually involving high energy levels and successful synchronization between the two characters—Diana can trigger a state that drastically enhances the protagonist's capabilities. This "Overdrive" state allows for faster movement, increased damage output, and access to "Luna-grade" heavy missiles that can shred even the toughest Sector Guards.

## Enemy Archetypes and Lunar Threats

The moon is inhabited by a variety of rogue AI and mutated biological entities. Understanding the behavior of these enemies is key to maintaining control during **pragmata combat** encounters.

### Robotic Sector Guards
Entities like "Edith" serve as the moon's security force. These unregistered guest deterrents utilize "Sector Guard Protocols" to identify and neutralize threats. They often come equipped with advanced targeting sensors that must be destroyed to reduce their accuracy.

| Enemy Name | Threat Level | Key Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| **Lunar Bats** | Low | Vulnerable to wide-spread fire; easy to stagger. |
| **Sector Guard (Edith)** | High | Targeting sensors; susceptible to Diana's Overdrive. |
| **Heavy Drones** | Medium | Rear exhaust ports; vulnerable during reload cycles. |
| **Maintenance Crawlers** | Low | Slow movement; easily kitted with long-range fire. |

> **💡 Tip:** When facing multiple airborne enemies, prioritize the ones with active targeting sensors. Once the red laser sight disappears, their threat level drops significantly.

## Advanced Strategies: Managing the Power Grid

Combat in Pragmata is often tied to the state of the local environment. Many encounters take place in facilities where the power has been severed or diverted. To fully restore operations and disable hostile lockdown protocols, players must often defend Diana while she reboots terminals.

1.  **Locate the Main Terminal:** Use the scanning function to highlight power lines and terminal locations through walls.
2.  **Establish a Perimeter:** Place traps or use Diana’s hacking to lock down secondary entry points.
3.  **Defend the Reboot:** As the energy levels climb (30%, 60%, etc.), hostile waves will intensify. This is the best time to use high-impact weapons.
4.  **Final Synchronization:** Once power reaches 100%, use the surge to trigger a finishing move on any remaining high-level threats.

Managing these objectives while under fire is what separates a novice from an expert in **pragmata combat**. The "rat in a maze" feeling can be overcome by consistently scanning the environment for elevators, drops, and hidden caches of "Luna-made" explosives.

## Tactical Positioning and Movement

Movement on the moon feels weighty yet precise. The protagonist's suit allows for short bursts of speed and verticality, which are essential for dodging missile barrages or closing the distance on "dirty bastards" who stay at range.

*   **Scanning for Advantage:** Never enter a new room without scanning. This reveals enemy positions, targeting sensors, and interactable objects.
*   **Targeting Sensors:** Many larger enemies rely on sensors to lock on. Taking these out early in the fight can render their most powerful attacks useless.
*   **Drop-downs and Elevators:** Use the verticality of the lunar bases to your advantage. Dropping down from a catwalk can lead to a devastating surprise attack, while elevators can provide a quick exit from a losing fight.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I trigger the Overdrive protocol in Pragmata combat?**

A: Overdrive is typically triggered by building up a synchronization meter through successful co-op actions, such as Diana hacking an enemy while the Explorer provides cover fire. Once the meter is full, a prompt will appear to initiate the protocol, granting massive buffs.

**Q: Can Diana be permanently damaged during fights?**

A: While Diana can be targeted, she generally acts as a support unit. If she takes too much pressure, she may be unable to perform hacks or protocols until the area is cleared or she is "rebooted" by the player. Protecting her is a core part of the tactical loop.

**Q: What is the best way to deal with the Sector Guard "Edith"?**

A: Edith is a high-tier threat that requires a mix of strategies. You should focus on destroying her targeting sensors first to disable her missile lock-on. Following this, use Diana's hacking to lower her defenses before finishing her off with an Overdrive-boosted attack.

**Q: Is there a way to avoid combat in Pragmata?**

A: While some encounters are scripted and mandatory, the scanning mechanic often reveals alternative routes or stealth opportunities. However, mastering **pragmata combat** is essential, as bosses and lockdown events will eventually force a confrontation.
