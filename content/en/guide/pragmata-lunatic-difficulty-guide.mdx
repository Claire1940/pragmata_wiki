## Shifting Your Mindset: Threat-Based Targeting

The most common pitfall for players entering Lunatic difficulty is "proximity bias"—the habit of attacking whatever enemy happens to be closest. In lower difficulties, this is often a viable strategy. However, in Lunatic mode, the enemies that aren't directly in your face are often the ones that will end your run.

You must transition to a **threat-based targeting** system. At the start of every encounter, take a split second to scan the room. Your primary targets should always be the units that apply consistent, unavoidable pressure from a distance. These enemies chip away at your health and, more importantly, stagger you, leaving you vulnerable to heavy hitters.

| Enemy Type | Priority Level | Reason for Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Drones & Turrets** | **Critical** | Constant chip damage and positioning interruption. |
| **Missile-Based Units** | **High** | High burst damage and large blast radii. |
| **Elite Melee Units** | **Medium** | Dangerous, but manageable if you maintain distance. |
| **Basic Swarmers** | **Low** | Primarily serve as distractions; clear them last or with AoE. |

> **💡 Tip:** Never let a turret or drone remain active while you focus on a boss. The cumulative damage from "minor" enemies is the leading cause of death in Lunatic runs.

## Choosing a Specialized Build Path

In Lunatic difficulty, being a "jack of all trades" is a recipe for disaster. Mixing different playstyles results in diluted damage output, making fights last longer and increasing the chances of a fatal mistake. You must commit to a specific build direction early in your playthrough and ensure every mod, weapon, and hacking upgrade supports that goal.

### Recommended Build Archetypes

1.  **Heat Buildup Build:** Focuses on sustained elemental damage. This is widely considered the most reliable path for Lunatic runs due to its consistent DPS and ability to melt high-armor targets.
2.  **Hacking Focus Build:** A control-oriented build that relies on turning the environment and enemies against each other. Excellent for players who prefer a tactical approach.
3.  **Burst Damage Build:** High-risk, high-reward. This build focuses on ending fights in seconds by stacking massive damage multipliers, though it often leaves you with fewer defensive options.

| **Core Mod** | Thermal Overload | Logic Virus | Last Resort |
| **Secondary Mod** | Rapid Venting | Board Compressor | Economize |
| **Playstyle** | Sustained Pressure | Room Control | Precision Striking |

## Advanced Combat: The Last Shot Mechanic

For elite enemies and bosses with massive health pools, traditional firing cycles often aren't enough. One of the most effective advanced techniques in this **Pragmata lunatic difficulty guide** involves abusing "last shot" mechanics. 

By equipping specific mods like **Last Resort** (which increases damage on the final round of a magazine) and **Economize** (which grants a chance to fire without consuming ammo), you can potentially stay on your final, high-damage bullet for multiple shots. Instead of reloading immediately, wait for the Economize proc to trigger. This allows for massive, repeated burst damage that can bypass the "tankiness" of Lunatic enemies.

## Proactive Overdrive Usage

A common mistake is treating the Overdrive meter as a "panic button" to be used only when health is low. In Lunatic difficulty, if you are in a panic situation, you are likely already dead. Overdrive should be used **proactively** at the start of an encounter to establish dominance.

By activating Overdrive early, you interrupt enemy spawn patterns and build immediate momentum. It also simplifies the hacking interface, allowing you to execute complex hacks in a fraction of the time. This proactive use prevents the "snowball effect" where too many enemies occupy the screen at once.

> **Warning:** Hesitation is your greatest enemy. In Lunatic mode, waiting even two seconds to decide on a target can result in being surrounded and overwhelmed.

## Hacking Efficiency and Room Control

Hacking is not just a side mechanic; it is a primary damage and survival tool. In the context of a **Pragmata lunatic difficulty guide**, your goal is to control the entire room rather than fighting 1v1 duels.

*   **Sticky Bombs:** These are essential for high-level hacking. They reduce the size of enemy hack boards, which is vital when facing bosses. The less time you spend in the hacking menu, the less time you are vulnerable to physical attacks.
*   **Crowd Control (CC):** Use tools like the **Stasis Gun** or **Hacking Mines** to freeze large groups. This creates "breathing room" to focus on high-priority targets.
*   **Multihack:** Upgrading your ability to hack multiple targets simultaneously is a non-negotiable requirement for the later stages of the game.

## Movement as Your Primary Defense

In Pragmata's harder modes, your health bar is an illusion. Most enemies can deplete your entire life pool in two hits—sometimes one. Therefore, you cannot rely on defensive stats or armor. Your only real defense is **movement**.

You must prioritize upgrading your **Thrusters** as early as possible. Constant repositioning, dodging, and maintaining distance from melee-focused enemies are the only ways to ensure survival. If you find yourself standing still for more than a second, you are likely about to take damage.

| Movement Action | Strategic Benefit |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Thruster Dash** | Avoids high-speed projectiles and resets positioning. |
| **Verticality** | Many ground-based enemies have limited vertical reach. |
| **Circle Strafing** | Keeps range-based enemies in view while making you a harder target. |
| **Distance Hacking** | Allows you to progress the fight while staying outside the "danger zone." |

## FAQ

**Q: Is it possible to beat Lunatic difficulty without upgrading thrusters?**

A: While theoretically possible, it is extremely difficult. Most enemies in Lunatic mode have attacks that cover large areas or track your movement. Without the increased speed and dodge window of upgraded thrusters, you will likely be overwhelmed by the sheer volume of incoming fire.

**Q: Which weapon is the best for a first-time Lunatic run?**

A: The **Plasma Repeater** is generally recommended for beginners. It offers a balance of range and fire rate, making it easier to apply the "Heat Buildup" strategy while maintaining a safe distance from aggressive enemies.

**Q: Do sticky bombs work on all bosses in Pragmata?**

A: Yes, sticky bombs are effective against almost all mechanical bosses. They are particularly useful for reducing the complexity of hack boards, which can become quite overwhelming during the final stages of the game.

**Q: How does this Pragmata lunatic difficulty guide recommend handling the final boss?**

A: The final boss requires a combination of everything learned: use Overdrive early to clear the initial wave of drones, rely on movement to dodge the beam attacks, and use the last-shot mechanic with a high-caliber rifle to chip away at its massive health pool during stun phases.
