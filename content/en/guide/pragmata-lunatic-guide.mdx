## Shift Your Mindset: Threat-Based Targeting

The most common pitfall in high-difficulty runs is focusing on the enemy closest to you. In Lunatic Plus, proximity-based targeting is a death sentence. Instead, you must adopt a threat-based targeting mindset. Before firing a single shot, scan the environment for "high-pressure" units that can chip away at your health from a distance.

Range-based enemies like drones, automated turrets, and missile units are your primary concerns. While you are busy dodging a melee-focused heavy unit, these smaller threats will interrupt your positioning and prevent your health from regenerating. By eliminating these nuisances first, you create a "safe" arena where you can focus on the more complex patterns of elite enemies.

| Priority Level | Enemy Types | Reason for Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Critical** | Drones, Turrets, Missile Units | Constant chip damage and positioning disruption. |
| **High** | Elite Melee Units | High damage potential; must be kited while clearing trash. |
| **Medium** | Standard Grunts | Low threat individually, but dangerous in large swarms. |
| **Low** | Shielded Heavies | High health pools; save for last once the room is controlled. |

## Build Specialization: Commitment is Key

One of the harshest lessons of Lunatic Plus is that a "jack-of-all-trades" build is a master of none. If you attempt to balance hacking, heat damage, and burst damage simultaneously, your overall DPS will fall behind the enemy's scaling. You must choose one of the three primary build paths early in your playthrough and funnel every upgrade, mod, and weapon choice into that specific direction.

### The Three Meta Builds

1.  **Heat Buildup Path:** Recommended for players who prefer sustained damage. This build focuses on maximizing the "Overheat" status on enemies, causing them to take massive damage over time.
2.  **Hacking Focus:** A control-oriented build that turns the environment and the enemies against each other. This is the safest path for Lunatic Plus as it prioritizes crowd control (CC).
3.  **Burst Damage Path:** High-risk, high-reward. This utilizes "Last Shot" mechanics and heavy weaponry to delete high-health targets before they can react.

> **💡 Tip:** Once you choose a path, do not deviate. Every equipment mod and hacking upgrade should synergize with your chosen playstyle to ensure maximum efficiency in late-game encounters.

## Mastering the "Last Shot" Mechanic

For those pursuing the Burst Damage path, the interaction between specific weapon mods is the secret to overcoming bosses with massive health bars. By combining the **Last Resort** and **Economize** mods, you can exploit the final bullet in your magazine for massive damage.

*   **Last Resort:** Significantly increases the damage dealt by the final shot in a magazine.
*   **Economize:** Provides a high percentage chance to fire a shot without consuming ammo.

Instead of reloading as soon as your magazine is low, you want to stay on that final round. If Economize triggers, you can repeatedly fire the "Last Resort" buffed shot, dealing astronomical damage that can end boss phases in seconds. This strategy is essential for reducing the duration of fights, which is critical in Lunatic Plus where the longer a fight lasts, the more likely you are to make a fatal error.

## Room Control and Hacking Efficiency

Survival in Lunatic Plus is not just about killing; it's about controlling the space. You must treat the entire room as a single entity rather than a collection of individual enemies. Tools like **Multi-Hack**, **Hacking Mines**, and the **Stasis Gun** are mandatory for managing large groups.

### The Power of Sticky Bombs
Sticky bombs are often overlooked, but they are the most practical tool for hacking efficiency. Their primary function in Lunatic Plus is to reduce the size of enemy "hack boards." A smaller board means you spend less time in the hacking interface, reducing your vulnerability to outside attacks.

| Tool | Primary Function | Lunatic Plus Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sticky Bomb** | Board Reduction | Completes hacks faster; reduces vulnerability windows. |
| **Stasis Gun** | Enemy Freeze | Stops aggressive elites in their tracks for easy targeting. |
| **Multi-Hack** | Group CC | Controls 3+ enemies simultaneously to reduce room pressure. |
| **Hacking Mines** | Passive Defense | Protects your flank while focusing on primary targets. |

## Movement: Your Only Real Defense

In Pragmata's hardest mode, your health bar is a lie. Most enemies can remove 50% to 100% of your health in one or two hits. Consequently, defensive stats are far less valuable than movement stats. Your primary defense is simply not being where the attack lands.

Prioritize upgrading your **Thrusters** as early as possible. Enhanced thrusters allow for longer dash distances and better aerial positioning. You should never stand still; even while hacking, you should be strafing or maintaining distance. If you find yourself surrounded, use your verticality to reset the fight. Standing still or getting caught in a long animation without situational awareness will result in an immediate death.

> **Warning:** Hesitation is the number one killer in Lunatic Plus. If you spend too long deciding which enemy to hack or where to move, the AI will overwhelm you. You must play fast, decisive, and aggressive.

## FAQ

**Q: Is it possible to beat Lunatic Plus without upgrading thrusters?**

A: While technically possible, it is extremely difficult. Most enemy attacks in this mode are designed to punish players with standard movement speed. Upgrading thrusters is the single best investment you can make for survivability.

**Q: Which weapon is best for a first-time Lunatic run?**

A: The Stasis Gun is highly recommended for beginners. Its ability to freeze enemies provides much-needed breathing room to execute hacks or line up "Last Shot" hits.

**Q: Does this pragmata lunatic guide apply to the standard Lunatic difficulty?**

A: Yes. While these tips are tailored for the extreme pressure of Lunatic Plus, the core principles of threat-based targeting and build specialization will make the standard Lunatic difficulty significantly easier.

**Q: How do I deal with the massive hack boards on bosses?**

A: Always use Sticky Bombs before attempting to hack a boss. This will shrink the board and allow you to finish the sequence before the boss can recover or launch a counter-attack.
