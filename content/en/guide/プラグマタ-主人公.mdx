## Meet the プラグマタ 主人公: Hugh and Diana

The narrative of Pragmata centers on the bond between two distinct individuals forced to cooperate in a hostile moon base environment. Unlike traditional solo-protagonist games, Pragmata requires you to manage both characters simultaneously or in quick succession to overcome challenges.

### Hugh Williams (The Protector)
Hugh is the primary "action" component of the duo. Clad in a high-tech EVA suit, he provides the muscle and firepower necessary to clear paths. His movements are weighted, emphasizing the "sense of gravity" that Capcom developers prioritized during the game's long development cycle. 

### Diana (The Key)
Diana, identified as D-I-0336-7, is an android girl with extraordinary capabilities. While she lacks Hugh's raw physical strength, she is essential for interacting with the lunar base's ancient systems. Her long, flowing hair is a testament to the RE Engine's advanced simulation technology, reacting realistically to low-gravity environments and combat maneuvers.

## Exploring the プラグマタ 主人公 Dynamics

The gameplay of Pragmata is built on the concept of "multitasking." Capcom’s vision was to create a "new sensation" where one player manages two distinct types of gameplay at once. This is why the **プラグマタ 主人公** are never truly separated for long.

### Combat and Puzzles in Parallel
In most encounters, Hugh handles the shooting via the controller's triggers, while Diana’s puzzle-solving actions are mapped to the face buttons. This requires the player to engage their brain in two different ways:
1.  **Tactical Shooting:** Using Hugh’s arsenal to suppress enemies.
2.  **Logic Processing:** Using Diana to unlock doors or disable enemy shields in real-time.

This synergy is what makes the **プラグマタ 主人公** feel like a cohesive unit rather than a main character with an AI follower. The "busy-ness" of the controls is a deliberate design choice intended to simulate the stress of a lunar survival mission.

## The Shelter: A Home for the プラグマタ 主人公

Between the intense action sequences, Hugh and Diana retreat to a "Shelter." This lunar base serves as your central hub. Here, you can:
*   **Upgrade Equipment:** Enhance Hugh’s Grip Gun and Diana’s processing power.
*   **Interact:** Give Diana presents found during exploration to unlock new dialogue and backstory.
*   **Manage Resources:** Use the **Diana amiibo** (on Nintendo Switch 2) to resupply items like weapons and healing kits.

The Shelter is where the emotional core of the game resides. It provides a necessary contrast to the cold, vacuum-sealed danger of the moon, allowing the player to see the **プラグマタ 主人公** in a more human light.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I play as Diana independently?**

A: While you control both **プラグマタ 主人公** simultaneously, the game is designed as a single-player experience where you manage their actions in tandem. Some specific puzzle segments allow you to move Diana freely, but she remains tethered to Hugh for the majority of the combat.

**Q: What are the benefits of the Diana amiibo?**

A: Scanning the Diana amiibo on the Nintendo Switch 2 version allows you to receive up to three types of supply items (weapons, recovery items, etc.) per sortie. It is a great way to gain an advantage in harder difficulty modes.

**Q: Is there a co-op mode for the two protagonists?**

A: No. Despite having two characters, Capcom decided to focus on a "single-player multitasking" experience. The directors stated that the fun comes from one person managing the "busy-ness" of both action and puzzles at the same time.

**Q: How does the "Neo Bushido" outfit for the プラグマタ 主人公 work?**

A: The "Neo Bushido" (Hugh) and "Neo Kunoichi" (Diana) outfits are early purchase bonuses. They change the character models to reflect a futuristic samurai and ninja aesthetic. These can be toggled in the Shelter's equipment menu at any time after they are redeemed.
