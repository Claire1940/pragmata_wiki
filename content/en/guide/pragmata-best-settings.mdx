## Understanding Ray Tracing and Path Tracing

Pragmata is a game defined by its setting—a giant space station filled with metallic surfaces, glass panels, and intricate lighting. To get the most out of these visuals, Capcom has included two distinct levels of ray-traced effects. Standard Ray Tracing handles global illumination and reflections, while the more intensive Path Tracing completely replaces the traditional rasterized lighting system.

However, more isn't always better. At lower Ray Tracing presets, indirect lighting can produce significant "noise" or a grainy texture on metallic floors. In certain areas, such as the Hideout, this can even look like raindrops falling indoors. If you aren't using a high-end card capable of Path Tracing, you may actually find that turning Ray Tracing off entirely provides a cleaner, more stable image.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** If you are using an NVIDIA card and choose to enable Path Tracing, always turn on **DLSS Ray Reconstruction**. This setting significantly cleans up visual noise and improves the accuracy of reflections, though it is only available when Path Tracing is active.

## Key Graphics Settings Explained

### Global Illumination
This is arguably the heaviest setting in the game. Moving from High to Medium Global Illumination can grant an immediate performance boost of approximately 11%. While High adds more atmospheric depth, Medium retains enough detail for the game to look modern without tanking your frame rate during intense combat.

### Hair Quality
Interestingly, high-fidelity hair physics are only applied to one main character, yet the performance cost remains high across the board. Setting this to **Medium** is the "sweet spot" for most desktop users. If you are playing on a handheld like the Steam Deck or ROG Ally, turning this to Low is one of the fastest ways to claw back frames.

### Texture and Mesh Quality
These settings are primarily limited by your GPU's Video RAM (VRAM). If you have 8GB of VRAM or more, you should keep Texture Quality and Mesh Quality at their maximum levels. Lowering these won't improve your FPS unless you are currently exceeding your VRAM limit, which causes stuttering.

### The Importance of Shadow Cache
The Shadow Cache is a vital toggle for the RE Engine. By allowing the game to store shadow data in your VRAM, it reduces the real-time processing load on your GPU.
> **⚠️ Warning:** Turning Shadow Cache **Off** can result in a 10% drop in average frame rates. Only disable this if you are severely limited by VRAM (less than 4GB).

## Post-Processing and Visual Flairs

Pragmata features several post-processing effects that are largely subjective. Because the game takes place in a sci-fi environment, effects like **Bloom** and **Lens Flare** fit the aesthetic perfectly.

1.  **Screen Space Reflections (SSR):** If you are struggling to hit 60 FPS, turning SSR off can provide a 10% performance boost. However, since the game features so many shiny floors, the world will look significantly "flatter."
2.  **Subsurface Scattering:** This affects how light penetrates skin. Since most characters you encounter are robotic, the impact is minimal. Keep this on for better cutscenes, or off to save a few frames.
3.  **Motion Blur:** This is a personal preference. While it can help hide frame rate fluctuations, many players find it distracting in fast-paced combat.

## Advanced Upscaling: DLSS vs. FSR

To truly achieve the **pragmata best settings**, you must utilize upscaling. The game supports NVIDIA DLSS and AMD FSR. 

*   **NVIDIA Users:** Use DLSS "Quality" for 1080p/1440p and "Performance" for 4K. If you have an RTX 40-series or 50-series card, **Frame Generation** is a must-use feature to double your perceived smoothness.
*   **AMD/Intel Users:** FSR provides excellent results, though it lacks the Ray Reconstruction feature found in the NVIDIA suite. If you are on an AMD card, avoid Path Tracing, as the performance hit is currently too high without specialized hardware acceleration.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pragmata support Path Tracing on AMD graphics cards?**

A: Currently, Path Tracing is optimized specifically for NVIDIA’s architecture and may be grayed out or perform poorly on AMD Radeon cards, even with FSR enabled. Standard Ray Tracing is the recommended maximum for AMD users.

**Q: Why do my floors look grainy or noisy in Pragmata?**

A: This is usually caused by using low-level Ray Tracing settings without enough "samples." To fix this, either upgrade to Path Tracing with Ray Reconstruction (NVIDIA only) or turn Ray Tracing off and rely on Screen Space Reflections for a cleaner look.

**Q: What are the best settings for the Steam Deck?**

A: For handhelds, set the resolution to 720p/800p, use FSR on "Balanced" mode, and turn Hair Quality and Global Illumination to Low. Ensure Shadow Cache is ON to maintain a stable 30-40 FPS.

**Q: How much VRAM do I need for the pragmata best settings at 4K?**

A: For 4K gaming at max textures, a GPU with at least 12GB of VRAM is recommended. If you have 8GB or less, you may experience stuttering at 4K unless you lower the Texture Quality to the "High (2GB/3GB)" setting.
