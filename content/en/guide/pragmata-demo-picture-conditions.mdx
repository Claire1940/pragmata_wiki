## Understanding Diana's Sketchbook Mechanic

In the Pragmata demo, the "Sketchbook" serves as a narrative and completionist hub. Diana, the mysterious young girl accompanying the protagonist, creates drawings based on your performance, the enemies you encounter, and specific environmental triggers. Unlike traditional achievements, these drawings are "unlocked" by meeting hidden gameplay criteria during specific combat sequences.

The most sought-after entry is Diana's second drawing, which is directly tied to a hidden boss encounter. To trigger this, you must adhere to strict combat rules during the mid-demo elevator sequence. Failing to meet these **pragmata demo picture conditions** results in the elevator arriving at its destination without the bonus encounter, effectively locking you out of the final sketchbook entries for that run.

## The Elevator Encounter: Step-by-Step Unlock Guide

The core of the hidden content lies in how you handle the three security bots that board the elevator. To unlock the hidden boss and fulfill the **pragmata demo picture conditions**, you must demonstrate "tactical variety." The game tracks which weapon delivers the final blow to each of the three bots.

### Weapon Variety Requirements

To trigger the secret, you cannot rely on a single weapon for all kills. You must rotate your arsenal. The rule only applies to the "finishing blow"—you are free to weaken the bots with any weapon of your choice, provided the killing shot is unique for each enemy.

| Bot Sequence | Recommended Weakening Tool | Required Finishing Weapon |

> **💡 Tip:** Be careful with the Shockwave Gun's splash damage. If it accidentally destroys a weakened bot you intended to finish with a Hack, you will fail the sequence and must restart the demo.

### Detailed Combat Strategy

1.  **The First Bot:** This is the simplest stage. Use your standard pistol fire to whittle down its health. Once its chassis begins to spark, fire one final aimed shot to the head unit to confirm the kill.
2.  **The Second Bot:** Switch things up. You can use the environment or light melee strikes to lower its health. Once it is near destruction, switch to the Shockwave Gun. A single blast at close range will satisfy the condition for the second unique kill.
3.  **The Third Bot:** This is where most players fail. You should use the Pistol to bring its health extremely low. Once the prompt for "Hacking" appears or the bot is in a stunned state, initiate the Hack. Successfully completing the mini-game to destroy the bot counts as the third unique weapon finish.

## Advanced Combat Tips for Completionists

The combat in Pragmata is deliberate and physics-based. To meet the **pragmata demo picture conditions** consistently, you need to master the stagger gauges of the enemy bots.

*   **Stagger Management:** Enemies have a hidden "posture" bar. Using the Shockwave Gun increases this bar rapidly. If you want to finish an enemy with a Hack, use the Shockwave Gun to stun them first, but be careful not to deplete their actual health bar.
*   **Ammo Conservation:** In the demo, resources are limited. Ensure you aren't wasting Shockwave charges on the first bot, as you absolutely need them for the second kill and for stunning the third.
*   **Diana's Assistance:** Diana will occasionally highlight environmental hazards. While these are useful for general play, avoid using them during the elevator sequence, as environmental kills do not count toward the "unique weapon" kill requirement.

> **Warning:** Using the same weapon for even two of the three kills will cause the hidden boss to fail to spawn. You must be 100% consistent with the weapon rotation.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use the Shockwave Gun to damage the first bot?**

A: Yes. You can use any weapon to lower an enemy's health. The only thing that matters for the **pragmata demo picture conditions** is which weapon delivers the final, killing blow.

**Q: I killed the bots correctly, but the hidden boss didn't appear. Why?**

A: This is usually caused by accidental damage. If a bot dies from a status effect (like fire or electricity) or an environmental explosion rather than your weapon's direct hit, it may not count as a "unique weapon kill." Try to be very precise with your final shots.

**Q: How many drawings are in the full Pragmata demo?**

A: There are currently three main drawings to collect. The most difficult one to obtain is the second drawing, which requires the specific weapon-rotation kills on the elevator.

**Q: Is there a reward for completing the sketchbook in the demo?**

A: While there is no in-game item reward, completing the sketchbook unlocks a special "Thank You" screen from the development team and provides additional lore snippets about Diana's origin.
