## Solar Power Plant Walkthrough

The Solar Power Plant is the first major zone where the **プラグマタ 攻略チャート** becomes essential. This area introduces complex environmental hazards, specifically laser grids and multi-layered locking mechanisms that require precise hacking.

### Navigating the Exclusion Zone
Upon arriving at the Solar Power Plant, your primary goal is to reach the Power Management Lobby. You will encounter several "Exclusion Zones" that are locked behind multiple security terminals. Use your scan ability frequently to highlight the wires connecting terminals to the doors.

1.  **Hacking the First Lock**: Move through the initial corridor and defeat the Walkers that ambush you from behind.
2.  **Laser Navigation**: In the lower levels, you must time your jumps to avoid moving laser beams. Touching these will deplete your shield instantly.
3.  **Weapon Acquisition**: Look for the **Shockwave Gun** in the room after the second lock. This weapon is vital for crowd control against swarms of small bots.
4.  **The Sector Guard**: At the top of the facility, you will face the Sector Guard. This boss utilizes heavy shield plating. Use your hacking nodes to disable its defense before unleashing your main arsenal.

| Key Item | Location | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Luna Filament** | Found in boxes throughout the plant. | Universal crafting and upgrade material. |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Lower level, near Lock #2. | Effective against multiple weak enemies. |
| **Cartridge Holder** | Power Management Lobby. | Increases the number of repair kits you can carry. |

## Large-scale Output Test Site

This sector is a haunting recreation of New York City. The **プラグマタ 攻略チャート** for this area focuses on activating Beacons to stabilize the simulation and clear "Red Zones" filled with hostile data-forms.

### Shopping Mall and Beacons
The Test Site is divided into several sub-districts. To progress, you must locate and hack six different Area Beacons.

*   **Beacon 1 & 2**: Located near the Shopping Mall entrance. You will need to find a **Red Card Key** inside one of the shops to access the mall's interior.
*   **The Giant Bot Encounter**: In the open street area, a massive mechanical behemoth will drop from the sky. Its weak point is the glowing filament core on its back. Use your thrusters to stay airborne and maneuver behind it.
*   **Filament Recycler**: This unique tool is found mid-way through the level. Use it to break down environmental obstacles made of "Dead Filament," opening shortcuts back to the main hub.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Mannequins in the mall area are not always static. Some will activate as "Mimic Walkers" when you turn your back on them. Use your scan to identify active threats.

## Terra Dome: The Botanical Lab

The Terra Dome is perhaps the most visually stunning and mechanically dense area in the game. It introduces **Cultivation Terminals**, which allow you to grow massive trees to create bridges and platforms.

### Cultivation and Weather Simulation
The **プラグマタ 攻略チャート** for the Terra Dome emphasizes resource management, as you need specific Cultivation Chips to progress through the foliage-heavy puzzles.

1.  **Central Lift**: Your first priority is restoring power to the central lift by rebooting the cultivation terminal near the entrance.
2.  **Weather Simulation Lab**: This sub-area features localized environmental effects like lightning and heavy rain. Use the **Pulse Carbine** (found in the underground section) to deal high damage to the airborne drones patrolling this lab.
3.  **Garden Keeper Boss**: The final challenge of the Terra Dome. The Garden Keeper moves through the trees and uses long-range vine attacks. You must burn away its protective foliage using the **Photon Laser** to expose its core.

| Weapon/Module | Location | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Pulse Carbine** | Terra Dome Underground. | High-velocity energy rounds. |
| **Photon Laser** | Geoscience Lab (Right Cave). | Continuous beam that melts armor. |
| **Just Shot Module** | Central Hall Upper Level. | Increases damage for perfectly timed shots. |

## Essential Gameplay Tips for Success

To master the **プラグマタ 攻略チャート**, you need to understand the underlying mechanics that the game doesn't always explain in detail.

### 1. The Power of the Scan
Your scan is your most valuable tool. Not only does it highlight items and enemies, but it also reveals the "health" of environmental structures. If a wall glows slightly during a scan, it can likely be destroyed or hacked.

### 2. Thruster Management
Movement in Pragmata is momentum-based. Using a dash immediately followed by a jump will give you a significant boost in height. This is often required to find hidden "Mini-Cabins" tucked away in the rafters of the Solar Power Plant and Test Site.

### 3. Escape Hatches
Always prioritize finding and activating Escape Hatches. These act as fast-travel points and respawn anchors. If you find yourself low on resources, use an Escape Hatch to return to the Shelter, upgrade your gear, and then teleport back to the exact spot you left.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I increase my maximum health in Pragmata?**

A: You must collect **Reinforcement Components** hidden throughout the stages. These can be traded at the Shelter's upgrade terminal to increase your suit's durability and HP.

**Q: What is the best way to use the プラグマタ 攻略チャート for boss fights?**

A: Focus on the "Boss Strategy" sections for each area. Most bosses in Pragmata have a "Shield Phase" that requires hacking or specific elemental weapons (like the Shockwave Gun) to break before you can deal actual damage.

**Q: Can I revisit old areas to collect missed items?**

A: Yes. Once you have unlocked the Escape Hatches in a sector, you can fast-travel back to them from the Shelter at any time to hunt for missed Mini-Cabins or Earth Memories.

**Q: What are Earth Memories used for?**

A: Earth Memories (like the Globe or Basketball) are collectible items that provide lore about the world before the lunar migration. Collecting them also unlocks new cosmetic options for Diana in the Shelter.
