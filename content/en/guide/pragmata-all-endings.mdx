## The Three Primary Narrative Branches

The narrative structure of Pragmata is governed by the "Synchronization Rate" between the protagonist, Hugh, and the mysterious young girl, Diana. Unlike traditional RPGs where a single dialogue choice at the end determines the outcome, Pragmata tracks your progress throughout the entire campaign. 

There are three main story endings that players can trigger based on their performance and thoroughness:

| Ending Name | Primary Requirement | Narrative Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Default Ending** | Finish the game with standard exploration (under 100% synergy). | A bittersweet conclusion with several unresolved character arcs. |
| **Abandonment Ending** | Extremely low synergy and multiple failed escort sequences. | A dark finale where the bond between Hugh and Diana is severed. |
| **True Ending** | 100% Diana Synchronization and all 20 Memory Fragments. | The complete resolution of the lunar colony mystery and Earth's fate. |

> **💡 Tip:** To avoid the Abandonment Ending, prioritize Diana's safety during combat encounters and ensure you use her hacking abilities frequently to maintain a high synergy score.

## How to Unlock the True Ending

Securing the True Ending is the ultimate goal for most players. This path requires a 100% Synchronization Rate with Diana before you step into the Orbital Elevator in Sector 9. If you enter this final zone without meeting the criteria, you will be mathematically locked into the Default or Abandonment routes.

### 1. Collect All 20 Missing Memory Fragments
These glowing holographic data drives are scattered across the lunar surface. Many are hidden behind verticality puzzles that require late-game upgrades like the **Kinetic Grapple**.
*   **Backtracking is Mandatory:** You will often see a fragment in Sector 2 that is impossible to reach until you acquire the thruster upgrades in Chapter 5.
*   **Lore Significance:** Beyond the ending, these fragments provide the primary context for the "Old Earth" lore database.

### 2. Complete Side Missions
Three specific side missions are required for the True Ending. These missions focus on repairing Diana’s internal modules and exploring her past. If you rush the main story, these missions will be marked as "Failed" once you pass a Point of No Return.

### 3. Diana’s Hacking Module
You must fully upgrade Diana’s hacking module at a workbench before reaching Chapter 6. This allows her to access the hidden armory codes and encrypted logs necessary for 100% completion.

## Strategy for the "Technical Specialist" Picture

One of the rarest gallery items is Picture No. 2. To trigger the necessary encounter, you must reach the elevator leading to the final boss. When the enemies appear, do not simply blast through them. Use the following sequence to ensure the high-level orange robot spawns:
1.  Use your standard pistol to take down the flying drone.
2.  Trigger Diana’s combat ability to eliminate the first ground robot.
3.  Switch to the Green Gun (Static/Stun Gun) to finish off the second ground robot.
If done correctly, the elevator will stop, and two elite red-chassis robots will spawn. Defeating them grants the unique screenshot for your collection.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the True Ending on my first playthrough?**

A: Yes, it is entirely possible to see the True Ending on your first run of Pragmata, but you must be extremely thorough. Ensure you do not pass the Sector 9 Point of No Return until you have found all 20 Memory Fragments and achieved 100% Synergy with Diana.

**Q: What happens if I fail an escort sequence?**

A: Failing to protect Diana or neglecting her during exploration lowers your Synchronization Rate. If this falls too low, you will be forced into the Abandonment Ending, which is considered the "Bad Ending" of the game.

**Q: Is there a New Game Plus mode to help find missing items?**

A: Yes, Pragmata features a New Game Plus mode that allows you to carry over your upgrades and spacesuits. This is the recommended way to collect all **pragmata all endings** and gallery pictures if you missed the requirements during your initial journey.

**Q: How do I reach the memory fragment in the Sector 2 Commercial Hub?**

A: This is a common sticking point for players. You cannot reach this fragment during your first visit to Sector 2. You must wait until Chapter 5 to acquire the Kinetic Grapple, then fast-travel back to the Commercial Hub to navigate the zero-gravity puzzle.
