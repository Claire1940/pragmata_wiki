In the hauntingly beautiful yet hostile world of Capcom’s latest sci-fi epic, survival is a matter of preparation and resourcefulness. The **pragmata shelter** serves as the central hub for players, acting as a sanctuary where the protagonist and the mysterious girl, Diana, can regroup between intense missions. Navigating the complexities of the **pragmata shelter** is vital for progression, as it houses the primary systems for equipment enhancement and character interaction. 

Whether you are looking to bolster your offensive capabilities or find a moment of peace amidst the lunar chaos, the shelter provides the tools necessary to succeed. This guide will walk you through the essential facilities found within the base, how to manage limited resources like backup power, and the ways you can personalize the space to make it a true home for your companions in 2026.

## The Core Functions of the Shelter

The shelter is more than just a menu screen; it is a physical space within the game world that evolves as you progress through the narrative. Upon entering, players are typically greeted by a diagnostic overview of their current status. The primary loop involves returning to the shelter after scavenging for materials on the lunar surface or within the simulated Earth environments.

The most critical function of the hub is the integration of "Backup Power." Without a stable power supply, many of the shelter's advanced features, such as the high-tier crafting benches and holographic displays, remain offline. Players must prioritize restoring and maintaining these power levels to ensure they can stay ahead of the escalating threats outside.

| Facility | Primary Function | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Power Core** | Manages backup power and lighting | Regular maintenance/Fuel |
| **Upgrade Bench** | Enhances weapons and armor stats | Scavenged Materials |
| **Living Quarters** | Character interactions and rest | None |
| **Holographic Map** | Mission selection and world lore | Active Power |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your backup power levels before heading out on a long mission. Running out of power while you are away can disable certain passive recovery systems in the shelter.

## Upgrading Gear and Combat Readiness

As seen in early tactical overviews, the shelter is the only place where you can significantly "hit harder and beat things faster." The upgrade system is modular, allowing players to focus on specific playstyles, whether that involves heavy ordnance or high-mobility tactics.

When you access the upgrade terminal, you can spend resources gathered during exploration to improve your suit's integrity or the damage output of your primary weapons. This is also where you manage your companion's involvement. Characters like Kenny and Diana provide support during combat, and their abilities can be fine-tuned within the shelter's confines.

### Weapon Enhancement Categories

| **Energy Output** | Boosts elemental/laser damage | Power Cells |

## Customization and "A Gift for Diana"

A unique aspect of the **pragmata shelter** is the emotional connection built through customization. Diana, the young girl who accompanies you, has a deep curiosity about "Real Earth." Players can collect various items during their journey—ranging from old-world artifacts to simple decorations—to brighten up the sterile lunar base.

Decorating the shelter isn't just for aesthetics; it often triggers unique dialogue sequences and deepens the bond between the characters. Diana might comment on specific items, such as maps or globes, leading to discussions about locations like Venezuela, Malaysia, or Denmark. These interactions provide much-needed context to the game's mysterious setting and the history of the characters.

### Decoration Types and Effects

1.  **Earth Relics:** Items like globes or books that trigger lore-based conversations.
2.  **Comfort Items:** Bedding or furniture that may provide small buffs to recovery speed.
3.  **Holographic Skins:** Changes the visual appearance of the shelter's walls and floors.
4.  **Flora:** Rare plant life that can be grown to provide organic resources.

> **Warning:** Some decorations require a constant draw of power. Be careful not to over-decorate if your backup power reserves are low, as this might prioritize aesthetics over functional machinery.

## Advanced Tactics: Training and Simulation

The shelter also serves as a training ground. Before heading into a high-stakes encounter, players can use the simulation deck to test out new weapon configurations. This is particularly useful for mastering the timing of missile deflections and coordinated attacks with Diana.

The "Missile Spotted" drills, for instance, allow you to practice the "Right back at you" counter-mechanic. Mastering these skills in the safety of the shelter ensures that you won't be caught off guard when facing the "tough as hell" enemies that roam the lunar wastes.

## Lore and the "Lines" of the World

One of the more cryptic elements of the **pragmata shelter** experience involves Diana's observations of the environment. She often notices "lines" that don't exist on the real Earth. This suggests that the shelter, and perhaps the entire lunar colony, is part of a massive simulation or a digitally reconstructed reality.

By exploring the shelter and interacting with its systems, players can find clues about the origin of these digital artifacts. Understanding the nature of these "lines" is likely the key to uncovering why the characters are on the moon and what happened to the Earth they remember. ## Managing Your Routine

To make the most of your time in the hub, it is recommended to follow a specific routine every time you return from a mission:

*   **Step 1:** Deposit all scavenged raw materials into the central storage.
*   **Step 2:** Check the Power Core and replenish backup power if necessary.
*   **Step 3:** Interact with Diana to check for any "Gift" prompts or new dialogue.
*   **Step 4:** Navigate to the Upgrade Bench to spend points on suit and weapon improvements.
*   **Step 5:** Set your next destination on the Holographic Map and verify your equipment loadout.

By following this structure, you ensure that you are always operating at peak efficiency. The shelter is your greatest tool; treat it with the same care you give your weapons.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change the location of the pragmata shelter?**

A: No, the shelter is a fixed location that acts as your central hub throughout the game. However, you can unlock fast-travel points that allow you to return to it quickly from various sectors of the moon.

**Q: How do I get more decorations for Diana?**

A: Decorations are typically found as "Earth Relics" hidden in secret areas during main missions. Some can also be purchased from specialized vendors using rare currency found in the late-game areas.

**Q: Does upgrading the shelter provide combat bonuses?**

A: Directly, the shelter provides the facilities to upgrade your gear. Indirectly, keeping the power levels high and Diana happy can unlock support abilities and passive buffs that make combat significantly easier.

**Q: What happens if the backup power runs out completely?**

A: If power hits zero, the shelter will enter a "Low Light" mode. You will lose access to the upgrade bench and map functions until you provide a new power source or complete a manual restart of the core.
