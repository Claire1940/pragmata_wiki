## The Foundation: Luna Filament and the Shelter

Before diving into specific weapon stats, every player must understand the primary currency of progression: Luna Filament. Refined from a unique mineral known as "Lunum," this material is the lifeblood of your technology. It is capable of absorbing and copying information, making it the perfect substrate for upgrading your suit, weapons, and hacking tools.

All significant enhancements take place within the **Shelter**, your safe hub between missions. Here, you can access the upgrade terminal to spend your collected resources.

### Upgrade Resource Overview

| Resource Name | Primary Source | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Luna Filament** | Lunum deposits / Enemy drops | Core currency for all mechanical upgrades |
| **Repair Cartridges** | Exploration / Crafting | Restores Hugh's suit integrity in the field |
| **AI Cores** | Elite AI enemies | Unlocks high-tier hacking abilities for Diana |
| **Thruster Fuel** | Supply crates | Increases maximum gauge for dodging and hovering |

> **💡 Tip:** Always use Diana’s scan ability (C key) when entering a new room. She can highlight hidden Lunum deposits that are easy to miss during high-speed combat encounters.

## Upgrading Hugh: Combat and Survival

Hugh Williams is your primary source of damage and defense. His upgrades focus on firepower, suit durability, and mobility. Since Hugh is often the target of physical attacks, prioritizing his defensive layers early in the game is a recommended strategy.

### Weapon Systems
Hugh starts with a standard-issue firearm, but you will quickly unlock the **Shockwave Gun**. This weapon provides frontal suppression and is excellent for staggering enemies. Upgrading this weapon increases its effective range and stagger potential, which is crucial when dealing with shielded AI units.

Your thruster gauge dictates how often you can dodge, jump, and hover. In the later stages of the lunar facility, verticality becomes a major factor in boss fights.

## Upgrading Diana: Hacking and Tactical Support

Diana is not just a companion; she is a tactical necessity. While Hugh handles the "loud" part of combat, Diana manages the "digital" battlefield. Her upgrades revolve around the hacking matrix, which allows you to disable enemy shields and place them in a vulnerable state.

### The Hacking Matrix
When you engage an enemy's EXE node, you enter a mini-game. Upgrades for Diana can make this process significantly faster and more rewarding.

1.  **Hacking Speed:** Reduces the time required to connect nodes.
2.  **Node Buffer:** Allows you to pass through more yellow nodes without resetting the hack.
3.  **Passive Buffs:** Increases the damage Hugh deals to "Open State" enemies.

### Tactical Scanning
Diana’s scan ability is your primary tool for navigation and resource gathering. By upgrading her sensor array, you can detect enemies through walls and identify weak points before the engagement even begins.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when navigating the hacking matrix. Passing through too many nodes without a "Buffer Upgrade" can cause a system feedback loop, temporarily disabling Diana's abilities.

## Advanced Tactics: Yellow Node Mastery

During the hacking mini-game, you will encounter yellow nodes. These are optional but highly beneficial. Passing through multiple yellow nodes of the same type increases the effectiveness of the resulting buff.

*   **Damage Nodes:** Temporarily increase Hugh’s gunfire damage against the current target.
*   **Stagger Nodes:** Makes the enemy significantly easier to knock down once the hack is complete.
*   **Resource Nodes:** Increases the amount of Luna Filament dropped by the enemy upon death.

Focusing your upgrades on "Node Retention" allows you to chain these buffs together, which is the key to defeating high-health bosses in the late-game lunar research facility.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to farm Luna Filament?**

A: The solar power plant’s maintenance tunnels are the most efficient area for early-game farming. Ensure you have upgraded Diana's scan range to find hidden deposits behind breakable crates.

**Q: Can I complete the game without upgrading Diana?**

A: While technically possible, it is not recommended. Many enemies possess "Hard Shields" that can only be disabled through the hacking matrix. This **pragmata upgrade guide** strongly suggests keeping Diana's hacking speed on par with Hugh's weapon damage.

**Q: Does the "Shockwave Gun" have limited ammo?**

A: The Shockwave Gun operates on a recharge system rather than traditional ammo, though its usage consumes a portion of your energy gauge. Upgrading the "Energy Efficiency" stat in the Shelter will allow for more frequent blasts.

**Q: What happens if Hugh’s suit integrity reaches zero?**

A: If your suit integrity fails, you will enter a "Critical State." You must use a Repair Cartridge immediately or find a safe spot to initiate a manual repair, which takes several seconds and leaves you vulnerable.
