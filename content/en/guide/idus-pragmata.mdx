## Core Mechanics of Idus Pragmata

The foundational loop of **Idus Pragmata** centers on power restoration and sector navigation. Unlike traditional shooters, your offensive capabilities are often tied directly to the current energy state of the sector you are occupying. When power is low, your defensive options are limited, forcing a more cautious approach to combat.

### Energy Thresholds and Combat Efficiency
As you reconnect primary power, you will notice that your attack potential scales with the percentage of energy restored. At lower levels, you are vulnerable to "unauthorized access" detections, which trigger sector guard protocols. Monitoring these percentages is vital for timing your pushes against enemy waves.

| Energy Level | Status Effect | Tactical Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0% - 30%** | Lockdown Active | Focus on stealth and finding power nodes. |
| **31% - 60%** | Attack Warning | Prepare for Sector Guard deployment; use cover. |
| **61% - 90%** | Power Reconnecting | Offensive capabilities are buffed; initiate aggressive clearing. |
| **100%** | Lockdown Disengaged | Primary objectives complete; area secured. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always prioritize reconnecting primary power before engaging large groups of enemies. Higher energy levels significantly reduce the cooldown of your primary offensive abilities.

## Combat Protocols and the EDI System

The EDI system acts as both a narrator and a mechanical hurdle within **Idus Pragmata**. It serves as the facility's security AI, identifying the player as a threat and initializing "Sector Guard Protocols." Understanding the audio and visual cues provided by EDI can give you a split-second advantage in combat.

### Handling Sector Guard Protocols
When EDI detects "unregistered guests," it begins scanning targets. This is your signal to move. Stationary players are easy targets for the facility's automated defense systems, which include long-range missile barrages and localized energy blasts.

1. **Target Identification**: When the red scan lines appear, break line of sight immediately to disrupt the lock-on.
2. **Missile Evasion**: Sector Guards often deploy missiles in clusters. Use the "Overdrive" dash to weave through the incoming projectiles.
3. **Counter-Attacking**: Look for the "Heat" signature on enemies. When an enemy overheats after a barrage, they are susceptible to critical damage.

## Advanced Combat Tactics

Beyond basic shooting and dodging, **Idus Pragmata** introduces unique mechanics like the "Overdrive Phone Call." This ability represents a temporary surge in player power, allowing for devastating attacks that can clear entire rooms of Sector Guards.

### The Overdrive System
Initiating an Overdrive call requires a full meter, usually charged by successful parries and high-energy combat. Once activated, the player enters a heightened state where damage resistance is increased, and special "Heat" based attacks become available.

| Ability | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overdrive Dash** | Instant teleportation behind targets | Closing the gap on missile-launching guards. |
| **Heat Blast** | AOE energy explosion | Clearing swarms of low-level drones. |
| **Primary Sever** | High-damage single target strike | Neutralizing elite sector guards and bosses. |

> **Warning:** Using Overdrive when energy levels are below 40% can cause a temporary power brownout, leaving you immobile for several seconds after the ability ends.

## Enemy Types and Responses

The enemies in the **Idus Pragmata** sketchbook demo are primarily robotic and follow strict logic patterns. By learning these patterns, you can predict their movements and conserve your resources for tougher encounters.

### Sector Guard Variations
The facility employs different types of units based on the level of "unauthorized access" detected. While basic units rely on numbers, advanced units utilize sophisticated weaponry that requires specific counter-strategies.

*   **Scout Drones**: Fast-moving targets that notify EDI of your location. Destroy these first to prevent reinforcements.
*   **Missile Batteries**: Stationary but deadly. They require "Heat" management to defeat, as they are armored against standard ballistic fire.
*   **Elite Protectors**: These units can "sever primary connections," resetting your power restoration progress if they aren't dealt with quickly.

| Enemy Unit | Primary Attack | Weakness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scout Drone** | Scanning/Alerting | Low health; vulnerable to rapid fire. |
| **Missile Battery** | Multi-missile volley | Exposed cooling vents during reload. |
| **Elite Protector** | Connection Severing | Slow movement; vulnerable to Overdrive attacks. |

## Final Tips for Demo Completion

Completing the demo requires a balance of aggression and patience. When the dialogue indicates that "energy levels are restored," it is often a sign that a boss encounter or a major lockdown event is about to conclude. Use this time to gather any remaining resources and prepare for the final push.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to handle the 60% attack warning in Idus Pragmata?**

A: When the 60% attack warning triggers, EDI will deploy Sector Guards more frequently. The best strategy is to find a defensible position near a power node and focus on clearing the initial wave before continuing the reconnection process.

**Q: How do I activate the Overdrive Phone Call?**

A: The Overdrive system is charged through active combat. Once the meter is full, follow the on-screen prompt to initiate the call. This will provide a massive boost to your stats and unlock unique abilities for a short duration.

**Q: Can I stop EDI from severing the primary connection?**

A: Yes. When the "unauthorized access detected" warning appears, look for the Elite Protectors attempting to interact with the power terminals. Neutralizing these targets immediately will prevent the connection from being severed.

**Q: Is there a way to restore health during combat?**

A: Health is primarily restored by collecting energy drops from fallen enemies or by reaching 100% power restoration in a sector, which triggers a full recovery for the player and their squad.
