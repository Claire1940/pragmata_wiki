## Key Gameplay Mechanics in the Demo

The demo introduces several layers of gameplay that differentiate Pragmata from standard third-person shooters. You aren't just firing weapons; you are managing resources and navigating complex hacking matrices.

### Hacking and the EXE Node
To make enemies vulnerable, you must interact with the hacking matrix. This involves moving a cursor to a green EXE node while holding specific inputs. Success places enemies in an "open state," allowing your gunfire to deal actual damage.

### Resource Management: Lunum and filaments
While exploring the lunar cradle, you will encounter a mineral known as **Lunum**. This is refined into **Luna filament**, a resource that absorbs and copies information. In the full game, these are used for shelter upgrades, but in the demo, they serve as the primary collectible to encourage exploration.

### Combat and Utility
Players have access to a standard firearm and a **Shockwave Gun**. The latter is a frontal suppression tool designed for close-range blasts that stagger enemies. You also have a thruster gauge that governs your ability to dodge, jump, and hover.

| Mechanic | Input (Default) | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Scan Environment** | **C Key** | Highlights objectives and Diana's location. |
| **Repair Suit** | **Hold Q** | Restores suit integrity using cartridges. |
| **Hacking Matrix** | **Hold Mouse 1** | Connects to EXE nodes to disable shields. |
| **Thruster Hover** | **Hold Space** | Allows for vertical positioning during combat. |

## Graphical Fidelity and Ray Tracing

Capcom has pushed the RE Engine to its limits in this trial. The demo defaults to a "High" preset on most modern hardware, often enabling ray tracing for both indirect lighting and reflections. When examining the reflections in the glass windows of the solar plant, you can see a simplified version of Hugh and the environment.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are struggling with performance, check the "Image Quality" setting. It defaults to 100% (native resolution). Lowering this to 90% or enabling DLSS/FSR can provide a significant FPS boost without a major loss in visual clarity.

One of the standout features of the 2026 demo is the inclusion of **FSR 4.0 (Machine Learning based upscaling)**. This allows AMD users to achieve performance parity with Nvidia’s DLSS Balanced mode, often pushing frame rates well over 100 FPS on mid-range cards like the RX 960 XT.

## Navigating the Solar Power Plant

The bulk of the demo takes place within the solar power plant. You must navigate through three distinct locking mechanisms to reach the top floor. This section acts as the primary tutorial for Diana’s abilities. As an android, Diana can interact with objects Hugh cannot, such as lowering heavy platforms or identifying hacking nodes with special modifiers.

### Yellow Hacking Nodes
During the hacking sequences, you may notice yellow nodes. Passing through these provides temporary buffs:
1. **Damage Boost:** Increases the damage dealt to the target once the hack is complete.
2. **Stacking Effect:** Passing through multiple nodes of the same type increases the effectiveness of the buff.
3. **Diminishing Returns:** Repeated use of the same node on the same enemy will eventually reduce its effectiveness.

## FAQ

**Q: How long is the Pragmata demo on a first playthrough?**

A: For most players, a first playthrough will take approximately **25 to 30 minutes**. This includes watching the introductory cutscenes and completing the hacking tutorials.

**Q: Does progress from the demo carry over to the full game?**

A: Currently, Capcom has not confirmed progress carry-over. The demo is intended as a standalone technical showcase and tutorial rather than a prologue that saves data for the final release.

**Q: Can I play the Pragmata demo offline?**

A: Yes, once the demo is downloaded via Steam, you can play it in offline mode. However, you will need an internet connection initially to handle the shader compilation and initial launch.

**Q: What should I do if the demo is stuttering on my PC?**

A: Ensure you allow the shader compilation step to complete at the main menu. If stutters persist, try turning off Ray Tracing in the main menu (requires a restart) or enabling DLSS/FSR 4.0 upscaling to reduce the load on your VRAM.
