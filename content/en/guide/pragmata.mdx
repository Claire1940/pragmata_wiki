## The World of Luna: Setting and Story

The narrative of **Pragmata** unfolds on a seemingly abandoned lunar research station managed by the Deli Corporation. This facility was once the pinnacle of human achievement, dedicated to the study of Lunum ore and the creation of a revolutionary material known as Lunafilament. This substance allowed Deli to duplicate objects with near-perfect accuracy, provided they had the necessary data. However, a catastrophic event known as a "moonquake" disrupted communications and sent the facility into a state of emergency.

Players take on the role of Hugh Matthews, a member of an investigation team sent to uncover the cause of the silence. After being separated from his squad during the lunar quake, Hugh encounters Diana, a highly advanced android created from Lunafilament. Together, they must navigate the hostile environment and evade the IDU, the station's central AI system that has turned lethal toward any perceived intruders.

## Combat Mechanics: A Hybrid Approach

Combat in the world of **Pragmata** is a seamless blend of third-person shooting and real-time puzzle solving. Hugh Matthews is not a super-soldier; he relies on his specialized suit's thrusters for mobility and a modular weapon system to handle the IDU's robotic enforcers. However, raw firepower is rarely enough. Most enemies are encased in heavy armor that negates standard projectile damage.

This is where Diana becomes indispensable. During encounters, players must protect Diana while she hacks into enemy systems. Successfully completing these combat puzzles exposes the "core" or weak points of the machines, allowing Hugh to deliver finishing blows. This synergy is the heart of the gameplay loop, requiring players to balance offensive pressure with defensive positioning.

### Weapon Units and Equipment

Hugh has access to four distinct weapon units, each serving a specific tactical role. Managing these units is critical because, unlike the primary weapon, advanced units are prone to breaking if their durability is depleted or ammo runs dry.

| Weapon Unit | Function | Durability/Ammo Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Unit** | Standard firearm for basic engagement | **Refills ammo automatically** over time; never breaks |
| **Tactical Unit** | Used for battlefield control and crowd management | High utility but limited usage before breaking |
| **Defense Unit** | Deploys shields and protective measures | Essential for protecting Diana during hack sequences |
| **Attack Unit** | Heavy ordinance for massive damage | Deals the highest damage but has the rarest ammo |

> **💡 Tip:** Use your suit's thrusters to maintain distance from armored enemies. Do not waste Attack Unit ammo on shielded foes; wait for Diana to complete a hack to expose their vulnerabilities first.

## Character Progression and Lunafilament

One of the most distinct departures from traditional RPG tropes in **Pragmata** is the lack of a standard leveling system. You will not earn "Experience Points" (XP) to increase your stats. Instead, progression is tied directly to the world’s resources, specifically Lunafilament.

By exploring the various sectors of the Deli facility, you will find Lunafilament caches and Lunum ore. These materials are used at the Shelter to fabricate new gear, upgrade suit thrusters, and enhance Diana’s hacking nodes. This system encourages thorough exploration and rewards players who engage with the environmental puzzles scattered throughout the station.

1.  **Scavenge for Materials:** Look for blue-glowing Lunafilament strands in hidden vents and storage lockers.
2.  **Analyze Data:** Collect "Earth Memories" to unlock blueprints for cosmetic items and Shelter decorations.
3.  **Upgrade at the Shelter:** Return to your hub to apply materials to your suit and Diana’s internal systems.
4.  **Enhance Hacking Nodes:** Improving Diana’s nodes allows her to hack faster and take control of more complex machinery, such as homing missiles.

## The Shelter: Your Lunar Safe Haven

The Shelter serves as the central hub for the player. It is a pressurized, safe environment where Hugh and Diana can rest between missions. You can return to the Shelter at any time by locating escape hatches found in most major sectors of the facility.

Within the Shelter, players can engage in several non-combat activities:
*   **Healing and Maintenance:** Restore Hugh’s suit integrity and Diana’s energy levels.
*   **Customization:** While you cannot change the physical features of the characters, you can swap out cosmetic outfits. Pre-order bonuses like the Neo Bushido and Kunoichi outfits can be equipped here.
*   **Earth Memories:** One of the primary side activities involves hunting for collectibles that represent life on Earth. These "toys" can be used to decorate the Shelter, triggering unique interactions between Hugh and Diana that flesh out their relationship.

## Development and Technical Innovation

The journey to the 2026 release of **Pragmata** was marked by several delays, which Director Cho Yong Hi attributed to the complexity of the hybrid combat system. The team spent years perfecting the balance between shooting and puzzle-solving to ensure neither felt like a chore. 

Technically, the game utilizes an upgraded version of the RE Engine. A notable highlight is the "strand hair system," which was specifically developed to handle Diana's long, flowing hair with realistic physics. This technology was so successful that it was later implemented for characters in other Capcom titles, such as [Resident Evil Requiem](https://www.capcom.com). Furthermore, the world-building was supervised by Shoji Kawamori, the legendary creator of the *Macross* series, ensuring a deep and cohesive sci-fi aesthetic.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Pragmata an open-world game?**

A: While the game features vast sectors and encourages exploration, it is structured as a series of interconnected zones rather than a seamless open world. You can return to previously visited areas via the Shelter's escape hatches.

**Q: Can I play as Diana?**

A: Players primarily control Hugh Matthews. However, Diana is a constant companion whose hacking abilities are manually triggered and directed by the player during combat and environmental puzzles.

**Q: What is the significance of Lunafilament in Pragmata?**

A: Lunafilament is the core resource of the game. It is used for narrative purposes (explaining how things are duplicated on the moon) and gameplay purposes (as the primary currency for upgrades and gear fabrication).

**Q: Will there be a multiplayer mode?**

A: As of the 2026 launch, the game is a dedicated single-player experience focusing on the narrative journey of Hugh and Diana.
