## The Final Confrontation: Defeating Eight

Before you can witness any of the **pragmata endings**, you must overcome the primary antagonist: Eight. Claiming to be the "light of the world," Eight utilizes "Dead Filament" spikes and reality-warping abilities to prevent Hue and Diana from reaching the transport tower. This fight is a multi-phase test of your ability to manage Hue’s combat prowess alongside Diana’s hacking and cleansing support.

| Phase | Boss Mechanics | Player Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1: Filament Spikes** | Eight launches massive spikes that track the player. | Use **Overdrive Protocol** to break defenses; Diana cleanses the area. |
| **Phase 2: The Wall** | A massive barrier blocks progress; Eight becomes invulnerable. | Protect Diana while she charges the hacking matrix to drop the wall. |
| **Phase 3: Final Stand** | High-intensity projectiles and "Deletion Protocol" attacks. | Utilize the heavy cannon; time your shots when Eight's core is exposed. |

During this encounter, Diana’s growth is a central theme. She moves from a passive observer to an active participant, eventually stating that "Being with Hue made me stronger." This sentiment is the emotional core that leads into the final cinematic sequences.

> **Warning:** During the final phase, Eight will attempt a "Deletion Protocol." If you fail to interrupt this with a charged cannon shot, you will trigger an immediate Game Over rather than a narrative ending.

## The Standard Ending: Hue’s Sacrifice

The most common of the **pragmata endings** involves a bittersweet sacrifice. After defeating Eight, Hue and Diana reach the cargo shuttle at the top of the tower. However, the damage sustained during the battle has rendered the shuttle's automated launch systems inoperable. 

To ensure Diana reaches Earth, Hue must manually push the cargo shuttle along the launch rail. This sequence is gameplay-integrated, requiring players to mash buttons to represent Hue’s physical exertion. Because the launch mechanism requires a manual trigger from the outside, Hue cannot join Diana on the journey.

### Key Moments in the Standard Ending:
1. **The Choice:** Hue realizes the automation is broken and decides to stay behind.
2. **The Goodbye:** A tearful farewell where Hue tells Diana to "go find the sea."
3. **The Launch:** Hue uses his "elbow grease" to send the shuttle into orbit.
4. **The Credits:** Diana is seen landing on Earth, looking at the ocean for the first time, fulfilling the promise made earlier in the game.

## Unlocking the "Unknown Signal" Secret Ending

The "Unknown Signal" variant is considered the "True" ending by many fans. While it follows the same general path as the standard ending, it adds a layer of mystery regarding the transmission Diana receives during her descent. To unlock this version of the **pragmata endings**, players must fulfill specific criteria throughout the campaign.

| Requirement | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Data Logs** | Collect all 15 "Doctor's Notes" scattered across the lunar base. |
| **Diana's Trust** | Maximize Diana's trust level by engaging in all optional "Bird Watching" and "Story Time" interactions. |
| **Eight's Mercy** | During the final fight, choose to "Cleanse" Eight's core rather than "Destroy" it during the final prompt. |

In the secret ending, the post-credits scene is extended. As Diana walks along the beach on Earth, her suit picks up an "Unknown Signal." A mysterious voice asks, "Traveling alone today?" implying that Hue’s consciousness may have been uploaded to the network, or that other survivors are present on the planet.

## Lore Implications of the Finale

The conclusion of Pragmata raises several questions about the nature of the "Dead Filament" and the "Doctor" mentioned by Eight. Eight claims the Doctor wished to send the inhabitants to Earth, but Eight viewed this as "painting the world black." This suggests that the Moon was a quarantine zone, and the **pragmata endings** represent the potential breach of that quarantine.

Diana’s role as a "Cure" is also vital. In the final dialogue, she mentions that the "Cure has side effects." This could hint at a sequel or DLC where Diana’s presence on Earth fundamentally changes the planet's ecosystem. For more information on the development of the game's lore, visit the [official Capcom website](https://www.capcom.com/) for developer insights.

> **Tip:** If you are struggling with the final boss on Hard difficulty, focus on Diana's "Overdrive" cooldowns. She can stun Eight, providing a 5-second window to deal massive damage without risk.

## FAQ

**Q: Can Hue survive the pragmata endings?**

A: In the current 2026 version of the game, Hue always stays behind on the Moon to ensure Diana's shuttle launches. However, the "Unknown Signal" ending strongly implies that his consciousness may have survived in a digital form or that he survived the initial explosion.

**Q: How many endings are in Pragmata?**

A: There are primarily two main narrative endings: the Standard Ending and the Unknown Signal (Secret) Ending. There are also various "Fail" states that result in unique cinematic deaths but do not count as official story conclusions.

**Q: Do my choices during the game affect the ending?**

A: Yes. Your interaction with Diana and how you handle the final boss prompt (Cleanse vs. Destroy) are the primary factors that determine whether you see the standard or secret **pragmata endings**.

**Q: What does the "Traveling alone today?" line mean?**

A: This is the cliffhanger for the game. It suggests that Diana is not as alone as she thinks. Whether the voice belongs to a new character, an Earth survivor, or a reconstructed version of Hue is left to player interpretation for a potential sequel.
