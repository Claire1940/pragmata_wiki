## The Three Primary Narrative Endings

The narrative structure of Pragmata is built on the RE Engine’s sophisticated story-flag system. Unlike traditional RPGs where a single dialogue choice at the end changes the outcome, Pragmata tracks your "Synchronization Rate" with Diana throughout the entire 50+ hour campaign. If you rush through the main objectives, you are mathematically favored to receive the Default Ending.

| Ending Name | Requirements | Narrative Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Default Ending** | Standard completion with less than 100% Synergy. | A cryptic conclusion with unresolved character arcs. |
| **Abandonment Ending** | Low Diana Synergy and multiple failed escort sequences. | A tragic outcome where the bond between Hugh and Diana is severed. |
| **True Ending** | **100% Synchronization**, all 20 Memory Fragments, and all side missions. | The complete resolution of the lunar colony mystery and Earth's fate. |

> **💡 Tip:** To ensure you stay on the path for the True Ending, check your Synchronization Gauge in the pause menu frequently. It should be steadily climbing as you complete optional hacking puzzles and find holographic data drives.

## Pragmata All Endings Guide: Reaching 100% Synchronization

To unlock the True Ending, you must be a completionist. The game tracks your thoroughness through Diana’s missing memories. Many of these require late-game mobility upgrades, such as the Kinetic Grapple or the zero-gravity thrusters.

### Critical Memory Fragment Locations
*   **Sector 2 (Commercial Hub):** Requires the Kinetic Grapple (obtained in Chapter 5).
*   **Sector 4 (Residential Ruins):** Requires high-altitude gliding modules.
*   **Sector 7 (Industrial Zone):** Hidden behind a hacking terminal that requires Diana’s module to be fully upgraded at a workbench.

Beyond the memories, you must complete three specific side missions that provide context for Hugh’s background. These missions often trigger after backtracking to earlier sectors once new tools are acquired. If you ignore these requests, your synergy will cap at 90%, locking you out of the best conclusion.

## Points of No Return (Warning)

Pragmata utilizes a semi-open world structure, but there are three hard "Points of No Return." Once you cross these thresholds, the story advances irreversibly, and any incomplete side quests or uncollected memories in those zones are lost for that playthrough.

| Chapter / Sector | Environmental Trigger | Necessary Action Before Proceeding |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chapter 3: Sector 4** | Activating the Deep Space Comm Relay | Collect all 3 Encrypted Logs in the ruins. |
| **Chapter 6: Sector 7** | Boarding the Mag-Lev Transport | Fully upgrade Diana’s Hacking Module. |
| **Chapter 9: Sector 9** | Entering the Orbital Elevator | **Reach 100% Synergy** and 20/20 Memories. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** The prompt at the Orbital Elevator in Sector 9 is your final warning. Once you accept, you are locked into the final boss gauntlet. If your exploration rate is below 100%, you will not see the True Ending.

## Unlocking the 8 Secret Completion Pictures

For those who spent time with the Pragmata demo or are looking for "Legacy" unlocks in the full game gallery, there are eight specific ending pictures that reward different playstyles. These are essentially mini-achievements that showcase your mastery of the game's mechanics.

1.  **The Pacifist:** Only use Diana’s abilities for combat after the initial mandatory shot.
2.  **The Tactical Stun:** Use the "Green Gun" (Static/Control gun) to stun a high-level orange robot on the final elevator.
3.  **The Scavenger:** Collect every yellow cube and big chest in the level.
4.  **The Ghost:** Complete a "No Hit" run of the boss encounter.
5.  **The Speedster:** Finish the entire sequence in under 10 minutes.
6.  **The Marksman:** Use a single shot from the Static Gun to control two enemies simultaneously.
7.  **The Hoarder:** Collect a high volume of resources (exact amount varies by difficulty).
8.  **The Survivor:** Simply finish the demo/sequence on any difficulty.

To trigger the elusive **No. 2 Picture**, you must kill the three enemies on the final elevator using three different methods: use the pistol for the drone, Diana’s ability for the first robot, and the Static Gun for the second robot. This forces a rare "Orange Robot" to spawn, which grants the unlock upon defeat.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the True Ending on my first playthrough?**

A: Yes, it is entirely possible to get the True Ending on your first run, provided you do not rush. You must explore every corner of the lunar sectors, complete all side missions, and collect all 20 Memory Fragments before the final Point of No Return in Sector 9.

**Q: What happens if I miss a Memory Fragment in an earlier sector?**

A: You can backtrack to most sectors using the fast-travel system until you reach the final act in Chapter 9. However, some areas in Sector 4 and Sector 7 become permanently locked after their respective story triggers. Always clear a map before moving to the next major objective.

**Q: Does difficulty affect which ending I receive?**

A: No, the narrative endings are tied to your exploration and synchronization with Diana, not the combat difficulty. However, beating the game on "Hardcore" is required to unlock specific cosmetic rewards like the N7-Vanguard suit.

**Q: How do I check my current Synchronization Rate?**

A: You can view your current Synchronization/Synergy level in the "Status" tab of the main menu. It is represented by a percentage bar. For the True Ending, this bar must be at exactly 100% before entering the Orbital Elevator.
