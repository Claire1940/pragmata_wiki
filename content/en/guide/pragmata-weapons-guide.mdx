## Primary Weapons: The Foundation of Your Build

Your primary weapons are your most reliable tools because their ammunition recharges over time. While they may seem basic compared to heavy attack units, they remain viable throughout the entire game. You generally have two main choices that cater to different playstyles: the Grip Gun and the Pulse Carbine.

### Grip Gun vs. Pulse Carbine
The Grip Gun serves as your versatile all-rounder. It delivers solid precision and respectable headshot modifiers. On the other hand, the Pulse Carbine is a fully automatic beast that prioritizes speed and elemental build-up over raw single-shot power.

| Weapon | Type | Primary Benefit | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grip Gun** | Semi-Auto Pistol | Reliable precision and ammo efficiency | General encounters and headshot hunting |
| **Pulse Carbine** | Fully Automatic | Rapid **Heat** build-up on targets | Critical-oriented builds and fast enemies |

> **💡 Tip:** If you prefer a build centered on critical hits, the Pulse Carbine is the superior choice due to how quickly it fills the enemy's overheat gauge.

## Attack Units: Heavy Ordnance

Attack units provide the burst damage and utility needed to take down larger threats. These units often require charging or have limited ammunition, making their placement and timing crucial.

### Long-Range and Area Control
For those who prefer keeping their distance, the **Charge Piercer** is arguably the strongest single-hit weapon in the game. It functions as a rail cannon that can be charged up to three levels, capable of piercing through multiple enemies to hit weak spots hidden on their backs.

If you find yourself overwhelmed by smaller "trash" mobs, the **Homing Missiles** offer excellent crowd control. By locking onto multiple targets, you can clear a room in seconds, though they deal less focused damage to primary bosses compared to the **Photon Laser**.

| Attack Unit | Damage Type | Key Characteristic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Shotgun / Spread | High projectile count; devastating at point-blank range |
| **Charge Piercer** | Rail / Pierce | Strongest single-hit potential; hits through enemies |
| **Photon Laser** | Sustained Beam | Highest raw DPS; requires the target to be locked down |
| **Homing Missiles** | Explosive / AoE | Multi-lock capabilities; ideal for clearing small mobs |
| **Jackhammer** | Melee / Pile Bunker | Post-game weapon; ignores "Open" state for massive damage |

## Tactical and Defensive Units

Secondary units are what truly define your tactical flexibility. These tools allow you to manipulate the battlefield, creating openings for your heavier weapons to shine.

### Tactical Utility
The **Riot Blaster** is a community favorite because of its staggering potential. A well-placed shot can knock down most standard enemies, allowing you to close the distance with a Shockwave Gun. Alternatively, the **Stasis Net** is perfect for boss fights where you need a target to remain stationary for a Photon Laser burst.

*   **Code Generator:** Essential for disabling "blocker" nodes that prevent you from hacking enemies.
*   **Sticky Bombs:** Shrinks the hacking matrix, making it faster to execute hacks during intense firefights.
*   **Hacking Mines:** Allows you to "pre-load" hacking nodes into a proximity trap.

### Defensive Options
Defense in Pragmata isn't just about blocking; it's about redirection. The **Drone Hive** acts as an offensive defense, deploying funnels that automatically target enemies while you focus on hacking.

> **Warning:** The **Impact Barrier** is highly situational. While it protects against projectiles and aerial enemies, it can limit your mobility. Use it primarily when facing missile-heavy encounters.

## Advanced Hacking Mechanics

A central pillar of any **pragmata weapons guide** is the hacking system. Hacking an enemy puts them into an "Open" state, significantly increasing the damage they take and applying various status effects.

### Essential Hacking Nodes
Nodes are the specific effects you apply during a hack. You typically have three slots to customize your digital assault.

1.  **Decode:** Increases all damage dealt to the target by up to 50% (1.5x modifier).
2.  **Confuse:** A superior alternative to Freeze, lasting roughly 40 seconds and causing enemies to ignore you or attack their allies.
3.  **Expose:** Causes subsequent hacks to deal massive critical damage.
4.  **Chain:** Allows for consecutive, rapid-fire hacks that build in power with each successful execution.

### Hacking Modes Comparison
Your Hacking Mode dictates how the "Open" status behaves and how damage is calculated.

| Mode | Focus | Mechanics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Offensive** | Balanced | Increases hack damage by ~8% per additional shot |
| **Strike** | Burst Damage | Double the cumulative damage of Offense; closes the target after execution |
| **Hybrid** | Sustain | Allows you to extend the hacking timer by hitting specific nodes |
| **Combust** | Criticals | Maximizes **Heat** build-up; essential for Carbine builds |
| **Boost** | Hacking-Only | Massive boost to active hacks; can kill elites without firing a bullet |

## Optimized Loadouts for 2026

To make the most of your gear, you should aim for synergy between your weapon's elemental properties and your hacking mode. For example, a "Heat" build utilizes the Pulse Carbine, the Heat hacking node, and the Combust hacking mode to trigger frequent, high-damage critical explosions.

For a more tactical approach, the "Rail-Hacker" setup uses the Charge Piercer for long-range kills, the Homing Missiles for crowd control, and the Confuse node to keep enemies from closing the distance. This allows you to pick off high-value targets while the rest of the mob is distracted.

> **💡 Tip:** Equip the **Hyperfocus** mod. It prevents your hacking matrix from resetting when you take damage, which is a life-saver during chaotic boss phases.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best weapon for beginners in the pragmata weapons guide?**

A: The Grip Gun paired with the Riot Blaster is the most reliable starting combo. The Grip Gun has manageable recoil and recharging ammo, while the Riot Blaster provides a safety net by knocking down enemies that get too close.

**Q: How do I unlock the Jackhammer?**

A: The Jackhammer is a post-game reward. It is specifically designed for New Game Plus playthroughs, allowing you to deal massive "Pile Bunker" damage to enemies regardless of whether they are in an open state or not.

**Q: Is Freeze or Confuse better for crowd control?**

A: Confuse is generally considered superior. While Freeze locks an enemy in place for about 8 seconds, Confuse lasts for 40 seconds and can force enemies to draw aggro away from you or even grant you their buffs.

**Q: Why is my hacking matrix resetting?**

A: By default, taking a direct hit from an enemy resets your hacking progress. To prevent this, you need to find and equip the **Hyperfocus** mod, which allows you to complete hacks even while under fire.
