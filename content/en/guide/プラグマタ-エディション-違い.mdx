## Pragmata Edition Comparison Overview

Pragmata is available in three primary versions. While the core gameplay experience remains identical across all editions, the additional digital content and physical goods vary significantly. It is important to note that the release dates differ slightly for the Switch 2 version compared to the other high-end platforms.

The Standard Edition is the baseline for most players, providing the full game and any applicable early purchase bonuses. However, the Deluxe Edition represents a 1,000 yen increase that bundles several cosmetic and audio additions that cannot be ignored by fans of the game's unique aesthetic.

## Deep Dive: The Deluxe Edition & Shelter Variety Pack

For those looking to customize their experience, the Deluxe Edition is the most popular choice. The primary **プラグマタ エディション 違い** here is the inclusion of the "Shelter Variety Pack." This pack is designed to enhance your time spent in the game’s safe zones (Shelters) and provides a variety of visual overhauls for both Hyu and Diana.

### Shelter Variety Pack Contents

The pack is not just a single skin; it is a collection of items that touch upon various aspects of the game’s presentation:

*   **Hyu Costumes**: Includes the "Hard Lifter" (a heavy, industrial yellow suit) and the "Luna Cat" outfit.
*   **Diana Costumes**: Includes the "Mech Builder" suit and the fan-favorite "Fluffy" (Mokomoko) outfit.
*   **Weapon Skins**: The "Grip Gun DS" skin provides a sleek alternative look for Hyu’s primary tool.
*   **Shelter BGM**: Three additional tracks for your base, including Lo-fi, EDM, and Jazz versions of the main themes.
*   **Diana Emotes**: Three unique animations for Diana: "Drowsy," "High Tension," and "Step."
*   **Data Library**: A digital artbook featuring high-resolution concept art of the lunar environment and mechanical designs.

> **💡 Tip:** If you purchase the Standard Edition and later decide you want these items, a digital upgrade is available for 1,500 yen. Buying the Deluxe Edition upfront saves you 500 yen.

## The Acrylic Block Set: For the Collectors

Exclusive to the e-Capcom store, the Acrylic Block Set is the premium physical option. This version is specifically targeted at fans who want a tangible piece of the Pragmata world on their desk. The "Acrylic Block" itself measures approximately 70mm x 80mm with a 10mm thickness.

One unique feature of this block is its curved bottom. When placed on a flat surface, the block can rock back and forth, making the "Diana" illustration appear to sway. At a price point of 10,200 yen, the physical block essentially costs an additional 1,210 yen over the Deluxe Edition. Given the high quality of Capcom's official merchandise, this is considered a fair price for a limited-run collector's item.

## Which Edition Should You Buy?

Choosing between the various versions depends on your budget and your interest in cosmetics.

*   **Buy the Standard Edition if**: You only care about the core gameplay and want to save money. You still get the Samurai/Ninja skins if you buy early, which many players find superior to the Deluxe skins.
*   **Buy the Deluxe Edition if**: You are a digital player who wants the full "vibe" of the game. The 1,000 yen premium is relatively low for the amount of content provided, especially the digital artbook which offers deep lore into the lunar facility.
*   **Buy the Acrylic Block Set if**: You are a hardcore Capcom collector or a fan of Diana’s character design. This set is likely to become a rarity in the future as it is tied to the initial launch window.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when buying "Used" physical copies later in 2026. The pre-order bonus codes for the Neo-Bushido and Neo-Kunoichi outfits are likely to have been redeemed by the original owner.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the main プラグマタ エディション 違い between Standard and Deluxe?**

A: The primary difference is the "Shelter Variety Pack," which includes four character costumes, a weapon skin, three BGM tracks, three emotes for Diana, and a digital artbook. The Deluxe Edition is digital-only on most storefronts.

**Q: Can I upgrade to the Deluxe Edition later?**

A: Yes, Capcom offers a "Deluxe Upgrade" DLC on all digital stores. However, it costs 1,500 yen separately, whereas buying the Deluxe Edition bundle at launch only adds 1,000 yen to the base price.

**Q: Is the Acrylic Block Set available for PC users?**

A: The Acrylic Block Set is primarily sold as a physical bundle for consoles (PS5, Switch 2). PC players who want the block usually have to purchase the merchandise separately through the e-Capcom store, though availability may vary by region.

**Q: Does the Deluxe Edition include a Season Pass?**

A: No. As of the April 2026 launch, Capcom has not announced a formal Season Pass. The Deluxe Edition contents are strictly "Day 1" cosmetic and media bonuses. Any future story expansions will likely need to be purchased separately.
