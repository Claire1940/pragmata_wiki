## Initial Difficulty Selection: Casual vs. Standard

The first opportunity you have to set the tone of your adventure is at the very beginning of a new game. Upon selecting "New Game" from the main menu, you are presented with two primary options. This choice is the most significant one you will make regarding the game's lethality, as it dictates the baseline for enemy behavior and player assistance.

| Difficulty Level | Enemy Strength | Aim Assist | Mid-Game Flexibility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Casual** | Significantly Reduced | Fully Enabled | **Cannot be increased** |
| **Standard** | Balanced / Normal | Optional / Manual | **Can be lowered** after death |

When you select **Casual**, the game provides a clear warning. This mode is designed specifically for players who want to focus on the bond between the protagonist and Diana without worrying about complex combat loops. Enemies have lower health pools, deal less damage, and the game’s lock-on mechanics are much more aggressive. It is important to note that if you start on Casual, you are locked into that setting for the duration of that specific playthrough.

Selecting **Standard** provides the intended experience. It requires a mastery of the game's unique gadgetry and movement mechanics. While it is more difficult than Casual, it offers more flexibility for the player later on. If you find the Standard experience too overwhelming, the game provides a "safety net" to drop down to Casual, though this is a one-way street.

## How to Change Difficulty in Pragmata During Gameplay

If you find yourself stuck on a particularly difficult boss or a high-intensity combat arena, you might look for a way to lower the stakes. You cannot simply open the "Options" menu and swap the difficulty slider. Instead, the game monitors your performance.

The only way to access the menu to lower your difficulty during an active playthrough is by failing. When your character’s health is depleted and you reach the "Game Over" state, the game transitions to a thematic **System Error** screen. 

### Steps to Lower Difficulty After Death:
1. **Initiate Combat**: Enter the encounter that is proving too difficult.
2. **System Error Screen**: Allow your character to be defeated by enemies or hazards.
3. **Access Options**: On the death screen, look for the prompt at the bottom of the display.
4. **Input Command**: Press **Triangle** (PlayStation) or **Y** (Xbox) to bring up the difficulty adjustment window.
5. **Confirm Change**: Select "Switch to Casual" and confirm the choice.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Changing from Standard to Casual is permanent for your current save file. You cannot revert to Standard or Lunatic mode once this change has been confirmed without starting an entirely new game.

## Unlocking Lunatic Mode and New Game Plus

For veteran players who find Standard mode too easy, there is a hidden tier of difficulty known as **Lunatic Mode**. This setting is not available to players during their initial run. It is designed as a "New Game Plus" (NG+) reward for those who have mastered the core mechanics of the lunar environment.

| Feature | Standard Mode | Lunatic Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Enemy AI** | Standard patterns | Faster, more aggressive tracking |
| **Resource Drops** | Common | Rare / Scarce |
| **Player Damage** | 1.0x (Base) | 0.5x (Reduced) |
| **Enemy Damage** | 1.0x (Base) | 2.5x (Massively Increased) |

To unlock this mode, you must complete the main story on any difficulty (though Standard is recommended for the best transition). Once the credits roll, you will receive a notification that Lunatic Mode is now available. When you start a New Game Plus, you will be given the option to select this extreme challenge. This mode is the ultimate test of **how to change difficulty in pragmata** to its absolute limit, requiring perfect timing and resource management.

## Impact of Difficulty on Gameplay Mechanics

Choosing a difficulty level in Pragmata affects more than just the health bars of your enemies. It fundamentally alters several underlying systems that dictate how you interact with the world. 

### Combat and Aiming
In Casual mode, the game employs a "Soft Lock" system that keeps your reticle centered on enemy weak points. This is particularly helpful during the high-mobility sequences where you must protect Diana while fending off robotic drones. On Standard and Lunatic, this assistance is either minimized or removed entirely, forcing you to rely on your own precision.

### Resource Management
Pragmata features a crafting and upgrade system that relies on materials found throughout the lunar base. On higher difficulties, the drop rates for rare components are adjusted. While you might find plenty of ammunition and health kits on Casual, Lunatic mode forces you to scavenge every corner, as enemies will rarely drop the supplies you need to survive the next encounter.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are playing on Standard and are struggling, try upgrading your "Lunar Suit" and "Multi-Tool" before deciding to lower the difficulty. Often, a few upgrades can bridge the gap in challenge.

## Strategic Recommendations for Your Playthrough

Selecting the right difficulty is essential for enjoying the narrative weight of Pragmata. If you are a fan of Capcom's other action titles like *Devil May Cry* or *Resident Evil*, starting on **Standard** is the recommended path. It provides enough resistance to make the upgrade systems feel meaningful without being inherently unfair.

If you are more interested in the mystery of the girl and the futuristic setting, **Casual** is a perfectly valid way to play. However, remember that you will miss out on some of the tension that comes from the survival-horror elements of the game. 

For those aiming for the Platinum Trophy or 100% Achievement completion, be aware that many of the high-tier rewards and trophies are tied to completing the game on Standard or Lunatic. Lowering the difficulty mid-game may disable specific "Completionist" rewards for that run. You can find more details on the [official Pragmata website](https://www.capcom.com/pragmata/) regarding seasonal updates and potential new difficulty patches.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change from Casual back to Standard if the game becomes too easy?**

A: No. Once you have selected Casual at the start or switched to it during a "System Error" screen, you are locked into that difficulty for the remainder of the playthrough. You would need to start a New Game to play on Standard again.

**Q: Does changing the difficulty affect the story or ending of Pragmata?**

A: The core narrative and endings remain the same regardless of difficulty. However, certain post-credit scenes and New Game Plus unlocks (like Lunatic Mode) require you to have completed the game on specific settings.

**Q: Is there a way to learn how to change difficulty in pragmata without dying?**

A: Unfortunately, no. The developers designed the difficulty adjustment to be a "last resort" triggered by the player's failure in combat. There is no menu option to lower the difficulty while your character is still alive and exploring.

**Q: Does Lunatic Mode offer better loot?**

A: While the drop rates for standard supplies are lower, Lunatic Mode often features exclusive cosmetic rewards and higher-tier upgrade materials that allow you to push your equipment stats beyond what is possible in a Standard run.
