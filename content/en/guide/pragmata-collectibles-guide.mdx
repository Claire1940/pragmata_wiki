## Understanding Collectible Types in Pragmata

Before diving into the specific locations, it is vital to understand what you are looking for. The game categorizes its secrets into four primary types. Each type serves a different purpose, from upgrading your suit’s "Overdrive Protocol" to providing background information on the enigmatic character Diana and her connection to the AI entity known as Edith.

| Collectible Type | Appearance | Primary Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Data Chips** | Glowing blue rectangular prisms | Upgrade points for hacking tools |
| **Consciousness Echoes** | Shimmering humanoid silhouettes | Lore snippets and character backstory |
| **Terminal Logs** | Interactive computer screens | Map data and security codes |
| **Walker Parts** | Metallic mechanical components | Cosmetic skins for the protagonist's suit |

Finding these items often requires backtracking after restoring power to specific sectors. As seen in recent gameplay, the environment changes significantly once the "Emergency Lockdown" is disabled, opening previously sealed maintenance hatches and elevator shafts.

## Sector 1: The Power Plant Collectibles

The Power Plant serves as the introductory hub where players first encounter the "Sector Guard Protocol." To maximize your efficiency, you should look for collectibles immediately after Diana disables the local lockdowns. There are three primary terminals in this area that must be rebooted to restore functionality, and each one is flanked by hidden items.

### The Main Power Terminal (Top Floor)
After fighting your way through the initial wave of haywire bots, you will reach the top floor to reboot the main power controller. Do not interact with the terminal immediately. Instead, check the alcove to the left of the primary console.

1.  **Consciousness Echo #1**: Located behind the cooling pipes. It depicts a former technician discussing the "Walker" efficiency.
2.  **Terminal Log: "Edith's First Directive"**: This is found on a small tablet near the emergency manual.

> **💡 Tip:** Always use your pulse scan (R3/Right Stick Click) after entering a new room. Collectibles will highlight with a distinct gold outline, even through thin walls or crates.

### The Lower Maintenance Bay
Once the power levels reach 60%, a secret compartment opens in the floor leading back to the lower levels. This area is easy to miss if you rush toward the next objective.

| Item Name | Location Detail | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Data Chip: Hacking Speed I** | Inside the ventilation shaft | Diana's Hack Level 1 |
| **Walker Part: Lunar Chrome** | Under the primary elevator platform | None |

## Advanced Hacking and Terminal Secrets

As you progress, the complexity of the puzzles increases. Diana’s ability to "hack the list" becomes the primary method for uncovering high-tier collectibles. When you encounter an "Unauthorized Access Detected" warning from Edith, it usually indicates that a high-value collectible is nearby, protected by a security firewall.

To successfully retrieve these items, you must protect Diana while she interacts with the terminal. Using the "Overdrive Protocol" during these segments is highly recommended, as the bots will prioritize attacking her over the protagonist.

### Terminal Reward Tiers
Hacking terminals isn't just about opening doors; it's about data extraction. Depending on how quickly you clear the room, the rewards can vary:

*   **Gold Tier (Under 60 seconds):** Unlocks a Permanent Stat Boost and a Lore Log.
*   **Silver Tier (Under 120 seconds):** Unlocks a Lore Log and 500 Upgrade Credits.
*   **Bronze Tier (Clearance only):** Unlocks the path forward.

Using a **pragmata collectibles guide** to pre-plan your route through these combat arenas can save you significant time and ensure you hit the Gold Tier requirements consistently.

## Hidden Consciousness Results

One of the more unique aspects of the game's collection system is the "Consciousness Results." These are not physical items but rather environmental triggers that occur when you stand in a specific spot for a set duration, allowing the protagonist to "sync" with the lunar base's memory.

In the Power Plant section, there is a specific Consciousness Result located on the bridge overlooking the main reactor. If you wait for Diana to finish her dialogue about "thinking up new innovations," a prompt will appear. Syncing here reveals the true purpose of the Walker project and Edith’s original programming.

## Achievement and Trophy Hunting

For those aiming for the Platinum trophy or 100% completion on Steam, the **pragmata collectibles guide** is your best friend. There are several achievements tied directly to the collection of these items:

1.  **"Archivist of the Moon"**: Collect all Terminal Logs in a single playthrough.
2.  **"Echoes of the Past"**: Experience every Consciousness Echo.
3.  **"Fully Loaded"**: Find all Data Chips and max out Diana’s hacking capabilities.
4.  **"Fashionable Walker"**: Collect all 15 Walker Parts hidden across the lunar sectors.

> **Warning:** Some collectibles are "Point of No Return" items. Specifically, once you leave the Power Plant for the Earth-Contact station, you cannot return to the lower maintenance bays until New Game Plus. Ensure you have 10/10 items before taking the final elevator.

## Strategies for Completionists

To make the most of this **pragmata collectibles guide**, we recommend a "two-pass" strategy for every new sector you enter:

*   **Pass 1 (Combat & Power):** Focus on reaching the terminals and clearing the "Sector Guard Protocol." This ensures the area is safe for exploration and that all electronically locked containers are powered.
*   **Pass 2 (Scavenging):** Once the "Emergency lockdown for all on-site locations has been disabled" message plays, go back to the start of the sector. Use Diana’s scan to pick up the items that were previously hidden behind powered-down doors.

You can track your progress in the "Database" tab of the main menu. It will show you exactly how many items are left in each sub-sector (e.g., Power Plant, Residential Zone, Research Lab). If you find yourself stuck at 95%, check the ceiling pipes; Capcom has a habit of hiding Data Chips in vertical spaces that require a double-jump or a boost from a "Walker" unit.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I find all collectibles in one playthrough?**

A: Yes, it is possible to find everything in a single run if you are diligent. However, some items in this **pragmata collectibles guide** require specific hacking levels that you might not have until later in the game. You can use the "Chapter Select" feature after beating the final boss to clean up anything you missed.

**Q: Does Diana help find collectibles?**

A: Absolutely. Diana will occasionally point toward hidden rooms or mention that she "smells something interesting," which is the game's way of hinting at a nearby Consciousness Echo or rare Walker Part.

**Q: Are there any missable collectibles in the Power Plant?**

A: Yes. The items located in the "Emergency Lockdown" zones must be collected before you initiate the final reboot of the power plant controller. Once the primary connection is severed by Edith, certain sub-levels become inaccessible due to the high radiation levels in the lore.

**Q: What do the Consciousness Results actually do?**

A: Beyond providing deep lore and context for the story, collecting all Consciousness Results unlocks the "True Ending" of Pragmata. They are essential for players who want to understand the relationship between the protagonist, Diana, and the Earth-Contact mission.
