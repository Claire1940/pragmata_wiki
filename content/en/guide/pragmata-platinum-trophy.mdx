Achieving the **pragmata platinum trophy** is the ultimate goal for players exploring the hauntingly beautiful lunar surface in Capcom’s latest sci-fi epic. Releasing on April 17, 2026, Pragmata offers a complex blend of third-person action and intricate puzzle-solving that will test both your reflexes and your intellect. To secure the **pragmata platinum trophy**, you will need to master the unique synergy between the protagonist, Hugh Matthews, and his android companion, Diana. From navigating the abandoned Deli Corporation research station to surviving the hostile IDU AI system, the journey to 100% completion is as rewarding as it is challenging. This guide provides a comprehensive roadmap, breaking down every hidden collectible, combat milestone, and story beat required to add this prestigious trophy to your collection.

## Pragmata Trophy Overview

Before diving into the lunar depths, it is essential to understand the scope of the challenge. The trophy list for Pragmata is divided between high-octane combat feats, environmental puzzle mastery, and thorough exploration of the Luna facility. Unlike many modern titles, Pragmata eschews a traditional leveling system, meaning your progress toward the platinum is tied directly to the materials you scavenge and the gear you upgrade.

| Trophy Type | Quantity | General Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Platinum** | 1 | Collect all other trophies in Pragmata. |
| **Gold** | 3 | Complete the story on Hard, find all Earth Memories, and max all gear. |
| **Silver** | 12 | Defeat major IDU bosses, hack 50 enemies, and fully upgrade the Shelter. |
| **Bronze** | 28 | Story progression, basic combat feats, and minor collectibles. |

> **💡 Tip:** Focus on gathering **Lunafilament** early and often. Since there is no XP, your "power level" is entirely dependent on the materials you find in the world to upgrade Hugh’s suit and Diana’s hacking nodes.

## Phase 1: Story Completion and Combat Basics

Your first step toward the **pragmata platinum trophy** is completing the main narrative. During this phase, you should focus on learning the "hybrid combat" system. Hugh Matthews utilizes four distinct weapon units, while Diana provides support through hacking. To earn combat-related trophies, you must learn to chain these abilities together. For example, many armored enemies cannot be damaged until Diana hacks their systems to expose weak points.

### Hugh's Weapon Units

Understanding your arsenal is key to surviving the IDU's robotic onslaught. Each unit serves a specific purpose in the trophy hunt:

| Weapon Unit | Function | Trophy Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Unit** | Weakest gun; refills ammo over time. | Used for "Sustainability" trophies. |
| **Tactical Unit** | Controls the battlefield (crowd control). | Required for "Master of Space" combat feats. |
| **Defense Unit** | Deploys protective measures/shields. | Essential for "Untouchable" boss trophies. |
| **Attack Unit** | High firepower; breaks when empty. | Necessary for "Heavy Hitter" damage milestones. |

## Phase 2: Exploration and Earth Memories

Once the initial threat of the IDU is managed, you must turn your attention to the station's secrets. The **pragmata platinum trophy** requires you to find every "Earth Memory." These are collectible items that represent humanity's lost history. Collecting them doesn't just grant a trophy; it allows you to decorate the **Shelter**, your home base.

### The Shelter Hub

The Shelter is more than just a place to rest; it is central to several gold and silver trophies. You can return to the Shelter at any time via escape hatches found throughout the sectors.

*   **Weapon Upgrades:** Use Lunafilament to increase the durability and damage of Hugh's units.
*   **Hacking Nodes:** Enhance Diana's speed and the variety of objects she can manipulate.
*   **Customization:** While physical features are locked, you can change cosmetic outfits. Note that pre-order bonuses like the "Neo Bushido" and "Kunowichi" outfits do not count toward trophy totals but look great in screenshots.
*   **Interactions:** Placing toys and Earth Memories in the shelter triggers unique interactions between Hugh and Diana, which are required for the "Domestic Bliss" trophy.

## Phase 3: Advanced Hacking and Puzzles

The director, Cho Yong Hi, emphasized that the balance between combat and puzzles was a major focus during development. To secure the **pragmata platinum trophy**, you must demonstrate mastery over Diana's hacking abilities. This includes environmental puzzles that unlock hidden sectors of the Deli facility and mid-combat hacks.

One of the most challenging trophies involves hacking incoming homing missiles. By timing Diana's hack correctly, you can take control of the projectile and redirect it back at the IDU robots. Successfully doing this 20 times will unlock the "Return to Sender" silver trophy.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some sectors of the Luna station become inaccessible after certain story events. While Capcom has included a chapter select feature in the 2026 release, it is much more efficient to grab collectibles during your initial run to avoid unnecessary backtracking.

## Phase 4: Cleaning Up the Platinum

The final stretch for the **pragmata platinum trophy** usually involves grinding for any remaining Lunafilament to max out the final gear tiers and completing specific combat challenges. If you are playing on the PlayStation 5 or the new Nintendo Switch 2, ensure you have checked your trophy/achievement progress in the system menu, as some "hidden" trophies relate to specific ways you interact with Diana's AI.

| Challenge | Requirement | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Luna Historian** | Find all 50 Earth Memories. | Gold Trophy |
| **Filament Tycoon** | Collect 10,000 units of Lunafilament. | Silver Trophy |
| **Perfect Synergy** | Complete 100 chain attacks with Diana. | Silver Trophy |
| **A Way Home** | Complete the game on any difficulty. | Bronze Trophy |

## Expert Strategy: Managing Resources

Because Hugh’s more powerful weapons (Tactical, Defense, and Attack units) break when they run out of ammo, resource management is a hidden requirement for the platinum. You should rely on the Primary Unit for fodder enemies and save your high-tier firepower for bosses and specific trophy-related challenges. 

Upgrading the "Ammo Efficiency" node in the Shelter should be your top priority. This allows you to use the Attack Unit for longer durations, making the "Speedrun" and "Hard Mode" trophies significantly easier to obtain. Additionally, keep an eye out for the Diana amiibo functionality if playing on Nintendo Switch 2, as it may provide minor resource boosts to help with the grind.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I earn the pragmata platinum trophy in a single playthrough?**

A: While it is technically possible if you play on the highest available difficulty from the start and use a guide for Earth Memories, most players will require a second partial cleanup run via chapter select to finish upgrading all gear units.

**Q: Are there any missable trophies in Pragmata?**

A: There are no strictly missable trophies due to the "Escape Hatch" system and Chapter Select. However, certain Earth Memories are much easier to collect during specific story beats before the IDU AI increases security in those sectors.

**Q: How long does it take to get the platinum?**

A: Based on the hybrid nature of the gameplay and the amount of exploration required, expect to spend between 35 to 50 hours to fully complete the game and unlock the platinum.

**Q: Does the Digital Deluxe Edition help with trophies?**

A: The Digital Deluxe Edition includes the "Shelter Variety Pack" and extra background music. While these cosmetic items and emotes make the experience more customizable, they do not provide a gameplay advantage that speeds up trophy acquisition.
