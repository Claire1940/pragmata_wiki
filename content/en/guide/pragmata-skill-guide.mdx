## The Dual-Protagonist Combat System

In Pragmata, combat is a dance between Hugh’s raw firepower and Diana’s technical support. Hugh handles the primary engagement using an exosuit that grants him enhanced mobility and the ability to wield heavy weaponry. However, many enemies are protected by advanced "Eater" armor that renders standard munitions ineffective. This is where Diana becomes indispensable.

Diana serves as your tactical eye. She can identify weak points and initiate hacks that "open up" enemies, exposing their core processors to Hugh’s weaponry. Unlike traditional companion AI, Diana’s actions are directly influenced by your positioning and aim. If you lose line-of-sight or move too far from a target during a hack, the connection will sever, forcing you to restart the process.

### Hugh’s Weapon Arsenal

Hugh’s weapons are categorized into two types: Primary and Special. Understanding the ammo economy for each is vital for long-term survival in the solar plant.

| Weapon Type | Example | Ammo Source | Durability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary** | Tactical Pistol | Auto-recharges over time | **Infinite** |
| **Short-Range** | Shockwave Gun | Floor pickups / Ammo crates | **Breaks when empty** |
| **Crowd Control** | Stasis Net | Floor pickups / Ammo crates | **Breaks when empty** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Special weapons like the Shockwave Gun and Stasis Net are highly powerful but fragile. If you deplete the ammo completely, the weapon will break, and you must find a replacement in the environment.

## Mastering the Hacking Mini-Game

Hacking is the core mechanic of this **pragmata skill guide**. When Diana targets an enemy, a holographic grid appears. To successfully breach an enemy's defenses, you must guide a data stream through a series of nodes. This system is often compared to a rhythmic puzzle or the classic game "Snake," where your pathing determines the success and strength of the hack.

### Grid Node Types and Effects

The hacking grid is populated with various nodes that can either aid or hinder your progress. Navigating these correctly is the difference between a quick kill and a prolonged, dangerous engagement.

| Node Color | Effect | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue Square** | Standard data node; required to progress the hack. | **High** |
| **Green Square** | The exit node; reaching this completes the hack. | **Mandatory** |
| **Yellow Square** | Bonus node; significantly lowers enemy defense. | **Optional/Strategic** |
| **Red Square** | Hazard node; causes hack failure or "lockout." | **Avoid** |

To maximize your damage output, you should attempt to pass through as many **Yellow Nodes** as possible before reaching the Green exit node. The more bonus nodes you collect, the more vulnerable the enemy becomes once they are "opened."

## Advanced Skills and Overdrive Protocol

As you become more proficient with the basic loop of hacking and shooting, you will unlock advanced maneuvers. One of the most critical defensive skills is the **Perfect Dodge**. By timing your movement just as an enemy attack is about to land, Hugh enters a brief state of heightened awareness, allowing for a quick counter-shot or an immediate Diana hack initiation.

### The Overdrive Protocol (Ultimate Ability)

The Overdrive Protocol is your "ace in the hole." This gauge fills as Diana successfully completes hacks and Hugh deals damage to exposed cores. Once the gauge is full, you can trigger the protocol by pressing **L3 + R3** (on controllers) or the designated keybind.

*   **Area of Effect:** Overdrive releases a massive pulse that stuns all nearby enemies.
*   **Automatic Exposure:** Any enemy caught in the blast is immediately "opened up," bypassing the need for the hacking mini-game.
*   **Tactical Use:** Save this for boss fights or when you are completely surrounded by high-tier walkers.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Overdrive Protocol during the "Sector Guard" boss encounters to instantly expose the main tank or pilot, allowing you to dump all your Special weapon ammo for maximum DPS.

## Survival and Exploration Strategies

The environments in Pragmata, such as the solar power plant and the lunar "Cradle," are filled with verticality and hidden resources. Exploration is just as important as combat skill.

1.  **Manual Reboots:** Many areas are under emergency lockdown. You will often need to find a main power terminal and defend Diana while she performs a manual reboot.
2.  **Escape Hatches:** These act as your checkpoints. If you fall in battle, you will be teleported back to the last activated hatch. Always ensure Diana hacks these hatches (holding **L1**) before moving into a new sector.
3.  **Loot Management:** Look for "Special Christmas Gifts" or supply crates scattered throughout the levels. These contain sugar cubes (for Diana) and essential ammo for your Shockwave and Stasis weapons.

## Enemy Analysis: Facing the Eaters

The primary antagonists in Pragmata are the "Eaters," a collective of haywire bots and security protocols. They range from small, agile walkers to massive, multi-story "Gundam-style" robots.

*   **Walkers:** These are common fodder. A simple hack and a few shots from the primary pistol will take them down.
*   **Sector Guards:** These bosses require multiple stages of hacking. They often have shields that must be depleted using the Shockwave Gun before Diana can even begin the hacking mini-game.
*   **Unregistered Guests:** These high-speed units will attempt to interrupt Diana. Use the Stasis Net to lock them in place so you can focus on the hacking grid without distraction.

## Summary of Combat Flow

To excel in the game, always follow this prioritized combat loop:
1.  **Identify the Threat:** Use Hugh’s scanner to find the strongest enemy.
2.  **Initiate Support:** Position yourself so Diana has a clear line of sight to the target.
3.  **The Puzzle Phase:** Complete the hacking grid, prioritizing Yellow Nodes for defense debuffs.
4.  **The Execution Phase:** Switch to your high-damage Special weapons and target the glowing core.
5.  **Reposition:** Use Perfect Dodges to avoid counter-attacks while your Primary weapon recharges.

## FAQ

**Q: Does my age entry affect the gameplay in Pragmata?**

A: No, the age you enter at the start of the game is for data collection purposes and does not change the difficulty, story, or combat mechanics.

**Q: What happens if my Shockwave Gun breaks?**

A: If you run out of ammo, the weapon breaks and disappears from your inventory. You must explore the environment to find a new Shockwave Gun pickup to regain access to that weapon type.

**Q: Can I complete a hack without hitting every blue node?**

A: Yes, you only need to create a path to the Green exit node. However, hitting more nodes (especially yellow ones) provides significant combat bonuses, making the subsequent fight much easier.

**Q: Is there a way to reset a hack if I get stuck in a red node?**

A: If you enter a red node or lose focus, the hack will fail. You can usually restart the hack immediately, though some advanced enemies may have a "lockout" period where hacking is disabled for a short duration. Utilizing this **pragmata skill guide**'s pathing tips will help you avoid these failures.
