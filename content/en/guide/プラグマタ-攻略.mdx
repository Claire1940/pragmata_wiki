## Story Walkthrough: Prologue and Solar Power Plant

The journey begins in a desolate urban environment that serves as a tutorial for basic movement and combat mechanics. During the Prologue, players are introduced to the thruster system and basic hacking. Following the initial cutscenes, you must navigate through a series of debris-filled streets, using the blue sheets as markers for climbable ledges. Your first major objective is to reach the Shelter's central hub, which eventually leads to the tram station for the Solar Power Plant.

Upon arriving at the Solar Power Plant, the difficulty spikes as you encounter more advanced "Walker" units. This area emphasizes environmental interaction, requiring you to jump over laser grids and hack terminals to unlock sealed sectors.

### Solar Power Plant Key Objectives

| Step | Objective | Key Rewards / Items |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Navigate to the blocked sector | **Luna Filament**, **Reinforcement Component** |
| **2** | Hack the terminal at the end of the hall | Walker combat encounter |
| **3** | Disable the laser grid in the lower levels | **Shockwave Gun** |
| **4** | Reach the Power Management Lobby | **Cartridge Holder**, **Earth Memory: Globe** |
| **5** | Defeat the Sector Guard boss | **Shelter Level Key**, **New Weapon** |

> **💡 Tip:** Always use your scan pulse when entering a new room in the Power Plant. It highlights hidden **Reinforcement Components** tucked behind crates or under floor panels.

## Exploring the Large-Scale Output Test Site

The Large-Scale Output Test Site is a massive industrial zone that features a recreation of New York City. This area is significantly more open than the Power Plant, requiring extensive use of the thruster dash to cross gaps between rooftops. Here, you will find the Shopping Mall, a critical location for obtaining the **Riot Blaster** and the **Red Card Key**.

The mission structure in this zone revolves around activating Area Beacons to clear "Red Zones." These zones are heavily guarded, and clearing them is the only way to progress the narrative. The climax of this chapter involves a multi-stage battle against a Giant Bot that requires precise targeting of the glowing vents on its back.

### Essential Weapon Locations in the Test Site

| Weapon | Location | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Riot Blaster** | Shopping Mall Interior | Found near the entrance |
| **Charge Piercer** | Connection Passage Lower Level | Dropped after the first mini-boss |
| **Decoy Generator** | Filament Recycler Room | Found in a crate near the terminal |

As you progress through the Test Site, your **プラグマタ 攻略** focus should shift toward managing your thruster gauge. Many of the Area Beacons are located on high-altitude platforms that can only be reached by chaining a jump, a hover, and a dash. If you fall, you often have to restart the climbing sequence from the street level.

## Navigating the Terradome and Geo-Science Lab

The Terradome represents the final major biome of the game, blending high-tech facilities with artificial nature. This area introduces the "Cultivation" mechanic, where you must find and insert Cultivation Chips into terminals to grow massive trees that act as bridges to higher levels.

The Geo-Science Lab, located deep within the Terradome, is a stealth-heavy section where you must disable jammer devices to restore your HUD and scanning abilities. This section culminates in the battle against the Garden Keeper, a boss that utilizes both physical melee strikes and long-range filament attacks.

### Boss Strategy: Garden Keeper

1. **Phase 1:** Focus on dodging its sweeping arm attacks. Use the **Stasis Net** to slow its movement.
2. **Phase 2:** The boss will retreat to the ceiling. Use the **Photon Laser** or **Charge Piercer** to hit the glowing core on its chest.
3. **Phase 3:** The Garden Keeper will create filament barriers. Use your **Filament Move** ability to clear a path and deliver the finishing blow.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The jammer devices in the Geo-Science Lab will disable your ability to use health kits. Locate and hack the jammers immediately upon entering a new sub-sector.

## Essential Gameplay Tips for Success

To master the **プラグマタ 攻略** curve, you must look beyond basic combat. The game rewards players who explore thoroughly and use their gadgets creatively.

*   **Scan Frequently:** Scanning is not just for finding items; it reveals the path to your next objective and shows the health bars of enemies through walls.
*   **Upgrade Thrusters Early:** Mobility is your best defense. Investing Reinforcement Components into thruster capacity allows you to stay airborne longer, which is vital for the later platforming sections in the Terradome.
*   **Unlock Escape Hatches:** These act as fast-travel points and checkpoints. Never skip an Escape Hatch, as they allow you to return to the Shelter to upgrade your gear without losing your current mission progress.
*   **Manage Luna Filaments:** This universal material is used for crafting ammo and temporary tools. Don't hoard it; use it to create **Stycky Bombs** or **Decoys** during large group encounters.

## Hacking and Customization

Hacking is a core pillar of the gameplay. As you collect **Hacking Nodes** like "Multihack" or "Confuse," you can customize your approach to combat. For example, the "Confuse" node allows you to turn Walker units against each other, which is an effective **プラグマタ 攻略** tactic for handling the overwhelming numbers found in the Large-Scale Output Test Site.

| Module Name | Effect | Priority |

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find all the Earth Memory collectibles?**

A: Earth Memory items are usually hidden in "Hidden Rooms" that require hacking a specific wall panel or using a thruster to reach a high-altitude vent. Use your scan pulse near any suspicious-looking walls or dead ends to reveal these items.

**Q: What is the best weapon for the final boss?**

A: The **Photon Laser** is highly recommended for the final encounter due to its high precision and ability to pierce through filament armor. However, ensure you have a full stock of **Repair Cartridges** before starting the fight.

**Q: Can I return to previous areas to collect missed items?**

A: Yes, once you have unlocked the Escape Hatches in a specific zone, you can use the tram at the Shelter to return to any previous area. This is essential for completing your collection of **Mini Cabins** and **Reinforcement Components**.

**Q: What should I prioritize in my プラグマタ 攻略 strategy for early game?**

A: Focus on upgrading your **Cartridge Holder** and **Thruster Gauge**. Being able to carry more healing items and move more freely will make the initial difficulty curve of the Solar Power Plant much more manageable.
