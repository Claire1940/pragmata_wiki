## Overview of Pragmata Difficulty Settings

Pragmata offers a tiered approach to challenge, allowing players to tailor the experience to their preferred playstyle. Unlike traditional action games where "Hard" simply increases enemy damage, this title adjusts the complexity of the hacking minigames and the cooldown rates of Hugh’s space suit thrusters.

| Difficulty Level | Combat Scaling | Hacking Complexity | Resource Scarcity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Explorer** | Reduced enemy aggression; Hugh takes 50% less damage. | Simplified grids; longer timers for Diana’s hacks. | Abundant Luna filament and ammo drops. |
| **Standard** | Balanced AI; enemies utilize flanking maneuvers. | Default grid speed; requires precise timing. | Moderate supplies; requires occasional backtracking. |
| **Veteran** | High enemy resistance; bosses gain new attack patterns. | Complex, multi-layered grids; failure triggers alarms. | Rare materials; weapons break more frequently. |
| **Lunum Master** | One or two-hit kills; maximum enemy aggression. | Ultra-fast hacking loops with randomized patterns. | Minimal resources; requires perfect meter management. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Choosing the Lunum Master difficulty (unlocked after one completion) disables certain checkpoints, meaning you must rely heavily on the Tram system and manual saves at the Shelter.

## The Synergy of Combat and Difficulty

The core of Pragmata's challenge lies in the relationship between Hugh and Diana. Hugh's firearms are notoriously weak against the armored plating of Idas' robotic army. To succeed on higher **pragmata difficulty settings**, you must master the "Slowdown Effect." When Hugh successfully dodges an incoming strike, time slows down, providing Diana with a critical window to bypass enemy armor.

### Combat Mechanics Comparison

| Mechanic | Impact on Standard | Impact on Veteran/Master |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hacking Nodes** | Diana can hack from a safe distance. | Diana must be closer; hacking is interrupted by damage. |
| **Armor Systems** | Breaking armor leaves enemies stunned for 5 seconds. | Stun window is reduced to 2 seconds; armor regenerates. |
| **Finisher Moves** | Easily triggered after shooting weak points. | Requires specific elemental status effects to trigger. |
| **Thruster Cooldown** | Hovering and dashing have fast recharge rates. | Meter management is strict; overheating leaves you vulnerable. |

## Mastering the Hacking Minigame

Diana’s ability to "get under the skin" of the enemy is the only way to deal significant damage. On higher **pragmata difficulty settings**, the grid-based hacking loop becomes significantly more tactical. You aren't just matching patterns; you are fighting against the rogue AI's counter-measures.

1.  **Analyze the Grid:** Before initiating a hack, look for the "Blue Nodes" which grant temporary invulnerability to Hugh while Diana is occupied.
2.  **Synergize Shots:** While Diana is hacking, Hugh must fend off "rank and file" robots. On Veteran difficulty, these robots will prioritize attacking Diana, forcing you to use Hugh’s thrusters to draw aggro.
3.  **Perfect Timing:** Completing a hack perfectly (without a single missed input) grants a "Lunum Burst," which instantly reloads all of Hugh’s equipped weapons.

## Weapon Units and Maintenance

Hugh’s space suit features four dedicated units for weapon slotting. These are split between standard tactics, defense, and heavy attack functions. As you progress through the station, you will find that weapon durability becomes a major factor in the game's overall difficulty.

| Weapon Type | Durability | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kinetic Rifles** | High | Standard crowd control and weak point targeting. |
| **Filament Launchers** | Medium | Creating traps and slowing down fast-moving Idas units. |
| **Heavy Ordnance** | Low | High-damage bursts against bosses; breaks after 10-15 shots. |
| **Energy Shields** | Infinite (Cooldown) | Blocking projectiles while Diana performs long-range hacks. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep at least one slot open for "Scavenged Weapons." These are powerful tools dropped by elite enemies that can turn the tide of a difficult encounter but cannot be repaired at the Shelter.

## The Shelter: Upgrading to Mitigate Difficulty

The Shelter acts as your de facto headquarters on the moon. Here, you can use the 3D printers and Luna filament to upgrade your gear. If you find the current **pragmata difficulty settings** too punishing, investing time in exploration to find upgrade materials is your best path forward.

*   **3D Printing Upgrades:** Focus on "Node Speed" for Diana early on. Reducing the time she spends in the hacking loop is the single best way to survive boss fights.
*   **Cabin the Robot:** Interact with Cabin to unlock Shelter expansions. These expansions provide better fast-travel options via the Tram and more efficient material recycling.
*   **Maintenance:** Regularly repair your high-tier weapons. A broken weapon in the middle of a lunar quake can lead to a quick "Game Over."

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change the pragmata difficulty settings after starting the game?**

A: Yes, you can lower the difficulty at any time from the options menu. However, increasing the difficulty (e.g., from Standard to Veteran) can only be done at the Shelter and may reset your progress to the last escape hatch checkpoint.

**Q: Does difficulty affect the ending of the game?**

A: While the core narrative remains the same, completing the game on Veteran or Lunum Master difficulty unlocks exclusive cosmetic suits for Hugh and Diana, as well as "The Sketchbook" gallery which features early concept art.

**Q: Is there a "Permadeath" mode in Pragmata?**

A: There is no native permadeath mode at launch, but the Lunum Master setting significantly limits the number of escape hatches available, creating a high-stakes environment where death can result in losing hours of progress.

**Q: How does the "Slowdown Effect" work on different difficulties?**

A: On Explorer and Standard, the slowdown lasts long enough for most players to complete a hack comfortably. On Veteran, the window is much tighter, requiring players to memorize hacking patterns or upgrade Diana's processing speed at the Shelter.
