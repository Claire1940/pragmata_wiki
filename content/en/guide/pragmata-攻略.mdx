## Exploration and the Lunar Environment

The moon base in Pragmata is not an open world, but rather a series of interconnected, highly detailed zones. While it may be tempting to sprint toward the next objective marker, the game heavily rewards those who take the time to scour every corner. Many of the most powerful weapon mods and narrative beats are hidden away in optional corridors.

One of the most important tools at your disposal is Diana’s scanning ability. As you progress, you should prioritize upgrading her HUD functions. A fully upgraded scanner will highlight collectibles, resource nodes, and enemy positions through walls, making it much harder to miss vital items.

| Feature | Description | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Diana's Scanner** | Highlights items, enemies, and interactive objects in the environment. | **High** |
| **Red Challenge Rooms** | Combat trials that require specific keys to enter; rewards rare materials. | **Medium** |
| **Mini-A-Chans** | Small collectibles hidden throughout the moon base; essential for 100% completion. | **Low** |
| **Data Terminals** | Provide lore entries and background story on the lunar facility's collapse. | **Medium** |

> **💡 Tip:** To achieve the "Best Ending," you must collect nearly all items and complete the majority of optional encounters. Start your search early to avoid tedious backtracking later.

## Master the Combat: Hugh and Diana Synergy

The combat system in Pragmata is built on the philosophy of equality. While Hugh Williams handles the heavy ordinance and physical traversal, Diana provides the technological edge needed to disable advanced robotic threats. Attempting to play the game as a standard third-person shooter without utilizing Diana’s hacking will likely lead to frustration.

Hugh is equipped with a high-tech spacesuit featuring integrated thrusters. These thrusters are not just for reaching high platforms; they are vital for repositioning during combat. Since enemies can emerge from any direction—including behind you—staying mobile is the only way to avoid being stunned and overwhelmed.

### Combat Mechanics Overview

| Mechanic | How to Use | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Initial Hacking** | Use Diana to target an enemy before Hugh opens fire. | Disables shields or temporarily freezes the enemy. |
| **Thruster Dash** | Tap the dodge button while moving in any direction. | Rapid repositioning; can trigger a "Perfect Dodge" slow-mo. |
| **Perfect Dodge** | Time your dash just before an attack lands. | Triggers a brief window of slowed time for counterattacks. |
| **Weapon Swapping** | Switch between projectile and energy weapons mid-combo. | Maximizes damage against specific armor types. |

## The Shelter: Your Lunar Hub

The "Shelter" serves as the central hub where you can catch your breath between missions. It is more than just a menu for upgrades; it is the emotional heart of the story. You should make it a habit to return to the Shelter whenever you discover a fast-travel point.

Aside from purchasing upgrades, interacting with Diana in the Shelter triggers unique dialogue and cutscenes. These moments, such as the famous "water gun fight" where Hugh tries to bond with his young companion, provide the emotional weight that defines the Pragmata experience.

### Upgrade Currencies and Resources

Upgrading your gear requires specific currencies. You cannot simply farm one type of material to max out everything.

*   **Lim:** The standard currency used for purchasing new weapons, basic mods, and consumable items.
*   **Specialized Nodes:** Rare resources required to upgrade the fundamental tiers of Hugh’s suit, primary weapons, and Diana’s hacking capabilities.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not hoard your Specialized Nodes. The difficulty spikes in the mid-game are significant, and having a low-tier suit will make boss encounters much more punishing than necessary.

As you progress through the story, you will unlock dozens of weapons and hacking modules. The game encourages experimentation rather than sticking to a single "best" build. Some enemies are almost entirely immune to physical damage but melt under specific hacking protocols.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the best ending in Pragmata?**

A: To get the best ending, you must focus on exploration and relationship building. Ensure you collect all major items, complete the "Red Challenge Rooms," and view all unique interaction scenes with Diana in the Shelter.

**Q: What is the most important upgrade to get early in a Pragmata 攻略?**

A: Prioritize Diana's scanning range and Hugh's thruster recovery speed. Being able to see items through walls saves time, and faster thruster recovery is the best defensive tool you have during early boss fights.

**Q: Can I play the game without using the hacking mechanics?**

A: While you can technically fire weapons without hacking, it is not recommended. Many enemies have regenerative shields or high armor that can only be effectively bypassed by Diana's hacking abilities. The game is balanced around the two characters working in tandem.

**Q: What is the "Unknown Signal" mode?**

A: This is a post-game mode unlocked after the first completion. It features higher difficulty, remixed enemy placements, and additional narrative content that explains the origins of the lunar base collapse.
