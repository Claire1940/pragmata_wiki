## The Cabin Stamp Card System Explained

The primary method for obtaining new threads is the Cabin Stamp Card system. As you progress through the main story, you will level up your Shelter. Each level provides access to a new stamp card, with a total of five cards available. To earn stamps, you must spend Cabin Coins, which are primarily gathered by completing Training Sim missions.

While there are more coins available than there are slots on the cards, you should prioritize "Blacking Out" a card—filling every single slot—to receive the most prestigious rewards.

| Shelter Level | How to Unlock | Primary Reward Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Complete Sector 1 | Early Exolayer variants |
| **Level 2** | Complete Sector 2 | Type B tactical suits |
| **Level 3** | Complete Sector 3 | Retro and Stealth gear |
| **Level 4** | Complete Sector 4 | Late-game White/Lunar sets |
| **Level 5** | Clear First Unknown Signal | Shadow and "Blackout" exclusives |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't hoard your Cabin Coins. Training Sims are repeatable, so you can always farm more coins if you find yourself short a few stamps for a Blackout reward.

## Post-Game and Special Rewards

Once the credits roll, the **pragmata outfits guide** doesn't end. There are several high-tier sets that can only be obtained by engaging with the "Unknown Signal" missions. These are specialized post-game encounters that test your mastery of the game's mechanics.

To reach Shelter Level 5 and unlock the final stamp card, you must clear at least one Unknown Signal mission after beating the final boss. Clearing all of these missions will grant you the **Obsidia** (Hugh) and **Black Neon** (Diana) outfits. These are considered some of the most visually impressive sets in the game, featuring reactive textures that respond to the environment.

## Pre-Order and Bundle Exclusives

If you purchased the deluxe edition or pre-ordered the game, you may have access to the Samurai and Ninja inspired outfits. These are separate from the in-game progression system and are usually available in the Shelter's customization menu from the start of the game.

*   **Samurai Hugh:** Features traditional plating adapted for space travel.
*   **Ninja Diana:** A sleek, stealth-focused suit with traditional Japanese motifs.

Additionally, a special bundle was released alongside the game that includes two extra outfits for each character. While these do not offer gameplay advantages, they provide more variety for players who want to move away from the standard lunar-base aesthetic.

## How to Change Outfits

Changing your look in *Pragmata* is handled within the Shelter. Once you have unlocked an outfit via a stamp card or mission reward, follow these steps:

1.  Return to the **Shelter** (your hub world).
2.  Navigate to the **Customization Terminal** near the training sim entrance.
3.  Select the character you wish to edit (Hugh or Diana).
4.  Browse through the unlocked categories and confirm your selection.

Note that some outfits are full sets, while others may allow for minor adjustments to headgear. Experimenting with different combinations is key to finding your favorite look for the lunar journey.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there any outfits locked behind difficulty levels?**

A: Most outfits are tied to stamp cards and mission completion. However, clearing the game on Lunatic difficulty is rumored to have a hidden cosmetic reward, though most of the primary sets in this **Pragmata outfits guide** are obtainable on any difficulty.

**Q: Do outfits provide any stat bonuses or gameplay advantages?**

A: No, outfits in *Pragmata* are purely cosmetic. They do not change your health, defense, or attack power. Your combat effectiveness is determined by your equipment upgrades and skill tree progression rather than your choice of clothing.

**Q: Can I miss any outfits during a single playthrough?**

A: No outfits are permanently missable. You can continue to earn Cabin Coins and complete stamp cards in the post-game. Even the boss-related rewards can be obtained after the final encounter is completed.

**Q: How do I get more Cabin Coins quickly?**

A: The most efficient way to farm Cabin Coins is to repeat high-rank Training Sim missions. Focus on missions that you can clear quickly with an "S" rank to maximize your coin-per-hour intake.
