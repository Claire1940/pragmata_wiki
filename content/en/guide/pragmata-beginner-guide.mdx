## The Protagonist Duo: Hugh and Diana

Success in Pragmata relies entirely on how well you manage the synergy between Hugh Williams and his android companion, Diana. Hugh provides the firepower and physical presence, while Diana offers the technical expertise required to bypass advanced robotic defenses. You are essentially controlling both characters simultaneously during high-stakes encounters.

| Feature | Hugh Williams | Diana (Android) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Role** | Combat & Heavy Weapons | Hacking & Support |
| **Movement** | Thrusters & Ground Dash | Riding on Hugh's Back |
| **Key Mechanic** | Finishing Moves | Armor Disabling (Hacking) |
| **Weakness** | Low Tech Skills | Non-combatant |

Hugh’s gunplay is significantly hampered if you ignore Diana’s abilities. Most Idas enemies are shielded by heavy armor that renders standard bullets nearly useless. You must use Diana to initiate a grid-based hacking minigame to strip away these defenses before Hugh can deal meaningful damage.

> **💡 Tip:** While Hugh is dodging attacks, a brief slowdown effect is triggered. Use this window to give Diana more time to complete her hacking nodes without being interrupted by enemy fire.

## Combat Mechanics and Meter Management

Combat in Pragmata is a tactical "mishmash" that rewards patience over mindless shooting. Hugh’s space suit is equipped with a limited energy meter that governs his mobility. Managing this meter is the difference between a successful escape and a quick death on the lunar surface.

### Suit Mobility Functions
Hugh can utilize thrusters for two main purposes:
1.  **Hovering:** Allows you to cross large gaps or gain a vertical advantage over ground-based robots.
2.  **Dashing:** A quick burst of speed used to reposition or avoid incoming projectiles.

### Weapon Slot System
Your suit features four distinct units for weapon slotting. These are categorized by their tactical function. It is important to note that weapons in Pragmata are not permanent; stronger variants often have limited ammunition and a durability rating.

| Weapon Category | Function | Example Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard** | Balanced fire rate | General crowd control |
| **Defense** | Shielding/Repelling | Managing aggressive melee units |
| **Attack** | High damage output | Boss fights & armored cores |
| **Utility** | Environmental interaction | Solving puzzles or traversal |

If a weapon breaks during combat, that slot remains open until you find a replacement or return to the Shelter. Always keep an eye on your durability icons to avoid being left defenseless in the middle of a boss encounter.

## The Shelter: Upgrades and Maintenance

The Shelter serves as your primary base of operations within the lunar station. It is a safe zone where you can perform maintenance, interact with the helpful robot "Cabin," and upgrade your gear using Lunafilament technology.

Accessing the Shelter is done via **Escape Hatches** found throughout the levels. These hatches also serve as checkpoints and fast travel nodes for the "Tram" system.

### 3D Printing and Upgrades
The core of your progression lies in the 3D printers located in the Shelter. By collecting Lunum ore and other materials during exploration, you can:
*   **Enhance Hacking Nodes:** Make Diana’s hacking faster or more resistant to interference.
*   **Weapon Modification:** Increase the ammo capacity or durability of your slotted weapons.
*   **Suit Upgrades:** Improve the recharge rate of your thruster meter.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some high-tier 3D prints require rare materials found only in optional, high-difficulty areas of the station. Don't rush the main story if you want the best gear.

## Exploration and The Tram System

The lunar station is a massive, interconnected environment. As you progress, you will unlock new areas that were previously inaccessible. The **Tram** system allows you to fast-travel between any unlocked Escape Hatch and the Shelter.

### Tips for Efficient Exploration
*   **Look for Nooks and Crannies:** Materials for 3D printing are often hidden behind destructible environments or in vent shafts.
*   **Backtrack with New Abilities:** Like many classic action titles, obtaining a new suit function (like an upgraded hover) often allows you to reach secret items in earlier levels.
*   **Use the Map:** The station's layout can be "sterile" and confusing; consult your HUD map frequently to ensure you aren't running in circles.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a way to play Pragmata before buying?**

A: Yes, a playable demo titled "The Sketchbook" is available on PC, PS5, Switch 2, and Xbox Series X/S. It features a sampling of combat, exploration, and a boss fight, though the Shelter is excluded from the demo build.

**Q: Can I play Pragmata solo, or is it co-op only?**

A: Pragmata is a single-player experience where you control both Hugh and Diana. While they act as a duo, there is no traditional multiplayer co-op mode.

**Q: How do finishing moves work in combat?**

A: Finisher moves are unlocked by systematically attacking an enemy's weaknesses. Once an Idas robot is in a weakened state, a prompt will appear for Hugh to perform a high-damage strike that usually ends the encounter.

**Q: What is the release date for the full game?**

A: Following several delays, Pragmata is officially scheduled for release on April 17, 2026. This **pragmata beginner guide** is based on the final launch build and the "Sketchbook" demo.

**Q: Does the game support Ray Tracing on all consoles?**

A: Ray Tracing is supported on PC, PS5, PS5 Pro, and Xbox Series X. However, the developer has explicitly stated that Ray Tracing is not supported on the Xbox Series S due to hardware limitations.
