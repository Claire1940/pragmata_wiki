## Understanding the Pragmata Demo Carry Over System

Capcom's approach to the Pragmata trial is similar to their recent "Chainsaw" or "RPD" demos, but with a more robust focus on persistent progression. Because Pragmata relies on a loop of exploration, resource collection, and shelter-based upgrades, the developers have implemented a system that tracks your "Shelter Level" and "Diana's Bond" metrics.

When you transition from the demo to the full game, the system checks for a valid demo save file on your local storage or cloud profile. If detected, you will be prompted to import your data upon starting a New Game. This import process typically includes currency, basic crafting materials, and any lore logs you discovered while exploring the lunar recreation of Earth's cities.

## Core Gameplay Mechanics: Hacking and Combat

The gameplay in Pragmata is a departure from the high-speed action of *Devil May Cry* or the survival horror of *Resident Evil*. It is often described as a "thinking man’s shooter," where the environment is just as much a weapon as your rifle. The core loop involves managing Hugh’s combat capabilities while simultaneously utilizing Diana’s hacking abilities to manipulate the world around you.

Hacking isn't a separate mini-game; it is integrated into the flow of combat. While you are holding off mechanical threats, you must decide which environmental objects to hack to gain a tactical advantage. This might involve activating a floor trap, turning an enemy's drone against them, or opening a shortcut to escape a losing battle. The **pragmata demo carry over** rewards often include "Hacking Nodes" that allow you to start the main campaign with more efficient energy management.

### The Combat Juggling Act

Combat in Pragmata feels like a high-stakes puzzle. You aren't just aiming and firing; you are constantly assessing which threat is the most immediate. The game lacks traditional slow-motion or "bullet time" mechanics, forcing you to make split-second decisions in real-time. If you fall behind the rhythm of the encounter, the situation can spiral out of control, much like a game of Tetris where the blocks are falling too fast.

## Managing Resources and Powerful Weaponry

One of the most unique aspects of Pragmata is how it handles its arsenal. While Hugh has access to standard gear with unlimited (though rechargeable) ammunition, the most powerful weapons in the game are consumable. These "Heavy Ordinance" items are found in the field and have a very limited number of uses. Once they are empty, they are discarded.

This design choice forces you to be precious with your best options. You have to decide if a particular elite enemy is worth the use of a high-damage railgun or if you should save it for a potential boss encounter later in the mission. The **pragmata demo carry over** bonuses frequently include a starter pack of these heavy weapons, giving you a safety net for the full game's first few chapters.

## The Shelter: Upgrades and Character Bonds

Between missions, Hugh and Diana return to a safe zone known as the Shelter. This is the heart of the game's progression system. Here, you can spend the resources you've gathered to upgrade your gear, but more importantly, it is where the relationship between the two protagonists grows.

Diana is not just a gameplay tool; she is an evolving character. In the Shelter, you can interact with her, give her items you’ve found in the world, and watch as she learns more about her surroundings. This "Bond Level" is a critical component of the **pragmata demo carry over**. Players who reach the maximum bond level available in the demo will unlock unique dialogue sequences early in the full game that provide deeper insight into Diana’s origins.

### Progression Loop at the Shelter

1.  **Review Mission Data:** Analyze the performance of your last outing to see which hacking techniques were most effective.
2.  **Spend Resources:** Use lunar scrap to improve Hugh's suit durability or Diana's hacking speed.
3.  **Interact with Diana:** Engage in optional activities to increase the Bond Level.
4.  **Prepare Loadout:** Choose which limited-use heavy weapons to take into the next zone.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some high-tier upgrades seen in the Shelter menu during the demo are "locked for trial." Don't worry about grinding for these, as they will only become purchasable in the full version.

## Exploring the Artificial Moon City

The setting of Pragmata is one of its most haunting features. While it looks like a modern metropolitan city, there is an "uncanny valley" effect applied to the entire environment. Street signs might have gibberish text, or building layouts might not make logical sense for human habitation. This is because the city is a recreation, built for a purpose that remains a mystery throughout the early hours of the game.

As you explore, you will find "Memory Fragments" that detail the history of this lunar colony. Collecting these fragments in the demo is highly recommended, as they are part of the **pragmata demo carry over** data. Having these lore entries already unlocked in your codex allows you to piece together the narrative faster once the full game expands on these clues.

## FAQ

**Q: Does my progress in the Pragmata demo carry over to the full game automatically?**

A: Not entirely automatically. You must have the demo save file on your system when you launch the full game. Upon starting a new game, you will be prompted to "Import Demo Data." If you skip this prompt, you may have to start a new save file to access the carry-over benefits.

**Q: What are the exclusive rewards for completing the Pragmata demo?**

A: Players who complete the demo storyline receive the "Lunar Pioneer" cosmetic set for Diana and a "Veteran Explorer" badge for Hugh's suit. Additionally, you will start the full game with a small cache of rare crafting materials and one "Heavy Ordinance" weapon voucher.

**Q: Can I carry over my save data between different platforms (e.g., PS5 to PC)?**

A: Currently, **pragmata demo carry over** is restricted to the same platform family. Save data from the PlayStation 5 demo will only transfer to the PlayStation 5 full version. Cross-platform progression has not been confirmed for the demo phase.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many items I can carry over?**

A: Yes, there is a cap on currency and materials to prevent players from being too overpowered at the start of the full game. Any credits earned beyond the 50,000 mark in the demo will not be transferred. Standard materials are capped at 99 units each.
