## Main Story Clear Time and Expectations

For the average player focusing primarily on the narrative, the estimated **プラグマタ プレイ時間** for the main story falls between **10 and 13 hours**. This range is intentionally designed by Capcom’s Development Division 1 to mirror the "dense" pacing found in recent *Resident Evil* titles. Rather than offering a bloated open world, the game provides a curated, high-intensity experience where every hour is packed with cinematic set pieces and evolving combat encounters.

Director Zhao has stated that while 10 hours is the baseline, players who take their time to explore the environment and engage with the optional "Read Earth Memory" collectibles will likely trend toward the 13-hour mark. The dual-protagonist system, featuring Hue’s shooting and Diana’s hacking, adds a layer of tactical depth that may extend play time for those who enjoy experimenting with different combat strategies.

| Playstyle | Estimated Time | Focus Area |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Clear** | 10–13 Hours | Main story, minimal side exploration |
| **Explorative Clear** | 15–18 Hours | Main story + collecting Earth Memories |
| **Speedrun** | 5–7 Hours | Skipping cutscenes and optimized routing |

> **💡 Tip:** Your first playthrough will likely be slower as you adapt to the hacking puzzles. These puzzles are designed to become "second nature" over time, much like learning to drive a car, which significantly speeds up subsequent runs.

## Completionist Run and Trophy Volume

If you are aiming for the Platinum trophy or 1,000 Achievement points, the **プラグマタ プレイ時間** increases to approximately **30 hours**. This is because the game is built with a "New Game Plus" philosophy in mind. To unlock the most prestigious trophies, you must complete the game at least twice.

A significant hurdle for completionists is the **Lunatic Difficulty**, which only becomes available after clearing the game once. This mode drastically alters enemy placements and increases the complexity of hacking nodes, requiring a deep understanding of the game's mechanics. Additionally, hunting for all "Mini Cabins" and "Read Earth Memory" logs scattered across the lunar surface requires meticulous exploration of every sector.

| Activity | Time Investment | Requirement |

| **Second Playthrough** | 8–10 Hours | Required for **Lunatic Difficulty** |

## Factors Influencing Your Play Time

Several gameplay systems directly impact how long you will spend on the moon. Since the game blends third-person shooting with puzzle-based hacking, your proficiency in these areas dictates your pace.

### 1. Hacking Skill Progression
Early in the game, hacking can feel like a bottleneck. You must manually navigate nodes while under pressure. However, as you upgrade your modules in the Shelter, you can eventually clear entire groups of enemies with a single hack. Players who invest heavily in hacking upgrades will find the latter half of the game much faster to navigate.

### 2. Difficulty Selection
Choosing the "Easy" or "Normal" path allows for a more relaxed pace. However, the game's DNA is rooted in the action-heavy traditions of Capcom. Playing on higher difficulties forces you to engage with the environment more cautiously, naturally extending the **プラグマタ プレイ時間**.

### 3. Exploration and Lore
The world of the moon is filled with "IDUS" creations—distorted, biological-mechanical hybrids. Players who stop to examine the environment, such as the eerie "failed prints" of Earthly objects like taxis and buses, will find a much richer (and longer) experience.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Skipping the optional "Shelter" upgrades can make the final bosses significantly harder, potentially leading to repeated deaths that inflate your total play time.
