Surviving the desolate, AI-overrun lunar landscapes of Capcom’s latest sci-fi epic requires more than just quick reflexes; it demands a deep understanding of the character progression systems. This **pragmata skill tree guide** is designed to help you navigate the complex relationship between Hugh’s engineering prowess and Diana’s advanced hacking capabilities. ## The Cradle: Centralized Progression and Upgrades

In Pragmata, all character growth is centered around a hub known as "The Cradle" (or the Shelter). This safe haven serves as your base of operations where Hugh and Diana can rest, interact, and most importantly, access the upgrade terminal. Progression is not just about increasing damage numbers; it involves unlocking utility skills that change how you interact with the environment and enemies.

The upgrade system is divided into several categories, focusing on Hugh’s physical combat capabilities, his weapon mod slots, and Diana’s hacking efficiency. To unlock these enhancements, you must collect materials and data fragments found during missions or rewarded after intense boss encounters.

| Upgrade Category | Focus Area | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Combat Mobility** | Hugh’s Movement | Unlocks dodges and time-slowing counters. |
| **System Engineering** | Weapon Efficiency | Increases mod slots and ammo replenishment rates. |
| **Hacking Protocols** | Diana’s Abilities | Improves the hacking grid and Overdrive gauge. |
| **Survival Logistics** | Health & Recovery | Enhances the effectiveness of healing items. |

## Essential Hugh Upgrades for Combat Survival

Hugh is an engineer, and his skill tree reflects a practical approach to combat. Unlike traditional shooters where you might focus solely on weapon damage, this **pragmata skill tree guide** recommends prioritizing mobility and utility first. Because ammo is scarce and weapons often break after their magazines are depleted, Hugh’s inherent abilities are your only constant.

### The Last-Second Dodge
One of the most critical upgrades available early in the game is the time-slowing dodge. When timed correctly, this ability allows Hugh to evade incoming projectiles or melee strikes while briefly slowing down the world around him. This provides a vital window to either reposition or line up a precise shot on an enemy's exposed core.

### Mod Slot Expansion
Hugh’s weapons can be customized with various mods that add elemental effects or statistical boosts. Initially, your access to these slots is limited. Investing in the "Mod Slot Expansion" nodes early on allows you to stack multiple benefits on your primary pistol, making it a reliable backup when your heavier, more specialized weapons—like the Shockwave Gun or Stasis Net—inevitably break.

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep at least one mod slot dedicated to "Ammo Efficiency" to reduce the frequency of weapon breakage during long skirmishes.

## Diana’s Hacking and Overdrive Progression

Diana is far more than an escort character; she is the key to defeating armored enemies. Her "Hacking Mini-game" involves navigating a digital maze on a graph grid. Upgrading her side of the skill tree changes how this grid functions and how much support she can provide during the heat of battle.

### Hacking Grid Optimization
When Diana initiates a hack, you are presented with a grid of nodes. By default, you simply need to reach the green exit node. However, as you progress through the **pragmata skill tree guide** recommendations, you can unlock the ability to see and interact with "Bonus Nodes" on this grid.

| Node Color | Effect | Upgrade Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue Nodes** | Basic pathing nodes for connection. | None (Base Ability) |
| **Yellow Nodes** | Lowers enemy defense significantly. | Tier 1 Hacking Upgrade |
| **Red Nodes** | Hazard nodes that reset the hack. | Avoidance (Skill-based) |
| **Green Nodes** | Completion point for the hack. | None (Base Ability) |

### Overdrive Protocol
The Overdrive Protocol is Diana’s "ultimate" ability. As she successfully hacks enemies, a gauge fills up. Once full, players can trigger a massive area-of-effect pulse (R3 + L3) that stuns and exposes the weak points of every enemy in the vicinity. Upgrading this gauge in the skill tree increases its duration and reduces the amount of "hacking data" required to charge it.

## Weapon Scarcity and Strategy

A unique mechanic in Pragmata is that specialized weapons like the Shockwave Gun are disposable. Once the ammo is gone, the weapon breaks, and you must find a new one on the battlefield. This makes the "Primary Weapon" (Hugh's pistol) upgrades even more important, as it is the only tool that recharges over time.

According to recent [hands-on previews from GameSpot](https://www.gamespot.com/articles/pragmata-hands-on-preview-more-than-a-throwback-shooter/1100-6538761/), managing this scarcity is the core of the gameplay loop. You should use Diana to hack and "open up" enemies before wasting your limited specialized ammo.

### Recommended Early Game Build Order

For players just starting their journey in 2026, we suggest the following priority for your first few visits to the Cradle:

1.  **Tactical Dodge (Hugh):** Essential for avoiding high-damage AI strikes.
2.  **Hacking Tier 1 (Diana):** Unlocks yellow nodes to weaken armored foes.
3.  **Healing Efficacy (Hugh):** Makes your limited healing items go further.
4.  **Overdrive Charge Rate (Diana):** Allows for more frequent crowd control.
5.  **Pistol Recharge Speed (Hugh):** Minimizes downtime when you are out of heavy weapons.

## Advanced Tactics: Synergizing Hugh and Diana

The true power of the **pragmata skill tree guide** lies in synergy. For instance, if you upgrade Diana's ability to pick up "Bonus Nodes" on the floor, those nodes will then appear in the hacking mini-game. If you pick up a yellow square in the physical world, passing through it on the digital grid during a hack will massively debuff the enemy's armor.

> **Warning:** Be careful when navigating the hacking grid. If Diana’s aim is knocked off the target by an enemy attack, the connection is lost. Prioritize "Stability" upgrades to help Diana maintain her lock-on even under pressure.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I earn points for the Pragmata skill tree?**

A: Skill points and upgrade materials are primarily earned by defeating hostile AI, completing story objectives, and finding hidden data caches within the lunar environments. Successfully completing difficult hacking mazes with Diana also provides additional data for her specific upgrades.

**Q: Can I respec my skills if I make a mistake?**

A: While the current 2026 build allows for significant customization, respec options are limited to specific intervals in the story. It is generally better to plan your build early, focusing on mobility and hacking efficiency before branching into niche weapon mods.

**Q: Does Diana have her own separate health bar to upgrade?**

A: Diana functions more as an extension of Hugh’s systems. While she can be "downed" or incapacitated, requiring Hugh to protect or "fix" her, her durability is largely tied to Hugh’s defensive upgrades and her own "Shielding Protocol" skills found in the Hacking branch of the tree.

**Q: What is the most important skill for boss fights?**

A: The "Overdrive Protocol" duration upgrade is arguably the most important for bosses. Bosses often have multiple layers of armor that can only be bypassed through Diana’s hacking. Being able to stun a boss and expose all its weak points simultaneously provides the only real window for high-damage weapons to be effective.
