## Unlocking New Game Plus in Pragmata

To access the New Game Plus (NG+) mode, you must first complete the main story campaign. The journey of Hugh and Diana concludes with a high-stakes encounter that tests everything you have learned about the hacking grid and suit modes. Once the credits roll, the game will prompt you to create a "Clear Save" file.

Loading this save file allows you to begin a new journey from the beginning of the story while retaining a significant portion of your progress. This is particularly useful because the initial playthrough often leaves players short on the Lunar Filament required to max out every weapon and node.

### What Carries Over to NG+?

One of the biggest draws of replaying the game is the power fantasy of taking endgame gear into early-level encounters. While the story resets, your technical progression remains largely intact.

| Category | Carries Over? | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Weapons** | Yes | All unlocked primary and secondary weapons remain available. |
| **Hacking Nodes** | Yes | Multihack, Confuse, and Freeze nodes are retained. |
| **Suit Modes** | Yes | Access to Heat and Fence modes from the start. |
| **Lunar Filament** | Yes | Your current currency balance stays with you. |
| **Upgrades** | Yes | Any stat increases for the Grip Gun or suit are permanent. |
| **Collectibles** | Yes | Earth memory fragments and pictures from Diana are saved. |

> **💡 Tip:** Even though your weapons carry over, ammo is still limited. Focus on upgrading your "Grip Gun" early in NG+ to ensure you have a reliable, high-damage fallback that doesn't rely on rare drops.

## New Game Plus Difficulty and Rewards

When you start a New Game Plus run, you aren't just playing the same game again. Capcom has introduced several layers of challenge to keep the experience fresh. Upon completion of the first run, you unlock higher difficulty settings that are specifically tuned for a fully-geared Hugh.

### Higher Difficulty Modes

In 2026, the community has noted that the third level of Pragmata serves as a major "skill check." In NG+, this ramp-up happens even sooner. Enemies have significantly higher health pools, and the AI system, Edis, deploys more aggressive robot variants in earlier chapters.

1. **Enhanced Enemy AI:** Robots will attempt to flank Hugh more frequently, forcing you to use the Stasis Gun or Decoy more tactically.
2. **Reduced Hacking Windows:** The time available to navigate the hacking grid is shortened, making the "Auto-Hack" node almost essential for crowded encounters.
3. **Exclusive Rewards:** Certain high-tier mods and outfits can only be obtained by completing challenges on these higher difficulties.

## Mastering Combat in Subsequent Runs

Replaying the game allows you to experiment with combat combinations that were too expensive or complex during the first run. Since you likely have more nodes unlocked, you can lean into specific playstyles.

### Suit Mode Synergies

The two primary suit modes—**Heat** and **Fence**—drastically change how you interact with Diana during combat. In NG+, mastering the transition between these modes is key to survival.

| Mode | Primary Benefit | Best Used With... |
|---|---|---|
| **Heat Mode** | Causes robots to stall and overheat. | **Critical Shot** move for massive health depletion. |
| **Fence Mode** | Amplifies Diana's hacking damage. | **Multihack** to clear groups of smaller drones. |

**Heat Mode** is generally considered the superior choice for boss fights in 2026. The ability to force a stall allows you to bypass the "spongy" nature of high-difficulty enemies. By using the Critical Shot, you can often finish off a boss's final health bar in one clean animation, saving you from the tedious run-back to the shelter if you were to fail.

## The Shelter and Side Content Replayability

The Shelter acts as your hub, similar to the bonfire mechanic found in the Souls series. In New Game Plus, the Shelter becomes even more important as you work through the "Bingo" mechanic and training levels.

### The Bingo System

The Bingo mechanic is a unique progression system where you spend Cabin Coins to unlock mods, packs, and cosmetic outfits. Because many of these unlocks are randomized, it is almost impossible to complete the entire board in a single playthrough. 

*   **Cabin Coins:** These are found primarily in side missions and hidden stashes.
*   **Grid Completion:** Making straight lines across the grid unlocks major rewards.
*   **NG+ Advantage:** You can carry over your coins, allowing you to "blitz" the board early in your second run.

### Red Zones and Training Levels

For those who find the main story too easy with endgame gear, the **Red Zones** offer the ultimate test. These are combat arenas that unlock as you progress through the world. In NG+, these zones feature "Remix" encounters, where enemy types from the end of the game appear in early-game environments.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Red Zones in New Game Plus do not feature checkpoints. If you die, you will be sent back to the Shelter, requiring a tram ride back to the combat area. Always ensure your health is above 500 before entering these zones.

## Exploration and Hidden Secrets

One of the most rewarding aspects of replaying Pragmata is using late-game abilities to access areas that were previously blocked. The lunar station is filled with holograms and constructs created from lunar filament.

With a fully upgraded hacking suite, you can open doors in the New York City simulation and the Forest areas that contain rare Earth memory fragments. These fragments provide more backstory on Diana and the fall of the station, adding much-needed context to the "tonal whiplash" some players feel during the initial story run.

According to the [Official Pragmata Site](https://www.capcom.com), the game's RE Engine allows for highly detailed environments, and replaying the game with features like **Path Tracing** and **DLSS 4** enabled on the latest hardware makes the second journey even more visually stunning.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pragmata have New Game Plus available at launch?**

A: Yes, New Game Plus is available to all players immediately after completing the final story mission. You must load your "Clear Save" to start the new cycle.

**Q: Do I lose my weapons if I start a new game?**

A: If you choose "New Game" from the main menu, you will start fresh. However, if you choose the New Game Plus option via your Clear Save, all weapons and suit upgrades will carry over.

**Q: Can I change the difficulty in New Game Plus?**

A: Yes. Upon starting NG+, you can choose from the standard difficulties or the newly unlocked higher difficulty modes designed for veteran players.

**Q: Is there any content exclusive to New Game Plus?**

A: While the story remains the same, certain high-end weapon mods, cosmetic outfits from the Bingo board, and "Remix" encounters in Red Zones are much easier to access or exclusively balanced for NG+.

**Q: How many times can I play through New Game Plus?**

A: You can continue into NG++, NG+++, and so on. Each cycle allows you to further refine your gear and collect any missing Earth memory fragments.
