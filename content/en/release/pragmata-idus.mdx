## Understanding the IDUS AI and the EDI System

In the world of *Pragmata*, IDUS is not just a boss you fight at the end of a level; it is an omnipresent threat that governs the entire lunar facility. The AI utilizes the EDI (Electronic Defense Interface) system to narrate your progress and, more importantly, to initialize "Sector Guard Protocols" whenever you are detected as an "unregistered guest."

Combat in *Pragmata* is unique because it is inextricably linked to the station's power grid. IDUS thrives when the station is in lockdown, limiting your offensive capabilities and forcing you into a defensive, stealth-oriented playstyle. As you work with Diana to restore primary power, the dynamic of the fight shifts from survival to aggressive counter-attacks.

> **💡 Tip:** Listen closely to the EDI system's verbal cues. When the AI announces "unauthorized access detected," it is a direct warning that elite units are about to be deployed to your exact coordinates.

## Core Mechanics: Energy Thresholds

Your combat efficiency is directly tied to the percentage of energy restored in any given sector. When **pragmata idus** has full control (0-30% energy), Hugh's firearms are less effective, and his jetpack recharge rate is significantly slowed. Reconnecting power nodes is the only way to level the playing field.

| Energy Level | Status Effect | Tactical Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0% - 30%** | **Lockdown Active** | Prioritize stealth; use Diana to scout power nodes. |
| **31% - 60%** | **Attack Warning** | EDI deploys Sector Guards; find cover and hold your ground. |
| **61% - 90%** | **Power Reconnecting** | Offensive buffs active; use Hugh’s firearms to clear waves. |
| **100%** | **Lockdown Disengaged** | Area secured; health and ammo fully restored. |

## Advanced Combat: The Overdrive System

To combat the overwhelming numbers of the Sector Guard, players must utilize the Overdrive system. This mechanic represents a temporary surge in the station's energy that Hugh can harness through his suit. Charging the Overdrive meter requires successful parries and maintaining high-energy combat flow.

The most powerful tool in your arsenal is the "Overdrive Phone Call," a unique ability that triggers a heightened state of combat. During this window, Hugh gains increased damage resistance and access to specialized "Heat" based attacks that can penetrate the heavy armor of IDUS-controlled robots.

### Overdrive Ability Breakdown

| Ability | Effect | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overdrive Dash** | Instant teleportation behind enemies | Closing the gap on long-range Missile Batteries. |
| **Heat Blast** | Large AOE energy explosion | Clearing swarms of Scout Drones in tight corridors. |
| **Primary Sever** | Massive single-target strike | Neutralizing Elite Protectors before they reset power. |

> **Warning:** Activating Overdrive while the sector energy is below 40% can lead to a "brownout" state. Once the ability ends, Hugh will be immobile for several seconds, leaving him vulnerable to follow-up attacks.

## Enemy Types and Counter-Strategies

The forces of **pragmata idus** are diverse, ranging from small, agile drones to massive, stationary artillery units. Each unit follows a strict logic pattern that can be exploited once understood.

### Sector Guard Variations

1.  **Scout Drones**: These are the eyes of IDUS. They do little damage but will alert the EDI system to your location, triggering immediate reinforcements. Destroy these first to maintain the element of surprise.
2.  **Missile Batteries**: These units provide heavy fire support. They are armored against standard ballistic fire, but they have a critical weakness: their cooling vents. Wait for the "Heat" signature to appear after a volley, then strike the exposed vents for massive damage.
3.  **Elite Protectors**: These are the most dangerous standard enemies. Their primary goal is to "sever primary connections," which resets your power restoration progress. They move slowly but possess high health and can only be effectively damaged after Diana hacks their defense systems.

| Enemy Unit | Primary Attack | Weakness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scout Drone** | Scanning/Alerting | **Low health**; vulnerable to rapid-fire weapons. |
| **Missile Battery** | Multi-missile volley | **Overheating**; vents are exposed during reload. |
| **Elite Protector** | Connection Severing | **Slow movement**; highly vulnerable to Overdrive. |

## Protagonist Synergy: Hugh and Diana

The core of *Pragmata*'s gameplay is the relationship between Hugh and Diana. While Hugh handles the physical combat with firearms and his jetpack, Diana is the only one capable of bypassing the digital defenses of **pragmata idus**. 

During combat, you can control both characters simultaneously. When facing armored robots, Hugh's bullets will simply bounce off. You must position Diana to initiate a hack. This triggers a real-time puzzle where you guide a cursor across a grid to reach a target tile.

While Diana is hacking, Hugh must act as her bodyguard. The puzzles are not paused; the world continues to move around you. Hugh must dodge incoming projectiles and parry melee attacks to ensure Diana isn't interrupted. If Diana completes the hack, the robot's armor is disabled, revealing a glowing weak point that Hugh can then destroy.

## Final Combat Tips for Success

*   **Manage Your Heat**: Both Hugh and his enemies generate heat. If Hugh overheats, he will be stunned. Use the environment to vent heat whenever possible.
*   **Prioritize Power**: It is tempting to try and kill every robot in a sector, but your primary goal should always be the power nodes. Higher energy levels make every fight significantly easier.
*   **Break Line of Sight**: When the red scan lines of the EDI system appear on Hugh, it means a missile lock is imminent. Quickly use your jetpack to get behind cover to break the lock.
*   **Collect Energy Drops**: Fallen enemies drop energy shards. These are essential for restoring health and charging your Overdrive meter mid-fight.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I stop the AI from severing the primary connection in Pragmata Idus?**

A: When you see the "unauthorized access" warning, look for Elite Protectors moving toward the power terminals you just activated. You must neutralize these targets immediately. Using a "Primary Sever" Overdrive attack is the fastest way to stop them before they can reset your progress.

**Q: What is the best way to handle the 60% attack warning?**

A: At 60% power, the EDI system becomes much more aggressive. The best strategy is to stop moving toward the next node and find a defensible position. Clear the incoming wave of Sector Guards first, then proceed once the "Attack Warning" status clears.

**Q: Can I play as Diana during combat?**

A: Yes, the game allows for dual control. While Hugh is the primary combatant, you must actively move Diana to hacking points and complete her grid-based puzzles in real-time to disable enemy defenses.

**Q: Is there a way to restore health without finishing the sector?**

A: Yes. While reaching 100% power restoration provides a full heal, you can also restore smaller amounts of health by collecting energy drops from destroyed Scout Drones and Missile Batteries. Additionally, Diana can occasionally find health kits in optional bonus nodes during her hacking sequences.
