## The Cabin Stamp Card System Explained

The primary method for unlocking new gear in Pragmata is through the Cabin Stamp Card system. As you progress through the five major sectors of the game, you will periodically level up your shelter. Each level granted to your shelter unlocks a new stamp card, for a total of five cards.

To fill these cards, you must spend Cabin Coins. These coins are earned by completing missions, with the Training Sim missions being the most lucrative source. It is important to note that you will typically earn more coins than there are slots on a single card, so you should have plenty of currency as long as you engage with the side content.

There are two ways to earn rewards from a stamp card:
1.  **Direct Stamp Rewards:** Some outfits are located on specific squares. Once you stamp that square, the outfit is yours.
2.  **Blackout Rewards:** These are the ultimate prizes for each card. To earn a Blackout reward, you must fill every single square on the card, achieving a full "Bingo" across the entire grid.

> **💡 Tip:** Focus on clearing Training Sim missions early. The extra Cabin Coins will allow you to "Blackout" the first three stamp cards before you even reach the mid-game boss encounters.

## Post-Game and Secret Outfits

Once the credits roll in Pragmata, the hunt for **pragmata all outfits** isn't over. Several of the most visually striking suits are locked behind post-game content.

### The Final Boss Rewards
Simply completing the main story will grant you two "gaudy" but fun outfits. Hugh receives the **Astroclass Type B** (a bright red variant), and Diana receives the **Pink Pop** clothes. These are automatically added to your inventory once you return to the shelter after the final encounter.

### Unknown Signal Missions
After the game ends, you will gain access to "Unknown Signal" missions. These are high-difficulty encounters that test your mastery of the game's mechanics.
*   **Shelter Level 5:** To reach the final shelter level and unlock the fifth stamp card, you must clear at least one Unknown Signal mission.
*   **Obsidia & Black Neon:** These suits are the ultimate rewards for completing the post-game mission chain. They feature "extra" details and glowing elements that stand out in the dark environments of the moon.

> **Warning:** The Shadow Walker and Shadow Executor outfits found on the fifth stamp card have a divisive "creepy" aesthetic. Some players find them a bit unsettling compared to the sleek sci-fi designs of the earlier tiers.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more Cabin Coins for the stamp cards?**

A: The most efficient way to farm Cabin Coins is by replaying Training Sim missions. These missions are designed to be shorter than main story sectors and provide a consistent payout of coins upon completion.

**Q: Do outfits provide any gameplay stats?**

A: No, the outfits in Pragmata are purely cosmetic. They do not change your defense, speed, or attack power. This allows you to choose your favorite look without worrying about "meta" builds for your gear.

**Q: Can I change outfits at any time?**

A: You can change your outfits at the wardrobe located inside your Shelter. You cannot change your clothes while out on a mission in the lunar sectors, so make sure you pick your favorite "fit" before heading out.

**Q: I finished a stamp card but didn't get the Blackout reward. Why?**

A: To get the Blackout reward, every single node on the card must be filled. Check the corners and the center carefully; sometimes a single stamp is missed, preventing the "Bingo" from completing the entire card. To get **pragmata all outfits**, you must be meticulous with your coin spending.
