## Analyzing the "Incurable" Infection

A major point of discussion within the community regarding the **abandonment ending pragmata** is the nature of Hugh’s infection. Throughout the game, it is described as a "heavy bugger" that "eats all his life." Lore entries found in the final labs suggest that this was a failsafe designed to prevent lunar residents from returning to Earth if they were compromised by the rogue AI systems.

Hugh’s decision to stay behind isn't just a matter of shuttle capacity; it is an act of "cleansing." If he were to accompany Diana, he risked bringing the infection to the last habitable zones of Earth. His death is a narrative necessity to ensure that Diana’s "new beginning" is untainted by the failures of the lunar colony.

## The Earth Arrival: Diana’s New World

The transition from the cold, sterile moon to the vibrant Earth beach is visually stunning, showcasing the power of the RE Engine in 2026. Diana lands safely, fulfilling Hugh’s wish for her to "find the sea." 

The presence of seagulls and crabs confirms that Earth is not the desolate wasteland some players feared. However, the ending remains bittersweet because Diana is now alone. The realistic hair physics and environmental lighting in this scene underscore her isolation—a small child in a massive, beautiful world she doesn't fully understand.
