## Essential Settings for Trophy Hunters

Before diving into your first run, adjusting your settings can significantly reduce the friction of combat and platforming. Pragmata follows the tradition of many Capcom titles where default camera speeds may feel sluggish, especially during intense boss encounters.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Camera Speed** | Full | Essential for tracking fast-moving Watchers. |
| **Aiming Camera Speed** | 60 | Prevents over-aiming when targeting weak points. |
| **Deceleration** | 0 | Provides consistent aiming speed for better muscle memory. |
| **Adaptive Triggers** | Off | Reduces finger fatigue during long play sessions. |
| **Motion Blur** | Off | Enhances visibility of small collectibles like Mini Cabins. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep the "Center Reticle" enabled. This helps with the quick-hacking mini-game, allowing you to hit the blue nodes more accurately.

## Traversal and Hacking Mechanics

Progressing toward the **platine pragmata** requires mastering the jetpack stamina system. Unlike standard action games, jumping in Pragmata involves a fuel-based "bum bag" jetpack. To cover the largest gaps—especially those leading to rare Upgrade Components—you must follow a specific input sequence: **Jump, then Dash, then Glide.** Gliding before dashing often leaves you with insufficient stamina to complete the maneuver, leading to a long trek back up through vertical environments.

Hacking is the core of the combat loop. When you encounter enemies like Walkers or Watchers, initiating a hack reveals their blue inner core.
1.  **Expose the Core:** Aim and initiate the hack through the blue nodes.
2.  **Increase Damage:** Once the core is blue, enemies take significantly more damage.
3.  **Weapon Cycling:** Use the Grip Gun (infinite ammo) while your heavy weapons like the Shotgun reload.

## The Shelter: Affinity and the Bingo System

The Shelter acts as your hub where you interact with Diana. Raising her affinity is non-negotiable for the **platine pragmata**. You must exhaust her dialogue every time you return from a mission. Look for the "musical note" icon, which signifies an increase in her bond level.

### Diana’s Affinity Gifts
*   **REM Globe:** Found after the early-game laboratory cutscene.
*   **Crayons:** Hidden behind a "Hollow Wall" in the Main Control Lobby.
*   **Gifts Reward:** Giving these items often yields Cabin Coins and unique trophies like "For Me?".

### The Bingo Game
The Cabin Guy in the Shelter offers a Bingo mini-game using Cabin Coins. For an optimal 100% run, focus on the **vertical middle line** first. This path provides the Red Gate Key, which is essential for accessing late-game secret areas containing high-level Upgrade Components.

| Bingo Stamp | Reward | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Top Middle** | Crusher Bot Data | Essential for the Bestiary trophy. |
| **Center** | Walker Bot Data | Standard enemy data. |
| **Bottom Middle** | **Red Gate Key** | Priority #1 for exploration. |

## Boss Strategy: Solar Power Plant Guardian

The first major hurdle on your path to the **platine pragmata** is the Power Plant boss. This encounter introduces the "Overdrive" mechanic and "Critical Attacks."

*   **Dashing Pattern:** The boss will always dash twice. Wait for the second dash to conclude before attempting to counter.
*   **Stasis Net:** Fire a Stasis Net to lock the boss in place. 
*   **Weak Point:** The orange tank on the boss's back is the primary target. Use your Shotgun at point-blank range for massive damage.
*   **Overdrive:** Once the gauge is full, press **L3 + R3** to trigger Diana’s support attack, which staggers the boss and opens it up for a finish.

> **Warning:** On Lunatic difficulty, the boss's dash recovery is much faster. You must be frame-perfect with your Stasis Net or you will be overwhelmed.

## Weapon Upgrades and Loadouts

To keep the Lunatic run manageable, you must fully upgrade your gear during your Casual playthrough. The Primary Unit gun should be prioritized to Level 6 for reduced recoil and increased ammo capacity.

1.  **Primary Unit:** High priority for consistent DPS.
2.  **Shotgun:** Essential for "Crusher" type enemies.
3.  **Stasis Net:** The best utility tool for crowd control.
4.  **Harden Suit Mod:** Always equip this for a 10% HP boost, which can prevent one-shot deaths on higher difficulties.

As you progress toward the **platine pragmata**, remember to manual save at the Shelter. The game does not allow saving during active missions, and a single death can reset significant collectible progress if an Escape Hatch hasn't been activated recently.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a map in Pragmata to help find collectibles?**

A: Surprisingly, Pragmata does not feature a traditional mini-map. You must rely on visual landmarks and the audio cues from Mini Cabins to navigate. Using the "Scanner" upgrade later in the game will help highlight nearby items through walls.

**Q: How many playthroughs are required for the platine pragmata?**

A: A minimum of two playthroughs is required. You should use the first run on Casual to collect 100% of the items and max out Diana's affinity. The second run on Lunatic difficulty focuses purely on survival and completion.

**Q: What happens if I miss a Mini Cabin in an earlier area?**

A: You can use the Tram to fast-travel back to previous areas like the Solar Power Plant. However, some areas may become locked due to story progression, so it is safer to collect them as you go using a checklist.

**Q: Does difficulty affect the "Bingo" rewards?**

A: No, the rewards for the Bingo board are fixed. However, having the extra health cartridges from the Bingo lines makes the Lunatic difficulty much more forgiving.
