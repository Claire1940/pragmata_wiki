## The Perk System: Core Gameplay Modifications

In Pragmata, perks act as the primary "mods" for your character's performance. These are equippable slots that grant passive bonuses to your damage, defense, and utility. Unlike traditional RPGs where stats are permanent, Pragmata allows you to swap these perks before every run, giving you the flexibility to adapt to specific mission requirements.

The most effective builds currently revolve around "Slowdown" and "Aerial" combat. By utilizing perfect dodges, you trigger a time-dilation effect that provides a massive window for critical strikes. Equipping mods that synergize with this state can increase your DPS by over 50%.

| Perk Name | Effect Description | Damage Bonus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Eagle Eye** | Increases all damage dealt while the player is in the air. | **+15%** |
| **Close Quarters** | Grants a damage bonus when attacking enemies at short range. | **+10%** |
| **Untapped Potential** | Temporary damage boost triggered immediately after a successful hack. | **+10% (15s)** |
| **Precision Shot** | Increases damage specifically during the Slowdown effect from dodges. | **+10%** |
| **Last Resort** | Massive damage boost to the final bullet in a non-primary weapon. | **+50%** |

> **💡 Expert Tip:** The **Eagle Eye** perk is arguably the most valuable mod in the early game. Since jumping and shooting is a core defensive mechanic, you will almost always benefit from this 15% boost.

## Hacking Nodes: Tactical Combat Mods

Hacking is the backbone of Pragmata’s combat loop. Through Diana, your companion, you can interact with various nodes on the battlefield that function as real-time combat modifications. These nodes are categorized by color, each serving a distinct tactical purpose. Mastering the "Multihack" combo is essential for late-game survival when the enemy density ramps up significantly.

### Node Types and Functions

1.  **Yellow Nodes (Tactical):** These are limited resources that provide crowd control. They can freeze enemies in place, lower their defensive stats, or even confuse them into fighting for you.
2.  **Blue Nodes (Diana Upgrades):** These enhance Diana’s hacking speed and damage. Unlike yellow nodes, these can be spammed more frequently.
3.  **Purple Nodes (Critical Shots):** These appear when an enemy's health drops below 30%. They allow for a stagger effect that opens the enemy up for a "Heat Gauge" execution.

| Node Combo | Required Nodes | Combat Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Freezer Burn** | Freeze + Defense Down | Enemies are locked in place with zero damage resistance. |
| **The Crowd Killer** | Multihack + Freeze + Defense | Applies status effects to every enemy in the vicinity. |
| **The Infinite Loop** | Blue Nodes + Diana Spam | Constant chip damage without consuming ammo. |

## Weapon Modifications and Build Synergy

Weaponry in Pragmata follows a "use and discard" mechanic for secondary firearms. While your primary rifle stays with you, high-tier weapons like the **Charge Piercer** or the Shotgun are found in the field or purchased at the shelter. To truly optimize these, you must align your **mods pragmata** perks with the weapon's unique traits.

For instance, the **Last Resort** perk is devastating when paired with the Charge Piercer. The default damage of a Charge Piercer is 1677, but with the Last Resort mod, the final shot in the chamber jumps to 2377. If you stack this with a perfect dodge (Precision Shot) and an aerial jump (Eagle Eye), the damage can exceed 2500 in a single hit.

### Recommended Loadouts

*   **The Sniper Build:** Focus on Eagle Eye and Precision Shot. Use the Charge Piercer to delete high-priority targets from a distance during slowdown.
*   **The Hacking Specialist:** Prioritize Untapped Potential and Multihack nodes. Use Diana to clear rooms while saving your ammo for bosses.
*   **The Tank/Brawler:** Use Close Quarters and the Stasis Net. Lock enemies in place and use the Heat Gauge execution to finish them off.

> **Warning:** Do not hoard your yellow nodes. While they are limited, using them against mid-tier "Elite" enemies will save you more health and ammo in the long run than saving them for a boss you might not reach.

## Advanced Combat Mechanics: Heat and Mobility

Beyond the standard **mods pragmata** offers, players must master the "Heat Gauge." This is the stagger meter located directly under an enemy's health bar. Once filled, you can perform a teleporting melee execution by pressing R2. This deals massive damage and can often one-shot standard enemies.

### Mobility and Energy Management
Staying mobile is non-negotiable. However, jumping and boosting consume energy.
*   **Boost Dashing:** It is often more energy-efficient to boost dash forward off a ledge than to perform a full jump.
*   **Animation Canceling:** The healing animation in Pragmata is notoriously slow. You can cancel the tail end of the animation by dashing or jumping the moment you see your health bar increase on the UI.
*   **Free Fire:** In tight corridors, avoid aiming down sights (L2). Tapping the shoot button allows for "Free Fire," which keeps your movement speed high and allows for quicker dodging.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the best mods Pragmata offers for early game?**

A: The best early-game modifications are purchased at the Shelter for 100 currency. Focus on the Eagle Eye perk and the Multihack node first, as these provide the most significant boost to your combat efficiency.

**Q: Does the "Last Resort" perk work on my primary rifle?**

A: No, Last Resort is specifically designed for non-primary weapons that are discarded after their ammo is depleted, such as shotguns and the Charge Piercer.

**Q: How do I increase the damage of Diana's hacking?**

A: You can increase hacking damage by pathing through Blue Nodes on the hacking board. Additionally, upgrading Diana at the shelter will provide permanent boosts to her hacking speed and damage output.

**Q: Can I use community mods with Pragmata?**

A: While internal gameplay "mods" are a core feature, external software modifications depend on the platform. PC players often have access to visual and performance mods via community sites, but these are not officially supported by Capcom and may interfere with online features.
