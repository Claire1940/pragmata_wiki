## Understanding the Hacking & Combat Loop

Unlike traditional shooters where raw firepower dictates success, this title requires a "dual-task" approach. To deal significant damage to enemies, Hugh must protect Diana while she initiates a hacking sequence. This manifests as a mini-game on the right side of your HUD, where you must navigate a grid using the PlayStation face buttons (Square, Triangle, Circle, and X). 

Passing through blue "gate" icons while heading toward the green standby symbol increases your damage multiplier exponentially. Failing to engage with this mechanic results in "chip damage," making even basic encounters feel like slogs. Mastering this system is the primary requirement for most combat-related **pragmata trofeos**.

| Mechanic | Input Method | Effect on Gameplay |
|---|---|---|
| **Grid Navigation** | Face Buttons (Shapes) | Moves the cursor through hacking nodes. |
| **G-Code Nodes** | Specific Grid Paths | Lowers enemy defense and reveals weak spots. |
| **Stasis Fire** | R2 (Alternate Fire) | Freezes enemies to allow safer hacking windows. |
| **Melee Takedown** | R2 (Close Range) | High-damage finisher after a successful hack. |

## Essential Trophy Categories

The **pragmata trofeos** list is generally divided into four main pillars: Story Progression, Combat Mastery, Hacking Proficiency, and Exploration. Because the game utilizes the RE Engine, the environmental detail is immense, and many hidden trophies are tucked away in the lunar landscape.

### Story & Progression Trophies
These are unmissable as long as you complete the main campaign. They track Hugh and Diana’s journey from their first meeting on the space station to their eventual confrontation with the corporate entities overseeing the moon project.

### Combat Mastery
These trophies reward players for using specific weapon types, such as the Stasis Gun or the heavy assault rifle. One of the most coveted **pragmata trofeos** involves defeating a boss by only using the "Overhead Smash" finisher after a successful hack.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't ignore the "weighty" feel of Hugh's armor. While he can sprint, his momentum makes quick turns difficult. Use the jet propulsion thrusters sparingly to dodge missiles rather than for general travel.

## Detailed Hacking Strategy for Platinum

To secure the hacking-related **pragmata trofeos**, you must become proficient at "patting your head and rubbing your stomach." You are effectively playing a rhythm/puzzle game while simultaneously dodging projectiles in a third-person shooter environment.

1.  **Prioritize Nodes:** Always aim for the blue icons in the grid. These provide the "G-code" necessary to strip enemy shields.
2.  **Avoid Cornering:** It is very easy to "bodge" the buttons and trap your cursor in a corner of the hacking grid. If this happens, the hack fails, and the enemy will likely bulldoze you.
3.  **Upgrade Diana:** As you progress, Diana gains new hacking nodes. Invest your resources into expanding her grid size to unlock more powerful combat abilities.

| Ability Level | Grid Complexity | Max Damage Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | 3x3 Grid | 2.5x Damage |
| **Level 2** | 5x5 Grid | 5.0x Damage |
| **Level 3** | 7x7 Grid | 10.0x Damage + Armor Pierce |

## Boss Battle Guide: The Mobile Tank (209-Type)

One of the early-game hurdles for the **pragmata trofeos** is the encounter with the 209-style mobile tank. This enemy is equipped with missile pods and rocket launchers, making it a lethal threat at long range.

To defeat it and earn the associated trophy, you must wait for it to fire its missiles. During the reload animation, Diana can initiate a hack. You must navigate the grid quickly to open the cooling vent on the tank's back. Once the vent is open, Hugh can use his jet suit to fly over the boss and perform a melee finisher for massive damage (often exceeding 45,000 points).

## Exploration & Collectibles

The moon isn't just a backdrop; it’s a character in itself. To find all the hidden **pragmata trofeos**, you will need to search for "Memory Fragments" left behind by previous inhabitants of the space station.

*   **Lunar Vistas:** Find all 10 locations where Earth is visible in the skybox.
*   **Tech Logs:** Collect 50 logs detailing the development of the "Diana" units.
*   **Weapon Blueprints:** Locate hidden crates to unlock the high-tier G-code launchers.

> **Warning:** Some areas become inaccessible after certain story beats. It is highly recommended to keep a manual save at the start of each lunar sector to avoid missing exploration trophies.

## Trophy Roadmap & Efficiency Tips

If you are aiming for the Platinum in a single playthrough, focus on the following:

*   **Difficulty:** Start on "Hard" if you are a veteran of Capcom games like *Lost Planet* or *Resident Evil*. This usually unlocks the lower-difficulty trophies simultaneously.
*   **Hacking Accuracy:** Try to maintain a 90% success rate in hacking mini-games during the first five hours. This will build the muscle memory needed for the late-game 7x7 grids.
*   **Diana's Safety:** Several **pragmata trofeos** are tied to Diana taking zero damage during specific boss fights. Use the Stasis Gun to keep enemies away from her at all times.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I unlock all pragmata trofeos in one playthrough?**

A: Yes, it is possible to earn the Platinum in a single run if you play on the highest available difficulty and use a guide for the Memory Fragment collectibles. However, a second "New Game Plus" run is recommended to fully upgrade Hugh's armor.

**Q: What happens if Diana is captured during a fight?**

A: If Diana is captured, you lose the ability to hack, reducing your damage to "chip" levels. You must immediately prioritize rescuing her to resume the hacking mini-game and progress the fight. There is a specific trophy for rescuing her within 5 seconds of capture.

**Q: Is there a trophy for the hacking mini-game speed?**

A: Yes, the "Digital Speedster" trophy requires you to complete a 5x5 hacking grid in under three seconds during an active boss battle. This is best attempted after unlocking the Level 2 grid upgrades.

**Q: Does the game have online multiplayer trophies?**

A: As of the 2026 launch, the **pragmata trofeos** are strictly single-player. There are no forced online components or co-op requirements for the Platinum, making it a great choice for solo trophy hunters.
