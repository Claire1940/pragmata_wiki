## The Cabin Stamp Card System

The primary method for unlocking cosmetics in Pragmata is through the Cabin Stamp Card system. As you progress through the five main sectors of the game, you will periodically level up your Shelter. Each level granted to your Shelter unlocks a new Stamp Card, for a total of five cards. 

To fill these cards, you must earn Cabin Coins. These are primarily acquired by completing Training Sim missions, which test your combat and traversal skills. Once you have enough coins, you can "stamp" slots on the active card. Some outfits are rewarded for simply landing on a specific slot, while others—often the most coveted ones—require a "Blackout," which means filling every single slot on that specific card.

| Shelter Level | Unlock Requirement | Stamp Card Rewards |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Complete Sector 1 | Hugh's Exolayer, Diana's Lunar Aqua |
| **Level 2** | Complete Sector 2 | Hugh's Exolayer Type B, Legion Zero |
| **Level 3** | Complete Sector 3 | Astroclassic, Lunar Black, Legion Zero Type B |
| **Level 4** | Complete Sector 4 | Diana's Lunar White |
| **Level 5** | Complete Game + 1 Unknown Signal | Shadow Walker, Shadow Executor, Heartbeat, Star Lily |

> **💡 Tip:** You will generally earn more Cabin Coins than there are slots to fill if you consistently run Training Sim missions. Don't be afraid to spend them early to get a head start on your collection.

## Diana’s Unlockable Outfits

Diana’s outfits often reflect her mysterious nature and her connection to the lunar environment. Unlike Hugh's heavy armor, her skins focus on lighter fabrics, specialized jackets, and high-tech environmental suits.

### Progression-Based Skins

Diana starts with the **Blue Cradle Jacket**, which serves as her iconic look throughout the early game. As you progress through the Stamp Cards, her outfits move toward the "Lunar" series. The **Lunar White** outfit is a particular fan favorite, often described as having "Spy Moon" vibes due to its sleek profile and clean aesthetic.

Similar to Hugh, Diana receives the **Pop Clothes** (a cutesy pink ensemble) after the credits roll. > **Warning:** Some outfits, like the Star Lily, change Diana's character model slightly (such as her hair length). Make sure you preview these in the Shelter before committing your coins if you prefer her default appearance.

## How to Efficiently Farm Cabin Coins

To unlock everything on the Stamp Cards, you will need a significant amount of currency. The most efficient way to farm these is through the **Training Sim missions** located in the Shelter.

1.  **Prioritize Gold Medals:** Higher ranks in simulations yield significantly more coins.
2.  **Repeat Sector Bosses:** Replaying boss encounters through the mission select screen provides a large lump sum of coins upon completion.
3.  **Explore Every Nook:** While the game is mostly linear, small side paths in the lunar sectors often contain hidden coin caches.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I miss any of the pragmata all skins in a single playthrough?**

A: No, none of the skins are permanently missable. You can always use the mission select feature or continue into the post-game to finish your Stamp Cards and unlock any remaining outfits.

**Q: Do the outfits provide any gameplay bonuses or stat increases?**

A: In Pragmata, skins are purely cosmetic. They do not increase your defense, speed, or attack power. This allows you to choose your favorite look without worrying about being underpowered in combat.

**Q: Is there a reward for clearing the game on Lunatic difficulty?**

**Q: How do I change my character's skin?**
