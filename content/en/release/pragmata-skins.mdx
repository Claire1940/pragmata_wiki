## Deluxe Edition Breakdown: The Shelter Variety Pack

For players who want the maximum amount of **pragmata skins** at launch, the Deluxe Edition is the primary option. The Deluxe Edition includes the following cosmetic and presentation-based items:

*   **Additional Character Outfits:** A secondary set of skins for both Hugh and Diana, providing a different aesthetic from their default space-faring gear.
*   **Grip Gun Weapon Skin:** A unique visual overhaul for Hugh’s primary utility tool, the Grip Gun.
*   **Shelter Background Music (BGM):** Alternate tracks that can be played while resting or upgrading gear in the Shelter.
*   **Diana Gestures:** A set of exclusive emotes and gestures for Diana, adding more personality to her AI interactions.

## Understanding the Role of Skins in Gameplay

It is vital to reiterate that **pragmata skins** do not alter the "systems-driven" nature of the game. Pragmata’s gameplay revolves around environmental puzzles, physics-based interactions, and a complex combat system involving Hugh’s mechanical suit and Diana’s unique abilities. 

Choosing a different skin for the Grip Gun, for example, will change the color and texture of the device but will not increase its grappling range or projectile strength. This design philosophy ensures that the "Standard Edition" player has the exact same mechanical experience as the "Deluxe Edition" player. 

> **💡 Tip:** If you are on the fence about the Deluxe Edition, you can safely purchase the Standard Edition first. Capcom typically offers a "Deluxe Upgrade" DLC on the [Official Steam Store](https://store.steampowered.com/) or console shops after launch, allowing you to get the skins later if you decide you love the game.

## Customization in the Shelter

The Shelter serves as the player's base of operations. This is where the majority of the **pragmata skins** and cosmetic changes are managed. Within the Shelter, players can access the customization terminal to:

1.  **Swap Outfits:** Change Hugh and Diana’s appearance using any unlocked DLC or earned in-game cosmetics.
2.  **Modify Equipment:** Apply weapon skins like the Grip Gun variant.
3.  **Manage Gestures:** Assign Diana’s emotes to specific interaction triggers.
4.  **Listen to BGM:** Use the music player to change the atmosphere of the hub world.

The interaction between Hugh and Diana is the emotional core of the game. Seeing them in different outfits during cutscenes and environmental exploration adds a layer of personal investment to their journey from Earth to the Moon.

## FAQ

**Q: Do any pragmata skins provide gameplay advantages?**

A: No. Capcom has confirmed that all skins, including the Neo Bushido and the Shelter Variety Pack items, are purely cosmetic and do not affect progression, combat, or difficulty.

**Q: Can I unlock skins just by playing the game?**

A: While the pre-order and Deluxe skins are paid extras, most Capcom titles include unlockable internal cosmetics earned through completing challenges, finding collectibles, or finishing the game on higher difficulties. However, the specific "Neo" and "Shelter" sets are currently exclusive to their respective editions.

**Q: If I miss the pre-order window, can I still get the Neo Bushido skin?**

**Q: Is there a weapon skin for anything other than the Grip Gun?**

A: Currently, the only confirmed weapon skin is for the Grip Gun included in the Deluxe Edition's Shelter Variety Pack. Other weapon customizations may be revealed closer to the launch date or as post-launch content.
