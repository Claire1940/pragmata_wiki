## Gameplay Mechanics: Combat and Hacking Synergy

At its core, the game is a third-person action-adventure that feels like a modern evolution of the "video game ass video game" style popularized during the seventh console generation. You play as Hugh, a "chunky" spaceman technician whose movement carries weight and momentum. While Hugh handles the heavy lifting and traditional firearms, the gameplay truly shines through the synergy with Diana, who rides on your back throughout the journey.

Combat is a strategic dance of shooting and hacking. Most enemies on the moon are protected by advanced shielding that Hugh’s standard weapons cannot penetrate effectively. This is where Diana becomes essential. While aiming, you can trigger a hacking mini-game that allows Diana to lower enemy defenses or expose their internal chassis.

## Exploration and the Lunar Base

While the game follows a linear path, it encourages exploration through hidden nooks and crannies. These areas often contain resources needed to upgrade Hugh’s EVA suit or Diana’s processing power. One of the most charming aspects of the game is the base of operations. Initially a sterile, cold laboratory, the base evolves as you bring back items from Earth to teach Diana about human culture.

Over time, you transform the space into a "kid’s playroom" filled with toys, books, and Earth memorabilia. This progression isn't just cosmetic; it unlocks new training simulations that function similarly to VR missions. These challenges test your mastery of the combat system and offer high-tier rewards for those who can 100% them.

> **Tip:** Don't rush through the gleaming sci-fi hallways. Check behind crates and inside glowing laboratories for "Bingo Coins" which can be traded for rare cosmetic skins and weapon upgrades.

## Progression and the "Bingo" System

One of the more quirky elements of the game is its progression system, which many have likened to a "battle pass" but with a distinct single-player twist. You collect coins throughout the world and cash them in on a digital bingo card. Completing rows and columns unlocks everything from new armor colors for Hugh to more complex hacking modes for Diana.

This system encourages players to engage with the optional challenge rooms. These rooms are significantly tougher than the main story encounters and often require a perfect understanding of the "perfect dodge" mechanic, which slows down time and allows for a devastating counter-attack.
