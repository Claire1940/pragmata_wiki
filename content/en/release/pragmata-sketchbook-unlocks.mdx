## The Secret Elevator Encounter

The core of the current mystery revolves around the elevator sequence, where a trio of robotic sentries attempts to halt your progress. While most players will clear this room using their most powerful weapon, doing so actually locks you out of the secret content. To trigger the hidden boss—which is a prerequisite for one of the most important **pragmata sketchbook unlocks**—you must demonstrate mastery over your entire arsenal.

The game tracks the "finishing blow" delivered to each of the three initial bots. If you use the same weapon to kill all three, the sequence ends normally. However, if you diversify your killing blows, the game detects your tactical proficiency and spawns a hidden, high-tier enemy once the elevator reaches its destination.

### Weapon Finishing Requirements

To successfully trigger the secret enemy spawn, you must adhere to the "Rule of Three." Each bot must be destroyed by a different damage source. It is important to note that you can weaken the enemies with any weapon you choose; the game only checks the final hit that reduces their health to zero.

| Enemy Target | Recommended Finishing Weapon | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bot A (First)** | **Pistol / Standard Fire** | Easiest to control for the first kill. |

| **Bot C (Third)** | **Hack / Multi-Hack Finish** | Using the hacking mechanic as a finisher is highly reliable. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be extremely careful when using area-of-effect (AoE) weapons. If a single explosion or shockwave kills two bots simultaneously, it counts as the same weapon source for both, and the secret unlock will fail.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Unlocking Diana's Second Drawing

Unlocking the hidden boss is directly tied to Diana’s second drawing. In the world of Pragmata, Diana’s sketches represent her perception of the threats you face together. Without encountering the secret elevator enemy, she never gains the "inspiration" to draw it, leaving a permanent gap in your Sketchbook.

Follow these steps during the elevator sequence to ensure success:

1.  **Isolate the Targets:** As the three bots drop onto the elevator, move to one corner to draw their aggro.
2.  **Softening Up:** Use your primary rifle to bring all three bots down to approximately 10% health. Do not over-commit to your combos.
3.  **The First Kill:** Switch to your sidearm (Pistol) and deliver a single precise shot to the first bot.
4.  **The Second Kill:** Switch to the Shockwave Gun. Wait for the second bot to move away from the third, then fire a blast to finish it off.
5.  **The Final Kill:** Approach the last bot and initiate a Hack. Completing the hack sequence should destroy the bot, satisfying the third unique weapon requirement.
6.  **The Reveal:** Once the elevator stops, instead of the usual exit, an "Intruders Detected" warning will flash red, and the hidden boss will phase into the arena.

## Combat Strategies for Perfectionists

When attempting **pragmata sketchbook unlocks**, your biggest enemy is often your own damage output. As you upgrade your gear in the demo, it becomes increasingly easy to accidentally "one-shot" the bots on the elevator, making it difficult to swap weapons in time.

### Managing Damage Thresholds

To avoid accidental kills, consider un-equipping high-damage mods before the elevator fight. You want the bots to be "spongy" enough that you can carefully tick their health down. If you find yourself struggling with the timing, try the following:

*   **Pistol Tapping:** Use the pistol for the majority of the fight. It has the lowest DPS but the highest precision.
*   **Visual Cues:** Watch for the bots to start sparking or smoking. This is the universal sign in Pragmata that an enemy is below 15% health.
*   **The Hack Indicator:** Once a bot is low enough, a prompt for a "Finisher Hack" will appear. This is your cue that the bot is ready to be harvested for the unlock.

> **💡 Tip:** If you mess up the rotation and kill two bots with the same weapon, you can quickly restart from the last checkpoint. The checkpoint triggers just before you step onto the elevator, allowing for rapid retries.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the "Multi-Hack" count as one weapon or many for pragmata sketchbook unlocks?**

A: The Multi-Hack is considered a single damage source. If you use a Multi-Hack to kill two bots at the same time, you will fail the requirement for the unique weapon finish. However, if you use it to kill just one bot, it counts as a unique "Hack" finish.

**Q: Can I use the Shockwave Gun to damage the bots as long as I don't kill them with it?**

A: Yes. The game only tracks the "Killing Blow." You can use any combination of weapons to lower an enemy's health. As long as the final hit that destroys the bot comes from a different weapon than the other two kills, the secret will unlock.

**Q: What happens if I unlock the hidden boss but die during the fight?**

A: If you die after the hidden boss has spawned, you usually have to redo the elevator sequence to trigger the spawn again. The Sketchbook entry for the boss only saves once the enemy is defeated and Diana completes her drawing animation.

**Q: Is there a specific order the weapons must be used in?**

A: No, the order does not matter. You can finish with the Hack first, the Pistol second, and the Shockwave Gun third, or any other variation. The only strict rule is that all three finishing blows must originate from different weapon types.
