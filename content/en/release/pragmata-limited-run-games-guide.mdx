## Mastering the Hacking Matrix

One of the most unique—and polarizing—aspects of gameplay is the real-time hacking system. Unlike traditional shooters where you simply aim and fire, many enemies in the lunar facility possess regenerative armor that can only be bypassed through a hacking sequence. This requires you to interact with a grid-based matrix while actively dodging incoming fire.

To successfully hack a target, you must navigate your cursor to the green "EXE" node. Each face button on your controller corresponds to a specific direction within the matrix.

### Hacking Matrix Controls

| Button Input | Matrix Direction | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Circle / B** | Right | Move cursor right in the grid |
| **Square / X** | Left | Move cursor left in the grid |
| **Triangle / Y** | Up | Move cursor upward |
| **Cross / A** | Down | Move cursor downward |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Hacking does not pause the game. You must maintain spatial awareness of the physical battlefield while looking at the hacking UI on the right side of your screen.

## Combat Strategy and Weaponry

Combat in this sci-fi epic is deliberate and tactical. You are encouraged to dismantle enemies piece by piece rather than relying on "spray and pray" tactics. Ammo is often scarce, forcing you to utilize specialized weaponry found throughout the environment.

### Primary Weapon Comparison

| Weapon Name | Effective Range | Ammo Type | Special Perk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trip Gun** | Medium | Kinetic (Self-Replenishing) | High accuracy for weak points |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Close | Single-Use Rounds | Massive stagger and area denial |
| **Luna Filament** | Long | Mineral-Based | Pierces energy shields |

The **Shockwave Gun** is particularly effective for frontal suppression. However, it is a "disposable" weapon; once you exhaust its four rounds, the protagonist will discard the unit. You must scavenge for new heavy weapons constantly to maintain your offensive momentum.

## The Role of Diana and Overdrive Protocol

Your companion, Diana, is not just a narrative focus; she is a core gameplay mechanic. As a synthetic being, she can interface with the facility's mainframe to provide tactical advantages. By successfully completing hacking sequences, you build up a gauge that allows for the activation of the **Overdrive Protocol**.

When Overdrive is initiated, Diana emits a massive pulse that:
1.  **Immobilizes** all nearby robotic threats.
2.  **Exposes** hidden weak points on boss-level enemies.
3.  **Deals** significant electrical damage over time.

Strategic use of the Overdrive Protocol is often the only way to survive the "Red Alert" ambushes that occur when exploring deeper levels of the lunar base.

## Lunar Exploration and Traversal

The Moon's low-gravity environment dictates how you move and fight. Verticality is a major component of the level design, with floating debris and multi-tiered research labs providing numerous tactical vantage points.

*   **Low-Gravity Jumps:** Holding the jump button allows for a slow, controlled ascent.
*   **Thruster Management:** Your suit's thrusters have a limited gauge. Use them for mid-air adjustments rather than constant flight.
*   **Escape Hatches:** These serve as your "bonfires" or checkpoints. Resting here replenishes your basic health and saves your progress, but may respawn local AI patrols.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a way to automate the hacking system?**

A: Currently, there is no "Auto-Hack" feature in the standard difficulty. Players must manually complete the grid puzzles to maximize damage. However, some accessibility settings may allow for simplified inputs in future updates.

**Q: Does the game feature fall damage on the Moon?**

A: Due to the low-gravity environment, fall damage is minimal. However, falling from extreme heights without using your thrusters to "brake" before landing can result in a brief stagger, leaving you vulnerable to enemies.

**Q: Can I play the entire game solo?**

A: Yes, the game is designed as a single-player experience where you control the protagonist and Diana acts as an AI-controlled support character. There are no mandatory co-op sections.

**Q: Where can I find the Shockwave Gun?**

A: Shockwave Guns are typically found in "Emergency Lockers" near boss arenas or high-density combat zones. They are highlighted by blue holographic markers on your map.
