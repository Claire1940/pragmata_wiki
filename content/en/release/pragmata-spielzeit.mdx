## Factors Influencing Your Total Play Time

Several unique gameplay loops can extend your **pragmata spielzeit** beyond the initial story. Understanding these mechanics will help you gauge how much time you'll actually spend on your first playthrough.

### The Hacking System and Grid Mini-games
Unlike traditional shooters, combat in Pragmata requires you to manage Diana’s hacking abilities while Hugh handles the weaponry. You are essentially solving grid-based puzzles during active combat. As the game progresses, these grids become more complex, requiring more attention and strategy. Mastering this system early can speed up boss fights, whereas ignoring it will make enemies feel like "bullet sponges," drastically increasing your time spent in encounters.

### The Shelter Hub and Backtracking
The Shelter acts as your central hub for fast travel, healing, and upgrades. A significant portion of the **pragmata spielzeit** is spent here preparing for the next run. However, the game utilizes a death mechanic similar to "soulslikes," where dying often sends you back to the Shelter. This can lead to some repetition as you backtrack through previously cleared areas, adding to the total hour count.

### Customization and RPG Depth
The game features a deep RPG system for both characters. You will spend time managing:
*   **Weapon Mods:** Choosing between DPS (Red) and Tactical (Green) weapons.
*   **Diana’s Nodes:** Upgrading her ability to hack multiple enemies or stun bosses.
*   **Outfits:** Collecting and equipping cosmetic items for both Hugh and Diana.

| Weapon Category | Function | Upgrade Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Red Weapons (DPS)** | Primary damage output (Pistol, Shotgun, Sniper). | High - Essential for boss phases. |
| **Green Weapons (Tactical)** | Status effects and crowd control. | Medium - Best for large groups of robots. |
| **Gadgets** | Utility items like decoys and traps. | Low - Situational but helpful for stealth. |

## Endgame Content and Replayability

Once the credits roll, the **pragmata spielzeit** doesn't necessarily end. Capcom has included a robust post-game suite designed to keep players engaged. 

1.  **New Game Plus:** Carry over your upgrades and weapons to face tougher enemy configurations.

3.  **NPC Interactions:** Use the currencies found in the world to unlock secret lore and character interactions at the Shelter.

The endgame content is highly rewarding, as every collected item contributes directly to the progression system. This avoids the "busy work" feel common in other titles, making the push toward the 25-hour mark feel earned and satisfying.
