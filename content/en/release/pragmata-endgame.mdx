## Understanding the Pragmata Endgame Loop

The core of the late-game experience revolves around the Shelter—a safe haven that functions similarly to a "bonfire" where you manage your resources. As you progress into the **pragmata endgame**, your focus shifts from mere survival to efficiency. You will find yourself constantly hunting for Lunar Filament to fund expensive gear upgrades and searching for Cabin Coins to fuel the Bingo system.

Combat in this stage is no longer about basic shooting. Enemies become "spongy," requiring you to utilize Diana’s hacking abilities to even stand a chance. The loop involves venturing out into the station, clearing high-intensity zones, and returning to the Shelter to refine your build.

### Core Progression Systems

| System | Primary Purpose | Key Resource |
|---|---|---|
| **The Shelter** | Saving progress and upgrading core gear stats. | Lunar Filament |
| **Bingo Board** | Unlocking new mods, equipment packs, and outfits. | Cabin Coins |
| **Training Levels** | Skill-based challenges that reward rare resources. | Mastery Points |
| **Red Zones** | High-difficulty combat arenas for elite gear. | Elite Filament |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not hoard your Cabin Coins. The Bingo board is your primary source of late-game mods that can drastically reduce reload times for heavy weapons like the Piercer.

## Advanced Combat: Suit Modes and Hacking

To excel in the **pragmata endgame**, you must move beyond basic hacking. The game introduces Suit Modes that activate once an enemy's weak spot is exposed. These modes transform your combat style and are essential for taking down the humongous robot bosses that dominate the later levels.

### Suit Mode Comparison

*   **Fence Mode:** This mode focuses on amplifying the damage dealt by Diana's hacks. It is ideal for players who prefer a "summoner" style of play, letting the android do the heavy lifting while you focus on evasion.
*   **Heat Mode:** Widely considered the superior choice for high-level play, Heat Mode causes robots to overheat and stall. This ties directly into the **Critical Shot** mechanic, allowing you to execute a massive damage move that can one-shot many elite enemies regardless of their remaining health.

| Mode | Playstyle | Best Used Against |
|---|---|---|
| **Fence** | Tactical/Defensive | Swarms of smaller automated units |
| **Heat** | Aggressive/DPS | High-health bosses and elite sentries |
| **Auto-Hack** | Convenience | Fast-moving targets that are hard to track |

## Mastering the Weapon Loadout

Ammo and items are strictly limited in Pragmata, meaning your choice of secondary weapons can make or break a run. In the **pragmata endgame**, you should categorize your weapons into "Damage" and "Tactics."

1.  **The Piercer (Damage):** A railgun-style weapon that fires a slow-moving, high-damage projectile. It is essential for boss fights but requires precise timing due to its abysmal reload speed.
2.  **The Stasis Gun (Tactics):** Perhaps the most valuable tool in your arsenal. It locks enemies in place, giving you the breathing room needed to complete complex hacking grids without being interrupted.
3.  **The Decoy (Defense):** Drops a hologram that draws all enemy aggro. This is a "get out of jail free" card when you are cornered by multiple robots.

> **Warning:** Reload times are punishingly slow. A common pro-tip for the endgame is to fire one weapon, swap to another, and swap back to effectively "cancel" or manage the downtime during intense encounters.

## Post-Game Challenges: Red Zones and Training

Once the credits roll, the real test begins. The **pragmata endgame** offers two distinct types of challenge content: Red Zones and Training Levels.

### Red Zones
Red Zones are tougher combat arenas scattered throughout the lunar station. These are not for the faint of heart; they feature reused enemies with significantly increased health pools and new, aggressive attack patterns. Completing these is the only way to unlock the most powerful mods in the game.

### Training Levels
These are optional stages with strict completion requirements (e.g., "Finish under 60 seconds" or "Take zero damage"). While some are straightforward, many require you to memorize enemy spawn patterns. They are less about spontaneous skill and more about perfect execution and memorization.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I die in the Pragmata endgame?**

A: The game uses a "bonfire" style system. You will respawn at the last Shelter you visited. Note that you will need to re-select your loadout and travel back to the area where you died, which can add significant downtime.

**Q: Is there a New Game Plus mode?**

A: Yes, completing the main story unlocks New Game Plus along with higher difficulty tiers. This is where your **pragmata endgame** build truly matters, as enemies in higher difficulties can kill Hugh in just one or two hits.

**Q: How do I get more Cabin Coins for the Bingo board?**

A: Cabin Coins are primarily found by exploring off-the-beaten-path areas and completing Training Levels with high rankings. They are also occasionally dropped by elite enemies in Red Zones.

**Q: Which hacking node is best for large groups?**

A: The **Multihack Node** is essential for crowd control. It allows Diana to expose the weak spots of all nearby robots simultaneously, preventing you from being overwhelmed while trying to hack targets one by one.
