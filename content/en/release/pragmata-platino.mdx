## Combat Mastery and Hacking Challenges

The combat system in Pragmata revolves around the synergy between your weaponry and Diana’s hacking abilities. Several silver trophies require you to perform specific feats that won't happen naturally unless you set them up.

### Dealing Massive Damage
The trophy **“IT’S OVER 6000!”** requires you to deal 6,000 damage in a single second. This is best achieved in the late game by stacking status effects on a boss or using Hugh’s special attack during an Overkill window. Similarly, **“Who Needs Guns!?”** tasks you with dealing 3,000 damage with a single hack. To do this, focus on upgrading your Hacking Nodes at the Printer and targeting enemy weaknesses.

### Multi-Target Eliminations
For **“Round ‘Em Up”** and **“Clean Up on Aisle Three”**, you need to take out multiple enemies simultaneously. The Lim Recycler is your best friend here. It pulls enemies into a central point, making them easy targets for an explosive finish.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not ignore the Collector Bots. The trophy **“You’re Not Getting Away That Easy”** is tied to defeating these rare, aggressive machines. They often appear in later sectors and can quickly overwhelm an unprepared player.

## The Collectible Hunt: REMs and Mini-Cabins

Exploration is the backbone of the **pragmata platino** experience. You cannot simply rush through the levels if you want the gold trophies.

*   **REMs:** These are Earth-themed holograms. Finding them allows you to gift them to Diana in the Safe Room. Completing a full row on the shelf unlocks **“Replicating Extraordinaire!”**.
*   **Mini-Cabins:** Small collectible figures hidden throughout the sectors. You need every single one for the **“Mini-Hunter Supreme”** gold trophy.
*   **Holo Walls:** These are illusory barriers. If you feel like a room is suspiciously empty, walk against the walls; you might find a hidden passage required for **“Wall? What Wall?”**.

## Safe Room Systems and Upgrades

The Safe Room acts as your hub between missions. Interacting with the systems here is mandatory for several trophies. You should regularly check the **Printer** to see what new gear is available. The trophy **“Can’t Stop, Won’t Stop”** requires you to print every weapon, node, and ability before the final mission.

### Training Simulations
After you find your first broken Cabin robot, training simulations become available. 
1.  **Simulant:** Complete any one simulation objective.
2.  **Master of the Simulator:** Complete every objective in all simulations. This requires finding extra training data hidden in the game world.

### The Bingo System
Using **Cabin Coins** found in the world, you can play a "Bingo" style game with the Cabin robot. This is how you unlock rare items like Red Security Keys, which are needed for the **“Red-Zoned and Loving It”** trophy.

## Advanced Completion Strategies

To finalize your **pragmata platino** run, you will need to tackle the "Lunatic" difficulty. This mode is unlocked after your first successful completion of the story. In this mode, enemies deal significantly more damage, and your resources are more limited.

For more information on the game's development and updates, visit the [official Pragmata website](https://www.capcom.com/pragmata/).

### 100% Sector Completion
The **“Cradle Surveyor”** trophy requires you to reach 100% completion in at least one sector. This includes:
*   Finding all REMs and Mini-Cabins in that area.
*   Opening all chests (including those in Red Zones).
*   Activating all data terminals.
*   Defeating all unique "Elite" encounters in the sector.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a missable trophy in Pragmata?**

A: Yes, **“Can’t Stop, Won’t Stop”** (printing all items) must be completed before the final story point. Additionally, some dialogue trophies with Diana can be missed if you do not visit the Safe Room frequently between story beats.

**Q: How do I find Diana during hide-and-seek?**

A: For the **“Peekaboo”** trophy, Diana hides in the Safe Room at the end of Sector 3. Move slowly and listen for her giggling. She is usually tucked behind the large consoles or the crates near the Printer. You have 60 seconds to find her.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Cabin Coins?**

A: The most reliable way to earn coins for the **pragmata platino** is to complete the training simulations and find hidden blue chests in the "Special Sectors." You can also receive them as rewards from Diana after giving her specific REMs like the Basketball.

**Q: Does "Lunatic" difficulty stack with lower difficulty trophies?**

A: Yes, completing the game on "Lunatic" will automatically unlock the trophy for completing the game on any lower difficulty if you haven't earned it yet.
