## Basic Game Mechanics and Controls

Pragmata blends traditional third-person shooter (TPS) mechanics with high-mobility platforming. Hugh’s suit is capable of "Space Boosts" and air dashes, though these actions are limited by an internal energy meter that recharges upon landing. Understanding your mobility is the first step toward mastering the demo.

| Action | Control (PS5 / PC) | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scan Environment** | L1 / Q | Highlights interactive terminals, items, and enemy weak points. |
| **Shift / Evade** | R1 / RB | A quick dash. Perfect timing triggers a slow-motion "Witch Time" effect. |
| **Air Dash** | X + X / A + A | Extends jump distance and allows for vertical repositioning. |
| **Hacking Command** | Square / X | Directs Diana to interface with nodes or terminals. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep your Scan (L1/Q) active. It doesn't just highlight hidden Refined Ore; it also reveals the "EXE Nodes" required to progress through locked sectors of the moon base.

## プラグマタ体験版 攻略: Hacking and Diana’s Abilities

Hacking is not just a narrative tool; it is a core combat mechanic. Diana can connect to "Hacking Nodes" scattered throughout the environment. These nodes trigger specific effects when passed through during a combat sequence.

### Types of Hacking Nodes

*   **Intel Disruption:** Temporarily increases the damage enemies receive. Repeated use on the same enemy yields diminishing returns, but chaining multiple nodes can maintain the debuff.
*   **Multi-Target Link:** Links nearby enemies together, causing damage dealt to one to resonate through the entire group.
*   **Overdrive Protocol:** Diana’s ultimate ability. Once the hacking gauge is filled, this neutralizes all surrounding threats and can even freeze incoming projectiles like missiles in mid-air.

To complete a hack, you must follow the button prompts in sequence, moving from the outer ring toward the center. Speed is essential, as Hugh remains vulnerable to physical attacks while Diana is busy interfacing with the terminal.

## Navigating the Solar Panel Factory

The primary objective of the demo is to reach the facility's upper levels to reboot the Main Power Terminal. The path is divided into several combat arenas and "Escape Hatches" that serve as checkpoints.

1.  **Entrance Hall:** Use your scan to locate the first three EXE Nodes. This will lift the initial lockdown.
2.  **Maintenance Tunnels:** Watch out for "Stasis Nets." These floor traps will root Hugh in place, making him an easy target for Walkers. Use your jump-dash to clear them.
3.  **The Lift Ascent:** While Diana hacks the lift, you must protect her from waves of flying drones. This is the perfect time to use the Shockwave Gun to keep the platform clear.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Falling off the map during the lift sequence results in an immediate restart from the last Escape Hatch. Watch your footing on the moving platforms!

## Boss Guide: EDIS (Sector Guard Protocol)

The demo concludes with a massive confrontation against **EDIS**, a high-mobility guard bot. EDIS utilizes a combination of heavy machine guns and "Lunar-made" missiles.

*   **Phase 1:** Focus on the missiles. When EDIS fires a volley, wait for Diana to call out the "Overdrive Protocol." Activating this will freeze the missiles in the air, allowing you to punch them back at the boss for massive damage.
*   **Phase 2:** EDIS will begin using physical dash attacks. Evade with R1/RB just before impact to trigger the slow-motion window. Switch to your Shotgun or Charge Piercer during this time to hit the glowing core located on its chassis.
*   **Final Stand:** Once its power level hits 90%, EDIS enters a desperate "Neutralization Mode." Dump all remaining high-powered ammunition into it. If you have the Charge Piercer, a full-charge shot to the head unit will end the fight instantly.

## Speedrunning and Unlocking Hidden Content

Capcom has encouraged replayability by including a ranking system based on your clear time. Depending on your performance, you will receive different "Drawings" from Diana (numbered 1 through 8) at the end of the game.

*   **Rank 7/8:** Standard completion.
*   **Rank 6:** Requires a clear time of under 10 minutes.
*   **Hidden Reward:** Achieving high ranks unlocks the "**Luna Suit (Graffiti Ver.)**," which covers Hugh’s armor in Diana’s colorful drawings. Additionally, you may unlock the "**Charge Pincer**" ability for use in subsequent runs.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock different endings or drawings in the demo?**

A: The drawings displayed at the end of the demo are based on your "Clear Time" and overall combat efficiency. To see all 8 variations, you will need to complete the game multiple times, aiming for both thorough exploration and sub-10-minute speedruns. This is a core part of the **プラグマタ体験版 攻略** for completionists.

**Q: Can I heal during the EDIS boss fight?**

A: Yes, you can use Refined Ore found in crates within the arena to restore health. However, using a healing item takes several seconds, so it is best to do so during "Witch Time" or while EDIS is staggered from a missile reflection.

**Q: What does the "Multi-Hack" ability do?**

A: Multi-Hack is one of the unlockable skills mentioned in this **プラグマタ体験版 攻略**. It allows Diana to link multiple terminals or enemies simultaneously, reducing the time spent on hacking mini-games and increasing Hugh's damage output against groups.

**Q: Is the Charge Piercer a permanent weapon?**

A: In the demo, once found, the Charge Piercer replaces your Shockwave Gun slot. It does not carry over if you die unless you reached a checkpoint after picking it up. In the full game, weapon slot customization is expected to be much more flexible.
