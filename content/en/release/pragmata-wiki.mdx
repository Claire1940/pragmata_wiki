## Character Profiles and Roles

Survival in the world of Pragmata is a collaborative effort. The narrative focuses on the partnership between Hugh, a skilled operative, and Diana, a young girl with unique technological capabilities. Understanding their individual strengths is the first step in mastering the gameplay.

### Hugh: The Combat Specialist
Hugh serves as the primary combatant, utilizing a variety of firearms and heavy-duty tools to neutralize threats. His role involves protecting Diana while she interacts with the facility's mainframe. Hugh's arsenal is designed for versatility, allowing him to switch between long-range precision and close-quarters crowd control.

### Diana: The Technical Prodigy
Diana is far more than an escort mission target. She possesses the ability to interface with the facility's AI, Edis, to disable lockdowns and provide tactical scans of the environment. In many scenarios, Diana is the one who opens the path forward, making her safety the player's top priority.

| Character | Primary Role | Key Ability | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hugh** | Frontline Combat | Charged Blasts | Neutralizing heavy sector guards |
| **Diana** | Support/Hacking | System Override | Disabling lockdowns and scanning |
| **Edis** | Facility AI | Logistics | Managing "Walker" utility bots |

> **💡 Tip:** Always stay within proximity of Diana during combat. If she is targeted, your ability to interact with terminals may be temporarily disabled.

## Core Combat Mechanics

The combat system in Pragmata is built on a foundation of "risk vs. reward." Players must balance their offensive output with the need to protect their companion and manage limited energy resources.

### Hacking and Bot Control
One of the most unique features of the gameplay is the ability to hack enemy bots. Instead of simply destroying every machine, players can use Diana’s abilities to turn the tide of battle. Hacking multiple bots at once can create a temporary army that distracts larger foes, allowing Hugh to charge his more powerful attacks.

### The Charged Shot System
Hugh’s primary weapon features a multi-stage charging mechanic. While a standard shot is sufficient for minor "Walkers," elite enemies require a fully charged blast to break through their armor. 

| Attack Type | Charge Time | Damage Output | Effect |

| **Medium Charge** | 1.5 Seconds | Moderate | Breaks basic shielding |

## Enemy Database: Recognizing Threats

The lunar facility is populated by a variety of mechanical units that have gone "haywire." According to the **pragmata wiki** lore, these machines were originally designed to assist humans in lunar colonization but have since turned hostile due to a localized system corruption.

### Walkers
Walkers are the most common enemy type. They are utility bots designed for heavy lifting and maintenance. While they lack advanced weaponry, their sheer numbers and physical strength can overwhelm an unprepared player.

### Sector Guards
Sector Guards are specialized security units. They are equipped with missile arrays and energy shields. When engaging a Sector Guard, players must watch for the "red circle" indicators on the ground, which signal incoming missile strikes.

### The "Rat in the Maze"
This mysterious entity acts as a recurring antagonist. It rarely speaks and prefers to manipulate the facility's environment to trap the players. Defeating its constructs requires a mix of environmental puzzle-solving and high-intensity combat.

## Mission Objective: Restoring the Main Power Terminal

A recurring mission structure involves the restoration of power to various sectors of the facility. This is a multi-step process that requires coordination between Hugh and Diana.

1.  **Locate the Terminal:** Use Diana's scanning ability to highlight the power lines within the walls.
2.  **Disable the Lockdown:** Hugh must defend Diana while she bypasses the emergency security protocols.
3.  **Manual Reboot:** Once the software is bypassed, Hugh must physically interact with the power plant controller.
4.  **Defend the Upload:** Restoring power often triggers a "Sector Guard Protocol," summoning waves of enemies that must be defeated before the power reaches 100%.

> **Warning:** During the power restoration process, unauthorized access alerts will trigger. Expect heavy resistance from the top floor security detail.

## Advanced Exploration and Scanning

Exploration is a core pillar of the experience. The facility is filled with "unregistered guests" protocols that make navigation difficult. By using the scan function frequently, players can identify hidden elevators and secret caches of lore.

As noted in the **pragmata wiki**, scanning doesn't just reveal items; it also highlights enemy patrol paths. This allows players to choose between a stealthy approach or a direct confrontation. Given the scarcity of resources on the moon, avoiding unnecessary fights is often the smarter tactical choice.

### Managing Energy Levels
Your equipment runs on a centralized energy pool. This pool is shared between Hugh's weapons and Diana's hacking tools. If the energy level drops below 30%, your combat effectiveness decreases significantly. Reconnecting to primary power terminals is the only way to fully restore these levels during a mission.
