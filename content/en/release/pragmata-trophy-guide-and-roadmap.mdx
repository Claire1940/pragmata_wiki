## The Pragmata Trophy Guide and Roadmap: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Story Completion and Mechanics Mastery
Your first playthrough should be focused on enjoying the narrative and learning the ropes. Don't stress too much about missing every single collectible, but try to upgrade your equipment consistently. Focus on the **Jackhammer** and **Photon Laser** early on, as these will be your best friends during boss encounters.

During this phase, pay close attention to the hacking nodes. Learning how to path through "Confuse" or "Offense Mode" nodes while protecting Diana is essential. The core of any **pragmata trophy guide and roadmap** is efficiency, so try to earn the combat-specific trophies (like dealing 6000 damage in one second) while you have access to late-game upgrades.

### Step 2: Unknown Signal Cleanup
Once the credits roll, you unlock the **Unknown Signal** mode. This is a post-game epilogue that allows you to fast-travel back to any sector. This is your "Cleanup Phase." You must reach 100% sector progress in every area to unlock the final post-game challenges.

*   **Collectibles:** Find all REM toys and Mini Cabin robots.
*   **Diana Interaction:** Spend time in the Shelter playing hide-and-seek and talking to Diana to exhaust her dialogue.
*   **Training Sims:** Complete all 30 Training Simulators available in the Shelter.

### Step 3: The Lunatic Run
This is the ultimate test. As mentioned in our **pragmata trophy guide and roadmap**, Lunatic is a fresh save. Enemies have significantly more health, and Hugh takes much more damage. You must rely on the **Deletion Protocol** to stun bosses and use the Photon Laser on their weak points to survive.

## Combat and Skill Execution Feats

Many of these require specific weapon loadouts or situational awareness. To earn these, you should visit the **Unit Printer** in the Shelter to unlock the necessary nodes and abilities.

| Trophy Name | How to Unlock |
|---|---|
| **You're Not Getting Away** | **MISSABLE.** Defeat a Sweeper bot. Use a Stasis Net to stop them from fleeing. |
| **IT'S OVER 6000!** | Deal 6000 damage in 1 second. Use a fully charged **Charge Piercer** on a line of enemies. |
| **Can I Borrow That?** | Use the Confuse node during a hack to steal a Defender bot's shield. |

| **Clean up on Aisle Three** | Use Diana's Cleanse ability to hit three corrupted enemies at once. |

> **💡 Tip:** For the "IT'S OVER 6000!" trophy, head to Training Sim 07. It features a perfect hallway of three low-level enemies that you can easily line up for a piercing shot.

## Exploration and Secret Trophies

The Cradle is filled with hidden rooms and collectibles. Keeping your **Object Scan** active is vital for finding the shimmering Holo-Walls.

*   **Mini-Hunter Supreme:** There are 3 Mini Cabin robots hidden in every single sector. You must shoot all of them to earn this.
*   **Thanks for Everything!:** This is a long-term goal. Keep giving Diana REM toys (found in chests) until she eventually gifts you a hand-drawn picture in return.
*   **Escape Artist:** You must interact with and unlock every escape hatch in the game. These serve as shortcuts and are essential for 100% sector progress.

## Surviving Lunatic Difficulty

The **Lunar Supremacy** trophy is the gatekeeper of the Platinum. Since you cannot use your upgraded gear from the first playthrough, you must be extremely picky with your **Pure Lunum** spending.

1.  **Prioritize the Jackhammer:** It provides the best burst damage for the early game.
2.  **Master the Dodge:** In Lunatic, many enemy attacks can one-shot Hugh. Learn the tell-tale neon glow before an enemy fires.
3.  **Diana is Key:** Always keep Diana's hacking abilities on cooldown. Stripping armor off enemies is the only way to make them manageable in the later sectors like the Nexus Tower.

Follow the **pragmata trophy guide and roadmap** steps for collectibles during your Unknown Signal run to ensure you have the best possible understanding of the map before attempting this final challenge.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there any way to skip the second playthrough for the Pragmata Platinum?**

A: No. The Lunatic difficulty is locked until you complete the game once. Because it requires a fresh save file, you must play through the story at least twice to earn all trophies.

**Q: Are there any missable trophies in Pragmata?**

A: Yes. The **Sweeper bots** are rare enemies that spawn in very specific locations and flee the moment they see you. If you progress too far without defeating one, you may have to wait until your next playthrough or a specific post-game mission to find another.

**Q: How do I get 100% Sector Progress for the Cradle Surveyor trophy?**

A: You must find all chests, shoot all 3 Mini Cabins, unlock the escape hatch, and complete any "Red Zone" combat encounters in that specific area. Using the advanced scanner upgrade from the Unit Printer will help ping missing items on your HUD.

**Q: Does this pragmata trophy guide and roadmap work for the Xbox and PC versions?**

A: Yes. While the PlayStation version has an exclusive Platinum trophy, the achievement list is identical across Steam and Xbox Series X/S. The strategies for Lunatic difficulty and collectible hunting remain the same.
