While this engine is legendary for its performance in Resident Evil, Pragmata's open-lunar environments and complex physics systems (like the "shattering sky" seen in the first trailer) likely required significant engine-level overhauls. 

### 2. The COVID-19 Pandemic
The 2020-2022 period saw massive disruptions in global game development. For a new IP that requires intense collaboration and "finding the fun" through iteration, the lack of in-person studio time likely hit Pragmata harder than it hit established sequels with existing frameworks.

### 3. Internal Reboots
Industry insiders have often whispered that Pragmata underwent a "soft reboot" around 2022. When a game's core loop isn't meeting Capcom’s internal quality standards, they are known to scrap work and start over. This would explain why the 2023 teaser featured an apology and a "reset" of expectations regarding the release date.
