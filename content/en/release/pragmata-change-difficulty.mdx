## How to Manage and Pragmata Change Difficulty

If you find yourself stuck on a specific boss or an arena filled with homing androids, you may wonder how to **pragmata change difficulty** mid-stream. It is important to note that the game uses a hub-based structure rather than a traditional linear progression.

When you die, the game does not simply reload the last checkpoint. Instead, it kicks you back to the central lunar base (the Hub). While there may not always be a slider in the options menu to lower enemy HP, you can change the difficulty of the encounter by modifying your build.

### The Hub: Your Difficulty Adjustment Center

The Hub acts as a sanctuary where you can prepare for the next mission. If the current level feels too difficult, follow these steps to lower the challenge:

1.  **Upgrade Diana:** Her hacking abilities are the primary way to exploit enemy weaknesses. If you are struggling, focus on the grid-navigation upgrades.
2.  **Weapon Modification:** Use resources found in previous levels to increase the damage output of your primary firearms.
3.  **Training Challenges:** Unlock and complete these in the Hub to master the "face button" hacking system without the risk of losing progress.
4.  **Gifts for Diana:** Building your relationship with Diana can unlock passive combat bonuses that make survival significantly easier.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Dying will reset the enemies along your path when you return from the Hub. Ensure you are fully equipped before heading back out, as you will have to fight through those encounters again.

When Diana initiates a hack, a grid appears on the screen. You must use the face buttons to navigate to a green marker. Success provides various tactical advantages that effectively nerf the enemies in the immediate area.

## Post-Game: Lunatic Mode and New Game Plus

For those who find the 7-to-10-hour campaign too easy, the game offers a significant jump in challenge once the credits roll. Unlocking these modes is the primary way players **pragmata change difficulty** for subsequent playthroughs.

### Lunatic Mode
Lunatic Mode is designed for players who have mastered the hacking grid and have a high-level build. In this mode, enemies are faster, more aggressive, and can often kill the protagonist in one or two hits. This mode also introduces new cosmetics that are unavailable on lower settings, providing a visual reward for the increased frustration.

### New Game Plus (NG+)
New Game Plus is the perfect middle ground. It allows you to replay the story with all your endgame gear. This effectively lowers the difficulty of the early levels, allowing you to breeze through sections that may have caused trouble during your first run. It is highly recommended for those looking to find all the hidden collectibles and text logs without the constant threat of death.

## Technical Factors Affecting Difficulty

It is worth noting that on certain platforms, such as the Nintendo Switch 2, performance can actually impact the perceived difficulty of the game. In the later levels, when the screen is filled with dozens of enemies and particle effects, frame rates can occasionally dip below 30 FPS.

This drop in performance makes it harder to dodge incoming attacks and navigate the hacking grid accurately. If you find the game becoming "unfairly" difficult during high-action sequences, it may be due to these technical fluctuations.

> **💡 Tip:** To mitigate performance-related difficulty spikes, try to lure enemies into smaller corridors where the engine has fewer objects to render at once.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change the difficulty at any time during a mission?**

A: There is no traditional "on-the-fly" toggle in the middle of a level. To **pragmata change difficulty** in an effective sense, you must return to the Hub to upgrade your gear or wait until you unlock New Game Plus to restart with better equipment.

**Q: What happens if I die repeatedly on the same boss?**

A: The game will continue to send you back to the Hub. While there is no "pity mode" that automatically lowers the difficulty, the resources you gather during each attempt stay with you, allowing you to eventually overpower the encounter through upgrades.

**Q: Is there an "Easy Mode" for players who just want the story?**

A: As of the 2026 launch version, there is no dedicated "Easy Mode." The game expects a certain level of mastery over its hacking and shooting mechanics. However, using New Game Plus after an initial completion allows for a much more relaxed experience.

**Q: Does Lunatic Mode offer better rewards?**

A: Yes. Completing missions on Lunatic Mode unlocks exclusive character cosmetics for both the astronaut and Diana, as well as unique weapon skins that are not accessible on Normal difficulty.

**Q: How long does it take to beat the game on Normal difficulty?**

A: Most players finish the main story in about 7 to 10 hours. This timeframe can vary significantly depending on how many times you die and how much time you spend grinding for upgrades in the Hub.
