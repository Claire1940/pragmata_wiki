## Exploration and Collectibles Guide

A significant portion of the **trophee pragmata** experience involves scouring the environment for secrets. Every sector contains hidden items that are required for the "Cradle Surveyor" trophy (100% sector progress).

### Read Earth Memories (REMs)
REMs appear as virtual holograms of Earth. Finding these and returning them to Diana at the Shelter is the primary way to earn Cabin Coins. 
*   **For Me?:** Give your first REM to Diana.
*   **Thanks for Everything!:** Receive a drawing from Diana after gifting her multiple REMs.
*   **Replicating Extraordinaire!:** Complete a full set of REMs from a single series.

### Mini Cabins
There are three Mini Cabins hidden in every level. They are small, physical models that must be shot to be "collected."
*   **Well Spotted!:** Shoot your first Mini Cabin.
*   **Mini-Hunter Supreme:** Find and shoot every Mini Cabin in the game.

### Red Zones and Holo-Walls
As you explore, you will encounter "Holo-Walls"—invisible barriers that hide rare resources. Finding your first one unlocks the **Wall? What Wall?** trophy. Additionally, you should use Red Gate Keys to enter **Red Zones**. These are combat arenas that reward you with high-tier upgrade materials like Pure Lunum.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Red Zones feature significantly tougher enemies than the standard sector. Ensure your weapons are upgraded to at least Level 3 before attempting these in the later sectors.

## Post-Game and Lunatic Difficulty

After the credits roll, your journey toward the **trophee pragmata** enters its final phase. You will unlock the **Unknown Signal** missions in the Hidden Chamber. These are ten secret missions that require you to defeat "Alpha" versions of previous bosses.

To unlock the gold trophy **Lunar Supremacy**, you must complete the entire campaign on Lunatic Difficulty. In this mode:
1.  Enemies deal 200% more damage.
2.  Hugh’s suit energy drains faster when using abilities.
3.  Checkpoints are much further apart.

We recommend using the **Top-Shelf Item** trophy (upgrading a weapon with Pure Lunum) to max out your Charge Piercer before starting Lunatic mode. The high burst damage is essential for survival.

## Weapon and Equipment Mastery

The final pieces of the puzzle involve the upgrade system. You must "Print" every available piece of gear in the game.

| Equipment Category | Completion Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Weapons** | Print all 6 main firearms, including the Charge Piercer and Stasis Net. |
| **Hacking Nodes** | Unlock all 4 nodes (Basic, Confuse, Overload, Siphon). |
| **Abilities** | Unlock all of Diana's support skills, including the Shield and Cleanse. |
| **Shelter Level** | Reach Max Shelter Level to unlock the final prints. |

Completing this checklist will grant you the **Can't Stop, Won't Stop** trophy, putting you one step closer to the Platinum.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there any missable trophies in Pragmata?**

A: No, there are no missable trophies. You can use the Shelter’s terminal to revisit any sector or complete Training Simulations at any time after finishing the main story.

**Q: How do I unlock the Lunatic difficulty for the trophee pragmata?**

A: You must complete the main story once on any difficulty setting. After the credits, Lunatic mode will be available when starting a New Game or New Game Plus.

**Q: Where can I find Pure Lunum for weapon upgrades?**

A: Pure Lunum is a rare resource found in hidden chests within Red Zones or as a reward for completing high-rank Training Simulations in the Shelter.

**Q: How long does it take to get the 100% sector progress?**

A: Achieving 100% progress in a sector usually takes about 30-45 minutes of thorough searching per area, provided you are looking for REMs, Mini Cabins, and Holo-Walls. Using Diana's scan ability frequently will highlight nearby collectibles.
