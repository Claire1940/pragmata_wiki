## The Weapon Printing System

In Pragmata, finding a weapon in the field does not automatically add it to your permanent inventory. The game utilizes a "printing" mechanic that requires a specific investment before a piece of gear becomes a permanent part of your loadout. When you discover a new weapon in one of the zones, you can use it immediately, but it will be lost if you swap it out or return to a shelter without "registering" it.

To permanently secure these **pragmata unlockables**, you must spend Luna Filament to purchase the Level 1 upgrade for that specific weapon. Once the first upgrade is purchased, the weapon is added to your Unit Printer, allowing you to include it in your loadout every time you leave a shelter.

## The Cabin Stamp Book and Rewards

One of the most robust sources of **pragmata unlockables** is the Cabin Stamp Book, managed by your friendly robot companion, Cabin. Shortly after meeting Cabin, you will unlock training exercises. These are not merely tutorials; they are high-yield challenges that reward you with Cabin Coins and Luna Filament.

Cabin Coins are the primary currency used to fill out the Stamp Book. Each stamp you place on the grid reveals a new reward. These rewards are essential for players who want to see everything the game has to offer.

### Stamp Book Unlock Categories

*   **Lore Snippets:** Diary entries and enemy intel that flesh out the mysterious world of Pragmata.
*   **Resource Bonuses:** Large drops of Luna Filament triggered by completing rows (Bingos) or filling the entire board (Blackout).

## Hacking and Combat: Tactical Pragmata Unlockables

Hacking is arguably the most powerful tool in your arsenal. Unlike standard shooters, Pragmata rewards players who manipulate the battlefield. Hacking is divided into "Nodes" (consumable items found in the world) and "Modes" (permanent chips that change how your hacks function).

### Understanding Hacking Modes

Hacking Modes are essential **pragmata unlockables** that you must print and equip. When you initiate a "Primary Hack," a gauge appears. By passing through blue open nodes on the hacking grid, you extend this timer. If you have a Mode equipped, such as "Offense Mode," these nodes provide additional benefits:

1.  **Offense Mode:** Increases the total damage the hack deals based on how many nodes you pass through and how much gunfire you land during the hack.
2.  **Chaos Mode:** Spreads status effects like "Confuse" or "Freeze" to nearby enemies upon completion.
3.  **Efficiency Mode:** Reduces the resource cost of secondary hacks and speeds up the hacking process.

> **Warning:** If the hacking gauge runs out before you complete the secondary hack, you will lose the bonuses provided by your current Mode. Always prioritize the timer over greedy node collection.

## Tactical and Defensive Units

Your D-pad is your gateway to various tactical and defensive **pragmata unlockables**. While it is tempting to focus entirely on raw damage, these tools provide the crowd control necessary for the game's more difficult encounters.

*   **Sticky Bombs:** These do not deal massive damage but are vital for hacking. Attaching one to a large robot removes an entire row from their hacking grid, making complex hacks significantly easier.
*   **Decoy Generator:** Essential for solo players. It draws aggro, allowing you to set up decontaminator traps or line up a sniper shot.
*   **Drone Hive:** An autonomous unit that targets the nearest threats. This is best used when you are overwhelmed by smaller, faster "Reassembler" drones.

## Exploration and Secret Unlockables

The various zones in Pragmata are dense with secrets, but you won't be able to access everything on your first pass. Many **pragmata unlockables** are hidden behind environmental puzzles that require specific tools.

### The Upgrader Tool and Scanner

One of the most important utility **pragmata unlockables** is the Upgrader Tool. This device allows you to bypass high-security doors and access previously unreachable areas. It is highly recommended to revisit earlier zones once you have obtained the Upgrader Tool and the Scanner.

The Scanner is a unit you can print that highlights nearby collectibles and safe boxes. If you are aiming for 100% completion, the Scanner is mandatory. It will indicate if a collectible is on a different floor or behind a wall, saving you hours of aimless searching.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the alternate costumes for Diana?**

A: Alternate costumes are primarily obtained through the Cabin Stamp Book. You must complete training exercises to earn Cabin Coins, then use those coins to fill spaces on the stamp board. Certain "Blackout" rewards and specific stamp spaces are dedicated to character skins.

**Q: Can I miss any pragmata unlockables during my first playthrough?**

A: No, you can return to any previously visited zone at any time from the shelter. Some areas require the Upgrader Tool, which is unlocked later in the story, so backtracking is actually encouraged to find everything.

**Q: What is the fastest way to farm Luna Filament for upgrades?**

A: The most efficient way to earn Luna Filament is by completing "Bingos" on the Cabin Stamp Book and finishing the optional training courses. Training courses often provide a large lump sum of resources upon first completion.

**Q: Why can't I add the weapon I just found to my loadout?**

A: You must purchase the Level 1 upgrade for any found weapon using Luna Filament at a shelter. This "registers" the weapon in your printer, allowing you to select it for future missions. If you lose the weapon before upgrading it, you will have to find it in the world again.
