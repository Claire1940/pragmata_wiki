## Core Combat Mechanics for Boss Encounters

Before engaging any **pragmata boss**, you must understand the synergy between Hugh and Diana. Unlike traditional action games, combat in *Pragmata* is a collaborative effort. Hugh provides the "Heat"—raw firepower and physical defense—while Diana utilizes the "Hacking Matrix" to manipulate the environment and exploit enemy vulnerabilities.

| Mechanic | Function | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Heat Levels** | Increases damage output and enables special finishers. | Use against armored bosses to break through physical shielding. |
| **Hacking Matrix** | Diana disrupts enemy sub-systems or takes control of smaller units. | Essential for clearing "little ones" during multi-phase fights. |
| **Replicator Tech** | Temporary construction of cover or specialized weaponry. | Use when the boss initiates wide-area "wipe" attacks. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on Diana’s energy levels. If she falls or loses power, your ability to exploit boss weak points is severely diminished.

## Early Game: Sector Guard Edith & EDIS

The first true test of your skills comes in the form of the Sector Guard Protocol, overseen by the AI known as Edith. This encounter introduces the player to high-velocity missile evasion and the importance of positioning.

### Edith Attack Patterns
Edith relies heavily on long-range suppression. The arena is often filled with missiles that track your movement, requiring precise dodging or the use of replicator-generated barriers.

*   **Missile Salvo:** Large clusters of Luna-made projectiles. These can be shot down or dodged at the last second to build Heat.
*   **Primary Power Surge:** A localized energy blast that occurs when Edith reaches 60% power. You must sever the primary connection to stop the neutralization protocol.

### EDIS Controlled Violence
Following the initial guard, EDIS enacts the "controlled violence" protocol. This phase is faster and requires you to manage "red things"—replicator-spawned anomalies that spread across the ground.

| Attack Name | Visual Cue | Counter Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Filament Spread** | Red gunk appearing on the floor. | Move to higher ground immediately or use heat-based clearing. |
| **Sector Guard Scan** | A red laser tracking Hugh. | Break line of sight using the environment or a replicator shield. |

## Mid-Game: The Replicator Beast & Mr. War

As you progress toward the communications tower, the **pragmata boss** variety increases, introducing specialized mechanical beasts. These encounters emphasize the "team" aspect of the game, as Hugh cannot handle the speed of these enemies alone.

### The Hammer-Tail Beast
This creature is a hybrid of organic movement and heavy machinery. Its most dangerous feature is the heavy hammer attached to its rear side, which it uses for massive AOE slams.

1.  **Watch the Tail:** The beast uses its tail for quick, sweeping strikes that can stun Hugh.
2.  **Antenna Shoot-out:** Diana will often call out the antennas. Destroying these temporarily blinds the beast, allowing Hugh to move in for a high-heat melee strike.
3.  **Hacking the Hammer:** Once the beast’s health is below 40%, Diana can initiate a local control override. This forces the beast to drop its guard, exposing the core.

### Mr. War (The War Machine)
Mr. War is a recurring threat that culminates in a massive showdown near the tower. This boss utilizes "little ones"—small, aggressive drones—to distract the player while it prepares its main cannon.

*   **Weak Zones:** Aim for the glowing red vents on the sides. These only open after a successful hack or a heavy impact.
*   **Minion Management:** Do not ignore the drones. Have Diana hack them to turn them against the main body, providing you with much-needed breathing room.

> **Warning:** Mr. War’s "Wide Open" state is a trap if you haven't cleared the drones. They will swarm you the moment you stop moving to aim.

## Late Game: The Dead Filament & Eight

The final stretch of *Pragmata* is emotionally charged and mechanically complex. You are no longer just fighting robots; you are fighting a manifestation of sadness and "black gunk" that threatens to consume Diana's memories.

### Dealing with the Black Filament
The filament is an out-of-control entity that spreads rapidly. In this fight, the environment is constantly shifting. You must help Diana navigate her own memories while fending off the physical manifestation of the AI known as Eight.

| Phase | Objective | Key Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Memory Sync** | Protect Diana while she processes data. | Keep enemies away from Diana's radius. |
| **Deletion Protocol** | Stop Eight from purging the system. | Target the central core with the Charged Cannon. |
| **The Final Rail** | Push the cargo shuttle to safety. | Use "Elbow Grease" (QTE) while Diana hacks the rail locks. |

### The Charged Cannon Finale
The final **pragmata boss** encounter requires you to charge a massive rail cannon. This is a multi-stage process where Hugh must defend the battery while Diana optimizes the hacking matrix. 

*   **Charging:** Hugh must stay near the cannon to feed it energy.
*   **The Shot:** Once the matrix is optimized, a single, precise shot will neutralize the filament. 
*   **The Escape:** After the shot, the boss will make one final desperate attempt to stop the shuttle. Use the hacking matrix one last time to initiate the "Safe Journey" protocol.

## Advanced Tips for Boss Success

To truly master every **pragmata boss**, consider these expert strategies:

*   **Animation Canceling:** Use Hugh's heat-venting move to cancel the recovery frames of heavy attacks. This allows you to dodge boss swipes more effectively.
*   **Diana’s Upgrades:** Prioritize upgrading Diana’s "Hacking Speed" and "Energy Recovery." In the late game, her ability to take over drones is more valuable than Hugh’s raw damage.
*   **Environmental Replicators:** Many boss arenas have hidden replicator nodes. Activating these can provide temporary turrets or healing stations that are not available through your standard loadout.
*   **Stay Mobile:** *Pragmata* bosses are designed to punish players who stay in one spot. Even if you are aiming a heavy weapon, keep strafing to avoid "unregistered guest" neutralization protocols.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most difficult pragmata boss in the 2026 version?**

A: Most players find Mr. War to be the biggest challenge due to the sheer number of drones he spawns. Mastering the hacking mechanic to turn those drones into allies is the key to winning that fight.

**Q: Can I fight bosses without Diana's help?**

A: No. The game is built around the partnership between Hugh and Diana. Many boss weak points are only accessible after Diana performs a hack or provides a tactical opening.

**Q: How do I recover health during a boss fight?**

A: Health is recovered through replicator-generated "Fix-its" or by having Diana initiate a repair protocol when your Heat levels are high enough. Always keep a reserve of energy for emergency repairs.

**Q: Is there a secret boss in Pragmata?**

A: While there are no hidden bosses in the traditional sense, the "Dead Filament" encounter has multiple endings and secret dialogue triggers depending on how many of Diana's memories you successfully recover during the fight.
