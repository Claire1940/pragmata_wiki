## The Structure of Sectors in Pragmata

Pragmata utilizes a "Metroidvania-lite" approach to its level design. While the story moves you forward through the narrative, the physical world is divided into large, semi-open Sectors. These aren't just simple levels; they are multi-layered environments where verticality and hidden shortcuts play a major role. 

The first major zone players encounter is the **Solar Power Plant (Sector 1)**. This area serves as the introduction to the game’s core mechanics, including hacking, combat with the Stasis Net, and basic platforming. However, a hallmark of the game's design is that Sector 1 cannot be fully completed on your first visit. You will frequently encounter barriers made of "filament" or high-security locks that require abilities granted in **Sector 2** and beyond.

## Sector 1: Solar Power Plant Deep Dive

The Solar Power Plant is a massive industrial complex that provides power to the lunar colony. It is divided into several sub-zones, including the Distribution Center, the Concourse, and the Generator Entrance. 

### Key Locations in Sector 1
When exploring the Solar Power Plant, you should keep an eye out for these specific locations that house the most valuable loot:

1.  **The Distribution Center:** This is a vertical playground where you will find your first safe boxes. It requires significant use of lifts and hacking terminals to move platforms.
2.  **The Concourse:** A hub area that features a Shelter shortcut. This is where you will find essential suit mods and health upgrades.
3.  **The Generator Entrance:** The final stretch of the sector that leads toward the transition to the next zone. This area is heavily guarded by walkers and flying drones.

## Mastering Combat and Hacking

As you move through the sectors, the combat complexity scales significantly. In Sector 1, you are introduced to the **Stasis Net**. This weapon is crucial for crowd control, allowing you to freeze enemies in place while you focus on high-priority targets like the "Crushers."

### Hacking Nodes and Diana
Your companion, Diana, is essential for navigating the various sectors. You will encounter Hacking Nodes that trigger mini-games or combat challenges. Completing these often unlocks shortcuts, such as the Concourse Shelter ladder, which allows you to bypass dangerous hallways on subsequent trips.

> **Warning:** Some hacking nodes will trigger an arena lockdown. Ensure your Stasis Net is fully charged before interacting with a node in a large, open room.

## The Importance of the Limb Eraser

One of the most frequent questions regarding **pragmata how many sectors** involve the "Limb" barriers. Throughout Sector 1, you will see doors and chests blocked by a glowing, organic filament. These are "Limb" growths. 

You cannot interact with these during your first hour of gameplay. You must progress into the second sector to obtain the **Limb Eraser**. Once you have this tool, you can return to the Solar Power Plant to unlock:
*   **Storage Expanders:** These increase Diana's hacking gauge, allowing her to take over more powerful machines.
*   **Pure Lun:** A rare crafting material found in the Distribution Center arena.
*   **High-Value Safe Boxes:** Some boxes in the early game contain up to 800 Limb, but they are tucked behind filament walls.

## Tips for 100% Completion

If you are a completionist, the sector system in Pragmata can be a bit daunting. Follow these steps to ensure you don't miss anything:

1.  **Listen for Audio Cues:** Mini Cabin figurines make a high-pitched singing sound. If you hear it, stop and look at the ceilings or under bunk beds.
2.  **Check the "Hidden" Channels:** The level design often features walkways that sit above or below the main path. If you see a trail of Limb currency leading off a ledge, follow it.
3.  **Upgrade Hacking Early:** Use your Storage Expanders as soon as you find them. Higher hacking levels allow you to turn enemy drones into allies, making the later, more difficult sectors much more manageable.
4.  **Use the Ziplines:** Many RAM units, like the "Crayons" RAM, are hidden on high pipes that can only be reached by jumping off a zipline at the peak of its ascent.

## FAQ

**Q: In Pragmata how many sectors are required to beat the main story?**

A: The main story spans four primary sectors: the Solar Power Plant, the Research Hub, the Lunar Surface, and the Shelter. However, there are several transitional areas and "hidden" sub-sectors that contain endgame content and secret bosses.

**Q: Can I travel between sectors at any time?**

A: Yes, once you have unlocked the Shelter shortcuts and the tram system within each sector, you can fast-travel back to previous areas. This is highly recommended once you obtain the Limb Eraser in the second sector.

**Q: Do I need to find all the holograms for the 100% map score?**

A: No. While holograms provide deep lore and background information on the collapse of the lunar colony, they are not counted toward the "Tracked Collectibles" score. You only need to focus on Safe Boxes, RAM, Mods, and specific upgrades.

**Q: What is the most important item to find in Sector 1?**

A: The Cartridge Holder found in the Medbay is arguably the most important. It increases your heal capacity, which is vital for the boss fight at the end of the Solar Power Plant and the difficulty spike in the Research Hub.
