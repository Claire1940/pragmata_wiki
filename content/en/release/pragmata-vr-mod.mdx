## Step-by-Step Installation Guide

Follow these steps carefully to ensure the **pragmata vr mod** initializes correctly. The process is split into two phases: the initial framework setup and the HUD optimization.

| Step | Action | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Download RE Framework** | Get the `Pragmata.zip` from the nightly builds repository. |
| **2** | **Initial Injection** | Copy `dinput8.dll` into the Pragmata root folder. |
| **3** | **Launch Game** | Start the game in flat mode to generate the necessary config files. |
| **4** | **Install Manager** | Extract and launch Fluffy Mod Manager, then select Pragmata. |
| **5** | **Add Scripts** | Drag `Script Core` and `HUD Controls` into the manager and toggle them **ON**. |

To find your game folder quickly, right-click **Pragmata** in your Steam library, select **Manage**, and then click **Browse local files**. This is where the majority of your modding work will take place.

## Fixing HUD and Double Vision Issues

One of the primary hurdles with the **pragmata vr mod** is the way the game renders its UI. By default, the hacking menu—a core combat mechanic—is rendered at an incorrect depth, causing a disorienting double-image effect in the headset. To fix this, you must use the HUD Controls script within the RE Framework menu (accessible by pressing the **Insert** key in-game).

Navigate to the "Script Generated UI" section and look for HUD Controls. You will need to manually adjust the offset and scale for three specific UI elements:
*   **Enemy Hack UI**
*   **Door Hack UI**
*   **Lock Hack UI**

| HUD Element | Recommended Offset (X/Y) | Recommended Scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Enemy Hack** | -0.15 / 0.10 | 0.85 |
| **Door Hack** | -0.12 / 0.08 | 0.90 |
| **Lock Hack** | -0.15 / 0.10 | 0.85 |

> **💡 Tip:** These values may vary based on your headset's IPD (Interpupillary Distance) and resolution. If you still see double images, try closing one eye while adjusting the sliders until the grid aligns perfectly with your dominant eye's view.

## Performance Tuning and Graphics Settings

Running Pragmata in VR requires significant optimization to maintain a smooth frame rate. Stuttering in VR is not just a visual annoyance; it can lead to motion sickness. For the best experience, visit the [official Pragmata Steam page](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1586750/PRAGMATA/) to check for the latest performance patches before applying these mod-specific tweaks.

### Resolution and Upscaling
The game's HUD has a tendency to freeze when jumping between menus. A reliable fix is to toggle your upscaling method. If the screen freezes, go to **Options > Graphics**, change your DLSS or FSR setting to a different preset, and then change it back. This forces the RE Framework to refresh the display buffer.

### Recommended Settings for VR
*   **Frame Generation:** **OFF**. Frame generation techniques often cause severe artifacts and latency issues in VR headsets.
*   **DLAA:** **Avoid**. This can cause flickering in high-contrast areas of the lunar base.
*   **Display Mode:** **Windowed**. Running in Fullscreen can sometimes force the VR headset to match your monitor's aspect ratio, resulting in a "squished" image.
*   **Upscaling:** **DLSS Balanced** or **Performance**. This provides the best balance between visual clarity and the high frame rates required for VR.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the pragmata vr mod support motion controllers?**

A: Currently, the mod is designed for use with a standard gamepad (Xbox or PlayStation controller). While you can see the world in VR, you still interact with the game using traditional button inputs. There is no "v-motion" or tracked hand support at this time.

**Q: Can I play the entire game from start to finish in VR?**

A: Yes, players have confirmed that the game is playable from the opening sequence through the major boss fights. However, be prepared for minor HUD issues, such as the weapon wheel being difficult to see. You will need to memorize your weapon slots or check your health via the character's physical cues.

**Q: Why is my screen black when I launch the game in VR?**

A: This is often caused by the resolution scaling bug. Use the "Upscale Toggle" trick: go into the graphics menu, change your DLSS/FSR setting, and exit the menu. This should trigger the image to appear in your headset.

**Q: Which VR API should I use: OpenVR or OpenXR?**

A: If you are using a Valve Index or an older HTC Vive, use the `openvr_loader.dll`. For Quest 3, Quest Pro, Pimax, or Pico users (especially those using Virtual Desktop), the `openxr_loader.dll` generally provides better performance and lower latency.
