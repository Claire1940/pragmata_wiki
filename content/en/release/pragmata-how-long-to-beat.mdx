## Breakdown of the Pragmata Sketchbook Demo

The *Pragmata Sketchbook* demo provides the first hands-on look at the game's loop. It introduces the heavy, weighted feel of the astronaut's suit and the tactical necessity of Diana's hacking abilities. Most players will find that the demo takes roughly half an hour to finish on a first blind run.

### Key Objectives in the Demo:
1.  **Infiltrate the Lunar Facility**: Navigate through the initial laser grids using thrusters and hover mechanics.
2.  **Restore Power**: Hack three primary door locks and reboot the main power terminal on the top floor.
3.  **The Sector Guard Boss Fight**: A massive combat encounter that tests your ability to manage Diana’s safety while dealing damage to a tanky robotic sentinel.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't ignore the "Luna filaments" (shining cubes) found in breakable boxes. While their full purpose in the 2026 release is still being detailed, they are the primary currency for suit and weapon upgrades.

## Factors That Influence How Long to Beat Pragmata

Several gameplay systems directly impact your total playtime. Unlike traditional shooters, *Pragmata* requires a tactical approach that can slow down progress if you aren't prepared for the "Sector Guard" level threats.

### 1. Tactical Combat and Hacking
The combat system is not a "run and gun" experience. You must manage your suit's integrity and use Diana to hack robots. Hacking nodes often appear during combat; passing through them grants damage buffs but requires careful positioning. If you struggle with the timing of these nodes, boss fights like the rolling "death ball" units will take significantly longer.

### 2. Exploration and Looting
The environments are filled with hidden escape hatches and loot boxes. Missing these items often means you'll enter later stages under-geared. Finding the **Shockwave** gun or the **Stasis Net** gun early can drastically reduce the time spent in combat encounters by allowing you to stagger or immobilize "absolute unit" enemies.

| Weapon Type | Primary Utility | Impact on Playtime |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grip Gun** | Standard ranged damage | Moderate; reliable for all ranges. |
| **Shockwave** | Close-range suppression | High; ends small-group encounters quickly. |
| **Stasis Net** | Crowd control/Immobilization | Very High; essential for speedrunning bosses. |

### 3. Diana’s AI Assistance
Diana is not just a passive follower; she calls out weak points and provides hacking support. Players who listen to her audio cues and follow her tactical advice will find they can beat the game much faster than those who try to brute-force encounters.

## Combat Strategies to Speed Up Your Playtime

If you are looking to beat the game faster, mastering the "Critical Shot" mechanic is mandatory. When an enemy is downed by Diana’s hacking or your Stasis Net, you can follow up with a powerful attack that deals upwards of 3,000 damage. 

1.  **Dash and Evade**: The suit feels heavy, but the dash ability is your best friend. Use it to avoid the red targeting sensors of turrets and boss rockets.
2.  **Hover for Vantage**: Use your thrusters to hover above the battlefield. This gives you a clear line of sight to hackable nodes that are often hidden behind debris.
3.  **Prioritize the "Chunky Boys"**: Large units with lasers can end your run quickly. Use the Stasis Net immediately to remove them from the equation while you clear smaller robots.
