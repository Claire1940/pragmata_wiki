## Detailed Breakdown of Deluxe Content

When considering the **pragmata deluxe edition differences**, the "Shelter Variety Pack" is the centerpiece of the upgrade. This pack is designed to enhance the player's experience within the game's safe zones, known as Shelters, where Hugh and Diana can interact and prepare for their next mission.

### The Shelter Variety Pack
This pack includes several items that change the visual and auditory atmosphere of the game:
*   **Extra Outfits:** Unique cosmetic skins for both Hugh and Diana that can be swapped at any point during the game.
*   **Grip Gun Weapon Skin:** A custom skin for Hugh’s primary utility tool, the Grip Gun.
*   **Alternate BGM:** A selection of background music tracks that can be played while inside the Shelter.
*   **Diana Gestures:** Exclusive emotes and gestures for Diana, adding more personality to her interactions with the player.

### Digital Artwork Data Library
For fans of the game’s striking art direction, the Deluxe Edition includes a small digital library. This is not a full-length 200-page artbook, but rather a curated collection of concept art and character data that provides insight into the development of the lunar environment and the "Cradle" security protocols.

> **💡 Tip:** Capcom has hinted that many of these cosmetic items may be sold separately as DLC later. If you aren't sure about the Deluxe Edition today, you can likely upgrade your Standard Edition at a later date.

## Pre-order Bonuses: The Neo Bushido and Kunoichi Sets

Regardless of the **pragmata deluxe edition differences**, all players who pre-order the game—whether Standard or Deluxe—will receive the same bonus content. This is a recurring trend for Capcom, ensuring that early adopters are rewarded regardless of their budget.

**The Hugh and Diana Outfit Set includes:**
1.  **Hugh Outfit:** "Neo Bushido" (Samurai-inspired armor)
2.  **Diana Outfit:** "Neo Kunoichi" (Ninja-inspired suit)

These outfits are purely cosmetic and do not provide any stats, such as increased defense or faster movement. They are designed to give the characters a stylized, traditional Japanese aesthetic that contrasts with the game's hard sci-fi setting.
