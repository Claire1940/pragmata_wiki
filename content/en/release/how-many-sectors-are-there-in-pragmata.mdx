## How Many Sectors Are There in Pragmata?

Each of these sectors represents a distinct ecological or industrial zone on the Moon, ranging from the cramped, neon-lit corridors of the Residential Hub to the sprawling, low-gravity expanses of the Oxygen Processing Plants.

While the main story takes you through these sectors in a linear fashion initially, the game eventually opens up, allowing for backtracking and deeper exploration. Each sector acts as a self-contained ecosystem with its own set of challenges, enemy types, and environmental hazards.

## Achieving 100% Sector Progress

Understanding **how many sectors are there in Pragmata** is only half the battle; the real challenge lies in achieving 100% progress in each one. Progress in this game is handled differently than in typical open-world titles. Instead of every single scrap of metal contributing to your percentage, the game focuses on "Trackable Items."

### The Escape Hatch Mechanic
Your progress in any given sector does not begin to track officially until you have unlocked the **Escape Hatch** for that specific area. The Escape Hatch serves as a fast-travel point and a safe zone. Once this is activated, the game’s UI will update to show your current completion percentage for that sector.

### What Counts Toward Completion?
It is a common misconception that every log and component counts toward the 100% mark. To save time, players should focus on the "Main Items."

*   **Main Items:** These are unique collectibles, story-related artifacts, and suit upgrades. These are mandatory for 100% completion.
*   **Upgrade Components:** Used for crafting but do not count toward the sector percentage.
*   **Data Logs:** Provide lore and background story but are not required for the 100% stat.

> **💡 Tip:** Don't burn yourself out looking for every single data log if your only goal is the "Sector Master" achievement. Focus on the trackable items revealed by your HUD.

## Using the Scanner for Efficient Exploration

Later in the game, the question of **how many sectors are there in Pragmata** becomes less about the count and more about the density of the content. To manage this, you can build the **Advanced Scanner**.

The Scanner is a game-changer for completionists. Once built and upgraded, it will reveal the locations of all trackable items within a certain radius. This eliminates the need for aimless wandering and allows you to pinpoint the remaining "Main Items" needed to top off your progress.

| Scanner Tier | Range | Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | 50 Meters | Highlights basic containers |
| **Tier 2** | 150 Meters | Detects Main Items through walls |
| **Tier 3** | Sector-Wide | Reveals all trackable items on the map |

Building the Tier 3 Scanner should be a priority once you reach the mid-game (around Sector 05). It significantly reduces the time spent on backtracking. For more information on crafting requirements, visit the [official Pragmata website](https://www.capcom.co.jp/pragmata/) to see the latest patch notes regarding item spawns.

## Sector-Specific Challenges

Each of the 9 sectors introduces a new mechanic that you must master to find all collectibles. For instance, in **Sector 03: Hydroponics Bay**, many trackable items are hidden behind dense foliage that can only be cleared using the incendiary attachment for your multi-tool.

In contrast, **Sector 06: Oxygen Plant** features low-gravity zones where items are often floating in hard-to-reach rafters. You will need to master the "Boost Jump" mechanic to reach these high-altitude Main Items.

### Navigation Checklist
1.  **Locate the Escape Hatch** to enable progress tracking.
2.  **Clear the main objective** to stop aggressive enemy patrols.
3.  **Deploy the Scanner** to identify the "Main Items" in the vicinity.
4.  **Check verticality**; Pragmata's level design uses a lot of overhead space.

## The Importance of Sector 09

While many players focus on the early game, the final sector, **The Lunar Core**, is unique. It is the smallest sector in terms of physical size but contains the highest density of Main Items. Many players finish the story and realize they are only at 90% total game completion because they neglected the hidden alcoves in Sector 09. 

Because this area is "Extreme" difficulty, it is recommended to finish the 100% progress for the previous eight sectors to ensure your suit and weapons are fully upgraded before attempting the final push.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some areas within the Research Lab (Sector 07) become inaccessible after certain story beats. Always try to reach at least 80% completion in a sector before moving the main plot forward to avoid unnecessary "New Game Plus" runs.

## FAQ

**Q: How many sectors are there in Pragmata for the base game?**

A: There are 9 main sectors in the base game of Pragmata. These range from the initial Landing Zone to the final Lunar Core. Each sector has its own unique environment and set of trackable collectibles.

**Q: Do I need to collect every log to get 100% sector progress?**

A: No. Sector progress is based specifically on "Main Items" and unique collectibles. Data logs and generic upgrade components are not tracked in the sector completion percentage, though they are useful for lore and crafting.

**Q: When do the items in a sector become trackable?**

A: Items become trackable on your HUD and map only after you have unlocked the Escape Hatch in that specific area. Before unlocking the hatch, you will have to find items manually without the aid of the progress bar.

**Q: Can I go back to previous sectors later in the game?**

A: Yes, Pragmata allows for backtracking. Once you have unlocked the Escape Hatch in a sector, you can fast-travel back to it at any time from the main hub or other hatches, provided you aren't currently locked into a specific story mission.
