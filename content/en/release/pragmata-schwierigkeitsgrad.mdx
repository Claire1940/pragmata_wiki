## Difficulty Levels Overview

Pragmata offers a structured progression in its challenge settings. Unlike some action games that allow you to select the hardest difficulty from the start, this title encourages a natural learning curve. The "Lunatic" mode is specifically designed for players who have already mastered the core mechanics of Hugh’s kit.

| Difficulty Level | How to Unlock | Primary Challenge | Recommended For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | Available from Start | Balanced combat and exploration | First-time players |
| **Lunatic** | Complete Base Game | High enemy damage, aggressive AI | Veteran players |
| **Unknown Signal** | Complete Lunatic | Randomized encounters, permadeath elements | Completionists |

In Lunatic mode, the game removes the "training wheels" regarding enemy behavior. While the placement of enemies remains largely consistent with the Standard mode, their aggression levels are significantly tuned up. Bosses will no longer give you wide windows for hacking, and minor enemies like flying turrets become lethal threats if ignored for more than a few seconds.

## Unlocking and Preparing for Lunatic Mode

To access the higher **pragmata schwierigkeitsgrad**, you must successfully reach the credits on Standard difficulty. Once Lunatic is unlocked, you are granted an "Open Kit." This is a significant advantage, as it allows you to start the game with almost every weapon and gadget accessible from the beginning, provided you unlocked them in your previous run.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Even though you start with an open kit, your currency (money) does not carry over to Lunatic mode. You will need to scavenge for upgrades and damage boosts from scratch, making every combat encounter a risk-reward calculation.

### Key Changes in Lunatic Difficulty
1.  **Reduced Hacking Windows:** Bosses will interrupt your hacking attempts more frequently.
2.  **Aggressive Boss AI:** Bosses like A2 and the Tunnel Worm utilize their second-phase attacks much earlier in the fight.
3.  **Increased "Squishiness":** Hugh takes significantly more damage. A single hit from an elite enemy can often deplete 50% of your health bar.
4.  **Limited Healing:** Healing items are rarer in the environment, forcing a reliance on the Barrier and perfect dodges.

## Essential Combat Tools for Harder Difficulties

When the **pragmata schwierigkeitsgrad** spikes, your choice of weaponry becomes the difference between victory and a "Game Over" screen. While the pistols are reliable for building heat, you must utilize the full breadth of Hugh's arsenal to survive the later stages of the lunar base.

| Tool/Weapon | Best Usage in Lunatic | Effectiveness |
|---|---|---|
| **Net Gun** | Crowning infected enemies and elites | **High** |
| **Barrier** | Providing safety during slow hack sequences | **Critical** |
| **Assault Rifle** | Rapid heat building and mid-range DPS | **Medium** |
| **Drones** | Distracting smaller mobs while focusing bosses | **High** |
| **Jackhammer** | High melee damage when hacking is impossible | **Medium** |

The **Net Gun** is arguably the most valuable asset during the final stretch of the game. It allows you to freeze aggressive infected enemies in place, giving you the necessary breathing room to settle the hacking gauge. Similarly, the **Barrier** is a lifesaver when fighting in enclosed spaces, such as the infamous "Room of Doom" before the final boss.

## Boss Strategy: Surviving the Lunatic Run

Bosses in Pragmata are massive, mechanical, and relentless. On the Lunatic **pragmata schwierigkeitsgrad**, these encounters transform from cinematic spectacles into grueling tests of patience.

### The Tunnel Worm
The Tunnel Worm is widely considered the most chaotic fight in the game. In Lunatic mode, the boss deploys a massive amount of cluster munitions and drones. 
*   **Strategy:** Stay mobile and prioritize the drones. If the arena becomes too cluttered, you will be overwhelmed by chip damage. Use the buildings for verticality; if you are above certain enemies, their targeting parameters often fail, giving you a brief moment to reload or heal.

### A2 (Final Boss)
A2 is the ultimate test of your dodge timing. On harder difficulties, A2’s follow-up swipes are much faster, and the window to execute a perfect dodge is tighter.
*   **Strategy:** Equipping a "Perfect Dodge Mod" is highly recommended. Some mods provide a 10-second window after a successful dodge that increases your melee damage. This allows you to use the Jackhammer for massive chunks of damage when the boss's aggressive AI prevents you from completing a long-range hack.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not get greedy with the hacking system during the A2 fight. If you see the boss charging an attack, cancel the hack immediately. The damage taken during a failed hack is often enough to end the run instantly.

## Advanced Tactics for the "Room of Doom"

One of the most difficult sections in the Lunatic run is the final sequence where you must protect Diana while she performs slow hacks. This area features invisible stealth bots and relentless infected elites.

*   **Verticality is Key:** Whenever possible, jump to adjacent platforms. Many ground-based enemies have limited vertical pathfinding, allowing you to pick them off from safety.
*   **Overload Management:** Save your Overload meter for the third wave of enemies. This wave typically includes artillery launchers that can one-shot you from across the room.
*   **Spider Bot Priority:** Always kill the spider bots first. If they exit the aggro range of your decoys, they will immediately lock onto Hugh with high-accuracy lasers.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Pragmata schwierigkeitsgrad harder than Resident Evil's Madhouse mode?**

A: Generally, no. While Lunatic mode is challenging, most players find it more accessible than the extreme modes in the Resident Evil series. The availability of an "Open Kit" at the start of the run makes the progression much smoother.

**Q: Can I change the difficulty mid-game?**

A: You can lower the difficulty from Lunatic to Standard at any time, but you cannot raise it back up without restarting the chapter or the entire run. Note that lowering the difficulty will disqualify you from earning the Infinite Ammo reward.

**Q: What is the best weapon for the Lunatic pragmata schwierigkeitsgrad?**

A: The Net Gun and the Barrier are essential for survival. For raw damage, the Assault Rifle is preferred for building heat, while the Jackhammer is excellent for finishing off bosses during their vulnerable states.

**Q: Does Infinite Ammo work in all game modes?**

A: Yes, once unlocked by completing Lunatic mode, Infinite Ammo can be toggled on for New Game Plus and the Unknown Signal challenge mode, allowing for much more aggressive playstyles.
