## The Scope of Pragmata: Development and Visual Ambition

Pragmata represents a significant shift for Capcom, marking their first major original IP in several years. The game is set on a lunar research station where humanity has harnessed a mysterious ore known as Lunum. This material allows for advanced 3D printing via "Lunafilament," a technology that dictates much of the game's visual language. Because the game utilizes heavy generative AI themes and complex environmental rendering, the technical **pragmata budget** for GPU memory is notably higher than previous RE Engine titles.

The narrative follows Hugh Williams, a witty protagonist who finds himself separated from his team after a lunar quake. Alongside him is Diana, a specialized droid with mysterious origins. Together, they must face off against Idas, a rogue AI that has turned the station's robotic population hostile. This dynamic duo system isn't just for story; it requires significant processing power to manage the real-time hacking and combat synergies that define the gameplay loop.

## Synergistic Combat and Resource Management

The gameplay in Pragmata is built around the "Codependent Cooperation" between Hugh and Diana. Hugh handles the heavy ordnance, while Diana manages the digital battlefield. Most enemies, particularly the Idas rank-and-file, are resistant to standard gunfire. You must use Diana to hack into their systems and disable their armor before Hugh can deal significant damage.

### Combat Mechanics Overview

1.  **Hacking Mini-game**: Diana initiates a grid-based loop to bypass enemy defenses.
2.  **Slowdown Effect**: When Hugh successfully dodges an attack, time slows down, giving Diana more "processing time" to complete her hacks.
3.  **Weapon Slots**: Hugh’s suit features four modular units for weapons. These can be customized for defense, standard tactics, or heavy attack.
4.  **Durability**: High-power weapons have limited ammunition and can eventually break, requiring players to find new equipment within the station.

> **Warning:** Managing your suit's thruster meter is critical. Overusing the dash or hover moves will leave you vulnerable, as these abilities have strict cooldown periods.

## The Shelter: Upgrades and 3D Printing

The Shelter serves as your primary hub for character progression. Here, you can use the materials gathered during exploration to upgrade your gear. The station's 3D printers utilize the Lunafilament technology to enhance your hacking nodes and weapon lethality.

### Shelter Facilities and NPCs

*   **3D Printers**: The heart of the upgrade system. You can plan specific paths for Hugh’s weapons and Diana’s hacking abilities.
*   **Cabin**: An affable, independent robot who manages the shelter's systems. Cabin provides story progression and shelter-specific upgrades.
*   **The Tram**: A fast-travel system that connects the Shelter to various "Escape Hatches" (checkpoints) you have unlocked across the lunar station.

The "Sketchbook" demo, currently available on all platforms, provides a glimpse into these systems, though the full Shelter functionality is reserved for the final release. Players are encouraged to explore every corner of the demo levels to find upgrade materials that hint at the depth of the final game's customization.
