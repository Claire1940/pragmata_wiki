## The Dual Protagonist System: Hugh and Diana

The core of Pragmata lies in its "Aim, Hack, Destroy" rhythm. While many games feature companion characters, Pragmata elevates the relationship to a symbiotic level. Hugh provides the physical presence and traditional firepower, while Diana acts as the tactical brain, manipulating the digital environment to make Hugh's weapons actually effective.

| Feature | Hugh (The Human) | Diana (The Android) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Role** | Damage Dealer / Battering Ram | Tactical Support / Heavy Hitter |
| **Movement** | Heavy, deliberate, 600lb suit feel | Fluid, agile, often hitches a ride |
| **Combat Input** | Traditional firearms and gadgets | Grid-based hacking interface |
| **Key Strength** | Crowd control and shield stripping | Massive damage and environmental control |

The game forces players to multitask in real-time. You aren't just switching between characters; you are often managing both inputs simultaneously. This creates a "cerebral rhythm" where your intellectual judgment is tested just as much as your reflexes.

## Hugh: The Armored Vanguard

Hugh is the "muscle" of the **pragmata protagonist** team, though "muscle" is a relative term in this universe. Clad in a massive, high-tech spacesuit, Hugh moves with a sense of weight and encumbrance that reflects the harsh lunar environment. His face remains obscured, emphasizing his role as the "human shell" protecting the more vulnerable-looking Diana.

Despite his appearance, Hugh's standard bullets are often described as "rubber" against advanced AI shielding. His primary job is to act as a battering ram, using his arsenal to strip away outer defenses so that Diana can perform her lethal rewiring.

### Hugh's Weaponry and Gadgets

Hugh utilizes four distinct weapon types that allow him to control the flow of battle. Proper sequencing of these tools is vital for setting up Diana's high-damage hacks.

| Weapon Type | Primary Function | Best Used For... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grenade Launchers** | Area-of-Effect (AoE) | Managing large groups of smaller units. |
| **Shock Wave Rifles** | Precision Weak Point Stripping | Exposing circuitry for Diana to target. |
| **Stasis Net** | Crowd Control / Delay | Buying time for complex hacking puzzles. |
| **Decoy Generator** | Threat Redirection | Deploying "Hugh-alikes" to distract AI. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use the Decoy Generator when Diana is performing a high-level node hack. This prevents enemies from interrupting her flow and forcing a restart of the puzzle.

## Diana: The Tactical Core

If Hugh is the body, Diana is the soul—and the primary source of damage—for the **pragmata protagonist** duo. Visually, Diana is designed to evoke a sense of vulnerability. She appears as a young girl with long hair, often barefoot and wearing a suit that seems too large for her. However, this design is a clever subversion.

Diana is an advanced android capable of interfacing with the lunar base's security systems. While Hugh struggles to make a dent with his rifle, Diana can redirect missiles, shut down entire security grids, and crush enemies with massive digital strikes.

### The Hacking Interface

When Diana initiates a hack, the player enters a grid-based interface. Using controller face buttons, you must channel through nodes to create routes.
*   **Longer Routes:** Yield longer vulnerability windows for enemies.
*   **Node Consumption:** Hacking nodes are consumables, but the game encourages frequent use.
*   **Upgradable Abilities:** As you progress, Diana gains access to multi-target hacks and faster shield-breaking capabilities.

## The "Aim, Hack, Destroy" Combat Loop

Success as a **pragmata protagonist** depends on mastering the three-step combat loop. The game rarely punishes restraint but heavily penalizes "running and gunning" without a plan.

1.  **Aim:** Hugh identifies the target and uses shock waves or grenades to peel back the enemy's outer armor.
2.  **Hack:** Diana locks onto the exposed circuitry. The player completes a real-time puzzle to bypass the AI's "brain."
3.  **Destroy:** Once the hack is successful, the enemy becomes vulnerable to Hugh’s high-damage bursts or Diana’s environmental overrides.

This loop becomes increasingly complex in the game's "Expansive Arenas," such as the digitally replicated Times Square. In these wider spaces, spatial awareness becomes a "soft skill" as vital as accuracy. You must position Hugh to protect Diana while she works, often while dodging projectiles from multiple angles.

## Narrative Themes: Sapiens and AI

Beyond the mechanics, the **pragmata protagonist** relationship explores deep philosophical territory. The developers have hinted that the story focuses on the validity of android sentience. Diana’s behavior—drawing pictures with crayons, singing to herself, and seeking human connection—raises questions about whether she is truly sentient or simply programmed to be "cute" to ensure human protection.

The contrast between the two is striking:
*   **Hugh:** A human in a robotic-looking, faceless shell.
*   **Diana:** A robot in a human-looking, expressive shell.

This reversal suggests that the game will challenge players to look "deeper than the shell" to find where true humanity lies. As the antagonist is a rogue AI that has seized the moon colony, the bond between Hugh and Diana serves as the emotional anchor against a cold, logical threat.

## Resource Management and Optimization

While Pragmata isn't a traditional survival horror game, it borrows elements of resource management from Capcom’s other legendary franchises. Diana’s hacking nodes are plentiful, but they aren't infinite.

> **Warning:** Do not hoard your most powerful nodes for "later." The game's difficulty scales by increasing the number of enemies, making real-time optimization and rapid-fire problem solving more important than saving resources for a final boss.

### Customization and Loadouts
Players can customize their **pragmata protagonist** build to favor different playstyles:
*   **Hacker Build:** Focus on Diana’s node efficiency and faster shield breaks.
*   **Tactical Build:** Focus on Hugh’s crowd control and decoy duration.
*   **Balanced Build:** The recommended path for first-time players, ensuring neither character outpaces the other.

## FAQ

**Q: Is there a solo pragmata protagonist, or do you always play as both?**

A: You control both Hugh and Diana as a symbiotic unit. While Hugh is the primary character you move through the world, Diana is mechanically integrated into almost every action, from combat to puzzle-solving.

**Q: Can Diana die in combat?**

A: Diana is the "heavy hitter" and is surprisingly durable, but if she is interrupted during a hack or takes too much direct damage, it can leave Hugh vulnerable. The game focuses more on the failure of their partnership rather than a traditional health bar for Diana.

**Q: What happens if I run out of hacking nodes?**

A: Hacking nodes are found throughout the lunar base. If you run out, you'll have to rely on Hugh's basic weapons to scavenge for more, which is significantly more difficult as his standard gunfire is less effective against armored AI.

**Q: Does Pragmata have co-op play for the two protagonists?**

A: As of the 2026 release, Pragmata is designed as a single-player experience where one player manages the "intellectual judgment" of both characters. The overlapping inputs are a core part of the game's unique challenge.
