## Defeating the Final Boss: The Out-of-Control Billamin

The final boss encounter is a multi-phase battle against a massive, mutated Billamin creature. Without Eight’s control, the creature becomes a chaotic force of nature. This fight is unique because it requires constant coordination between Hue’s heavy weaponry and Diana’s hacking capabilities.

### Phase 1: The Cannon Charge
In the initial phase, Hue must defend a stationary position while Diana prepares a high-output energy cannon. The creature will attempt to disrupt the charging process with long-range projectile attacks and ground slams.

> **Warning:** Do not attempt to engage the Billamin in melee combat during this phase. Its armor is impenetrable until the cannon is fully charged.

### Phase 2: The Hacking Matrix
Once the Billamin is staggered by the cannon, Diana must initiate the "Optimizing Hacking Matrix." During this time, Hue must fend off smaller "dead Billamin" spawns. The player must successfully complete a series of rhythmic inputs to help Diana bypass the creature's internal safety protocols.

### Phase 3: The Final Lock-On
The creature will make a desperate attempt to destroy the shuttle rail. Hue must use the specialized lock-on system to target the creature's core while Diana provides a protective barrier. This is the most intense part of the **pragmata true ending** sequence, as the timing for the final shot is extremely tight.

## The Sacrifice: Hue’s Final Choice

As the battle concludes, the lunar station begins to collapse. Hue and Diana reach the cargo shuttle, which is their only remaining hope for reaching Earth. However, due to the damage sustained during the fight, the automated launch system is offline. 

In a heartbreaking turn of events, Hue realizes that someone must remain behind to manually push the shuttle onto the launch rail. Despite Diana’s protests and her desire to stay with him, Hue insists that she must fulfill her own purpose—not the one assigned to her by the colony, but the life she has chosen for herself.

Hue’s final dialogue emphasizes his growth as a character. He mentions that he had someone looking out for him in the past, and now it is his turn to be that guardian for Diana. He promises her that once she is on Earth, she will be able to walk on her own, see the ocean, and go bird watching—dreams they shared throughout their journey.
