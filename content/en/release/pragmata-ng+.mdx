## Understanding Unknown Signal: The Pragmata NG+ Experience

In *Pragmata*, the "Unknown Signal" mode is unlocked immediately after the first set of credits roll. Unlike many action-adventures where New Game Plus is simply a harder difficulty setting, here it represents a fundamental shift in how the game plays. The difficulty is tuned significantly higher than the standard "Hard" mode, introducing "Adaptive Armor" on common mobs that specifically resists the damage types you used most frequently in your first run.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not ignore the "Unknown Signal" prompt at the end of the game. If you start a standard New Game instead of selecting the Signal, you will lose your progress and carryover bonuses.

The enemies in the lunar base gain fresh attack patterns, particularly the bosses in the final third of the game. You will find that standing still and shooting is no longer a viable strategy; you must rely on Hugh's thrusters and Diana's advanced hacking modules to survive the claustrophobic arena encounters.

## What Carries Over in Pragmata NG+?

One of the biggest questions for players entering **pragmata ng+** is what happens to their hard-earned gear. Capcom is relatively generous with carryovers, allowing you to bring your combat effectiveness into the higher difficulty. However, narrative-related items and exploration progress are reset to ensure the world remains interactive.

| Element | Carries Over to NG+ | Resets in NG+ |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hugh’s Suit Level & Skill Points** | **Yes** | — |
| **Weapon Modifications & Blueprints** | **Yes** | — |
| **Diana’s Hacking Skill Trees** | **Yes** | — |
| **Specialized Nodes & Lim (Currency)** | **Yes** | — |
| **Collectibles & Audio Logs** | — | **Yes** |
| **Red Test Chamber Completion** | — | **Yes** |
| **Story Trigger States** | — | **Yes** |
| **Shelter Dialogues** | — | **Yes (New Branches)** |

By carrying over your suit level and weapon mods, you can focus your Lim spending on the "Experimental Modules" that only become available during the second playthrough. These modules, found at the merchant in the Vault, offer game-breaking perks like doubled damage during accelerated hacking windows.

## The 100% Completion Checklist

Achieving a 100% completion rating in *Pragmata* requires at least two full runs. Certain items, such as the final three modifiers for Diana, are hard-locked behind boss fights that only occur in the "Unknown Signal" mode. Furthermore, your scanner needs to be at its maximum level to detect the rarest Lunar Fragments.

To reach the Platinum trophy or 100% achievement status, follow this checklist:

*   **Collect all 47 Audio Logs:** Note that some only appear after specific plot events in the later chapters.
*   **Clear all 12 Red Test Rooms:** The final room in the Theia sector requires a Level 3 scanner, which most players don't obtain until the very end of their first run.
*   **Obtain the "Silence" Ending:** This alternative ending is triggered by making a specific choice regarding an object in the prologue—plan this for your NG+ run.
*   **Unlock the "Echo" Chapter:** Talk to Diana in the Shelter more than 30 times across both playthroughs to trigger this hidden narrative segment.
*   **Lunar Fragments:** There are 60 in total. Three are highly missable, appearing only in a narrow window between Chapters 4 and 5.

## Advanced Combat Strategies for Unknown Signal

The difficulty spike in **pragmata ng+** is noticeable from the first encounter. Enemies are more aggressive, and the environmental hazards in the lunar base are more lethal. To succeed, you must shift your focus from raw gun damage to a "Hack-and-Burst" playstyle.

### The Hacking Synergy
Diana’s hacking is the foundation of your damage output. In NG+, you should prioritize the "Digital Overload" skill tree. This allows you to stun multiple targets simultaneously. Once stunned, Hugh can close the distance and use high-velocity rounds to shatter enemy armor.

### Perfect Dodging
Hugh’s suit is equipped with thrusters that allow for a slow-motion dodge. In the base game, this is a luxury; in **pragmata ng+**, it is a necessity. Timing a dodge perfectly just before an enemy strike connects will slow down time, giving you a 3-second window to land critical hits on weak points.

> **💡 Tip:** Always return to the Hideout between missions. While it may seem like downtime, interacting with Diana unlocks new Shelter dialogues that are exclusive to the second playthrough, providing deeper insight into the game's lore.

## Exploring the "Echo" Hidden Chapter

One of the most well-kept secrets in *Pragmata* is the "Echo" chapter. This is not part of the standard mission select and can only be accessed during an Unknown Signal run. To trigger it, you must have a high "Bond Level" with Diana, achieved by engaging in optional activities in the Shelter, such as the water fights or playing dead to make her laugh.

The Echo chapter provides a flashback to the origins of the lunar research station and explains the true nature of the "Unknown Signal" itself. Completing this chapter is mandatory for unlocking the game's best weapon skin: the **Grip Gun DS (Delphi Custom)**.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Pragmata NG+ harder than the first playthrough?**

A: Yes, the "Unknown Signal" mode is significantly more challenging. Enemies have more health, adaptive armor that resists your favorite weapons, and new attack patterns. However, your carried-over upgrades help balance the scales.

**Q: Can I change my difficulty during an Unknown Signal run?**

A: No. Once you commit to the Unknown Signal, the difficulty is locked at a level higher than "Hard." If you find it too difficult, you will need to spend more time in the Vault upgrading your experimental modules.

**Q: Do I need to collect all the audio logs again in pragmata ng+?**

A: Yes. While your character stats carry over, all collectibles and exploration progress reset. This is necessary because the higher-level scanner you possess in NG+ allows you to find items that were invisible during your first run.

**Q: How many endings does Pragmata have?**

A: There are currently three known endings: the Standard ending, the "Silence" alternative ending, and the "True" ending which requires completing the hidden "Echo" chapter and collecting all Lunar Fragments.
