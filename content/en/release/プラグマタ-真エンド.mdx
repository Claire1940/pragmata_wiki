### The "Unknown Signal" Questline
The most important factor in securing the **プラグマタ 真エンド** is the Unknown Signal. This questline begins in Chapter 4 when you encounter a strange interference in the communication array. Instead of following the primary waypoint to the evacuation ship, you must manually calibrate the signal to track a transmission originating from the "Seaside" simulation.

1.  **Locate the Signal:** In the Lunar Maintenance Sector, find the terminal labeled "Project Diana."
2.  **Calibrate the Frequency:** You must match the waveform to the melody Eevee hums during the quiet moments of the game.
3.  **Follow the Echoes:** This will lead you to a hidden area of the moon that mimics an Earth-like beach, setting the stage for the final revelations.

## Key Lore Elements: The Seaside and the Globe

The narrative of the **プラグマタ 真エンド** revolves around a specific promise: "Go find the sea." Throughout the game, the protagonist carries a globe, which serves as a symbol of the "Sweet Home" they are trying to reach. In the true ending, this globe is revealed to be more than a memento—it is a data storage device containing the biological blueprints of Earth’s ecosystem.

During the final cinematic of the **プラグマタ 真エンド**, Eevee remembers the seaside they walked along in a past life or simulation. The loneliness she feels is resolved when the protagonist confirms that their souls are brought together regardless of the distance between the Moon and Earth. This emotional payoff is only available if you have collected all the "Earth Memories" fragments mentioned earlier.

### Necessary Collectibles for the Final Scene
To trigger the extended "Homecoming" cutscene, you must have these specific items in your inventory before entering the Lunar Core:
*   **The Cracked Globe:** Found in the prologue.
*   **The Seaside Shell:** Located in the hidden simulation room during the "Unknown Signal" quest.
*   **Diana’s Data Log:** Dropped by the secret boss.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the プラグマタ 真エンド on my first playthrough?**

A: Yes, it is entirely possible to get the true ending on your first run as long as you complete the "Unknown Signal" questline and collect all 12 Earth Memories fragments before the final chapter.

**Q: What happens if I fail the Diana boss fight?**

A: If you lose the fight, you can retry from the last checkpoint. However, if you choose to "Skip" after multiple failures, the game will default to the normal ending, and you will miss the true conclusion.

**Q: Is the "Seaside" a real place in the game?**

A: The Seaside is a digital simulation used by the Lunar Core to preserve human memories. In the **プラグマタ 真エンド**, it is implied that the characters eventually reach a physical version of it on Earth.

**Q: Are there any specific dialogue choices required for the true ending?**

A: While there isn't a traditional "dialogue tree," your interactions with Eevee (pressing the 'Interact' button during story moments) affect her Trust Meter. High trust is a hidden requirement for the final cinematic.
