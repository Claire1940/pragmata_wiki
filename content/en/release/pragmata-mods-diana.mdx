## Understanding Diana’s Customization in Pragmata

Customization in Pragmata is primarily handled through the Shelter, which serves as your hub between missions. Unlike traditional RPGs where gear might be found in chests, Pragmata utilizes a "Cabin Stamp Card" system. Players earn Cabin Coins by participating in various activities, most notably the Training Sim missions. These missions test your combat and traversal skills, rewarding you with the currency needed to "stamp" your way toward new cosmetic rewards.

As of 2026, there are five distinct levels for your Shelter. Each level corresponds to a new stamp card, and each card contains a variety of rewards, including Hugh’s Exo-layers and Diana’s dresses or tactical suits. To maximize your efficiency, you should focus on "blacking out" these cards—a process that involves filling every single slot on the card to unlock the final, most prestigious reward for that tier.

> **Warning:** Cabin Coins are plentiful, but they are not infinite in the early game. Prioritize stamping cards that offer the specific Diana outfits you want before spending coins on minor consumables or Hugh’s secondary color palettes.

## How to Level Up Your Shelter Fast

To unlock the best outfits for Diana, you need to reach Shelter Level 5. This requires more than just playing through the story; it requires a dedication to the game's side content.

1. **Complete All Five Sectors:** The main story takes you through five distinct sectors. Completing a sector automatically unlocks the next tier of stamp cards.
2. **Master Training Sim Missions:** These are the primary source of Cabin Coins. Aim for "S" ranks to maximize your coin payout per minute.
3. **The Unknown Signal:** After beating the final boss, a new mission type called "Unknown Signal" appears. Clearing the first of these is a hard requirement for reaching Shelter Level 5 and unlocking the Star Lily and Shadow Executor sets.
4. **Bingo Strategy:** Don't just place stamps randomly. Look at the rewards for specific lines (Bingos) on the cards. Some Diana outfits are rewarded for a single line, while others require the full blackout.
