## Official Pragmata System Requirements Breakdown

Capcom has categorized the hardware needs into three distinct tiers: Minimum, Recommended, and Ray Tracing. While the RE Engine is known for its excellent optimization in titles like Resident Evil and Street Fighter, the expansive and vertically-inclined environments of the lunar "Cradle" introduce new demands on both the CPU and GPU. 

The minimum specs are designed for a 1080p experience at roughly 45 FPS using the "Performance" preset. For a smoother 60 FPS experience, players will need to step up to the recommended tier, which targets high settings at native 1080p.

## Ray Tracing and High-End Performance

For players who want to enable the full suite of ray-traced indirect lighting and reflections, the **pragmata system requirements** jump significantly, particularly in the VRAM department. Technical analysis of the demo version indicates that enabling Ray Tracing (RT) pushes VRAM usage past the 8GB threshold, even at 1080p. This makes 12GB cards the new baseline for enthusiast-level visuals.

The RT implementation in this title affects both reflections—visible in the glass partitions of the solar power plants—and indirect lighting, which provides a more natural glow to the lunar base interiors. While the 8GB cards can technically toggle these settings on, users may experience "texture swapping" or flickering as the GPU struggles to manage the memory overflow.

## RE Engine Optimization & Upscaling Technologies

One of the standout features of the PC port is the inclusion of cutting-edge upscaling technology. In 2026, we are seeing the widespread adoption of machine-learning-based upscaling across all major hardware vendors. The game supports **DLSS 3.5** (including Frame Generation) and the latest **FSR 3.1.4**, which includes the new ML-based upscaling formerly known as FSR 4.

### Upscaling Performance Comparison (1440p Native vs Upscaled)

| Mode | Average FPS (RTX 5060 Ti) | Visual Quality |
|---|---|---|
| **Native 1440p** | 85 FPS | Highest |
| **DLSS Quality** | 112 FPS | Excellent |
| **FSR ML Balanced** | 108 FPS | Very Good |
| **Frame Gen On** | 155 FPS | High (Potential Latency) |

Technical tests on mid-range hardware like the RTX 5060 Ti and the RX 960 XT show that the game handles closed-in environments with remarkable ease, often exceeding 100 FPS when utilizing balanced upscaling. However, larger combat arenas with multiple "Cradle" abnormalities can see 1% lows dip, making a strong CPU like the Ryzen 5 5600 or i5-13600K a wise investment to maintain frame time consistency.

## Best Graphics Settings for Performance

If your PC sits right on the edge of the **pragmata system requirements**, you can claw back significant performance by tweaking specific "Heavy" settings. The RE Engine offers a granular menu that allows you to balance visual fidelity with raw speed.

1.  **Mesh Quality:** Set to Medium. This reduces the geometric complexity of distant objects with minimal impact on the immediate environment.
2.  **Ray Traced Reflections:** If you have an 8GB card, turn this OFF. The standard SSR (Screen Space Reflections) are well-implemented and much lighter on VRAM.
3.  **Variable Rate Shading (VRS):** Turn this ON. It reduces shading detail in areas where it isn't noticeable, providing a 5-10% FPS boost.
4.  **Image Quality:** Keep this at 100% and use DLSS or FSR instead of lowering the internal resolution scale manually.

> **💡 Tip:** Ensure you allow the game to complete its "Shader Compilation" step on the main menu before starting your first session. This prevents the "compilation stutter" that often plagues modern PC releases during the first few minutes of gameplay.

## Lunar Combat and Mechanics

The gameplay in the demo introduces a unique "Hacking Matrix" mechanic. Players must move a cursor across a grid while holding the mouse button to connect EXE nodes. This places enemies in a vulnerable state. Because this mechanic requires precision, maintaining a stable frame rate is more than just a visual preference—it directly impacts your ability to execute hacks during high-intensity combat.

The game also features a "Thruster Gauge" for dodging and hovering. Lower frame rates can lead to increased input latency, making it harder to time dodges against the shockwaves emitted by larger enemies. For the best experience, aim for a minimum of 60 FPS, even if it means dropping from "High" to "Medium" settings.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I really need 12GB of VRAM to play with Ray Tracing?**

A: While you can enable Ray Tracing on 8GB cards, you will likely encounter stuttering and low-resolution texture pop-in. For a stable experience with RT enabled, 12GB is the recommended baseline in the **pragmata system requirements**.

**Q: Does the game support Frame Generation?**

A: Yes, the game supports both Nvidia DLSS Frame Generation and the FSR 3.1.4 ML-based Frame Generation, allowing players on a wider range of hardware to achieve high refresh rate gameplay.

**Q: Is there a significant difference between the Minimum and Recommended specs?**

A: Yes. The minimum specs target 45 FPS at 1080p on lower settings. To reach the gold standard of 60 FPS at 1080p with high-quality assets, you should aim for the recommended hardware, specifically a GPU with at least 8GB of VRAM.

**Q: Can I run Pragmata on a Steam Deck or handheld PC?**

A: Thanks to the scalability of the RE Engine and FSR support, the game is playable on handhelds at 800p/720p. However, you will need to use the "Performance" preset and set the 30 FPS cap for the most consistent experience.
