## Unlocking the Shelter and Outfit Storage

Before you can begin swapping skins, you must progress through the initial stages of the game. Customization is not available immediately upon starting a new game; instead, it is tied to the narrative progression of the prologue.

To access your **pragmata outfits**, you must complete the opening sequence and reach the **Shelter**. The Shelter serves as the primary hub for Hugh and Diana, offering a place of respite between missions. Once you have unlocked this location during the prologue, you can find the outfit storage area.

Follow these steps to locate the customization terminal:
1. Enter the Shelter after it is unlocked in the story.
2. Navigate to the back section of the living quarters.
3. Look for the interaction prompt near the storage lockers.
4. Press **Y** (on controller) or **F** (on keyboard) to open the Outfit Storage menu.

> **💡 Tip:** If you purchased the Deluxe Edition or the Shelter Variety Pack, these items will automatically appear in your storage once you reach this point. There is no need to manually "claim" them from an external menu.

## Changing Your Pragmata Outfits: A Step-by-Step Guide

The customization menu is divided into two main categories: Hugh (the protagonist) and Diana. Each character has a unique set of costumes that do not overlap, allowing for distinct visual pairings during gameplay.

| Step | Action | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Open Menu** | Interact with the Outfit Storage terminal in the Shelter. |
| **2** | **Select Character** | Choose between **Hugh** or **Diana** to view their specific wardrobe. |
| **3** | **Browse Styles** | Use the D-pad or arrow keys to cycle through available skins. |
| **4** | **Equip** | Press **A** (or the confirm key) to wear the selected outfit. |
| **5** | **Save & Exit** | Press **B** or Escape to return to the Shelter with your new look. |

When browsing, you will notice that the game provides a full 360-degree preview of the characters. This is particularly useful for checking the intricate details on the back of Hugh’s EVA suits or the textures on Diana’s specialized dresses.

## Available Outfits for Hugh

Hugh’s wardrobe primarily consists of high-tech exosuits and specialized lunar gear. These **pragmata outfits** reflect his role as a protector and explorer. While the default Delphi Suit is iconic, the alternative options provide a much different silhouette during combat and exploration.

| Outfit Name | Source | Visual Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Delphi Suit** | Default | The standard white and blue EVA suit seen in trailers. |
| **Heavy Lifter** | Shelter Variety Pack | A bulkier, industrial-grade suit with yellow accents. |
| **Lunar Cat** | Shelter Variety Pack | A sleek, dark-themed suit featuring cat-ear helmet sensors. |

The **Neo Bushido** suit is a fan favorite for its unique blend of feudal Japanese aesthetics and futuristic lunar technology. If you missed the pre-order window, check the digital storefront for your platform, as Capcom occasionally releases pre-order bonuses as standalone DLC months after launch.

## Available Outfits for Diana

Diana’s outfits are generally more varied in theme than Hugh’s, often leaning into more whimsical or "cute" designs that contrast with the harsh environment of the Moon. These **pragmata outfits** change her appearance in cutscenes and during the various gameplay segments where she assists Hugh.

| Outfit Name | Source | Visual Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Dress** | Default | The simple, functional dress Diana wears at the start. |

| **Mecha Builder** | Shelter Variety Pack | A practical, tool-belt equipped outfit for "engineering." |

> **Warning:** Changing outfits does not grant gameplay advantages or stat boosts. These items are strictly cosmetic and will not affect your defense or oxygen consumption levels.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my outfit at any time during a mission?**

A: No, you can only change your **pragmata outfits** while inside the Shelter. Once you depart for a mission or enter a combat zone, your current equipment is locked until you return to the hub.

**Q: Do I need an internet connection to use my DLC outfits?**

A: While you need an internet connection to download and verify the licenses for DLC like the Shelter Variety Pack, the outfits can be used offline once they are successfully installed on your system.

**Q: Are there secret outfits unlocked by completing the game?**

A: Yes, completing the main story on higher difficulty settings (such as "Hard" or "Lunar Veteran") typically unlocks "Mastery" skins for Hugh and Diana. These reflect their experiences throughout the campaign and often feature battle-worn textures.

**Q: Does the "Neo Bushido" outfit include a sword?**

A: The Neo Bushido outfit is a visual skin for Hugh's armor. While it features aesthetic elements of a samurai, it does not change your primary weapons or add a functional katana to your loadout.

**Q: How do I access the Diana Gestures included in the Variety Pack?**

A: Gestures are accessed through the Photo Mode menu or by using the emote wheel (defaulted to the Right D-pad or the 'G' key). These are separate from the outfit selection but can be used while wearing any of the available costumes.
