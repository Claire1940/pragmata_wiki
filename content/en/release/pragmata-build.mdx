## Understanding the Overheat Mechanic

The foundation of this build lies in the "Overheat" gauge, a red meter located directly beneath an enemy's health bar. In *Pragmata*, most mechanical enemies have a threshold for thermal stress. Once this gauge reaches 100%, the enemy enters a "Downed" state. While downed, they are completely vulnerable, allowing you to execute a high-damage critical melee strike (often triggered with the R2/RT button).

This build isn't just about dealing damage; it's about the economy of movement and resources. By utilizing Diana’s hacking abilities to spike this heat gauge, you can save ammunition for the toughest boss encounters while clearing standard mobs with ease. The "Combustion" aspect of the build refers to the explosive nature of the critical strikes, which, when properly modded, can clear surrounding enemies in a single blow.

## Essential Mods for the Combustion Build

To make this **pragmata build** truly "broken," you must equip specific mods that create a self-sustaining loop of heat and energy. The goal is to reach a state where your hacking nodes generate heat, your heat triggers criticals, and your criticals recharge your hacking abilities.

### Core Mod Breakdown

1.  **Heat Transfer**: This is the mandatory baseline for the build. It provides a flat 10% increase to all heat generation sources. This applies to your bullets, your hacking nodes, and even environmental hazards.
2.  **Nice Nodes**: In *Pragmata*, hacking nodes are semi-random drops. This mod increases the drop rate of these nodes by 10%. Specifically, we are looking for "Heat Nodes" to drop more frequently during combat.
3.  **Collateral Damage**: This is the primary AOE (Area of Effect) tool. When you land a critical strike on an overheated enemy, 30% of that damage is dealt to all nearby enemies. Given that critical strikes can hit for upwards of 5,000 damage, the splash damage alone is enough to kill weaker drones.
4.  **Untapped Potential**: A versatile buff that rewards your hacking playstyle. Using a hacking node increases your attack power by 10% and reduces incoming damage by 10% for 15 seconds.
5.  **Close Quarters**: Since the critical strike requires you to be in melee range, this mod is a natural fit. It increases all damage by 10% when you are within 10 meters of your target.

> **Warning:** While this build is extremely powerful, it requires you to stay within 10 meters of enemies. Ensure your dodge timing is perfected, as Lunatic mode enemies can punish a missed hack attempt instantly.

## The Hacking Loop: Infinite Overcharge

The true power of this setup is the "Self-Fulfilling Loop" between hacking and Overcharge. When you use your Overcharge attack (Diana’s ultimate hacking ability), the hacking grids on all enemies become significantly simplified. This removes the complex "red error" nodes that usually slow down your progress.

Because the grids are easier to solve, you can spam hacks faster. Each successful hack contributes to your Overcharge meter. This results in a cycle where you are almost always in a state of Overcharge, making the entire battlefield susceptible to instant overheating. This is particularly effective against shielded enemies who would otherwise require minutes of sustained gunfire to break down.

| Build Component | Synergistic Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Overcharge Active** | Simplifies hacking grids; removes error nodes |
| **Heat Nodes** | Rapidly fills the red gauge during simple hacks |
| **Combust Nodes** | Default blue nodes that add bonus thermal damage |
| **Critical Strike** | Finishes the enemy and spreads damage via Collateral mod |

## Recommended Weapon Loadouts

While this **pragmata build** can technically function using only hacking and melee, weapons serve as a vital "bridge" to maintain heat levels while your hacking cooldowns reset. 

The **Shotgun** is the undisputed king for this build. Because you are already incentivized to stay within 10 meters for the *Close Quarters* mod, the shotgun’s high pellet count allows for rapid heat application. Each pellet that strikes a mechanical surface contributes a small amount of friction-based heat. When combined with *Heat Transfer*, a single well-placed blast can jump the heat gauge by 20-30%.

Alternatively, a **Rapid-Fire SMG** can be used to "tickle" the heat gauge from a slightly safer distance. This is useful for bosses with high-damage point-blank AOE attacks that prevent you from standing directly on top of them.

## Advanced Combat Strategy

To maximize the efficiency of your **pragmata build**, follow this tactical rotation during encounters:

1.  **Initiation**: Deploy a Decoy Generator to draw initial aggro. This gives you the breathing room to start your first hack.
2.  **Hacking**: Focus on a single high-priority target (like a Heavy Shield Bearer). Use your hacking nodes to build heat until the gauge turns orange.
3.  **Overcharge**: If the room is crowded, trigger your Overcharge immediately. This will "soften" the grids of every enemy in sight.
4.  **The Finisher**: Once the target is downed, move in for the R2 critical strike. Watch as the *Collateral Damage* mod knocks down or destroys the surrounding support units.
5.  **Cleanup**: Use your shotgun to finish off any stragglers, which will also help refill your remaining Overcharge meter for the next wave.

## Scaling for Lunatic Mode

In Lunatic mode, enemies have significantly higher resistance to heat and can "cool down" faster if they aren't hit continuously. To counter this, you must prioritize the **Untapped Potential** mod. The 10% damage reduction is a lifesaver when you are forced into close-quarters combat with aggressive AI.

Additionally, ensure you are actively hunting for "Heat Nodes" dropped by downed enemies. These are blue or orange glowing pickups that appear after a critical strike. Collecting these ensures that your next hack starts with a "Heat Multiplier," allowing you to overheat even the tankiest Lunatic mode bosses in seconds.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the Heat Transfer mod stack with weapon-specific heat upgrades?**

A: Yes, the 10% increase from Heat Transfer is a global multiplier. It stacks additively with any thermal-based upgrades you have equipped on your shotgun or rifle, making it a cornerstone for any heat-focused **pragmata build**.

**Q: Can I use this build effectively in co-op play?**

A: Absolutely. In co-op, one player can focus on the Combustion/Overheat setup while the other provides long-range sniper support. The player using the overheat build acts as the "tank" and crowd controller, creating openings for the sniper to land massive damage on downed targets.

**Q: What should I do if I run out of hacking nodes?**

A: If you run out of nodes, rely on your shotgun to maintain the heat gauge. The *Nice Nodes* mod should be equipped to prevent this scenario, as it significantly increases node drops from every enemy you defeat.

**Q: Is the Collateral Damage mod effective against bosses?**

A: While the 30% splash damage is less useful against a single lone boss, it is invaluable during boss phases that spawn "adds" or smaller minions. The critical strike on the boss will often clear the entire room of smaller threats automatically.
