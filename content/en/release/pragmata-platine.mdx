## Optimizing Settings for your Pragmata Platine Run

Before jumping into the lunar wasteland, adjusting your settings is a critical step for a smooth trophy hunt. Pragmata features a somewhat slow default camera speed, which can be a hindrance during fast-paced boss encounters. We recommend maximizing your camera sensitivity to ensure you can track mobile enemies like Watchers and Walkers effectively.

Additionally, fine-tuning your aiming deceleration is highly recommended. Setting deceleration to zero allows for consistent aiming speeds, making it easier to build muscle memory for hitting enemy weak points. For PlayStation 5 players, turning off adaptive triggers can reduce finger fatigue during the long sessions required for the **pragmata platine** journey.

| Setting | Recommended Value | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Camera Speed** | Max / 100 | Compensates for the naturally slow rotation speed. |
| **Aiming Sensitivity** | 60 | Balances precision with target acquisition speed. |
| **Deceleration** | 0 | Ensures consistent crosshair movement across the screen. |
| **Motion Blur** | Off | Improves visual clarity during high-speed dashes. |
| **Adaptive Triggers**| Off | Provides a more consistent firing feel for the Grip Gun. |

## Movement and Combat Fundamentals

Movement in Pragmata relies heavily on a stamina-based jetpack system. To cover large gaps—which is essential for reaching several hidden Upgrade Components—you must master the "Jump, Dash, Glide" combo. If you glide before dashing, you will often find yourself short on fuel, leading to a fatal fall. Always initiate your dash at the peak of your jump before deploying the glide function for maximum distance.

Combat revolves around the Hacking mechanic. When you aim at an enemy, you can initiate a hack that exposes their blue inner core. Attacking these exposed parts deals significantly more damage. It is highly effective to hack an enemy once and then switch to high-damage weapons like the Shotgun to finish them off.

## Collectibles: Mini Cabins and Upgrade Components

The path to a **pragmata platine** requires finding every Mini Cabin and Upgrade Component hidden across the lunar surface. Mini Cabins are small, destructible objects that emit a distinct audio cue when you are nearby. Upgrade Components are used at the Firmware Updater in the Shelter to increase your weapon's level, ammo capacity, and reload speed.

1. **Mini Cabin #1**: Located high above the main path in the Solar Power Plant. Look for the small cabin icon near the vertical rail.
2. **Mini Cabin #2**: Found inside a bunk behind a mesh net in the laboratory area.
3. **Mini Cabin #3**: Positioned on a vent above the entrance door in the Main Control Lobby.
4. **Hollow Walls**: These are illusory walls that can only be passed by pressing the interact button. One is located near the third Mini Cabin and contains the "Crayons" gift for Diana.

## The Shelter: Diana's Affinity and Bingo

The Shelter acts as your hub. Between missions, you must interact with Diana to raise her affinity. This is tied to several trophies, including "Small Talk" and "For Me?". To maximize affinity, always exhaust her dialogue until she begins to repeat herself and present her with gifts like the REM Globe or Crayons.

The "Cabin Guy" in the Shelter offers a Bingo mini-game using Cabin Coins earned from gifts. Completing lines on the Bingo card provides essential rewards, such as the Red Gate Key and additional Repair Cartridges.

| Bingo Line | Reward 1 | Reward 2 | Reward 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Vertical Middle** | Crusher Bot Data | Walker Bot Data | **Red Gate Key** |
| **Horizontal Top** | 100 Lunar Filament | Upgrade Component | Stasis Ammo |
| **Diagonal** | 500 Lunar Filament | Hardened Suit Mod | Repair Cartridge |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always make a manual save at the Shelter before starting a new mission. The game does not allow manual saving while in active combat zones.

## Boss Strategy: The First Crusher

The first major boss encounter tests your mastery of the Stasis Net and the Overdrive system. This boss typically performs two consecutive dashes; dodging both creates a window for a counter-attack. Use the Stasis Net to lock the boss in place, then move behind it to target the orange fuel tank on its back.

Once the boss's health is low enough, you will unlock the **Overdrive** ability (L3 + R3). This mode grants temporary invincibility and massive damage boosts. Use this to bypass the boss's final phase and secure the "Critical Attack" trophy. For more details on boss patterns, visit the [official Pragmata site](https://www.capcom-games.com/pragmata/) for community updates.

## Roadmap to the Platinum Trophy

To achieve the **pragmata platine** efficiently in 2026, we recommend a two-playthrough approach:

*   **Playthrough 1 (Casual/Normal):** Focus on 100% collectible completion, exhausting all of Diana's dialogue, and mastering the Bingo cards. This allows you to enter the final difficulty with a fully upgraded character.
*   **Playthrough 2 (Lunatic):** This is the hardest mode. Use your knowledge of enemy weak points and the Stasis Net to bypass difficult encounters. Since you already have the collectibles, you can focus purely on survival and speed.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I miss any trophies during my pragmata platine run?**

A: Yes, affinity trophies with Diana can be missed if you do not talk to her between every major mission. Additionally, some Mini Cabins are in areas that become inaccessible later in the story, so following a guide for each area is recommended.

**Q: How do I get more Repair Cartridges?**

A: You start with one. You can find a Cartridge Holder in the Solar Power Plant side room, and another can be earned by completing a line on the Cabin Guy's Bingo card.

**Q: What is the best way to farm Lunar Filament?**

A: Defeating enemies and looting crates are the primary methods. However, completing Bingo lines and finding hidden chests (often containing 100+ filament) is the most efficient way to build your currency for weapon printing.

**Q: Does difficulty affect the drop rate of Upgrade Components?**

A: No, Upgrade Components are fixed collectibles found in specific locations. Their placement remains the same regardless of whether you are playing on Casual or Lunatic difficulty.
