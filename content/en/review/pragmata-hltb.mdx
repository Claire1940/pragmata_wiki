## All Sectors in Pragmata

The journey of Hugh and Diana is divided into six distinct sectors. These areas serve as the game's levels, each offering a unique visual theme and mechanical challenge. While most sectors are large and filled with secrets, some are narrative-heavy and can be completed much faster than others.

### 1. Solar Power Plant
As the introductory area, the Solar Power Plant sets the tone for the game's dystopian lunar setting. You will spend roughly 1.5 to 2 hours here learning the basic combat mechanics and how to interact with Diana. There are several "Earth Memory" collectibles hidden near the cooling towers that are easy to miss on a first pass.

### 2. Mass Production Array
This sector shifts the focus toward environmental puzzles and stealth. The industrial corridors are patrolling with hostile drones, requiring a more tactical approach. Expect to spend about 2 hours here, especially if you are hunting for the manufacturing blueprints required for high-level gear upgrades.

### 3. Terra Dome
The Terra Dome is arguably the most beautiful sector in the game, featuring a simulated Earth environment. Because of its open layout, this area often takes players longer to navigate—usually around 2.5 hours. It contains the highest density of side objectives and lore entries.

### 4. Lunum Mines
A claustrophobic and dangerous trek through the moon's crust. The Lunum Mines emphasize resource management and verticality. Combat encounters here are more frequent and intense, which can pad out the playtime to roughly 2 hours depending on the difficulty setting.

### 5. Experimental Pragmatics
This is a unique, narrative-driven sector. Unlike the others, Experimental Pragmatics contains no collectibles and is relatively linear. Most players will breeze through this section in 30 to 45 minutes as the story begins to reach its climax.

### 6. Central Port
The final gauntlet. Central Port is a massive orbital elevator and docking station. It features the game's most difficult boss encounters and a series of high-stakes set pieces. Reaching the final credits from the start of this sector usually takes about 2 hours.

## Factors That Influence Your Playtime

While the **pragmata hltb** averages provide a solid baseline, several variables can extend or shorten your stay on the moon. Capcom has designed the game to reward curiosity, but they also provide tools for those who want a streamlined experience.

### Difficulty Settings
Pragmata features three standard difficulty modes at launch. On "Easy," combat encounters are trivial, allowing you to focus entirely on the story and exploration. On "Hard," enemies have significantly more health and more aggressive AI patterns, which can easily add 2-3 hours to a total playthrough due to retries and the need for more careful resource gathering.

### Exploration and Backtracking
The game uses a "Metroidvania-lite" approach in certain sectors. Some areas are locked behind gear upgrades that Hugh acquires later in the story. If you are the type of player who enjoys returning to previous sectors to unlock secret rooms and high-tier loot crates, you should expect your playtime to lean toward the 12-hour mark.

### Post-Game Challenges
Once the credits roll, Pragmata unlocks several "Lunar Trials." These are combat and movement-based challenges that test your mastery of the game's mechanics. Completing all of these trials is required for the 100% completion rating and can add an additional 2 to 3 hours of gameplay.

## Achievement Hunting and 100% Completion

For the trophy hunters and achievement seekers, Pragmata offers 35 distinct challenges. Most of these are tied to story progression and combat milestones (e.g., "Defeat 100 Drones"), but a handful require specific, missable actions within the sectors.

*   **Earth Memory Collector:** Find all 50 lore fragments scattered across the moon.
*   **Diana’s Protector:** Complete the game without Diana taking significant damage during escort sequences.
*   **Lunar Historian:** Unlock every entry in the game's codex.
*   **Apex Explorer:** Reach the highest point in the Terra Dome sector.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pragmata have a New Game Plus mode?**

A: Yes, Pragmata features a New Game Plus (NG+) mode that allows you to carry over your gear upgrades, cosmetics, and codex entries. Playing through NG+ is often faster, as you can bypass early-game resource grinding, but it is a great way to clean up any missing achievements for your **pragmata hltb** completionist goals.

**Q: Can I fast travel between sectors to find missed collectibles?**

A: Fast travel becomes available once you reach the Central Port sector. However, it is limited to major relay points. It is generally recommended to explore as much as possible during your first visit to a sector to minimize the need for late-game backtracking.

**Q: Is there any co-op or multiplayer content that extends the playtime?**

A: At launch, Pragmata is a strictly single-player experience. There are no multiplayer modes, though there are online leaderboards for the post-game "Lunar Trials" where you can compare your clear times with friends and the global community.

**Q: How many endings does Pragmata have?**

A: The game features one primary ending, but there are minor variations in the final cutscene based on how many "Earth Memory" collectibles you have recovered and your overall relationship level with Diana. To see the "True Ending," you will likely need to invest about 15 hours to achieve high completion stats.
