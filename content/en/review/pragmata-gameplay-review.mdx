## Pragmata Gameplay Review: Combat and Hacking Mechanics

The heart of the experience lies in the "Codependent Cooperation" between Hugh and Diana. Hugh provides the raw firepower, while Diana handles the technical side of warfare. Enemies encountered on the station are often encased in advanced armor that is nearly impervious to standard gunfire. To succeed, players must utilize Diana’s hacking abilities to strip away these defenses.

Hacking is not a passive event; it is a real-time, grid-based minigame. When Hugh aims his weapon, a square grid appears. Players must navigate a cursor through blue nodes to build power before activating a final hack on green nodes. This occurs while enemies are still actively attacking, requiring players to balance movement and puzzle-solving simultaneously.

| Combat Element | Function | Strategic Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Gunfire** | Basic damage output | Used to finish off enemies after armor is removed. |
| **Grid Hacking** | Diana's primary ability | Disables enemy armor and shields in real-time. |
| **Perfect Dodge** | R1 / RB maneuver | Triggers a slowdown effect, giving Diana more time to hack. |
| **Finisher Moves** | Contextual attacks | High-damage strikes triggered by targeting enemy weaknesses. |

> **💡 Tip:** Use Hugh’s thrusters to hover during combat. This verticality often provides a better view of the hacking grid while keeping you out of reach of ground-based robots.

## Weapon Systems and Resource Management

Hugh’s spacesuit features four dedicated units for weapon slots. These are categorized into standard tactics, defense, and attack. Unlike many modern shooters where you carry an infinite arsenal, weapons in this game are treated as "standalone packages." Once the ammo is depleted, the weapon may break or become unusable until a new icon or resource is found in the environment.

### Weapon Categories

*   **Standard Pistol:** Your reliable 12 o'clock slot weapon for consistent, low-impact fire.
*   **Shotgun:** High-impact close-range weapon ideal for clearing "rank and file" droids.
*   **Net Trap:** A tactical secondary used to immobilize faster enemies, allowing for easier hacking.
*   **Heavy Ordnance:** Limited-use weapons found in arenas, capable of massive burst damage against bosses.

## Exploration: The Shelter and The Tram

Survival on the moon requires more than just quick reflexes; it requires careful preparation. The game features a safe zone known as "The Shelter." This is where players meet Cabin, an affable robot who assists with station upgrades. Within the Shelter, players use 3D printers and collected Lunafilament to enhance weapon nodes and hacking efficiency.

To move between the sprawling sectors of the research station, players utilize the Tram system. This fast-travel network connects various "Escape Hatches" that serve as both checkpoints and access points to the Shelter. As you unlock new abilities, you will often find yourself returning to previous areas to access previously locked containers or shortcuts.

| Exploration Feature | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Escape Hatches** | Act as checkpoints and fast travel nodes for the Tram. |
| **3D Printers** | Found in the Shelter; used to craft permanent gear upgrades. |
| **Lunum Ore** | The primary currency used for upgrading Hugh's suit and Diana's nodes. |
| **Thruster Meter** | Limits how long Hugh can dash or hover during platforming sections. |

## The Sketchbook Demo Experience

For those hesitant to purchase the full game, "The Sketchbook" demo provides a substantial look at the opening hours. It includes a challenging boss fight that tests everything you have learned about the synergy between Hugh and Diana. Completing the demo also rewards players with a unique suit design and early access to specific weapon types in the full 2026 release.

The demo highlights the "Vanquish-style" mobility, where Hugh can slide-dodge and use jetpack bursts to navigate tight boss arenas. It serves as a perfect vertical slice of the game's mechanics, proving that the long development cycle has resulted in a polished and unique sci-fi experience.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Ray tracing is not supported on Xbox Series S versions of the demo or the full game due to hardware limitations. Ensure your settings are adjusted for framerate stability on that platform.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Pragmata a soulslike or a traditional shooter?**

A: It is primarily a third-person sci-fi shooter. While it features challenging boss fights and resource management, the focus is on the synergy between the two protagonists and real-time hacking rather than the high-punishment loop typical of the soulslike genre.

**Q: Can I play as Diana in the game?**

A: Players primarily control Hugh Williams. However, Diana is an active participant in every battle. You control her hacking nodes via the face buttons while simultaneously moving and aiming with Hugh.

**Q: How long is the Pragmata gameplay review period for the campaign?**

A: Early reports suggest the main story takes approximately 15-20 hours to complete, with additional time required for players looking to fully upgrade their gear at the Shelter and discover all secrets on the moon station.

**Q: Does the game support co-op play?**

A: Pragmata is designed as a single-player experience focusing on the relationship between Hugh and Diana. There is currently no official support for local or online co-op.
