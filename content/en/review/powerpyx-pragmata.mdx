## Getting Started: Core Mechanics and Controls

Pragmata is not your typical third-person shooter. It emphasizes a "hack-and-shoot" rhythm where environmental interaction is just as important as ammunition management. You play as Hugh, an armored operative tasked with protecting Diana, a young girl with mysterious hacking abilities.

### Combat Synergy
Hugh handles the heavy ordinance, utilizing a variety of Attack and Tactical Units. However, enemies often deploy "Jamming" fields that render your high-tech weaponry useless. This is where Diana comes in. You must protect her while she interfaces with the network to disable enemy shields or turn automated turrets against their masters.

| Mechanic | Function | Strategy Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Attack Units** | Primary damage dealing (Rifles, Launchers) | Switch units based on enemy armor type. |
| **Tactical Units** | Support tools (Gravity Wells, Shields) | Use Gravity Wells to group enemies for Diana’s AOE hacks. |
| **Hacking Puzzles** | Disabling locks and enemy buffs | Keep Hugh close to Diana to prevent her from being interrupted. |
| **Earth Memories** | Lore-based collectibles | Scan glowing blue silhouettes to reconstruct past events. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always keep an eye on Diana’s "Sync Gauge." When fully charged, you can perform a Dual Tech move that clears the immediate area of robotic threats.

## Character Progression: Upgrading Hugh and Diana

Progression in Pragmata is tied to "Lunum," a rare lunar mineral used to synthesize new parts. You should prioritize upgrading Hugh’s suit integrity and Diana’s hacking speed before focusing on raw weapon damage.

### Priority Upgrade Path
1.  **Suit Integrity:** Increases Hugh’s health pool. Essential for surviving the "IDUS" bot swarms.
2.  **Hacking Speed:** Reduces the time Diana is vulnerable during combat puzzles.
3.  **Unit Slots:** Allows you to carry multiple types of Attack Units, giving you versatility against different bot archetypes.
4.  **Scanner Range:** Makes finding hidden collectibles like the Mr. Cabin figures much easier.

> **Warning:** Do not spend all your Lunum on weapon damage early on. The game’s difficulty spikes in Sector 3, where survival depends more on tactical utility than pure firepower.

## Training Simulations and Optional Objectives

Scattered throughout the Lunar Base are Training Simulation terminals. These are VR-style challenges that test your mastery over specific mechanics. Completing these with "Gold" rankings provides the best rewards in the game, including exclusive outfits.

### Simulation Guide
*   **Bad Blood:** You must defeat 4 enemies using friendly fire. The best way to achieve this is by using a Gravity Well Tactical Unit to pull bots into the path of a "Charger" bot’s dash attack.
*   **Catch Me If You Can:** A traversal challenge requiring you to reach the exit within 100 seconds. Utilize the "Jump Jets" upgrade to skip the lower platforms entirely.

## Advanced Combat Tactics

As you progress deeper into the Lunar Base, you will encounter "Elite" variants of the standard bot units. These enemies require a combination of specific elemental Attack Units and Diana’s hacking.

### Dealing with Enemy Jamming
If you see a red "WARNING" icon on your HUD, an enemy is jamming your weapons. You will be unable to fire or use Tactical Units until the source is destroyed. Use Hugh’s physical melee attacks to stun the jamming unit, then command Diana to "Purge" the signal. This opening is the best time to unleash your heaviest ordinance.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find all the Mini Cabin Buddies in Pragmata?**

A: Mini Cabin Buddies are often hidden behind destructible walls or on high ledges. The best way to find them is to upgrade your Scanner Range early. If you are following a **powerpyx pragmata** guide, look for the golden glimmer in dark corners of the Solar Power Plant and Mass Production Array.

**Q: Can I revisit sectors to collect missed items?**

A: Yes, once you reach Sector 4, you unlock the "Fast Travel" terminal at the main hub. This allows you to return to previous areas with new abilities, such as the Sector 2 Hacking Tool, to access previously locked Safe Boxes.

**Q: What is the best early-game loadout?**

A: We recommend the **Standard Issue Rifle** paired with the **Gravity Well Tactical Unit**. The Rifle is versatile for all ranges, and the Gravity Well allows Diana to hack multiple enemies simultaneously, making crowd control much easier.

**Q: Is there a New Game Plus mode?**

A: Yes, completing the main campaign unlocks New Game Plus. You retain all your suit upgrades, weapons, and collectibles. This mode also introduces "Nightmare" difficulty, which provides exclusive materials for the final weapon tiers.
