## The Core Premise: Hugh and Diana

In *Pragmata*, the narrative focuses on the isolation of the Moon and the desperate attempt to reconnect with Earth. Hugh Williams is an engineer who appears to be a "grunt" caught in a larger conspiracy involving the Delphi Corporation. His companion, Diana, is not just a secondary character but a vital mechanical asset. 

While Hugh handles the heavy lifting and direct combat, Diana provides tactical support through hacking and environmental manipulation. Their journey to the top floor of the solar power plant serves as the primary tutorial for the game's mechanics, introducing players to the "Cradle" and the "Edis" AI system that manages the facility's automated defenses.

## Combat Systems: A Pragmata First Impression Guide to Weapons

Combat in *Pragmata* is a deliberate affair that rewards positioning and resource management over mindless spraying. Your primary unit features rechargeable ammo, meaning you must balance your aggression with downtime. As you progress, you unlock specialized tactical units that change the flow of battle.

### Primary and Tactical Weaponry

| Weapon Type | Range | Primary Effect | Best Used For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary Unit** | Mid | Consistent Damage | Standard Walkers and Grunts |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Short | Stagger & Suppression | Close-range crowd control |
| **Stasis Net** | Mid | Immobilization | Freezing fast-moving targets |
| **Critical Shot** | Melee/Close | High Finisher Damage | Downed or staggered enemies |

The **Shockwave Gun** acts as a futuristic shotgun, dealing massive damage at close range and making enemies significantly easier to stagger. However, its effectiveness drops off sharply with distance. On the other hand, the **Stasis Net** is a tactical necessity for managing large groups, as it fires an electromagnetic net that freezes enemies in place for a short duration.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Dodging and jumping consume your Thruster Gauge. If the gauge falls too low, your mobility actions are disabled, leaving you vulnerable to enemy projectiles.

## Hacking and Environmental Interaction

One of the most unique aspects of the *Pragmata* experience is the hacking matrix. Unlike many action games where hacking is a simple button press, *Pragmata* utilizes a cursor-based mini-game where you must navigate through "executable nodes."

### Understanding Hacking Nodes

During a hack, you will encounter various nodes that provide buffs or trigger environmental effects. Passing through multiple nodes of the same type increases their effectiveness, encouraging players to plan their route through the matrix carefully.

| Node Type | Color | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Executable Node** | Green | Completes the hacking objective |
| **Disruption Node** | Yellow | Temporarily increases damage dealt to the target |
| **System Node** | Blue | Standard progress markers within the matrix |

Successfully navigating these matrices is required to open locked doors, activate elevators, and even weaken boss-level enemies. Diana’s **Overdrive Protocol** is also tied to your hacking performance. Once the hacking gauge is full, you can trigger a massive area-of-effect blast that immobilizes nearby enemies and deals significant damage.

## Survival and Exploration Mechanics

Exploration in *Pragmata* is vertical and often requires the use of Hugh's jetpack legs. However, survival isn't just about avoiding damage; it's about utilizing the environment to your advantage.

### Escape Hatches and Shelters

Throughout the lunar facility, you will find **Escape Hatches**. These serve as vital checkpoints.
1. **Activation:** Diana must hack the hatch to make it operational.
2. **Restoration:** Returning to a shelter via an escape hatch fully recovers your suit integrity.
3. **Upgrades:** The shelter is the primary hub for upgrading gear and interacting with Diana to deepen the narrative bond.

> **💡 Tip:** Always look for Hologram Surveillance logs. These provide lore about the Delphi Corporation and often hint at upcoming enemy encounters or hidden loot caches.

## Boss Strategy: The Sector Guard

The first major hurdle in your journey is the Sector Guard, a massive mechanical construct deployed by the facility's AI, Edith. This boss fight tests your ability to switch between weapons and utilize the environment.

*   **Phase 1: Shield Removal.** Use the Shockwave gun to stagger the guard while dodging its missile volleys.
*   **Phase 2: The Hacking Window.** Once the guard is staggered, Diana can initiate a hack. Focus on hitting **Yellow Disruption Nodes** to maximize your damage output for the next phase.
*   **Phase 3: The Finisher.** When the guard's health is critical, move in for a **Critical Shot**. This involves a quick-time event (QTE) that initiates a powerful follow-up attack, effectively neutralizing the threat.

The combat feels weighty, and according to early [official Capcom development updates](https://www.capcom.com), the team has focused heavily on the tactile feel of the lunar environment.

## Technical Performance and Controls

While *Pragmata* is a visually stunning title with impressive reflections and particle effects, the control scheme is clearly optimized for controllers. Mouse and keyboard players may find the hacking mini-game slightly more challenging due to the precision required to navigate the matrix while under fire.

| Control Action | PC Keybind (Default) | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Attack** | Left Mouse Button | Fires equipped weapon |
| **Tactical Swap** | Q / E | Cycles through Shockwave and Stasis units |
| **Dodge/Jump** | Space / Shift | Consumes Thruster Gauge for mobility |
| **Hacking Matrix** | Alt + Mouse | Navigates the hacking interface |

The sensitivity settings are quite high by default, so it is recommended to adjust your DPI or in-game settings before entering the first major combat zone.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Pragmata an open-world game?**

A: Based on our **pragmata first impression guide**, the game features large, interconnected sectors within the lunar colony rather than a traditional open world. It focuses on mission-based progression with environmental exploration.

**Q: Can I play as Diana?**

A: In the current build, you primarily control Hugh Williams. Diana acts as a sophisticated AI companion who handles hacking and tactical support, though she is central to many gameplay mechanics and the story.

**Q: How do I heal in Pragmata?**

A: Healing is primarily done at Escape Hatches or by using specific repair items found in the environment. Returning to a Shelter via a hacked hatch will fully restore your suit's integrity.

**Q: What platforms is Pragmata available on in 2026?**

A: *Pragmata* is available on PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC. The game features high-end ray tracing and haptic feedback support for the DualSense controller.
