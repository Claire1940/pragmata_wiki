## Analyzing the Core Sentiment in Pragmata Steam User Reviews

### Combat Mechanics: The Risk-Reward Loop
The combat is not a simple "point and shoot" affair. Enemies are often invulnerable to standard ballistic fire until their shields are neutralized. This requires the player to protect Diana while she navigates a hacking grid. According to many **pragmata steam user reviews**, this creates a high-tension environment where you must balance positioning, thruster management, and timing.

1.  **Engage the Enemy:** Use thrusters to maintain distance and draw aggro.
2.  **Initiate Hacking:** Diana begins the shield-stripping sequence.
3.  **Navigate the Grid:** The player (or Diana, depending on the tool) must route a cursor through a digital maze.
4.  **Execute:** Once the shield drops, weapons become devastatingly effective.

## Systems-Heavy Gameplay and AI Interaction

Beyond the shooting, the game is a "systems-heavy" experience. This means that physics, lighting, and AI behaviors interact in real-time rather than being scripted. For instance, an explosion near a glass partition doesn't just play an animation; the shards interact with the energy shields of nearby enemies.

Many positive **pragmata steam user reviews** highlight this emergent gameplay. Players have reported using Diana's hacking tools to turn facility security turrets against enemies, or using thruster hovers to avoid ground-based shockwaves that would otherwise interrupt a hacking sequence.

> **Warning:** Be mindful of Diana's positioning. While she is not a "glass cannon," if she takes too much damage, her hacking speed significantly decreases, leaving you vulnerable to armored enemies for longer periods.
