## How to Unlock the Secret Elevator Enemy

The most difficult reward to obtain is Diana’s second drawing. This is tied directly to a hidden encounter during the elevator ascent. Most players will encounter three standard bots and move on, but there is a fourth, "Secret Enemy" that only appears if you follow a very specific combat protocol.

To trigger this encounter, you must finish off each of the three original elevator bots with a **different weapon source**. If you use the same weapon to deliver the final blow to more than one bot, the secret enemy will not spawn, and you will miss the requirement for the second drawing.

### Step-by-Step Secret Unlock Guide

1.  **Engage the First Bot:** Reduce its health using any method, but ensure the final, killing blow is dealt with your **Pistol**.
2.  **Engage the Second Bot:** Soften the target, then switch to the **Shockwave Gun** for the finishing shot.
3.  **Engage the Third Bot:** Use your remaining tools to lower its HP, then finish it off using a **Hack** or a specific environmental interaction.
4.  **Confirm the Spawn:** Once all three are destroyed using unique finishers, the elevator will stall, and the secret "Intruder" bot will emerge.

> **Warning:** Be careful when using area-of-effect (AoE) attacks. If an accidental explosion finishes off two bots at once, the game counts it as the same damage source, and the secret enemy requirement will fail.

## Weapon Management and Combat Strategy

To successfully claim your **pragmata demo rewards**, you need to understand the nuances of the demo's combat system. The "Multi-Hack" ability is particularly useful, but it can be a double-edged sword when trying to unlock secrets.

| Weapon/Ability | Best Usage | Final Blow Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pistol** | Single target precision. | **High** - Easy to control for the first kill. |
| **Shockwave Gun** | Crowd control and staggering. | **Medium** - High damage, watch the blast radius. |
| **Multi-Hack** | Dealing chip damage to multiple foes. | **Low** - Best used for softening targets, not finishing. |
| **Melee Strike** | Finishing off weakened enemies. | **High** - Very reliable for controlled kills. |

As long as you do not finish off an enemy with more than one single damage source shared by another, you are still on track. It is often a good idea to focus on one enemy at a time rather than spreading damage across all three bots simultaneously. This prevents accidental kills from stray projectiles or secondary explosions.

## Advanced Tips for Demo Completion

If you are struggling to manage the three-weapon requirement, consider these advanced tactics used by the community to secure their rewards:

*   **Chip Damage Control:** Use the Pistol for the first 90% of every bot's health. This ensures you have total control over the damage output. Only switch to your "finisher" weapon when the bot's health bar begins to flash red.
*   **Multi-Hack Safety:** Contrary to some early rumors, damage from the Multi-Hack does *not* count as "breaking" the requirement, provided the Hack itself doesn't land the killing blow on more than one enemy. You can safely use it to weaken the group.
*   **Restarting Quickly:** If you realize you've accidentally killed two bots with the same weapon, you don't need to finish the demo. You can quickly reload the "Elevator Approach" checkpoint from the main menu to try again.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the pragmata demo rewards on any difficulty?**

A: Yes, Diana’s drawings can be unlocked on both Normal and Hard difficulties. However, the Prototype Suit Skin is strictly tied to completing the demo on the highest available difficulty setting.

**Q: Do I need to finish the demo after getting the secret drawing for it to save?**

A: Yes. It is highly recommended that you reach the "Thank You For Playing" screen to ensure your save data is updated with the drawing. Closing the application immediately after the boss fight may result in lost progress.

**Q: How many weapons are available in the demo?**

A: There are three primary weapon types available for the elevator challenge: the Standard Pistol, the Shockwave Gun, and the Hack tool. You must utilize all three as finishers to trigger the secret enemy.

**Q: Is there a reward for finding all the lore terminals?**

A: While there is no specific "item" reward for lore terminals in the demo, collecting all of them unlocks a hidden dialogue sequence between the protagonist and Diana during the final cutscene.
