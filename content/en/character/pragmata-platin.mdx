### The Three-Stage Roadmap

1.  **Stage 1: Story Completion & Collectibles:** Play through the game on Casual or Normal. Focus on enjoying the narrative, learning the combat mechanics, and gathering as many of the 100+ collectibles as possible.
2.  **Stage 2: The Unknown Signal (Post-Game):** Enter the post-game mode to clean up missing trophies, complete training simulations, and unlock the "True Ending" requirements.
3.  **Stage 3: Lunatic Difficulty Run:** Start a fresh save on Lunatic. This is the hardest part of the **pragmata platin** journey, as it requires perfect execution and cannot be done in New Game Plus.

## Stage 1: Navigating the Cradle

During your initial playthrough, the primary goal is to familiarize yourself with the environment. Pragmata is structured around several distinct sectors within the Cradle, each offering unique challenges and specific trophies.

### Sector 1: Solar Power Plant
The first major area serves as an introduction to the game's puzzles and the 3D printing technology. While here, focus on the following:
*   **Wellspotted Trophy:** Locate and shoot the mini-cabin figurine. These are hidden throughout the game and are essential for the collectible hunt.
*   **Holo Walls:** Keep an eye out for flickering textures. Finding your first Holo Wall—a hidden room containing rare items—will trigger a trophy.

### Sector 2: Mass Production Array
This sector resembles a dystopian New York City and introduces more complex combat scenarios.
*   **Red Zones:** These are locked combat arenas requiring a Red Key. Completing your first Red Zone grants the "Red Zoned and Loving It" trophy.
*   **Sweeper Bots:** These rare enemies spawn in specific locations. You must use Diana’s hacking ability to disable them before they escape to earn the "You're Not Getting Away That Easy" trophy.
*   **Limb Recycler:** Utilize the environmental lasers in the indoor sections to defeat three enemies simultaneously for the "Round Them Up" achievement.

### Sector 3: The Terra Dome
A sprawling forest-like environment that relies heavily on "Gen Chips" for progression.
*   **Gen Chip Puzzles:** You must find three Gen Chips to 3D print a path forward. 
*   **Top Shelf Item:** By this point, you should have found Pure Aluminum. Use it at a printer to upgrade a weapon to its maximum level.

## Stage 2: The Unknown Signal and Cleanup

Once the credits roll for the first time, you will unlock "The Unknown Signal." This is a free-roam mode that allows you to revisit any sector. This stage is crucial for the **pragmata platin** as it involves the more repetitive, grind-heavy tasks.

### Training Simulations and Home Base
The Home Base (The Shelter) serves as your hub. Several trophies are tied specifically to your interactions with Diana and the robot assistant, Cabin.

| Activity | Trophy Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Socialize** | First Words | Talk to Diana at the Home Base for the first time. |
| **Gifting** | Generous Soul | Give Diana a present found in the world. |
| **Mini-Games** | Hide and Seek | Play and win a game of hide-and-seek with Diana. |
| **Stamps** | Blackout | Complete a full Cabin Stamp Board by finishing side tasks. |
| **Simulations** | Lunar Challenge | Complete all 30 training simulations available at the terminal. |

> **💡 Tip:** Complete all training simulations before starting your Lunatic run. The rewards from these simulations unlock the "Can't Stop, Won't Stop" trophy, which requires you to print every weapon and hacking node in the game.

## Stage 3: Conquering Lunatic Difficulty

The final hurdle for the **pragmata platin** is the "Lunar Supremacy" trophy. Unlike many modern games, Pragmata requires a **Fresh Save** for its highest difficulty. You will not have your endgame upgrades or weapons, making the early sections significantly more dangerous.

### Survival Strategies for Lunatic
*   **Resource Management:** Do not waste 3D printing materials on cosmetic items or low-tier ammo. Focus entirely on health pods and high-damage hacking nodes.
*   **Diana's Hacking:** On Lunatic, enemies have significantly more health. You must rely on Diana's hacking to expose cores. Trying to brute-force enemies with standard gunfire will lead to a quick death.
*   **Avoid the "Dead Filament":** In the final stages, the black goo (Dead Filament) deals massive damage over time. Always prioritize "Cleansing" moves to keep the area safe.

### Final Boss Strategy: Eight
The fight against Eight is notorious for its difficulty spikes. To succeed on Lunatic, follow this optimized loop:
1.  **Overdrive Entry:** Enter the fight with a full ultimate charge. Activate it immediately to gain invulnerability and high damage.
2.  **Laser Focus:** Aim the laser gun at Eight's head while holding R2.
3.  **Simultaneous Hacking:** While firing, spam the hack command. This charges your ultimate while simultaneously dealing damage.
4.  **Overheat Management:** If Eight overheats, perform the special melee attack to deal massive burst damage and reset her attack patterns.

## Essential Combat and Hacking Mechanics

To earn the **pragmata platin**, you must master the synergy between Hugh and Diana. Diana is not just a companion; she is your primary offensive tool.

*   **Confuse Hacks:** Use these on shielded enemies (Large Bots) to deactivate their defenses. This unlocks the "Can I Borrow That?" trophy.
*   **Deletion Protocol:** This ultimate ability is unlocked late in the story. It is essential for clearing crowds of robots infected with Dead Filament.
*   **3D Printing on the Fly:** Learn to print ammo during boss fights. The "Gigantic Robot" and "Lunar Digger" fights require constant fire, and running out of ammo is the most common cause of failure.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The "Lunar Digger" worm in the Lumen Mines can instant-kill you on Lunatic if you remain on the ground too long. Always use the crates and elevated platforms to stay mobile.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there any missable trophies in Pragmata?**

A: No. After completing the main story, you can access "The Unknown Signal" mode, which allows you to revisit all areas, collect missing items, and complete any combat-specific trophies you may have skipped.

**Q: Can I use New Game Plus for the Lunatic difficulty trophy?**

A: Unfortunately, no. The "Lunar Supremacy" trophy specifically requires a fresh playthrough. This means you must start from zero without your previous upgrades, making it the most challenging part of the **pragmata platin** hunt.

**Q: How many collectibles are required for the Platinum?**

A: There are over 100 collectibles in total, including Mini-Cabins, Holo Walls, and REM Presents. You will need to find all of them to complete the various collection-based trophies and unlock all 3D printing blueprints.

**Q: What is the fastest way to farm materials for upgrades?**

A: The best way to farm materials is by replaying Red Zones in the Mass Production Array and Terra Dome sectors. These areas offer high enemy density and guaranteed material drops upon completion.
