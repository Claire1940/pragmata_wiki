## The Synchronization System Explained

The core of Pragmata’s narrative progression is the Synchronization Level. This percentage represents the mechanical and emotional resonance between the protagonist, Hugh, and the mysterious young girl, Diana. Your actions during exploration, combat efficiency, and even downtime activities directly influence which of the **pragmata enden** you will trigger during the final sequence.

A high sync level doesn't just change the dialogue; it alters the physical outcome of the final boss encounter. If you neglect the bond with Diana, the game defaults to a more somber conclusion. Conversely, players who take the time to interact with the environment and fulfill Diana's requests will find a much more hopeful resolution.

| Sync Action | Sync Boost | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Interact with Holographic Cat** | High | Every Rest Point |
| **Complete Combat Without Damage** | Medium | Every Encounter |
| **Find Digital Echo Fragments** | Medium | Per Fragment |
| **Protect Diana during Swarms** | Very High | Scripted Events |

> **💡 Expert Tip:** Make it a priority to find the holographic cat at every single rest point. Petting this digital feline is the most consistent way to max out your sync level before the point of no return.

## Pragmata Enden: All Conclusion Requirements

There are three primary conclusions to the main story, followed by a secret scene available only to those who complete the post-game content. The **pragmata enden** you achieve is determined by two main variables: your Sync Percentage and the number of Data Fragments collected.

### 1. Lunar Paradox (The Standard Ending)
This is the "low sync" ending and is the most common result for players focusing primarily on the main objectives. In the Lunar Paradox, the synchronization between Hugh and Diana is insufficient to fully override the failing lunar AI. As a result, the astronaut must stay behind on the lunar surface to stabilize the reactor manually, allowing Diana to be launched back to Earth in a solo escape pod. While heroic, it leaves the fate of Hugh's digital consciousness in question.

### 2. Earthbound Sacrifice (The Bittersweet Ending)
To reach this ending, you need a high synchronization level (over 70%), but you must have failed to collect all 12 data fragments. In this version of the story, Diana successfully uses her powers to restore the Earth's failing atmosphere from the lunar station. However, the energy required for this feat—and the lack of complete data to stabilize the process—costs the astronaut his digital consciousness. Hugh survives physically, but his mind is lost to the digital void.

### 3. New Dawn (The True Ending)
The New Dawn ending is the ultimate goal for completionists. It requires a 100% Sync Level and the retrieval of all 12 Memory Cubes. In this scenario, the full data set allows Diana and Hugh to work in perfect harmony. Not only is the Earth restored, but the lunar colony itself begins a process of "rebirth." A secret post-credits scene is unlocked here, showing the first signs of greenery returning to the moon's domed cities.

| Ending Name | Sync Level Req. | Data Fragments Req. | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lunar Paradox** | < 40% | Any | Diana escapes; Hugh stays behind. |
| **Earthbound Sacrifice** | > 70% | < 12 | Earth saved; Hugh’s mind is lost. |
| **New Dawn (True)** | **100%** | **12** | Total Restoration; Colony Rebirth. |

## How to Find All 12 Memory Cubes

The 12 Memory Cubes are essential for the True Ending. These items are hidden within "Digital Echoes"—glitches in the environment that represent the memories of the lunar colony's former inhabitants. Many of these are tucked away in the Abandoned City sector, requiring the player to use Diana’s scanning ability to reveal hidden paths.

1. **Sector 1 (Residential):** Behind the holographic billboard in the central plaza.
2. **Sector 1 (Medical):** Inside the locked quarantine ward (requires level 2 bypass).
3. **Sector 2 (Industrial):** At the very top of the cooling tower, accessible via jump-thrusters.
4. **Sector 2 (Storage):** Hidden behind a stack of crates in the drone manufacturing wing.
5. **Sector 3 (Research):** Within the AI core chamber after the mini-boss fight.

Finding these cubes is a prerequisite for seeing the best **pragmata enden** options. If you miss even one, the New Dawn ending will remain locked, forcing you to rely on a secondary save or New Game Plus.

## Post-Game: Unknown Signal Mode

Once you have cleared the game once, you unlock "Unknown Signal Mode." This is a high-difficulty challenge mode that introduces 10 specific trials across the different lunar sectors. Completing these challenges is the only way to unlock the final secret scene attached to the **pragmata enden** lore.

To complete this mode, you must:
*   Achieve 100% collectibles in every sector (all files, cubes, and cat interactions).
*   Defeat the "Buffed" versions of the game's five main bosses.
*   Complete the "Ghost Run" of the Abandoned City without taking damage.

Completing all 10 challenges rewards you with a **Special Mod**. When you equip this mod and replay the final boss encounter on your True Ending save, you will unlock an extra alternate ending scene that reveals the true origin of the "Unknown Signal" that started the game's events.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Unknown Signal bosses are significantly more aggressive than those in the standard campaign. Ensure your combat suits are fully upgraded before attempting these encounters.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get all pragmata enden in a single playthrough?**

A: Not easily. While you can use "Chapter Select" after finishing the game to go back and find missing Memory Cubes, your Sync Level is tracked throughout the entire run. It is often easier to aim for the True Ending first and then intentionally lower your sync in a second run for the other variations.

**Q: Does petting the cat actually change the ending?**

A: Yes, indirectly. Petting the holographic cat at rest points provides a significant boost to your Sync Level. Since a high Sync Level is required for the "Earthbound Sacrifice" and "New Dawn" conclusions, missing these interactions can prevent you from seeing the best **pragmata enden**.

**Q: What happens if I lose Diana during a combat sequence?**

A: If Diana’s health bar reaches zero, the synchronization level drops significantly. If this happens too often, you will be locked out of the 100% Sync requirement for the True Ending. Always prioritize her safety over aggressive DPS.

**Q: Where do I equip the Special Mod for the secret scene?**

A: The Special Mod is equipped in the "Suit Customization" menu at any rest point. Once you have earned it by completing the 10 Unknown Signal challenges, make sure it is active before you enter the final elevator to the Moon's Surface.
