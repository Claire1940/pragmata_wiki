## Core Pragmata Supporting Characters and AI Entities

The narrative of Pragmata is intentionally intimate, focusing on a "two-in-one" character dynamic. However, the world is far from empty. The supporting cast is divided between physical companions, digital consciousnesses, and the remnants of a corporate hierarchy that once thrived on the lunar surface.

### Diana: The Android Companion
While Diana is technically a co-protagonist, her role shifts into the realm of **pragmata supporting characters** during high-intensity combat sequences. As an android with unique hacking capabilities, she provides the utility required to bypass "Cradle" security. Her presence is not merely for flavor; she is a mechanical requirement for progression.

### The Lunar Station AI (Cradle Security)
Acting as the primary "supporting antagonist," the Lunar Station AI—often referred to as the "Cradle Overlord"—serves as the voice of the environment. It views Hugh and Diana as "unregistered guests" and a violation of security protocols. This entity isn't just a faceless voice; it manifests through various robotic shells and environmental hazards throughout the game.

## Gameplay Mechanics: How Supporting Characters Assist Hugh

The synergy between Hugh and the **pragmata supporting characters** defines the combat loop. Capcom has introduced a "Two-in-One" approach where you must manage two sets of abilities simultaneously. Diana’s support abilities are categorized into three main branches: Hacking, Distraction, and Direct Support.

### Diana’s Support Skill Tree

1.  **Electronic Pulse (EMP):** A short-range burst that temporarily paralyzes robotic enemies, allowing Hugh to follow up with heavy melee attacks.
2.  **Logic Bomb:** Diana can infect a nearby drone, turning it against its peers for a limited duration.
3.  **Nano-Repair:** A passive ability that slowly restores Hugh’s suit integrity when they are in close proximity.

## Factions and Enemy Variety

While the cast of friendly **pragmata supporting characters** is small, the variety of AI-driven entities is vast. These entities are governed by the Cradle Security Protocol and represent different "tiers" of the station's defensive hierarchy.

1.  **Worker Drones:** Generally non-aggressive unless provoked or overridden by the Central AI.
2.  **Stalker Units:** Specialized combat robots designed to track "unregistered guests" across the lunar surface.
3.  **The Warden:** A massive, multi-stage boss that acts as the physical embodiment of the station's will.

> **Warning:** Attempting to fight Stalker Units without Diana's hacking support is extremely difficult. Always ensure her "Logic Bomb" is off cooldown before engaging.

## The Quest for Earth: Narrative Stakes

The driving force for all **pragmata supporting characters** is the dream of Earth. For Hugh, it is a return to a forgotten home. For Diana, it is an existential quest to see if her programming can exist in a natural world. This shared goal creates a unique dynamic where the characters are constantly testing their trust in one another.

Capcom has emphasized that the relationship between these characters will evolve based on player choices and performance in combat. While there are no traditional "romance" paths, the level of cooperation between Hugh and Diana influences the ending you receive in 2026.

## FAQ

**Q: Who are the main pragmata supporting characters?**

A: The primary supporting character is Diana, an android who assists the protagonist, Hugh. Other supporting entities include the Lunar Station AI (the antagonist) and various "Echo" characters found through audio logs like Dr. Aris Thorne.

**Q: Can you play as the supporting characters in Pragmata?**

A: Pragmata features a "two-in-one" control scheme. While you primarily control Hugh, you simultaneously issue commands to Diana, making her a playable support character rather than a traditional NPC.

**Q: Is there a co-op mode for the supporting characters?**

A: As of the 2026 release, Pragmata is primarily a single-player experience where the player manages both characters. However, certain challenge modes allow for a second player to take direct control of Diana's hacking abilities.

**Q: Do the pragmata supporting characters have different outfits or skins?**

A: Yes, players can unlock different "Shells" for Diana and suit upgrades for Hugh by finding blueprints hidden throughout the Cradle facility. These provide both aesthetic changes and minor stat boosts.
