## The Cabin Stamp Card System

The primary method for obtaining new threads in the game is through the Cabin Stamp Card system. As you progress through the five major sectors, you will naturally level up your Shelter. Each level granted to your Shelter unlocks a new Stamp Card, for a total of five cards. To claim rewards on these cards, you must spend Cabin Coins, which are primarily earned by completing training simulation missions.

| Shelter Level | Unlock Requirement | Primary Reward Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Complete Sector 1 | Early-game tactical gear |
| **Level 2** | Complete Sector 2 | Specialized Exolayer variants |
| **Level 3** | Complete Sector 3 | Retro-futuristic and stealth suits |
| **Level 4** | Complete Sector 4 | High-mobility lunar outfits |
| **Level 5** | Complete Post-game Signal | "Shadow" series and endgame gear |

> **💡 Tip:** You often earn more Cabin Coins than there are slots on a single card. Do not hesitate to spend them early, as "Blacking Out" a card (filling every slot) is often the only way to unlock the most prestigious outfits.

## How to Farm Cabin Coins Efficiently

Since most of the **pragmata all costumes** are locked behind the Stamp Cards, you will need a massive influx of Cabin Coins. The best way to farm these is through the Training Sim missions found at the Shelter terminal.

1. **Focus on "Perfect" clears:** Higher rankings in sims yield significantly more coins.
2. **Replay Sector Bosses:** Once you have better gear, replaying early bosses is a fast way to bank currency.
3. **Daily Challenges:** Check the terminal for rotating objectives that offer coin bonuses.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there any missable outfits in the quest for Pragmata all costumes?**

A: No outfits are strictly missable. You can always return to the Shelter to spend coins on previous Stamp Cards, and post-game missions can be replayed as many times as necessary to secure the rewards.

**Q: Does the "Lunatic" difficulty unlock any secret suits?**

A: While the developers have not officially confirmed a unique suit for Lunatic difficulty, completing the game on the highest setting typically grants a special emblem and may be tied to a hidden "Type C" variant of the final boss rewards.

**Q: Can I mix and match pieces from different costumes?**

A: Currently, the game treats outfits as full sets. You cannot mix Hugh's Astroclassic helmet with his Legion Zero body armor, though this is a highly requested feature for future updates.

**Q: Do costumes provide any gameplay buffs?**

A: Most costumes are purely cosmetic. However, certain "Exolayer" variants for Hugh provide minor bonuses to defense or stamina recovery in specific training simulations, though these effects are negligible in the main story missions.
