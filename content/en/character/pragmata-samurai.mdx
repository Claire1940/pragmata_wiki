Pragmata represents a bold new frontier for Capcom, blending high-concept science fiction with the polished action gameplay the studio is known for. Players take on the role of Hugh, a technician navigating a desolate lunar facility alongside a mysterious young girl named Diana. As you progress, you will encounter the **pragmata samurai**—advanced ninja-style robots that challenge your reflexes and strategic thinking. This title eschews the traditional remake or sequel path, offering a fresh IP that feels like a spiritual successor to the robust single-player adventures of previous console generations while utilizing 2026 cutting-edge technology.

Understanding the relationship between the **pragmata samurai** enemies and your own arsenal is key to surviving the moon's hostile environments. The game balances over-the-shoulder shooting with a deep hacking system managed by Diana, ensuring that every encounter requires more than just mindless firing. ## Core Gameplay & Movement Systems

Controlling Hugh feels deliberate and weighty, often drawing comparisons to classic survival horror protagonists but with significantly enhanced mobility. Hugh is a "chunky" spaceman, yet his suit is equipped with advanced thrusters that allow for fluid navigation through the gleaming corridors of the lunar station. Mastering these movement options is essential for positioning yourself against faster enemies.

| Maneuver | Description | Tactical Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Thruster Dash** | A quick burst of speed in any direction. | Dodging incoming projectiles or closing gaps. |
| **Hover** | Temporary suspension in mid-air using thrusters. | Gaining a height advantage or avoiding floor hazards. |
| **Air Dash** | A secondary dash performed while in the air. | Extending jump distance or repositioning mid-combat. |
| **Perfect Dodge** | A frame-perfect evade that triggers a slowdown. | Slows time to allow for precise hacking or headshots. |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't neglect the "Hover" mechanic during boss fights. Many ground-based shockwave attacks can be completely negated by staying airborne for a few extra seconds.

## Mastering the Hacking Combat Loop

The combat in Pragmata is a symbiotic relationship between Hugh's firepower and Diana's technical prowess. Most high-tier enemies, including the lethal **pragmata samurai** variants, utilize energy shields that make them impervious to standard ballistic damage. To defeat them, you must engage in a hacking mini-game while simultaneously dodging attacks.

When you aim at a shielded foe, a hacking interface appears on the side of the screen. Diana navigates this digital maze to lower shields or expose a robot's chassis. However, hacking isn't just a "gate" to damage; it is a strategic layer. By navigating Diana through optional nodes in the mini-game, you can trigger specific status effects:

*   **Damage Nodes:** Increase the raw damage Hugh deals once the shield drops.
*   **Stun Nodes:** Temporarily paralyze the robot, preventing it from counter-attacking.
*   **Freeze Nodes:** Stop the enemy's movement entirely, allowing for easy weak-point targeting.
*   **Overheat Nodes:** Build up a meter that leads to a powerful cinematic finisher.

## The Pragmata Samurai and Enemy Variety

As you delve deeper into the moon's laboratories and mines, the enemy variety shifts from basic ambling robots to highly specialized combat units. The **pragmata samurai** (or ninja robots) represent a significant difficulty spike. These enemies move with incredible speed and often require the "Perfect Dodge" mechanic to create an opening for Diana to begin hacking.

| Enemy Category | Characteristics | Recommended Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Laborer Drones** | Slow, predictable melee attacks. | Standard shooting; keep distance. |
| **Shielded Sentries** | Stationary or slow-moving with heavy shields. | Priority target for Diana's hacking mini-game. |
| **Ninja/Samurai Units** | High mobility, melee focus, cloaking abilities. | Use Snare Traps and time your Perfect Dodges. |
| **Metal Gear Types** | Massive, multi-legged tanks with heavy artillery. | Focus on joint weak points and use Rocket Launchers. |

Defeating these enemies provides the materials necessary to utilize the game's unique 3D printing lore. In Pragmata, humanity discovered an element that allows for the perfect replication of any object, which serves as the narrative backbone for your gear progression.

## Gear Arsenal & Tactical Management

Inventory space in Pragmata is limited, forcing you to make hard choices about what to bring into the field. Your primary weapon has recharging shots, but heavier ordnance like shotguns and rocket launchers rely on scarce ammo found through exploration.

1.  **Standard Issue Pistol:** Your bread and butter. Best used for finishing off enemies whose shields have been dropped.
2.  **The Snare Launcher:** Fires a trap that entangles fast-moving targets like the **pragmata samurai**. Essential for crowd control.
3.  **Charged Railgun:** High damage but requires a long wind-up. Best used after Diana has triggered a Stun node.
4.  **Defensive Drones:** Temporary automated turrets that provide cover fire while you focus on the hacking mini-game.
5.  **Decoy Launcher:** Creates a holographic distraction to draw fire away from Hugh and Diana.

> **Warning:** Ammo management is critical. If you run out of heavy shells during a boss encounter, you'll be forced to rely entirely on the slow-recharging primary weapon, making the fight significantly longer and more dangerous.

## Progression and the Lunar Base

Between missions, you return to a base of operations that serves as your hub. This base is not just a menu; it is a living space that evolves as you find items in the world. You can bring back "Earth relics" to teach Diana about human culture, which gradually transforms the stark laboratory into a cozy, cluttered playroom.

The progression system is multi-tiered. You can spend collected resources to upgrade Hugh’s armor plating and thruster capacity, or focus on Diana’s hacking speed and node variety. There is also a "Bingo Card" system—a tongue-in-cheek take on modern battle passes—where you cash in found coins for randomized but helpful rewards like new skins and weapon mods.

### Upgrading Your Capabilities

| Upgrade Type | Effect | Resource Required |
|---|---|---|
| **Armor Reinforcement** | Increases Hugh's total health and damage resistance. | Lunar Scrap & 3D Blueprints |
| **Hacking Bandwidth** | Allows Diana to bypass more complex shields faster. | Encrypted Data Segments |
| **Thruster Efficiency** | Reduces the stamina cost of dashing and hovering. | Energy Cells |
| **Weapon Nodes** | Adds permanent buffs (crit chance, reload speed) to guns. | Bingo Reward Tokens |

For players looking for extra challenges, the base offers a "Training Room" simulation. These are reminiscent of VR missions from classic stealth-action titles. Completing these high-difficulty challenges is the best way to earn top-tier currency for the Bingo system and unlock the most powerful weapon variants. ## FAQ

**Q: How do I beat the fast-moving ninja robots?**

A: The **pragmata samurai** style enemies are best handled by using the Snare Launcher to slow them down. Once they are trapped, use Diana’s hacking to disable their cloaking or shields, then follow up with high-damage weapons like the shotgun.

**Q: What happens if Diana gets hit during combat?**

A: Diana acts as your support and is generally invulnerable to standard damage while on your back. However, certain enemy attacks can "disrupt" her hacking. If she is separated from you, you must retrieve her quickly, as you lose access to all hacking abilities and ultimate attacks while she is away.

**Q: Is Pragmata an open-world game?**

A: No, Pragmata is a primarily linear action game. However, it features large, interconnected zones with plenty of hidden nooks, crannies, and challenge rooms that reward exploration and backtracking once you unlock new abilities.

**Q: How long does it take to finish the main story?**

A: A standard playthrough takes approximately 12 hours. If you aim to complete all training simulations, find every Earth relic for Diana, and 100% the Bingo progression cards, you can expect to spend upwards of 20-25 hours in the game.
